心灵哲学

你知道意识是什么：你生活在灵魂之地

Nicholas Humphrey·Aeon·2026年5月17日·14分钟阅读·原文

在他的小说《企鹅岛》（Penguin Island，1908）中，阿纳托尔·法朗士（Anatole France）讲述了一个绝妙的故事：一位失明的老修道士从不列塔尼（Brittany）出发前往赫布里底群岛（Hebrides），最终登上一座只有企鹅栖息的岛屿。虽然这些鸟说着奇怪的语言，他却认定它们一定是人类。于是他着手为它们施洗。

In his novel _Penguin Island_ (1908), Anatole France spins a wonderful tale about a blind old monk who sets off from Brittany on a mission to the Hebrides and lands on an island inhabited only by penguins. Though the birds speak a strange language, he assumes they must be human beings. So he proceeds to baptise them.

当这个消息传到天国，引起了轩然大波。上帝本人也感到为难。他召集了一群教士和博士，请他们就一个微妙的问题发表意见：这些鸟是否必须被赋予灵魂。这不仅是一个理论问题。「基督教的状态，」圣科尼利厄斯（St Cornelius）指出，「对一只企鹅来说并非没有严重的不便……鸟类在很多方面的习性与教会的诫命相悖……」经过漫长的讨论，他们达成了一个折中方案。受洗的企鹅确实将被授予灵魂——但根据圣凯瑟琳（St Catherine）的建议，它们的灵魂应该是小号的。

When the news of this reaches heaven, it causes a major stir. God himself is embarrassed. He gathers an assembly of clerics and doctors, and asks them for an opinion on the delicate question of whether the birds must now be given souls. It is a matter of more than theoretical importance. 'The Christian state,' St Cornelius points out, 'is not without serious inconveniences for a penguin … The habits of birds are, in many points, contrary to the commandments of the Church …'After lengthy discussion, they settle on a compromise. The baptised penguins are indeed to be granted souls – but, on St Catherine's recommendation, their souls are to be of small size.

对企鹅们来说，灵魂是一份额外的奖赏。正如17世纪的哲学家兼科学家勒内·笛卡尔（René Descartes）所解释的，非人类动物一般来说，在自然状态下，不过是没有灵魂的机器。这就是一只笛卡尔式企鹅的素描，连一丝灵魂的痕迹都没有。

For the penguins, souls were an unexpected bonus. As René Descartes, the philosopher-scientist of the 17th century, had explained, nonhuman animals in general, in a state of nature, are mere soulless machines. Here's a sketch of a Cartesian penguin, without even a smidgen of a soul.

笛卡尔认为人类也是一种机器。但他认为，谢天谢地，上帝在人类身上安排了灵魂作为标准配置。在婴儿期早期，人脑的物质实体通过松果体与心灵的独立实体建立了沟通：「广延之物」（res extensa）与「思维之物」（res cogitans）相结合。由此产生的意识为灵魂奠定了基础。

Descartes believed that humans too are machines of a kind. But he held that, with humans, thankfully, God has arranged the addition of a soul as standard practice. Early in infancy, the material substance of the human brain is put into communication via the pineal gland with the separate substance of the mind: _res extensa_ (extended stuff) is joined by _res cogitans_ (thinking stuff)_._ The consciousness that results lays the foundation for the soul.

今天，我们可能觉得这种「实体二元论」（substance dualism）荒唐可笑。在笛卡尔之后一个半世纪，伟大的法国散文家德尼·狄德罗（Denis Diderot）就确实在嘲笑。「一个还算聪明的人，」他在1780年写道，「在书中这样开头：『人……由两种不同的实体组成，即灵魂和身体。』……我差点合上这本书。哦！荒唐的作者……你不知道你所称的灵魂是什么，更不知道它们是如何结合的。」

Today, we may think such 'substance dualism' laughable. A century and a half after Descartes, the great French essayist Denis Diderot was certainly laughing. 'A tolerably clever man,' he wrote in 1780, 'began his book with these words: _"Man … is composed of two distinct substances, the soul and the body_._"_ … I nearly shut the book. Oh! ridiculous writer … you do not know what it is that you call soul, less still how they are united.'

然而，大约在1838年，查尔斯·达尔文（Charles Darwin）显然没有领会到这个笑话。「灵魂，」他在一本青年时代的科学笔记本中写道，「经所有人同意是附加的，动物没有。」

Yet, by around 1838, Charles Darwin had apparently not seen the joke. 'The soul,' he wrote in one of his youthful scientific notebooks, 'by the consent of all is superadded, animals not got it.'

我们该笑吗？还是说作为当代科学家应该有所理解？在我看来，事情并不像我们许多人愿意相信的那样非此即彼。相反，任何客观审视人类自然史的人都可能得出结论：笛卡尔和年轻的达尔文基本上说对了。人类学、心理学、宗教、哲学、艺术，所有这些都表明，拥有灵魂——建立在意识基础之上的灵魂——是人类存在不可或缺的一部分。也许荒唐的倒是狄德罗。

Should we laugh? Or should we as contemporary scientists show some understanding? As I see it, it is not as clear-cut as many of us would like to believe. To the contrary, anyone who looks objectively at the natural history of human beings might well conclude that Descartes and the young Darwin were pretty much on target. Anthropology, psychology, religion, philosophy, art, all suggest that the possession of a soul – founded on consciousness – is part and parcel of being human_._ Perhaps it was Diderot who was being ridiculous.

首先，我们确实知道我们所称的灵魂是什么。按照传统，灵魂——你的灵魂——我接下来将用第二人称叙述，你会明白原因——就是你存在核心处的精神。它就是你，你有意识的自我，你私密思想和感受的主体。它是你认识到自己所是的那个人——也是别人当作你来对待的那个人。

To start with, we _do_ know what it is that we call soul. By tradition the soul, _your_ soul – I'll turn to the second person, you'll see why as I go on – is nothing less than the spirit at the core of your being. It's _you_, your conscious self_,_ the subject of your private thoughts and feelings. It's the _person_ you know yourself to be – and the person other people treat you as being_._

你的这个灵魂显然是随你的身体一起产生的。但它同样显然不是由身体物质构成的。当你身体沉睡时，它在夜晚中持续存在。当你做梦时，它游离出去，离开你的身体。它不会像你的身体那样衰老和衰败。期望它能比你的身体活得更久，并非不合理。

This soul of yours has obviously come into existence with your body. Yet equally obviously it's not made of bodily stuff. It lasts through the night when your body sleeps. It wanders off and leaves your body when you dream. It does not grow old and decrepit, as your body does. It's not unreasonable to hope it will be able to outlast your body's death.

而且，与狄德罗的说法相反，我们确实对灵魂和身体如何结合有一个相当清楚的认识。灵魂与身体的结合方式恰恰就是笛卡尔所认为的那样：作为一种额外的资源，一种控制性的影响。当你清醒时，灵魂与身体交融，赋予你的生命目的和方向。但它有自己的生命。它能退隐，能请假休息。它能与其他灵魂会面，分享故事，规划旅程。

What's more, contra Diderot, we _do_ have a pretty good idea of how soul and body are united. The soul is united to the body in just the way Descartes thought it was: as an added resource, a controlling influence. The soul is intermingled with the body while you are awake, giving your life purpose and direction. But it has a life of its own. It's able to retire and take shore leave. It can meet up with other souls, share stories and plan voyages.

在重要的意义上，你的灵魂是人类共同体将你塑造成的样子

In an important sense, your soul is what the human community has made of you

在世界各地，人们都有这种概念。灵魂是我们关于人类意味着什么的显见形象的一部分。笛卡尔无疑把握到了某种重要的东西。然而，在认同他的观点时，我是否让持怀疑态度的读者感到不安了？你是否差点合上了这篇文章？以下是我要补充的重要限定条件：这个人类灵魂不是由上帝安放在我们体内的，但同样，也不是由基因选择写入大脑的。不，事实是，我们的灵魂是由人类文化添加的——文化一如既往地与自然协同工作，但自由地创造出空中楼阁般非凡的杰作。

Everywhere in the world people have a conception of this kind. Souls are part of the manifest image we have of what it means to be a human being. Descartes undoubtedly grasped something important. Yet, in agreeing with him, am I making sceptical readers nervous? Have you nearly closed this essay? Here's the major qualification I want to add: this human soul has not been put on board by God but, equally, has not been written into the brain by genetic selection. No, the fact is, our souls have been added by _human culture –_ culture working with nature as it always does, but free to invent remarkable castles in the air.

说来奇怪，你的灵魂并不完全是你自己的。在重要的意义上，它是人类共同体将你塑造成的样子。它是他们对你是谁、你是什么——你在万事万物格局中处于什么位置——的看法。直白地说，就好像你拥有灵魂的方式，与你获得护照的方式差不多。你的灵魂是一种文化认可的保证，保障你的精神身份和权利。就像你的护照一样，它提升了你在自己和他人眼中的重要性。

Strange to say, your soul isn't exactly _yours at all._ In an important sense, it's what the human community has made of you. It's their view of just who and what you are – where you belong in the scheme of things. To put it bluntly, it is as if you've come to have a soul in rather the same way you've come to have a passport_._ Your soul is a kind of culturally sanctioned guarantee of your spiritual identity and rights_._ Like your passport, it adds to your significance in your own and others' eyes.

看看英国护照第一页上的话：「女王陛下之国务大臣以女王之名请求并要求所有相关人士允许持照人自由通行……」当我还是个男孩第一次拿到护照时，我花了很长时间欣赏它……我多了不起啊！而且，正如我曾想象可以依靠女王来维护我的权利，人们通常相信他们可以依靠某种形式的神奇高级权威来支撑他们的精神地位。「天主教会在尘世通往上帝之桥以救主之名请求并要求。」「莫霍克部落（Mohawk）的首席萨满以祖先之名请求并要求。」

Just look at that first page of a British passport, for example: 'Her Britannic Majesty's Secretary of State requests and requires in the name of Her Majesty all those whom it may concern to allow the bearer to pass freely …' When I got my first passport as a boy, I spent ages admiring it… what a fine fellow am I! And, just as I imagined I could rely on the queen to uphold my rights, people commonly believe they can rely on some form of magical higher authority to underpin their spiritual status. 'The Catholic Church's Bridge to God on Earth requests and requires in the name of the Saviour.' 'The Mohawk tribe's Chief Shaman requests and requires in the name of the Ancestors.'

你的灵魂也是你的私人所有物。没有其他任何人分享你的意识，因此没有其他人拥有和你一样的灵魂。所以，你还有另一种关于你重要性的保证，盖在扉页上——就在记录你个人信息的那个位置。不仅仅是你从镜子里看到的那张脸，还有它背后的东西：你的现象自我（phenomenal self），那个建立在——事实上，由——你对有意识感觉的体验所构成的自我。这就是每天早晨当你从睡眠中重新浮现、重新发现做你是什么感觉时闪现到生命中的那个自我：当你看到晨曦、听到鸟鸣、感受凉爽的床单、闻到咖啡的香气。当感觉重新填满你存在的湖泊。

Your soul is your private possession too. No other human shares your consciousness, and therefore no one else has the same soul you do. So, you have another kind of guarantee of your significance, stamped into the title page – right there where your personal details are recorded. Not just the face you see in a mirror, but what lies behind it: your _phenomenal self_, the self that's founded on – indeed, constituted by – your experience of conscious sensations. This is the self that blinks into life every morning, when you re-emerge from sleep to rediscover _what it's like to be you_: when you see the dawn, hear the birds, feel the cool sheets, smell the coffee. When sensations re-fill the lake of your being.

你的感觉是你自己的作为，具有生物特征标记，将它们与所有其他人的区分开来。没有人能像你那样去体验一朵虞美人的红、一条凤尾鱼的咸、一只蜜蜂蜇刺的痛。「不知我在夜间是否发生了变化，」《爱丽丝漫游奇境记》中的爱丽丝自问。「让我想想：今天早上起床时我还是同一个我吗？」但她不必担心。她只需感受她的感官在做什么，就能确认此刻做她的感觉延续了昨天的感觉。

Your sensations are _your doing_ and have biometric markers that distinguish them from all other people's. No one does the redness of a poppy, the saltiness of an anchovy, the paininess of a bee-sting the way you do. 'I wonder if I've been changed in the night,' Alice in Wonderland asked herself. 'Let me think: was I the same when I got up this morning?' But she need not have worried. She had only to feel what her senses were up to, to confirm that what it was like to be her now was a continuation of what it had been like yesterday.

「色彩，」画家瓦西里·康定斯基（Wassily Kandinsky）在1911年写道，「是一种直接影响灵魂的力量。色彩是键盘，眼睛是琴槌，灵魂是有着许多琴弦的钢琴。」但我不会这样表述。与其说感觉影响灵魂，不如说它们将灵魂锚定在你存在的磐石之上。你就在这里，活在我所称的「意识的浓稠时刻」之中。你在这里，是宇宙中一种焦点式的奇点。你在这里，处在这个私密的感受气泡中。你在这里，我们所有人都在这里，分享着那神秘的、不被分享的自我世界。

'Colour,' the painter Wassily Kandinsky wrote in 1911, 'is a power which directly influences the soul. Colour is the keyboard, the eyes are the hammers, the soul is the piano with many strings.' But I wouldn't put it that way. It's not so much that sensations influence the soul; it's that they anchor the soul to the rock of your existence. Here _you_ are_,_ living in what I've called 'the thick moment of consciousness'_. Here you are, a kind of focal singularity within the Universe. Here you are in this private bubble of feeling_. Here _you_ are and here _we all are_, sharing in the mysterious unshared world of self.

而哲学的麻烦恰恰从这里开始。没有什么比这个被感受到的自我更亲密的了，也没有什么比把它安置在物质的自然界描述中更困难的了。

And that is exactly where the philosophical trouble begins. Nothing is more intimate than this felt self, yet nothing is harder to place within a material account of nature.

对意识自我本质的困惑由来已久。「难问题」（the hard problem）这个说法属于大卫·查尔默斯（David Chalmers）。但我最近在《多马福音》（Gospel of Thomas）——一份可追溯到公元2世纪的科普特文本——中读到一段话，将这一问题的表述归于耶稣：「肉体若因灵而生，这是奇事。但灵若因肉体而生，这是奇事中的奇事。我惊叹于此，如此巨大的财富何以栖身于如此贫困之中。」

Puzzlement about the nature of the conscious self goes back a long way. The phrase 'the hard problem' belongs to David Chalmers. But I recently came across a passage in the Gospel of Thomas, a Coptic text dating to the 2nd century CE, that attributes the formulation of the problem to Jesus: 'If the flesh came into existence because of the spirit, it is a marvel. But if the spirit came into existence because of the body, it is a marvel of marvels. I wonder at this, how this great wealth made its home in this poverty.'

心灵如何因大脑而产生？意识的巨大财富如何从神经细胞电活动的贫瘠中涌现？

How does the mind come into existence because of the brain? How does the great wealth of conscious experience emerge from the poverty of the electrical activity of nerve cells?

对笛卡尔来说，答案显而易见：它不是这样的。当男孩的脚碰到火时，脚趾中的感觉器官拉动大脑中的弦线，引起脚的反射性回缩。但痛苦的感觉是另一回事。不是物质实体，而是纯粹的心灵之物。

For Descartes, it was plain obvious: it doesn't. When the boy's foot touches the fire, sense organs in his toes pull the strings in the brain that give rise to a reflex withdrawal of his foot. But the _feeling of pain_ is something else. Not physical matter but pure mind stuff.

然而，如果我们相信现代科学的故事，即心灵和大脑是同一回事，我们就有了问题。今天的哲学家确实倾向于假设心灵就是物质：「思维之物」实际上是「广延之物」的一种形式。于是，解释这如何可能的问题将他们逼到抓狂。哲学家科林·麦金（Colin McGinn）生动地表达道：

If, however, we believe the modern scientific story, that mind and brain are the same thing, we have a problem. Philosophers today do tend to assume that mind _is_ matter: _res cogitans_ is actually a form of _res extensa_. And then the problem of explaining how this can be so drives them to distraction. The philosopher Colin McGinn has put it colourfully:

难道你不觉得——我们也都觉得——[大脑]根本就不是那种能孕育意识的东西……你完全可以不加解释地断言空间从时间中产生，数字从饼干中产生，伦理从大黄中产生。

Isn't it perfectly evident to you, as it is to us, that [the brain] is just the wrong kind of thing to give birth to consciousness … You might as well assert, without further explanation, that space emerges from time, or numbers from biscuits, or ethics from rhubarb.

这确实很难。答案一定值得去了解。然而令人惊讶的是：放眼更广阔的世界，这个让哲学家们揪头发的问题在大多数普通人看来根本不是问题。相反，它是值得庆祝和自豪的理由。一个奥秘？是的，我正是如此——一朵绽放的奇迹！多了不起啊！而你，还有你，也是一样。

It's hard. The answer must be worth knowing. Yet here is the surprising thing: turn to the wider world, and the problem that has philosophers tearing their hair out is not seen by most ordinary human beings as a problem at all. Rather, it's a cause for celebration and for pride. A mystery? Yes, that's exactly what _I am_, a blooming marvel! What a fine fellow! And _you_, and _you_ too.

你的狗是否属于这个扩展的圈子？嗯，为什么不呢，至少在某种程度上？尽管有笛卡尔和基督教的教导，对许多人来说，这是下一个问题。这些自我概念是人类独有的吗？

Is _your dog_ part of the expanding circle? Well, why not, at least in a small way? Despite Descartes's and Christian teaching, for many people, it's the next question. Are these self-conceptions unique to human beings?

人类文化将感知能力转化为人格，将人格转化为某种神圣之物

Human culture turned sentience into personhood, and personhood into something sacred

如今，几乎所有人都理所当然地认为许多非人类动物物种拥有某种程度的感觉意识。这些动物也感觉到自己的存在。它们和我们一样，体验着作为感觉主体所带来的那种内在性、私密性和个体性。我在《感知》（Sentience，2022）一书中论证，这可能并不延伸至整个动物王国：例如可能不包括蠕虫、蜗牛或蚂蚁。但是，比如说，肯定包括所有哺乳动物和鸟类。

Nowadays, almost everyone takes it for granted that many nonhuman animal species possess some degree of sensory consciousness. These animals too feel that they exist. They, like us, experience the sense of interiority, privacy, individuality that comes with being the subject of sensations. I have argued in my book _Sentience_ (2022) that this may not extend to the whole animal kingdom: probably not to worms, snails or ants, for example. But, let's say, certainly all mammals and birds.

我们人类之前的祖先拥有现象意识——它们每一个都「有某种感觉」去做我——但仅仅如此并不构成它们拥有自己的灵魂。人类文化所添加的是诠释、声望和规范性：它将感知能力转化为人格，将人格转化为某种神圣之物。关键的催化剂是语言的进化，大约20万年前，语言赋予了人类描述内心生活、将其归因于他人、并将其提升为共同理想的新方式。

Our pre-human ancestors were phenomenally conscious – for each of them, it was 'like something' to be _me_ – but that alone did not amount to their having their own soul. What human culture added was interpretation, prestige and normativity: it turned sentience into personhood, and personhood into something sacred. The crucial catalyst was the evolution of language, which, about 200,000 years ago, gave human beings new ways to describe inner life, attribute it to others, and elevate it into a shared ideal.

与现代灵魂观念相连的荣耀化，很大程度上可能是一厢情愿：一种集体的虚构。但这提出了一个对进化心理学至关重要的问题。它可能是一种有用的虚构吗？对我们的祖先来说，以这种膨胀的方式看待自己是否帮助他们过上更好的生活？灵魂，无论多么虚幻，是否赋予了一种适应性优势？

The glorification bound up with the modern idea of the soul is, presumably, in large part wishful thinking: a kind of collective make-believe. But this raises a crucial question for evolutionary psychology. Could it have been _useful_ make-believe? For our ancestors, did thinking of themselves in this inflated way help them to lead better lives? Did the soul, however imaginary, confer an adaptive advantage?

狄德罗会嘲笑这种暗示。「如果灵魂与机器的结合是不可能的，」他写道，「请向我证明。如果是可能的，请告诉我这种结合会产生什么效果。」他问道：「一块有感知的、活的手表，与一块由金、铁、银、铜制成的手表有什么区别？如果将灵魂加到后者身上，它会在其中产生什么？」他显然预期的答案是：什么也不会产生。它不会造成任何可观察到的差异。

Diderot would have scoffed at the suggestion. 'If the union of a soul to a machine is impossible,' he wrote, 'let someone prove it to me. If it is possible, let someone tell me what would be the effects of this union.' 'What difference,' he asked, 'between a sensitive and living watch and a watch of gold, of iron, of silver and of copper? If a soul were joined to the latter, what would it produce therein?' The answer he clearly expected was: nothing at all. It would make no observable difference.

但多么糟糕的类比！狄德罗提议将灵魂加到一只怀表上——一种专为单一功能设计的机器，即计时——然后嘲笑它没有表现出什么灵魂的行为。是的，如果你是一块只有那一个维度的表达的手表，完全没有爱、温柔或创造力的空间——那么加上灵魂你也不会注意到任何差别。但如果你是一个人类共同体的一部分，周围所有其他人也有灵魂，如果你们共同要做的事情是友谊、合作、发明，那就是另一回事了。

But what a bad analogy! Diderot proposed adding a soul to a pocket-watch – a machine designed for just a single function, to tell the time – and then mocked it for not coming up with soulful behaviour. Yes, if you are a watch with just that one dimension of expression, with no scope whatever for love, or tenderness, or creativity – then add a soul and you won't notice any difference. But if you are part of a human community and all the other humans around you have souls too, if what you are about _together_ is friendship, cooperation, invention, why, then it's another story.

我论证过，在语言进化之后不久，人类从上到下重新创造了自己的物种。灵魂模因被证明具有非凡的力量——在心理上、伦理上、政治上。从它在我们的祖先中开始传播的那一刻起，它一定具有高度的适应性，改变了人际关系，鼓励了新的相互尊重水平，并大大提高了每个人对自己和他人生命的珍视程度。

I've argued that, soon after the evolution of language, humans recreated their species _from the top down_. The soul meme proved to be extraordinarily potent – psychologically, ethically, politically. And from the moment it took off among our ancestors, it must have been highly adaptive, transforming human relationships, encouraging new levels of mutual respect, and greatly increasing the value each person puts on their own and others' lives.

神学家基思·沃德（Keith Ward）在《为灵魂辩护》（In Defence of the Soul，1998）中说得好：

The theologian Keith Ward said it well in _In Defence of the Soul_ (1998):

谈论灵魂的全部意义在于不断提醒我们自己，我们超越了我们物质存在的所有条件……我们之所以超越它们，恰恰在于我们是不可定义的，总是多于所能看到或描述的，是体验和行动的主体，独特而不可替代。

The whole point of talking of the soul is to remind ourselves constantly that we transcend all the conditions of our material existence … We transcend them precisely in being indefinable, always more than can be seen or described, subjects of experience and action, unique and irreplaceable.

对人类物种的成员来说，生活在一个人们普遍对自己持有这种看法的世界中，就是生活在我所说的「灵魂生态位」（soul niche）中。我说的「生态位」，现在用的是这个术语在常规生态学中的含义：一个物种已经适应并被设计来繁荣的环境。鳟鱼生活在河流中，大猩猩生活在森林中，臭虫生活在床上。人类生活在灵魂之地。

For members of the human species to live in a world where people in general have this opinion of themselves is to live in what I've called the 'soul niche'. I mean 'niche', now, in the conventional ecological use of the term: an environment to which a species has become adapted and where it is designed to flourish. Trout live in rivers, gorillas in forests, bedbugs in beds. Humans live in soul land.

灵魂之地是一片精神的领土。这是一个地方，在这里人类心灵神奇的内在性在四面八方都能被感受到。在这里我们自然而然地假设每一个其他人也和我们一样，生活在现象意识的延展当下之中。在这里我们承认并尊重他人的人格，把每个人当作独立的、值得尊敬的、负责任的、自由意志的意识存在来对待。在这里我们识别并颂扬个体的、私密的欢乐与痛苦所蕴含的令人敬畏的可能性。

Soul land is a territory of the spirit. It's a place where the magical interiority of human minds makes itself felt on every side. A place where we naturally assume that every other human being lives, as we do, in the extended present of phenomenal consciousness. Where we acknowledge and honour the personhood of others, treating everyone as an independent, respectable, responsible, free-willed conscious being in his or her own right. Where we recognise and celebrate the awesome possibilities of individual, private joy and suffering.

意识在功能上是否过于奢华：一个对并非真正问题的回答？

Is consciousness functionally extravagant: an answer to no real problem?

这是一个我们自己和他人灵魂的命运是持续话题的地方。在这里灵魂是闲聊的对象、温柔关怀的对象、刻薄揣测的对象，是被祈祷和咒语操控的对象。在这里精神诉求开始与肉体诉求拥有同等地位。

It's a place where the fate of our own and other people's souls is a constant talking point. Where souls are the subject of gossip, of tender concern, of mean speculation, of manipulation by prayer and spells. It's a place where the claims of the spirit begin to rank as highly as the claims of the flesh.

我可以继续沿着这个方向说下去，但我不需要。你就生活在那里。你知道的。

I could go on in this vein, but I don't need to. You live there. You know.

而结果是什么？结果就是我们人类注定要栖居于那些永恒的问题之上：我们从哪里来，我们是什么，我们要到哪里去？正是在不断追问和回答这些问题的过程中，我们这个物种——作为一个生物实体——将自身提升到了近乎神的层次。

And the consequence was? The consequence _is_ that we humans are destined to dwell on the eternal questions: _Where have we come from, what are we, where are we going?_ And it has been in asking and answering these questions that our species – as a biological entity – has raised itself nearly to the level of the gods.

人类真的需要上升到神的层次吗？进化论理论家可能会反对说，这个想法似乎在功能上过于奢华。它意味着灵魂进化成了对一个并不存在的需求的回答。

Do humans really need to ascend to the level of the gods? Evolutionary theorists might object that the idea appears to be functionally extravagant. It would imply that the soul has evolved to be the answer to a non-existent need.

我认为这把事情颠倒了。你大可以反对说鸟类本来不需要飞翔。它们的陆生祖先在任何个体升空之前都过得很好。飞行在那个意义上是对一个非问题的解决方案。然而翅膀和飞行为鸟类打开了一个全新的世界可供开发。可以说，在我们自己物种的历史中，意识使我们能以一种更加奇妙的方式超越自身。

I think that gets things backwards. You might as well object that birds did not need to fly. Their terrestrial ancestors were surviving perfectly well before any of them took to the air. Flight, in that sense, was a solution to a non-problem. Yet wings and flight opened up a new world for birds to exploit. And, arguably, in the history of our own species, consciousness has enabled us to rise above ourselves in a more wonderful way still.

即便如此，问题依然存在。翅膀在其结构不牢固时可能是危险的。飞得离太阳太近，它们就会融化。如果意识自我是一种无法承受我们施加于其上的重量的心灵幻觉呢？

Even so, a problem remains. Wings can be perilous when their construction is unsound. Fly too near the Sun, and they melt. What if the conscious self is a mental _illusion_ that can't take the weight we put on it?

近年来，为解决意识的解释之谜最有希望的尝试之一是一种在许多人看来毫无希望的理论：意识只存在于想象之中的理论。

In recent years, one of the most promising attempts to solve the explanatory mystery of consciousness is a theory that, to many people, has seemed to be decidedly unpromising: the theory that consciousness exists only in the imagination.

笛卡尔本质上是对的：感觉没有物质实体；它们确实只是思维之物

Descartes was essentially right: sensations have no material substance; they are indeed just thought stuff

所谓的「幻觉主义」（illusionism）的出发点是这样一种认识：意识经验不多也不少，正是一套观念。它是我们每个人在心灵中再现周围发生的事、发生在我们身上的事以及因我们而发生的事的方式。

The starting point for 'illusionism', as it's come to be called, is the realisation that conscious experience is no more – and no less – than a _set of ideas._ It is the way each of us _represents_ in our minds what's happening around us, to us and because of us.

心理再现意味着发明和建构。正如丹尼尔·丹尼特（Daniel Dennett）在《内容与意识》（Content and Consciousness，1969）中所写：

Mental representation means invention and construction. As Daniel Dennett wrote in _Content and Consciousness_ (1969):

没有传入的感觉信号可以说是具有『A』的意义的，直到它被大脑的传出（产生行动的）一方「当作」具有『A』的意义。

No afferent [incoming sensory signal] can be said to have the significance 'A' until it is 'taken' to have the significance 'A' by the efferent [outgoing and action-generating] side of the brain.

感觉是你作为主体对冲击你身体的感官刺激所做出的东西：你脚趾中的疼痛是可怕的；你舌头上的甜味是发腻的；你眼前的红光令你躁动。

Sensations are what you as a subject _make_ of sensory stimuli impacting your body: the pain in your toe is horrible; the sweet taste on your tongue is sickly; the red light before your eyes stirs you up.

你从来不是一个被动的接收者；你是一个行动者。而你做出感觉的方式已经进化得相当特别。例如我在《看见与某物性》（Seeing and Somethingness，2022）中论证，它的工作方式是这样的：当红光到达你的眼睛时，你的大脑不会像照相机记录波长那样简单地将其记录下来。相反，发生了某种更主动、更有趣的事情。你对刺激做出一种微妙的、内化的身体反应，我称之为「发红」（redding）——一种表达正在发生什么以及你对此有何感受的反应。使之成为意识的，是一个进一步的步骤：你的大脑对自身活动生成一份运行中的评论，一种反馈信号循环回来，告诉你在你做出反应时你在做什么。可以说，你的心灵注视着自身向红色的方向伸去，正是这种自我监控构成了有意识的感觉。感觉在某种意义上总是一幅自画像。它不是世界的读数；它是你的读数。

You are never merely a passive receiver; you are a doer. And the way you do sensations has evolved to be something quite special. I've argued, for example in 'Seeing and Somethingness' (2022), that it works like this: when red light arrives at your eyes, your brain doesn't simply log it like a camera registering a wavelength. Instead, something more active and more interesting happens. You mount a subtle, internalised bodily response to the stimulus that I've called 'redding' – a response that _expresses_ what's happening to you and how you feel about it. What makes this conscious is a further step: your brain generates a running commentary on its own activity, a feedback signal that loops back and tells you what you are doing as you do it. As it were, your mind watches itself reaching out toward the red, and it's this self-monitoring that constitutes the conscious sensation. Sensation is, in a sense, always a self-portrait. It's not a readout of the world; it's a readout of you.

这意味着——现在你可以震惊了——笛卡尔本质上是对的：感觉没有物质实体；它们确实只是思维之物。

This means – now you can be shocked – that Descartes was essentially right: sensations have no material substance; they are indeed just thought stuff.

当然，按照今天科学家的看法，是物质的大脑作为一台类似计算机的「语义引擎」在运作，对自身的所作所为采取这种看法。今天没有人像笛卡尔那样相信大脑通过松果体接收从外部传递过来的编码信息形式的完整思想。但在我们嘲笑笛卡尔的电报模型完全天真之前，值得注意的是今天的神经科学家正在积极研究通过物理的「神经链路」（neuralink）将外部生成的思想植入大脑的可能性。笛卡尔关于灵魂可能如何被添加到身体上的模型，不再显得科学上荒谬可笑。

Of course, as scientists see this today, it is the material brain that, functioning as a computer-like 'semantic engine', takes this view of its own doings_._ No one today believes, as Descartes did, that the brain receives fully fledged thoughts from outside transmitted as coded messages via the pineal gland. But before we dismiss Descartes's telegraph model as totally naive, let's note that neuroscientists today are actively investigating the possibility of implanting externally generated thoughts into the brain via a physical 'neuralink'. Descartes's model of how the soul might be _added_ to the body no longer appears to be scientifically ridiculous.

幻觉主义在理论上为我们带来了什么？关键在于心理再现——即使是由物质产生的——并非由物质构成，因此不局限于具有符合物理现实的属性。既然如此，解释意识的许多困难和神秘之处便消散了。我们不必解释拥有诸如现象性红色这样奇怪非物理属性的大脑状态的存在，而只需解释产生关于这些属性的观念的大脑状态的存在。正如丹尼特所说：紫色感觉的现象质量可以像「一场关于紫色的美丽讨论，只是关于一种颜色，其本身并非有颜色的」。

What does illusionism buy for us theoretically? The crucial point is that mental representations, even if they are made _by_ matter_,_ are not made _of_ matter, and are therefore not restricted to having properties that conform to physical reality. And in that case, much of the difficulty and mystery of explaining consciousness falls away. We don't have to explain the existence of brain-states that possess strange non-physical properties such as phenomenal redness, but only the existence of brain-states that give rise to the idea of these properties. As Dennett has put it: the phenomenal quality of a purple sensation can be like 'a beautiful discussion of purple, just _about_ a colour, without itself being coloured'.

简言之——抱歉这话有点拗口，但我最好说清楚——我们只需解释大脑如何在命题意义上将一个人定位为持有某种关于红、甜、冷、痛等的信念的持有者。我说「只需」——无疑这不会容易；这确实是一种非凡的信念——但没有理由认为它是不可能做到的。

In short – sorry if this is a mouthful, but I'd better be explicit – we have only to explain how the brain situates a person, _propositionally_, as the holder of a certain kind of belief about redness, sweetness, coldness, pain or whatever_._ I say 'only' – no doubt it's not going to be easy; it is indeed a remarkable kind of belief – but there's no reason to think it's going to be impossibly hard.

我们是否应该担心这种隐含的主张，即这个建立在关于想象属性的讨论之上的意识自我「并不真正存在」？哲学家盖伦·斯特劳森（Galen Strawson）称之为「有史以来最愚蠢的主张」。我同意，如果幻觉主义者真的在说这个，斯特劳森可能说得有理。但斯特劳森所称的「伟大的否定」并不是我所理解的严格意义上的幻觉主义所暗示的。说感觉是再现，并非否认它们的存在：它们恰恰作为想象物而存在。

Should we be worried by the implicit claim that this conscious self, founded on a discussion of imaginary properties, 'doesn't really exist'? The philosopher Galen Strawson has called this 'the silliest claim ever made'. I agree that if illusionists were really saying this, Strawson might have a point. But the great denial, as Strawson calls it, is not what illusionism proper, as I understand it, implies. To say that sensations are representations is _not to deny that they exist_: they exist precisely _as_ imaginings.

即便如此，许多人对幻觉主义感到的不安是可以理解的。我们的语言很重要。丹尼特的类比——像一场美丽的「讨论」——我认为有点过于冷淡了。哲学家们确实在「讨论」感觉。我更愿意说普通人把它们唱出来、跳出来。可以说，感觉是一种进化出来的艺术品，旨在提振我们的精神。用艺术家巴勃罗·毕加索（Pablo Picasso）的话说：「自然与艺术，既然是不同的东西，就不可能是同一件东西。」

Even so, the unease that many people feel about illusionism is understandable. Our language matters. Dennett's analogy – like a beautiful 'discussion' – is, I think, a little too cold. Philosophers may indeed 'discuss' sensations. I'd rather say that ordinary people _sing and dance_ them. Arguably, sensations are an evolved _work of art_, designed to raise our spirits. In the artist Pablo Picasso's words: 'Nature and art, being different things, cannot be the same thing.'

真相是，感觉——作为思想——并不属于大脑实体深处

The truth is that sensations – as thoughts – don't belong down there in the substance of the brain

我们倾向于将「虚幻的」等同于「虚假的」，将「想象出来的」等同于「凭空想象的」。在生活的许多领域，想象物确实可疑。如果你想象桌子底下有一头狮子（而实际上没有），你就有行为失调的风险。如果你想象你的腿受了伤（而实际上没有），也是一样。但有意识的经验属于不同的类别。如果你因腿部受到伤害而产生疼痛的感觉，你不可能对此「出错」。这正是「做你是什么感觉」。在我看来，如果你据此产生的自我概念而行动，你很可能在生活中获得成功，而一个缺乏现象意识的僵尸则会失败。这正是它进化出来的原因。

We tend to conflate 'illusory' with 'false', and 'imagined' with 'imaginary'. In many areas of life, imaginings are indeed suspect. If you imagine there's a lion under the table (when there isn't), you are at risk of behaving maladaptively. If you imagine your leg is injured (when it isn't), the same. But conscious experience is in a different category. If you respond to damage to your leg by conjuring up the sensation of pain, the possibility of your being _wrong_ about it doesn't arise. This just _is_ 'what it's like to be you'. And, as I see it, if you act on the concept of self that arises from it, you are likely to succeed in life where a zombie who lacked phenomenal consciousness would fail. That's precisely why it has evolved.

幻觉主义作为一种理论是解放性的。它意味着我们可以逃离整整一代现代哲学家和神经科学家从错误起点出发所陷入的泥潭。如果你像科学家弗朗西斯·克里克（Francis Crick）那样去寻找「意识的神经相关物」，你太可能会执迷于一种不可能的心灵-大脑同一性的可能存在。但真相是，感觉——作为思想——并不属于大脑实体深处。

Illusionism as a theory is liberating. It means we can escape the quagmire of a generation of modern philosophers and neuroscientists who have started from the wrong place. If, like the scientist Francis Crick, you go looking for 'the neural correlate of consciousness', you are all too likely to get fixated on the possible existence of an impossible kind of mind-brain identity. But the truth is that sensations – as thoughts – don't belong down there in the substance of the brain.

这不是贬低认知神经科学。相反，这是承认心灵的运作超越了神经细胞的运作。托马斯·内格尔（Thomas Nagel）在他的《心灵与宇宙》（Mind and Cosmos，2012）一书中写道：

This isn't to disparage cognitive neuroscience. Rather, it's to recognise that the workings of the mind surpass the workings of nerve cells. Thomas Nagel, in his book _Mind and Cosmos_ (2012), wrote:

意识的存在似乎意味着，对宇宙的物理描述，尽管其丰富性和解释力，只是真理的一部分，而自然秩序远不像物理学和化学能解释一切时那样简朴。

The existence of consciousness seems to imply that the physical description of the universe, in spite of its richness and explanatory power, is only part of the truth, and that the natural order is far less austere than it would be if physics and chemistry accounted for everything.

但是，尽管他没错，他回避了问题。谁说过物理学和化学解释了一切？物理学不能解释一般的观念。它不能解释素数、正义或立体主义——更不用说灵魂了。实际上，它甚至不能解释物理学和化学。它充其量只是设定了这些观念得以在我们心灵中存在的先决条件。

But, while he's not wrong, he has begged the question. Whoever suggested that physics and chemistry account for everything? Physics doesn't account for _ideas_ in general. It doesn't account for prime numbers or justice or Cubism – let alone for souls. Actually, it doesn't even account for physics and chemistry. At best, it sets out the preconditions for these ideas to come to exist in our minds.

幻觉主义者有一个不太可能的盟友——精神分析学家卡尔·荣格（Carl Jung），他坚持认为，生活在一个梦中——远非一种低等的存在方式——也许恰恰是人类心灵的终极成就。

Illusionists have an unlikely ally in the psychoanalyst Carl Jung, who insisted that, to live in a dream, far from being an inferior kind of existence, is perhaps the ultimate achievement of the human mind.

在他的《红书》（Red Book，他哲学思考的日记，死后出版）中，荣格讲述了他与苏美尔神王——巨人伊兹杜巴尔（Izdubar）相遇的故事。荣格向他解释科学的发现如何将自然界的奇迹，包括巨人自己，还原为物理学和化学。伊兹杜巴尔听到的越多，他就变得越虚弱——那些理性解释像毒镖一样刺中了他。但随着故事的推进，荣格治愈了伊兹杜巴尔。他通过说服这位巨人承认自己是一个幻想、一个想象世界的真实居民来做到这一点。「就这样我的上帝找到了救赎，」荣格写道。「他恰恰被那种人们实际上会认为是致命的东西所拯救——即宣布他为一个想象的产物。」

In his _Red Book_ (the journal of his philosophical musings, published after his death), Jung tells the story of his meeting with the Sumerian god-king, the giant Izdubar. Jung explains to him the discoveries of science that have reduced the wonders of the natural world, including the giant himself, to physics and chemistry. The more Izdubar hears, the weaker he gets – the rational explanations strike him like poison darts. But as the story advances, Jung heals Izdubar. He does this by convincing the giant to recognise himself as a fantasy, a real occupant of the imaginary world. 'Thus my God found salvation,' writes Jung. 'He was saved precisely by what one would actually consider fatal, namely by declaring him a figment of the imagination.'

但为什么要称之为虚幻的产物呢？当如此巨大的财富栖身于如此贫困之中时，这是奇事中的奇事。

But why call it a figment? When such wealth makes its home in such poverty, it's a marvel of marvels.
