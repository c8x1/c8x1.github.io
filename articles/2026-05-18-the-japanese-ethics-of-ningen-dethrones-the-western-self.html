日本「人间」伦理学：颠覆西方自我观

和辻哲郎如何以「间」的思想重塑伦理学，揭示人之为人不在于孤独的主体，而在于人与世界之间无尽的关联

Takeshi Morisato·Aeon·2026年2月5日·21分钟阅读·原文

和辻哲郎（1889-1960）如今最为人称道的是他对思想史和伦理思想的全面分析。在日本思想史上，他被视为存在主义研究的先驱——他在1910年代出版了关于尼采的早期研究著作，并撰写了第一部日文版的克尔凯郭尔专著。他对日本伦理学的探索也以对西方哲学家及思想的批判性对话而著称，其中包括与黑格尔辩证法、海德格尔现象学和阐释学的深度交锋。

Watsuji Tetsurō (1889-1960) is best known today for his comprehensive analysis of intellectual history and ethical thought. In Japanese intellectual history, he is regarded as a pioneer in existentialism studies, publishing an early secondary resource on Friedrich Nietzsche and the first book on Søren Kierkegaard in Japanese in the 1910s. His investigation into Japanese ethics is also known for its critical engagement with Western philosophers and thought, including G W F Hegel's dialectic and Martin Heidegger's phenomenology and hermeneutics.

1934年，和辻以《作为人学之伦理学》奠定了日本伦理学的方法论基础，又于1935年在《风土》中最早提出了日本环境伦理学的构想。随后他出版了代表作《伦理学》——这是1937至1949年间写成的一系列论文，正写在日本现代史上最为动荡的时期（当然，也是世界最为动荡的时期之一）。

In 1934, Watsuji laid out the methodological foundation of Japanese ethics with his Ethics as the Study of the Human [ningen], and gave the earliest formulation of Japanese environmental ethics in Fūdo (1935) – translated as Climate and Culture: A Philosophical Study. He then published his magnum opus, Rinrigaku – translated as Watsuji Tetsurō's Rinrigaku: Ethics in Japan – which was originally a series of essays written between 1937 and 1949, during one of the most tumultuous periods for modern Japan (and, indeed, the world).

在《伦理学》中，和辻主张伦理学就是对「作为人意味着什么」的研究。他认为，我们如何理解人之存在的本性，决定了我们如何理解伦理价值。因此，他批判了西方哲学中关于现代主体的种种构想，认为西方的主体性观念既充满问题，又与东亚及日本哲学数千年来对「人间」（人間）的思考方式格格不入。

In Rinrigaku, Watsuji argues that ethics is the study of what it means for us to be human. How we think about the nature of human existence, he says, dictates the ways in which we understand our ethical values. Hence, he criticises Western philosophical conceptions of the modern subject, arguing that the Western rendering of subjectivity is both problematic and foreign to the ways in which what it means to be human (ningen, 人間) has been thought about for millennia in East Asian and Japanese philosophy.

首先，和辻指出，西方主体的构想既是个人主义的，又是自我指涉的，尽管大多数伦理体系都试图将其描绘为普遍适用的。以笛卡尔的「我思」论证所推导出的「自我」为例：笛卡尔把自己关在房间里，决定怀疑自己的知觉及其他一切，最终得出结论——即使他怀疑一切，也无法怀疑自己正在思维的活动，并以此作为其主体性的根基。对一位日本读者来说，这不过是一个有闲工夫独自冥想心灵运作方式的法国人的故事，他在孤独中对自身意识展开了一番反思。然而，这位形单影只的法国人提出的西方哲学思维模式，竟不知怎的成了描述全人类心灵结构的规范性模型。

First, Watsuji shows that the conception of the Western subject is both individualistic and self-referential, although most ethical systems have tried to paint it as being universal. Take the example of the Cartesian ego, derived from his cogito argument. René Descartes locked himself in a room, then decided to doubt his perception, among other things, concluding that, even when he doubted everything, he could not doubt his activity of thinking as the foundation of his subjectivity. To a Japanese reader, this is a story about a Frenchman who could afford the time to meditate on how his mind works, thereby laying out a reflection on his consciousness in his solitude. But then this Western philosophical model of thinking, which a solitary Frenchman set forth, somehow became the prescriptive model to describe the structure of the mind for all human beings.

且不论诉诸创造宇宙的神圣超越性概念，在这一「自我-我思」的认识论框架中，没有任何东西表明我们的心灵运作方式应该与笛卡尔在17世纪对自己心灵的思考完全一致。康德的绝对命令或黑格尔的自我决定理性的辩证法亦是如此。和辻认为，现代西方哲学的基本方法论如出一辙：一位来自特定文化与历史背景的哲学家反思自己如何构想心灵结构，然后宣称这种「自我指涉的抽象」就是适用于所有时空一切有觉知之物的普遍模型。（我们也不应忘记，在实践中，许多此类模型实际上否认了某些人群通达理性普遍性的资格。）对和辻而言，作为伦理思想体系的基础，这是极其成问题的。

Aside from appealing to the conception of divine transcendence that created the universe, nothing in this ego-cogito framework of epistemology suggests that we should think of our minds as working in exactly the same way as Descartes thought about his own mind in the 17th century. The same goes for Immanuel Kant's categorical imperative or Hegel's dialectic of self-determining reason. The basic methodology of modern, Western philosophy is the same, according to Watsuji: a philosopher from a specific cultural and historical background reflects upon how they conceive of the structure of their mind, and declares that what we might call their 'self-referential abstraction' is the universal model that theoretically applies to every sentient being across all space and time. (We should also not forget that, in practice, many of these models denied certain demographics access to the universality of reason.) For Watsuji, this is deeply problematic as a foundation for a system of ethical thinking.

和辻认为，犯下这种「自我指涉抽象」的现代主体构想之所以如此成问题，是因为它不得不虚构出一个以社会幸福或人类福祉为目标的超个体自我，以此来掩饰其根基处的个人主义自我中心问题。更糟糕的是，和辻指出，尽管向主体间性意识做了这一转向，现代主体的构想仍在作为伦理价值源泉的人类主体与作为无意义「此在」的客观世界（即自然）之间制造了冲突。在此框架下，自然被构想为一个他律性的他者——人类自主性的威胁、需要通过「我思」的自我决定的可理解性来征服的非理性外在之物。

What makes the modern conception of the subject that commits this 'self-referential abstraction' so problematic, according to Watsuji, is that it had to come up with a supra-individual self that aims at the happiness of society or the welfare of mankind, in order to cloak the foundational problem of individualistic self-centredness. What is worse, Watsuji argues, is that, despite this move towards intersubjective consciousness, the conception of the modern subject creates conflict between human subject as the source of ethical values, and the objective world or nature as meaningless 'thereness'. Nature, in this case, is conceived of as a heteronomous other – a threat to human autonomy, an irrational outside entity that needs to be conquered through the self-determining intelligibility of 'I think.'

到了这一步，西方哲学所描述的「理性现代主体」，倒更像是一个不自量力的暴君——自诩为最高形式的有意识存在；甚至像一个叛逆的孩子——声称自己是世界可理解性的唯一决定者，而实际上不过是被其母亲——自然——所割裂的孤儿。顺便说一句，这也正是西方主体性在大多数非西方哲学背景的思想者眼中的模样。和辻只是出于同样关切、提出替代性人类存在思考方式的众多哲学家之一，他所借助的是根植于自身文化与思想土壤的伦理学体系。

At this point, what Western philosophy describes as an intelligent modern subject looks more like an unreasonable despot who believes themselves to be the highest form of conscious existence, or even a delinquent child who claims to be the sole determiner of world intelligibility, yet in truth is just severed from its mother, Nature. This, incidentally, is how Western subjectivity appears to most thinkers from a non-Western philosophical background. Watsuji is just one of many who, out of the same concern, proposed an alternative way of thinking about human existence through the system of ethics particular to his own cultural and intellectual milieu.

在东亚哲学的大背景下，日本关于人之存在（人間存在，ningen sonzai）的概念与西方的人性观截然不同。使我们成为「人间」的，不是第一原则或神圣超越性所创造的本体论结构，不是理性，不是精神，甚至也不是为伦理价值提供理论基础的元伦理意义结构，而是构成我们人性（人間性，ningen-sei）的「间之具体实践」或「行为关联之『间』」。而这种间的实践，始终已然伴随着对空性的实践性自觉。没有上帝，没有理性，没有现代主体性或元伦理学的普遍基础，我们怎么可能成为人或伦理的存在？

The Japanese conception of human being (ningen sonzai, 人間存在) in the larger context of East Asian philosophies is radically different from the Western conception of humanity. What makes us human (ningen) is not the ontological structure made by the first principle or divine transcendence. Nor is it reason, spirit, nor even the metaethical structure of meaning that provides a theoretical ground for our ethical values, but rather the 'concrete practice of betweenness' or 'in-betweenness of act connections' that constitute our humanity (ningen-sei, 人間性). And this practice of betweenness always already comes with the practical self-awareness of emptiness. How could we possibly be a human or an ethical being without God, or reason, or the universal ground of modern subjectivity or of meta-ethics?

和辻认为，要在日本哲学中构想人之存在的本性，我们必须从欧洲将人视为现代主体的构想出发，进行一种双重的解构-重构运动。首先，我们必须解构从欧洲哲学史继承而来的固定的自我概念——亚历山大·道格拉斯在其发表于Aeon的文章《本质在飘荡》（2025）中，通过与庄子的对话论及自我的非实体性（即「无我」）时，也提出了类似的见解。其次，我们必须在此基础上确立对人之存在的恰当理解——将其视为一种处于世界之中的动态生命。这就是在与历史、社会、文化和风土（包括其中一切有知觉的存在和自然环境）不可分割的关系中，通过彼此积极的交往来重构我们对自己是谁的认知。

To conceive of the nature of human existence in Japanese philosophy, Watsuji argues that we must make a double de-/re-constructive movement away from the European conception of humanity as the modern subject. First, we must deconstruct the fixed notion of the self that we inherited from the history of European philosophy – Alexander Douglas made a similar point with regard to an argument with Zhuangzi on the non-substantial nature of the self (or 'non-self') in his Aeon essay 'Essence Is Fluttering' (2025). Second, we must then triangulate the proper understanding of human existence as a dynamic life in the midst of the world. This is to reconstruct our sense of who we are in our active engagement with each other in our inseparable relation to history, society, culture and climate (including all sentient beings and natural environments therein).

将人为主体构想为一种不可还原为其客观环境的固定存在，对大多数亚洲和佛教思想家来说是非常陌生的——无论这种主体被构想为犹太-基督教传统中按照神圣形象创造的灵魂或人之存在的整全性，还是被构想为世俗理解下的理性或精神的拥有者。正如道格拉斯那篇涉及道家思想的文章所表明的，一些亚洲思想家（包括和辻）甚至直言，这种固定的、本质性的主体性概念是一种妄想，因为它倾向于将每个人视为一个「自足的存在」，从其社会、历史和风土/自然群体中抽离或剥离出来。

Imposing a fixed conception of the human subject as something irreducible to its objective environment is very foreign to most Asian and Buddhist thinkers, whether it is conceived as the soul or integrity of human existence as created in the image of the divine in the Judeo-Christian tradition, or as the possessor of reason or spirit, in line with a secular understanding of the human subject. As Douglas's Daoist essay shows, some Asian thinkers (including Watsuji) even go so far as to say that this fixed, essential conception of subjectivity is delusional because it suffers from a propensity to view each human as a 'self-sustaining being', abstracted or removed from their social, historical and climatic/natural groups.

世界始终处于流变之中，是彻底动态的。如果我们有机会去不同的大陆、去大国的不同地区旅行，很快就会发现我们的生活方式、交流模式、艺术媒介、历史感等等可以有多么巨大的差异。然而西方哲学家却抵不住诱惑，坚持一种独立于这个彻底多元的世界而存在的固定自我概念，以便通过个体意识的自我指涉的普遍化来构建某种普遍伦理理论。正因为西方哲学家及其追随者优先选择了一种通过自我中心的建模来推动普遍伦理的模式，我们才饱受殖民思维之苦——这种思维将欧洲关于「何为人」的思考方式凌驾于一切其他替代性构想之上。

The world is always in flux and uncompromisingly dynamic. If we have a chance to travel to different continents and to different parts of large nations, we quickly learn how vastly different our ways of living, modes of communication, art media, senses of histories and so on can really be. But Western philosophers succumb to the temptation to hold on to a fixed notion of the self that exists independently of that uncompromisingly polyphonic world, so that we can somehow construct a universal theory of ethics through the self-referential universalisation of individual consciousness. And because Western philosophers and those in their thrall prioritise one model that drives towards universal ethics through its self-centred modelling, we endure the problem of colonial thinking that privileges the European way of thinking about what it is to be human above all other alternative conceptions.

与此不同，和辻对「人间」存在的理解采纳了一种源自大乘佛教空性概念的辩证结构——动态的个体性与多元的开放总体性之间的辩证。这一概念（也受到道家影响）旨在打破对人在无尽时空中应当为何的固定化构想。它意味着我们最终无法固定任何事物的定义，因为没有什么是永恒不变的：万物皆在流变，无物固定。一切最终都归于无，而正因为这种「归于无」，一切才有了成为其所是的空间。世界由《心经》所说的「色与空」的无尽循环所构成；在佛教哲学中，空性的逻辑还要求一种超脱、自我否定和慈悲的行为——即自我为他者成为其所是或所将成为者让出空间，而他者也为自我做同样的事。和辻以这种作为空性的自我概念——即佛教徒所说的「缘起」——来奠定日本伦理学作为人学的基础，这是一种在文化上特有的对人之存在（即「人间」）的把握。

Instead, Watsuji's understanding of the existence of ningen adopts the dialectical structure of dynamic individuality and polyphonic open totality derived from the Mahayana Buddhist notion of emptiness. This concept (which also has a Daoist influence) is meant to break down a fixed conceptualisation of what humans, or anything else, should be across infinite space and time. It means that we cannot ultimately fix any definition of anything because nothing remains what it is forever: every thing is in flux, and nothing is fixed. Everything must ultimately turn to nothing and, thanks to this 'turning into nothing', everything has a space to be what it is. The world is made of this endless cycle of what the Heart Sutra calls 'form and emptiness', and in Buddhist philosophy, this logic of emptiness also requires an act of detachment, self-negation and compassion (that is, for a self to give room for others to be what they are, or are becoming, as others do the same for the self). Watsuji uses this concept of self as emptiness, or what Buddhists call 'interdependence', to ground Japanese ethics as the study of the human, a culturally specific grasp of human existence as ningen.

佛教对空性概念的辩护（姑且搁置道家）大致如下：正因为人之存在没有固定的本质（即「无我」的教义），每个处于特定历史、文化和自然环境中的个体群体都能构成自己的人性感，同时意识到这种场所特有的关联性人性构想既是短暂的，也是有限的。但正因为他们实践着自我否定——从将自我设想的自身概念本质化、超越其短暂存在的诱惑中超脱出来——他们才能真正觉知自身。这在佛教语言中常被表达为觉悟或无我，即存在总体性（世界与自我之如是）作为空性的显现。

A Buddhist defence of the concept of emptiness (pace Daoism) goes something like this: because there is no fixed essence to human existence (the doctrine of 'no-self'), each group of individuals in a specific historical, cultural and natural milieu can constitute their sense of humanity with their self-awareness that this place-specific conception of their inter-relational humanity is both transient and finite. But because they practise self-negation – a detachment from the temptation to essentialise their self-conceived notion of themselves beyond their transient existence – they can be truly aware of themselves. This is often expressed in Buddhist language as enlightenment or no-self, as a manifestation of existential totality (of the ways the world and the self are) as emptiness.

西方伦理学追求一种永恒的自我概念——一种拥有固定的、不变的、可发现的本质的存在；日本伦理学则与此不同，它致力于觉知自我之为一种暂时的、有限的表达——一种「自空」之人意味着什么的表达。和辻进而论证说，每种语言都必然包含这种「有」与「空」之间辩证互动的结构，而日语（根植于古典汉语及其他亚洲思想传统）有一种独特的方式来保存说话者对这种辩证思维的觉知。

Unlike the Western ethics that strives for the perennial conception of self as a being with a fixed, unchanging, discoverable essence, Japanese ethics strives to become aware of the self as a temporary and finite expression of what it means for it to be a self-emptying human. Watsuji, then, argues that each language necessarily contains this dialectical structure of the interplay between being and emptiness, and that the Japanese language (rooted in classical Chinese and other Asian intellectual traditions) has a peculiar way of preserving the speaker's awareness of this dialectical thinking.

关于语言，和辻论证道：「没有任何一个人有权声称词语是由她独自创造的。尽管如此，对每个人而言，词语又属于自己的。词语是一座熔炉，通过它，由个体人类所建立的仅仅是主观的联系被转化为意向性意义。换言之，词语关涉的是前意识之存在被转化为意识的活动。」「意向性意义」是我们认作主观意识之一部分（区别于客观世界）的观念、知觉和思想内容。和辻要说的是，世界是一簇实践的「行为关联」，而为了在其中表达我的思想或情感，我们（包括你我）必须在意向性意义产生之前，通过空性的实践来介入部分与整体之间的辩证关联。

Regarding languages, Watsuji argues that: 'No one person has the privilege of declaring that she alone has created [words]. In spite of this, for everyone, words are one's own. Words are the furnace by means of which merely subjective connections made by individual human beings are converted into noematic meanings. In other words, words are concerned with the activity whereby preconscious being is turned into consciousness.' 'Noematic meanings' are ideas, perceptions and thought contents that we recognise as being a part of our subjective consciousness, apart from the objective world. Watsuji is saying that the world is a cluster of practical act-connections and, in order for me to express my thought or feeling therein, then, we (including you and I) must engage in the dialectical interrelation of parts and the whole through the praxis of emptiness prior to the rise of noematic meanings.

如果我们把自我与语言的关系比作此刻读者与作者在阅读这篇文章时的关系，就不难理解这种空性的自我否定辩证法了。为了让我在这篇文章中表达自己，每一位读者（你）都必须以我的方式给我一个表达的空间。作为听众，你方需要有一种自我否定的意识，才不会将你对世界或自身的理解强加于我对日本世界观的理解之上。在我用几页篇幅讲述我的文化和我自己时，你必须尽可能地清空你的文化和个人预设。但与此同时，我也必须了解你们作为读者如何使用这门语言，以便预判可能的误解。

It should be easy for us to understand this self-negating dialectic of emptiness if we compare the relation of self and language with that of author and reader in reading the words of this essay now. In order for me to express myself in this article, every reader (you) would have to give me a space to do that in my own terms. There is a sense of self-negation needed from your side as the audience if you are not to impose your understanding of the world or yourself on my explanations of the Japanese worldview. You must empty your cultural and personal assumptions as much as you can when allowing me to speak about my culture and myself for a few pages. But, at the same time, I must know how you, as the readers, speak this language so that I can anticipate misunderstanding.

除了了解英语的运作方式——其独特的语法规则和标点习惯——之外，我还必须与编辑们一起思考如何最好地将这些理念与目标读者连接起来，因为其中许多人的文化规范和知识背景与我的截然不同。作为作者，我们一方同样有一种自我否定的意识——我们并不将我们对日本哲学的学术理解以其原始形式全盘强加于人，那种形式只有熟悉日本历史、哲学和语言的专家才能理解。一篇好文章通常包含这两个方面的自我空性之间的平衡。

In addition to knowing how the English language functions, with its peculiar grammatical rules and unique punctuations, I have to think with the editors about how we can best connect the idea with the target readership, many of whom will come with a very different set of cultural norms and knowledge from my own. There is a sense of self-negation on our side as writers, in that we do not entirely impose our scholarly understanding of Japanese philosophy in its original form, a form that would make sense only to specialists familiar with Japanese history, philosophy and language. A good article usually consists of the balance between these two sides of self-emptiness.

当作者未能达成这种平衡时，结果往往是一篇高度抽象、鲜有人能理解的文章，或者是一篇任由读者将自己的意见和既有偏见投射其上的文章——作者的本意反而逃逸了，读者以一种完全主观的方式解读文章。和辻论证说，作为人类交往基础的相互自我否定的辩证关系，同样发生于个体言语行为与语言之间的平衡之中。正因如此，和辻认为，如果我们通过这一辩证空性的模型来审视一种语言与其说话者之间的互动关系（反之亦然），我们就能更好地理解伦理学在世界哲学各领域中——尤其是在日本哲学中——是如何被实践和理论化的。

When the author fails to achieve such a balance, the result is often a highly abstract article that few can understand, or else one that allows readers to project their opinions and pre-existing prejudices on the article with the result that the authorial intent escapes, and they interpret the article in an entirely subjective way. Watsuji argues that the same dialectical relation of mutual self-negation as a basis of human communication takes place in the balance between individual speech acts and language. Because of that, if we look at how a language functions in relation to its speakers, and vice versa, through this model of dialectical emptiness, Watsuji thinks we will better understand how ethics is both practised and theorised in each domain of world philosophies in general and in Japanese philosophy in particular.

如果语言如和辻所论证的那样是我们经验的构成性储存库，那么关注一种特定语言中的一组哲学术语如何与其符号和规则一起运作，就能帮助我们理解我们如何在那种文化特有的思维框架中表达「何为人」和「何为伦理」的意义。在这个意义上，和辻邀请我们探索日语中「伦理」（rinri）和「人间存在」（ningen sonzai）这些词汇的功能，以捕捉日本思想传统中独特的伦理概念。

If language is a constitutive depository of our experience, as Watsuji argues, paying attention to how a set of philosophical terms in a particular language functions with its symbols and rules can help us understand how we articulate the sense of what it means to be human, and to be ethical, in that culturally specific framework of thinking. In this sense, Watsuji invites us to explore the function of Japanese words as rinri (ethics) and ningen sonzai (human being) and to capture distinct ethical concepts in the Japanese intellectual tradition.

在日本伦理学中，无论是将伦理理解为「伦理」（rinri），还是将人之存在理解为「人间存在」（ningen sonzai），都保留着个体性与总体性、单一性与多元性、自我与他者这些对立术语之间的强烈相互蕴含。「伦理」一词由两个汉字组成：「倫」与「理」。「倫」指的是同伴或「仲间」（nakama），即遵循一定关系规则的有情众生群体，也就是儒家所说的「伦常」。这包括我们以父母与子女、兄弟与姐妹、丈夫与妻子、君与臣等方式相互关联的种种方式。同样，这些关系角色并非固定不变的：伴侣之间的相处方式不必遵循某一单一模式。但它们是人类关系中可确定的表达，保留着一些可沟通的模式，这些模式始终处于变化之中。此外，日语中的「仲间」（nakama）一词兼含单一性与多元性。我们可以指着一个人说他是我们的「仲间」（盟友），也可以用「仲间」来描述我们自己（朋友）。它同时保留了单一和多元两种含义。

In Japanese ethics, both the conception of ethics as rinri and of human existence as ningen sonzai retain the strong mutual implication of such opposing terms as individuality and totality, singularity and plurality, and self and other. The term rinri, meaning 'ethics', consists of two characters: rin (倫) and ri (理). Rin refers to fellows or nakama, a group of sentient beings that follow a certain set of relational rules, or what Confucianism calls 'constancies'. This includes the ways in which we relate to each other as parent and child, brother and sister, husband and wife, minister and subject, and so on. Once again, these relational roles are not fixed: the ways in which partners relate to each other do not have to follow one model. But they are determinable expressions of human relations that retain some communicable patterns, which are always subject to change. Additionally, the Japanese term nakama, or fellows, signifies both singularity and plurality. We can point to a person to say that he is our nakama (an ally) or describe ourselves as nakama (friends). It retains both meanings of singularity and plurality.

「理」在哲学语境中常被译为原则或理性，但它也指一种可感的模式（kata）或惯例约定，我们借此构成彼此的关系。在我们写文章或以朋友、家人的身份相处的方式中，存在某些期望或恒常性。同样，这些并非必须以相同方式永久遵守的固定规则，而是一种相对稳定的社会、文化和风土行为的集合，我们借此构成归属感和相互理解。因此，「理」指的是那些构成我们作为「人间」的意义感的互动模式。

Ri (理) is often translated as principle(s) or reason in the context of philosophy but it also refers to a kind of sensible pattern (kata) or conventional agreement through which we constitute our relations. There are certain expectations or constancies in the ways that we write our essays or relate to each other as friends or families. Once again, these are not fixed rules that have to be permanently observed in the same way but are a relatively stable aggregate of social, cultural and climatic behaviours through which we constitute our sense of belonging and mutual understanding. Thus, ri refers to the patterns of interactions that constitute the sense of what it means for us to be human (ningen).

「人间」一词由两个汉字组成：「人」（nin 或 hito）和「间」（gen 或 aida）。这两个汉字的组合也是彻头彻尾辩证的。「人」通常指一个人或人类，但也保留了「人性」或「人类」的某种复数含义。正如我们可以从汉字字形中想象的那样，「人」的形象表示两个人相互支撑，以构成一个人或人格感。换言之，在日本伦理学中，「人」既是单数也是复数。如果我们失去了两者之间的平衡，就失去了作为「人」的意义感。

The term ningen consists of two characters, nin or hito (人) and gen or aida (間). A combination of these characters is also dialectical, through and through. The term nin or hito usually means a person or a human but also retains some sense of plurality as 'humanity' or 'mankind'. As we can imagine from the shape of the Chinese character, 人 indicates two persons supporting each other to constitute a sense of a person or personhood. In other words, in Japanese ethics hito or person is both singular and plural. If we lose the balance between the two, we lose the sense of what it means to be hito or person.

第二个字「间」（aida）由两部分组成：「門」和「日」。第一个字看起来像西部片中的酒吧入口——当主角推开弹簧门走进去时，钢琴声戛然而止，所有人都默然望向他。我认为这是哲学家詹姆斯·海西格所说的「记汉字」的好方法。确实，这个字象征的是佛教寺庙的山门，通常是敞开的，我们应该能感受到门后那空旷的空间。第二个字「日」象征太阳：因此，空间感是由穿过星辰之门的时间运动所决定的（可能也由它投下的阴影所决定）。

The second character, aida (間), consists of two parts, 門 and 日. The first character looks like the entrance to a saloon bar in a western movie. When the protagonist pushes through the swing door, the piano stops and everyone looks at him in silence. I think this is a good method of 'remembering the kanji', as the philosopher James Heisig put it. Indeed, the character symbolises a Buddhist temple gate, which is usually open, and we should be able to feel the hollow expanse behind it. The second character, 日, symbolises the sun: so, the sense of space is dictated by the temporal movement through the gate of the star (and also, probably, by the shadows it casts).

和辻指出，将这个象征空间性「间」之概念（也隐含时间性空间意味）的汉字加在「人」字之上并非偶然。相反，它揭示了一个历史事实：古代中国思想家以及作为其批判性后继者的日本思想家，都采纳了这样一种洞见——人类个体不可能脱离其存在于世界的社会-自然整体而存在。因此，对他们而言，正如对当代日本伦理学理论家而言一样，构成人之存在的是「内在于人的双重特征的辩证统一」。和辻论证说，这种「矛盾的统一」并不停留在西方语言中所谓的「人性」的边界之内，而是「[间，即开放总体性]的概念已经包含了人之存在的历史的、风土的和社会的结构」。他继续写道：「仅仅以「人」（即个体的人）的形式来看待「人间」，只是从个体本性的视角来看待人。如果仅仅持此一种观点——即便将其作为一种方法论上的抽象予以承认——也无法具体地把握「人间」。我们必须将「人间」（中日意义上的人）彻底地把握为……矛盾特征的统一。」

Watsuji shows that adding this character symbolising the spatial concept of betweenness (with some implication of temporal space) to the character of person or hito was not accidental. Rather, it indicates the historical fact that ancient Chinese thinkers, and Japanese thinkers as their critical followers, adopted the insight that human individuals cannot exist apart from the social-natural whole of their being in the world. Hence, for them, as much as for contemporary Japanese ethical theorists, what makes human existence is the 'dialectical unity of those double characteristics that are inherent in a human being'. This 'unity of contradiction', Watsuji argues, does not remain within the bounds of what we call 'humanity' in Western languages, but the 'concept of [betweenness, or the open totality] already involves the historical, climatic, and social structure of human existence.' He continues: 'To see ningen only in the form of hito [ie, an individual human being] is to see a human being merely from the perspective of his individual nature. This view, if it be held alone, and even if it is allowed as a methodological abstraction, cannot come to grips with ningen concretely. We must grasp [the Chinese-Japanese sense of the human] ningen through and through as the unity of the … contradictory characteristics.'

这种双重结构意味着，个体自我要行动时，它始终是在与其「间」的关系中行动——这种「间」的参与形式不仅涵盖主体间关系，还包括人类通过对共享的社会、文化、语言和自然环境的参与而相互交往的方式。日本伦理学中表征人之存在本性的「间」之概念，并不意指世界作为一个固定场所——我们可以在其中找到自然事物的总和。既然它不是一个固定的场所，我们就无法给出一个逃脱根本性变化的确定性身份或框架。我们必须将它想象为佛寺山门后面那片空旷的空间，或者西部片中阴影笼罩的主角身后的那片空地。它意味着一种以个体为根基的无限关联可能性，允许个体将这些关联表达为他们的社会、文化和自然归属。它是使你我——作为本文的读者和作者——成为自身的自空之「力」（古希腊语中的dunamis，即「潜能」），也是维系我们交往的、作为相互自我否定的共享实践的、非固定的基础。世界就是由这种前本体论的空间构成的——我们在此空间中相互交往，以构成我们的多元归属感。

This dual structure means that, in order for an individual self to act, it always acts in relation to its betweenness, or the form of engagement that covers not only intersubjective relations but also the ways in which human beings interact with each other through their engagement with shared social, cultural, linguistic and natural environments. The concept of betweenness that characterises the nature of human existence in Japanese ethics does not designate the sense of the world as a fixed place where we can find the sum total of natural objects. Since it is not a fixed place, we cannot give a determinate identity or framework that escapes radical change. We must instead imagine it as the hollow expanse behind the gate of the Buddhist temple, or as the clearing behind the shadowy protagonist in a western film. It implies an infinite possibility of interrelations that ground individuals, which allow them to express these interrelations as their social, cultural and natural belongings. It is the self-emptying dunamis (ancient Greek for 'potentiality' or 'potency') of what makes you and me, as the reader and the writer of this article, as much as it is the unfixed foundation sustaining our communication as the shared practice of mutual self-negation. The world consists of this pre-ontological space where we interact with each other to constitute our sense of polyphonic belongings.

古代中国和日本的思想家强调个体人类与开放总体性之间的动态关联——这些个体在与彼此和自然的交织中生生死死。他们所指涉的是社会-自然共同体的动态空间，在那里世界和个体以矛盾而又相互蕴含的关系共同存在。「人间」的「间」是纯粹的转化——也就是说，作为一个概念，它本身是空的，但它呼唤每个人在实践中否定自身的自我中心，以实现他者，反之亦然。正如佛教的缘起或空性概念一样，自我否定的原理是有与非有之间的运动——不是作为自我决定理性的牺牲品，而是作为一个慈悲者的开放共同体，他们允许彼此以自己的方式表达自身。它使他们能够在彼此的关系中被完整地理解。

Ancient Chinese and Japanese thinkers, who emphasise the dynamic interrelation between a single human being and the open totality of such individuals as they live and die in the midst of one another and nature, refer to the dynamic space of social-natural community, where both the world and the individual exist in their contradictory and mutually implicated relation. The betweenness of ningen is pure transformation, that is to say, it is, as a concept, empty in and of itself, but it calls for the practical negation of each person's self-centredness for the realisation of the other and vice versa. Like the Buddhist notion of codependent origination or emptiness, the principle of self-negation is the movement between the zone of being and of non-being, not as a victim of self-determining reason, but as an open community of compassionate beings who allow each other to express themselves in their own terms. It allows them to be understood fully in relation to each other.

和辻的日本伦理学构想（即「伦理学」作为rinrigaku）蕴含着许多引人深思的意涵。其中最为显著的一项是：早在1930年代，和辻就在为去殖民伦理学奠基——他通过将伦理学作为人之存在的研究来实践其思想谱系，将其与世界哲学各个子领域相关联，而非走西方那条更为常见的寻求普遍伦理的路径。这意味着，每一个以伦理学为专长的哲学项目——从传统的理论阐释到应用领域（如医学伦理学和环境伦理学）——都应鼓励研究者掌握不同的语言，并考察伦理学在世界哲学不同领域中被概念化和被生活的各种历史方式。

There are many interesting implications from this Watsujian conception of Japanese ethics as rinrigaku. One of the more remarkable implications is that, as early as the 1930s, Watsuji was laying the foundation for decolonial ethics by practising the intellectual genealogy of ethics as the study of human existence in relation to each subfield of world philosophies, rather than taking the more usual Western route of seeking a universal ethic. This is to say that every philosophy programme that specialises in ethics – ranging from the traditional theoretical exposition to applied fields, such as medical and environmental ethics – should encourage its researchers to gain fluency in different languages and to investigate various historical ways in which ethics has been both conceptualised and lived in different spheres of world philosophies.

和辻论证说，如果我们真心想要超越西方普遍伦理的殖民性自我指涉抽象，我们就应当基于不同的文化和语言来实践伦理学，走出盎格鲁-欧洲哲学的边界。与那种将一个独立于自然的自足主体作为出发点的人类中心主义伦理学构想不同，日本伦理学的起点是一个预设：每一个体以及每一个体群体，始终已然在与他们的社会、历史和自然环境的关联中构成他们对何为人的意义感。从这个意义上说，日本伦理学并非在伦理学学科中增加一个子领域，而是向世界哲学中众多伦理思想的构想敞开了边界。

If we care to move beyond the colonial, self-referential abstraction of Western universal ethics, Watsuji argues, we should practise ethics based on different cultures and languages, beyond the confines of Anglo-European philosophy. Unlike the Western anthropocentric formulation of ethics that sets forth a self-subsisting subject apart from nature, Japanese ethics starts with an assumption that each individual, and each group of such individuals, constitutes their sense of what it means for them to be human always already in their interrelation with their social, historical and natural surroundings. Japanese ethics, in this sense, does not add one subfield to the discipline of ethics but opens up its border to a plethora of world philosophical formulations of ethical thought.
