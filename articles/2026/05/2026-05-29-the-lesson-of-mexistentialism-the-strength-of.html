墨西哥存在主义的启示：不确定性的力量

Carlos Alberto Sánchez·Aeon·2026年5月29日·11分钟阅读·原文

最近有一天晚上，我费尽口舌想劝父亲不要回米却肯（Michoacán）。我告诉他现在不是好时候。他提醒我，自从老布什（George H W Bush）执政时期起，他就是美国归化公民了。我向他转述了我在新闻上看到的：一些在美国居住了几十年的合法永久居民，持有绿卡，积极参与公民事务，却遭到遣返，而理由似乎与过去的犯罪行为有关——比如商店盗窃或停车罚单。我提醒他，早在1990年代，他曾因工作中的一次冲突被逮捕并以人身攻击罪起诉。「我害怕，」我说，「那足以让你被拘留、被剥夺公民身份、然后被遣返！」他耸了耸肩。「更糟的事都发生过。再说了，我都74岁了。我可能死在路上。_Nada es seguro_。」什么都不确定。

I recently spent an evening trying to convince my father _not_ to go back to Michoacán. I told him it wasn't a good time. He reminded me that he's been a naturalised US citizen since the George H W Bush administration. I reported what I've seen on the news: people with decades of US residency, with Green Cards and outstanding civic engagement, are being deported, and the rationale seems to have something to do with previous acts of criminality, like shoplifting or parking tickets. I reminded him that, back in the 1990s, he was arrested and charged with assault for an incident at work. 'I'm afraid,' I said, that 'that's enough to detain you, strip you of your citizenship, and deport you!' He shrugged. 'Worse things have happened. Besides, I'm 74 years old. I may die on the way. _Nada es seguro_.' Nothing is certain.

我不再劝了。我姐姐惊慌地给我打电话：「告诉他别去！」

I let it be. My sister called me in a panic: 'Tell him not to go!'

「没用，」我说。「他要去。等他明年某个时候试着回来，我们就知道他的命运了。」于是漫长的等待开始了。

'It's no use,' I said. 'He's going. We'll find out his fate when he tries to come back sometime next year.' And the waiting game began.

父亲那种不以为意的态度曾经让我困惑。成长过程中，我以为他过于多愁善感、郁郁寡欢。我确信他有什么问题。我想，也许他患了抑郁症。他每天过日子就像一个被背叛了太多次、现在已甘于过令人失望的生活的人。

My father's dismissive attitude used to confuse me. Growing up, I thought he was overly sentimental and melancholic. I was sure that there was something wrong with him. I thought that, maybe, he was depressed. He lived each day like a man who had been betrayed one too many times and was now resigned to a disappointing life.

现在我明白了当初我错得多离谱。现在我明白了，他不过是在映照处境中的磨难与苦难，他是一段历史的产物——这段历史无情地让他直面一个真相：更糟的事随时可能发生，而真正地、确实地，什么都不确定。现在我看到，我曾以为是漠然的东西，其实是对一种根本性的脆弱性和偶然性的觉察与接纳。

Now I see how wrong I was. I see now that he was merely reflecting the trials and tribulations of circumstance, that he was a product of a history that had mercilessly exposed him to the truth that a 'worse thing' can always happen and that, really and truly, _nothing is certain_. Now I see that what I thought was dismissiveness was awareness and acceptance of a fundamental fragility and accidentality.

在阅读了墨西哥哲学家关于偶然性和脆弱性的论述之后，我对父亲有了更清晰的认识。墨西哥哲学家，尤其是存在主义传统中的那些——我在别处称之为（墨）存在主义（(M)existentialism）——在谈论做一个墨西哥人意味着什么时，似乎心目中就有我父亲这样的人。他们谈论的是这样一种人：理解或认可「什么都不确定」这句话的含义。这个人理解或认可自己的 _nepantla_——即自己处于空间之间、时间之间、目的地之间、生与死之间——因此认识到自己的不确定性、不稳定性和根本性的不确定感，即墨西哥存在主义者所说的 _zozobra_。简而言之，他们确信一件事：「_nada es seguro_」。那就是我父亲。

I came to see my father with even more clarity after reading what Mexican philosophers had to say about accidentality and fragility. Mexican philosophers, especially those of the existentialist tradition – what I've elsewhere referred to as (M)existentialism – seem to have a person like my father in mind when they talk about _what it means to be Mexican_. They talk about a type of person who understands or recognises the meaning of the phrase 'nothing is certain'. This person understands or recognises their _nepantla –_ that they are in between spaces, times, destinations, life and death – and so they recognise their indeterminacy, instability and radical uncertainty, what Mexistentialists call _zozobra_. In short, they are sure of one thing: that '_nada es seguro_'. That's my father.

想到我父亲，是我认为现在——或在危机时刻——阅读墨西哥存在主义之所以重要的一个原因。当我们这样做时，我们不仅获得了一套词汇来帮助表达我们当下的危机——诸如偶然性、_zozobra_、_nepantla_ 和 _relajo_ 这样的词语——而且它帮助我们理解我们的危机与我们的哲学如何密不可分地联系在一起，历史创伤如何塑造或影响我们的视角，以及为什么我们的视角在根本上如此重要。毕竟，我父亲认为「什么都不确定」是有原因的，这与他读过的东西或别人告诉他的话毫无关系。这与他所经历的生活息息相关。

Thinking of my father is one reason why I think it's important to read Mexistentialism _now_, or to read it _in times of crisis_. When we do, not only are we gifted with vocabulary that can help us articulate our current crisis – words like accidentality, _zozobra_, _nepantla_ and _relajo_ – but it helps us understand how our crises and our philosophies are intimately tied to one another, how historical trauma shapes or informs our perspectives, and why our perspectives matter in the first place. After all, there is a reason why my father thinks that 'nothing is certain', and it has nothing to do with something he's read or something someone's told him. It has everything to do with the life he's lived.

一些哲学家一谈到视角或历史事件的塑造力量就焦虑不安，但在墨西哥存在主义中，这些恰恰是理解我们自身和我们的世界的核心，尤其是理解身处危机世界中的我们自身。不可否认，这正是我们今天所处的位置：一个危机四伏的世界。而我所说的「我们」，指的是那些生活在危机之中或持续面临危机威胁的人。在这一点上，我遵循墨西哥哲学家埃米利奥·乌兰加（Emilio Uranga，1921–88）所说的将阅读和欣赏他的哲学分析的「我们」：他们是「那些因千般历史、文化或社会的偶然事件而被灾难所框定的人」。对乌兰加以及他的同时代人来说，那些「被灾难所框定」的人就是墨西哥人以及所有与墨西哥人一样、能够认同一段遭受压迫、边缘化和历史暴力之经历的「他者」。

Some philosophers get anxious with talk of perspectives or the shaping power of historical events, but in Mexistentialism these are central to understanding ourselves and our world, and especially ourselves in a world in crisis. Undeniably, this is where we find ourselves today: a world in crisis. And by 'we', I mean those of us who exist in crisis or under the constant threat of crisis. In this, I follow what the Mexican philosopher Emilio Uranga (1921-88) means when he refers to the 'we' that will read and appreciate his philosophical analyses: they are 'those others that through a thousand accidents of history, of culture or society, have been framed by the catastrophic'. For Uranga, as well as for his contemporaries, those 'framed by the catastrophic' are Mexicans and all 'others' who, like Mexicans, can identify with a history of oppression, marginalisation and historical violence.

乌兰加未能预见的是，自从他写下这些话大约75年以来，被灾难所框定的「我们」已经壮大了。那些视角被创伤、暴力、迫害和恐惧所塑造的「我们」，已扩展到包括全球各地许多其他民族。就近而言，在美国，移民或那些看起来像移民的人每天都在被背叛、不安全感和恐惧所框定——塑造、影响、留下伤疤——而对这些人来说，「什么都不确定」是唯一的确定性。

What Uranga couldn't have foreseen is that the 'we' that has been framed by the catastrophic has grown since he wrote those words some 75 years ago. The _we_ whose perspectives are shaped by trauma, violence, persecution and fear has expanded to include many other peoples from across the globe. Closer to home, in the US, it is immigrants or those who _look like_ immigrants who, every day, are framed – shaped, informed, scarred – by betrayal, insecurity and terror, and for whom 'nothing is certain' is the only certainty.

对我而言，在危机时刻阅读墨西哥存在主义是一种揭示和赋能。它对你也可以是揭示和赋能的。它在提醒我们人性是脆弱和偶然的（因此，什么都不确定）的同时，教导我们，我们的危机——即使它们被灾难所框定——也不过是表面上看起来如此。我们的危机不会摧毁我们！而我们之所以知道危机不会摧毁我们，是因为这些危机铭刻在历史之中，正是历史框定了我们是谁。这至少是墨西哥存在主义及其底层历史主义的启示。

For me, reading Mexistentialism in times of crisis has been revealing and empowering. And it can be revealing and empowering for you too. While reminding us that our humanity is fragile and accidental (thus, that nothing is certain), it teaches us that our crises, even if they are framed by the catastrophic, are that only in appearance. Our crises will not destroy us! And we know that our crises will not destroy us because these crises are inscribed in history, and it is history that _frames_ who we are. This is, at least, the lesson of Mexistentialism and its underlying historicism.

借鉴何塞·奥尔特加·伊·加塞特（José Ortega y Gasset）、威廉·狄尔泰（Wilhelm Dilthey）、马丁·海德格尔（Martin Heidegger）等人的思想，20世纪中叶的墨西哥哲学家们开始吸收「历史主义」，西班牙哲学家胡利安·马里亚斯（Julián Marías）于1967年将其概括为如下主张：

Taking a cue from José Ortega y Gasset, Wilhelm Dilthey, Martin Heidegger and others, Mexican philosophers of the mid-20th century come to appropriate 'historicism', summarised by the Spanish philosopher Julián Marías in 1967 as the claim that:

人类生活的视界是历史的；人由其所处的命运注定的历史层面来定义；人曾经是什么，构成了人现在是什么的本质组成部分；他今天之所以是他，恰恰因为他曾经是其他的东西；人类生活的领域包含历史。

the horizon of human life is historical; man is defined by the historical level at which it has been his lot to live; what man has been is an essential component of what he is; he is what he is today precisely because he was other things formerly; the realm of human life includes history.

对于墨西哥存在主义者来说，历史主义的真理是不言而喻的：殖民主义、独立、革命和其他历史经验创造了他们所理解的——追随奥尔特加——「人类生活的视界」或「墨西哥境况」的条件。根据莱奥波尔多·塞亚（Leopoldo Zea）的开创性著作《走向[拉丁]美洲哲学》（_Toward a [Latin] American Philosophy_，1942），这一境况「总是作为一个问题出现」。哲学思考就是试图解决那个问题。

For Mexistentialists, the truth of historicism is obvious: colonialism, independence, revolution and other historical experiences created the conditions for what they understood, after Ortega, as 'the horizon of human life', or as the 'Mexican circumstance' – one that, according to Leopoldo Zea's pioneering work _Toward a [Latin] American Philosophy_ (1942), 'presents itself always as a problem'. To philosophise is to attempt to solve that problem.

归根结底，墨西哥存在主义作为一种哲学世界观之所以具有吸引力，是因为它为处境化的、即境性的历史视角或观点进行了辩护或确认——它赋予了这种视角力量。

Ultimately, what makes Mexistentialism attractive as a philosophical worldview is that it vindicates or validates the situated, or circumstantial, historical viewpoint or perspective – it empowers it.

墨西哥哲学，尤其是在20世纪中叶，给自己设定了一项艰巨的任务：概括而言，它的哲学家们试图在后殖民的版图上，为自己解读存在、生存和自由的意义——这片版图上叠加着几个世纪以来最初为了削弱、压迫和边缘化而构建的宗教、政治和本体论阐释。四百多年来，西方尽其所能地沉淀了一种特定的欧洲中心主义世界观。这种沉淀体现在各种存在和行为方式上，而这些方式反映的是非墨西哥式的存在和行为；这成为了一个需要哲学干预的问题。

Mexican philosophy, particularly in the mid-20th century, set for itself a difficult task: broadly speaking, its philosophers sought to decipher, for themselves, the meaning of being, existence and freedom within a postcolonial landscape layered with centuries of religious, political and ontological interpretations originally constructed to disempower, oppress and marginalise. For more than 400 years, the West did its best to sediment a certain Eurocentric worldview. This sedimentation manifested in ways of being and doing that were reflections of a non-Mexican being and doing; this became a problem needing a philosophical intervention.

我说这些是为了强调这样一个观念：墨西哥哲学总体上，尤其是墨西哥存在主义，倾向于扎根于历史、文化或「境况」之中。对概念、思想或现象的分析往往由真实的事件、特定的时刻、运动和动荡所驱动。对现象或概念的抽象考量虽然并非这一传统所排斥，但并不是定义这一传统的东西。因此，墨西哥存在主义哲学家声称要「把哲学带到街头」和「给亚里士多德穿上瓦拉切」（put huaraches on Aristotle），这是一种戏剧化的说法，意思是他们的目标是将哲学拉到日常、到熟悉和具体、到生活世界和墨西哥人的生活经验中去。

I say this to highlight the idea that Mexican philosophy in general, and Mexistentialism in particular, tends to root itself in history, or culture, or in the 'circumstance'. Analyses of concepts, ideas or phenomena are often motivated by actual events, by moments in time, by movements and upheavals. The abstract consideration of phenomena or concepts, while not alien to this tradition, is not what defines it. Thus, Mexistentialist philosophers claim to, as it were, 'take philosophy out to the streets' and 'put huaraches on Aristotle', a dramatic way of saying that their goal is to pull philosophy down to the everyday, to the familiar and concrete, to the lived world and to the lived experience of Mexicans.

墨西哥存在主义的历史主义坚持认为历史事件塑造了我们思考的方式和内容。墨西哥历史本身——暴力的、创伤的、灾难性的——要求哲学家以非理想主义的方式解释世界，而给亚里士多德穿上瓦拉切、让哲学为普通人所能理解的努力，正是对这一要求的回应。哲学家阿韦拉多·维列加斯（Abelardo Villegas）将这种要求归因于事件的「结构」。事件的结构是历史学家穷尽心力去解读之后仍然完整的东西。这种结构是无法被解读消解的，而正是对这种结构的关注，最终赋予了墨西哥历史其独特性，赋予了墨西哥哲学洞见其价值。

Mexistentialist historicism insists that historical events shape how we think and what we think. Mexican history itself – one that is violent, traumatic and catastrophic – _demands_ that philosophers interpret the world in a non-idealistic way, and the effort to put huaraches on Aristotle, or make philosophy intelligible to the common person, is answering that demand. The philosopher Abelardo Villegas attributes such demands to the 'structure' of events. The structure of an event is what remains intact after historians exhaust themselves trying to interpret it. This structure is what cannot be interpreted away and the centring of which, ultimately, lends Mexican history its uniqueness and Mexican philosophical insights their value.

历史力量将影响哲学所说的内容以及像我父亲这样的人所认为是真实的东西。一段充斥着暴力、征服、殖民和革命的历史催生了乌兰加关于偶然性的哲学；一段充斥着移民、迫害、死亡和破碎承诺的历史催生了我父亲「什么都不确定」的悲观看法。虽然这些思想或观念是在独特的历史经验中锻造的，但它们被传达为对所有人都有价值的教训。

Historical forces will inform what is said by philosophy and what is thought as _true_ by folks like my father. A history of violence, conquest, colonialism and revolution motivates Uranga's philosophy of accidentality, for instance; a history of immigration, persecution, death and broken promises motivates my father's pessimistic outlook that 'nothing is certain'. And while these ideas or outlooks are forged in a unique historical experience, they are communicated as valuable lessons for all.

维列加斯说，墨西哥哲学家将会「向其他民族提供一种他们可能未曾拥有的历史和文化经验」。这呼应了乌兰加在其《墨西哥存在的分析》（_Analysis of Mexican Being_，1952）中的主张，他坚持认为墨西哥哲学家「有教训可以教授」，而且这种教训绝不能是「不可传达的」。这将是从经验语言翻译为哲学语言的教训，这意味着它将是可分享的、可传递的，并且潜在地具有赋能作用。这种赋能是必要的，尤其是对那些被灾难所框定的人。对他们来说，从自己的视角谈论自己的经验可能从来都不是被授权的事情，即使被授权了，也不被认为有任何价值。

Villegas says that the Mexican philosopher will come to 'offer other peoples … a historical and cultural experience that they may not have.' This echoes Uranga's claim in his _Analysis of Mexican Being_ (1952), where he insists that Mexican philosophers 'have a lesson to teach', and that this lesson must not be 'incommunicable'. It will be a lesson translated from the language of experience to the language of philosophy, which means it will be shareable, transferable and potentially empowering. Such empowerment is necessary, especially for those framed by the catastrophic. For them, speaking from their own perspective about their own experience may not be something that has ever been authorised, and, if authorised, deemed of any value.

然而，如果哲学是由历史（由时间性的和可变的东西）所影响的，那么客观性怎么办？我们中最理性的人坚持客观视角的至高地位——定义为非个人的、超然的、非历史的视角——因为它赋予我们的知识以正当性，赋予我们的科学以信心。一个受历史影响的视角威胁到了这种至高地位。

Yet, if philosophy is informed by history (by the temporal and changeable), then what about _objectivity_? The most rational among us insist on the supremacy of the objective point of view, defined as the impersonal, detached and ahistorical perspective, because it lends justification to our knowledge, and confidence to our science. A historically informed perspective threatens this supremacy.

但对墨西哥存在主义者来说，客观性也许并不像人们吹捧的那么好。根据莱奥波尔多·塞亚（Leopoldo Zea，1912–2004）的观点，客观性不过是殖民世界观的又一残余。塞亚将对客观性的坚持称为一种「帝国的激情」（imperial passion），即一种追求超然、去人格化和匿名性的帝国主义欲望。在这种观点下，客观性是一种对当权者（帝国）有用的意识形态建构，他们将其作为衡量什么算作「真实」和值得传达的标准。如果一个「真理」不是严格意义上的「客观的」，那么它就被贬低为文化或历史特异性的产物——被视为习俗、神话或迷信。

For Mexistentialists though, objectivity may not be all that it's cracked up to be. According to Leopoldo Zea (1912-2004), objectivity is but another remnant of a colonial worldview. Zea referred to the insistence on objectivity as an 'imperial passion', that is, an imperialist desire for detachment, depersonalisation and anonymity. On this view, objectivity is an ideological construct useful for those in power (the Imperium), who set it as a metric over what counts as 'true' and worth communicating. If a 'truth' is not 'objective' in this strict sense of the term, then it is discounted as a product of cultural or historical idiosyncrasy – as custom, myth or superstition.

我们无法触及「无立场的视角」（view from nowhere）。我们所能触及的只有自己的生活、自己的历史和自己的文化。

We cannot access the 'view from nowhere'. We have access only to our own lives, our history and our culture.

但将真理或知识贬低为文化和历史的特异性是一种否定处境化历史视角或观点的方式。然而，根据塞亚和乌兰加等哲学家的看法，处境化的历史视角是我们唯一可用的视角，因为我们自己就是处境化的和历史性的存在（我们不可能不是如此）。我们无法触及欧洲中心主义的客观性（以及哲学）所承诺的那种「无立场的视角」。我们所能触及的只有自己的生活、自己的历史和自己的文化。

But discounting truth or knowledge as a cultural and historical idiosyncrasy is a way to invalidate situated, historical perspectives or viewpoints. However, according to philosophers like Zea and Uranga, the situated, historical perspective is the only one available to us, since we are situated and historical ourselves (we can't be otherwise). We cannot access the 'view from nowhere' that Eurocentric views of objectivity (and philosophy) promise. We have access only to our own lives, our history and our culture.

批评者指出，在最好的情况下，历史主义的哲学路径仅仅反映了构成某一特定时期文化的价值观、信仰和习俗。这种观点促使古巴裔美国哲学家豪尔赫·格拉西亚（Jorge Gracia，1942–2021）对原创的、自主的和真正的拉丁美洲哲学的可能性提出质疑，理由是哲学的文化基础不具备普遍适用性，因此缺乏哲学价值。格拉西亚将此称为文化主义（culturalist）路径，并论证它部分导致了拉丁美洲真正的哲学传统的延迟到来。在格拉西亚看来，即使文化在知识和价值观的产生中发挥了形成性作用，其时间维度也使它不具备真正哲学的资格。历史主义也面临同样的指控。

Critics point out that, in the best-case scenario, the historicist approach to philosophy merely reflects those values, beliefs and customs that constitute a culture at a particular time. This view leads the Cuban-born American philosopher Jorge Gracia (1942-2021) to question the possibility of an original, autonomous and genuine Latin American philosophy on the grounds that a cultural foundation to philosophy has no universal applicability and thus lacks philosophical value. Gracia calls this the _culturalist_ approach and argues that it is partly responsible for the delayed arrival of a proper philosophical tradition in Latin America. In Gracia's estimation, even if culture plays a formative role in the production of knowledge and values, its temporal dimension disqualifies it from being properly philosophical. Historicism is subject to the same indictment.

塞亚在其开创性论文《作为承诺的哲学》（Philosophy as Commitment，1952）中写道：「将自己奉献给普遍的和永恒的东西，而不做出任何一个具体的承诺，这并没有让我们承诺任何东西。」虽然这写于格拉西亚对拉丁美洲哲学的批评数十年之前，但它可以作为对那种批评的回应。在这里，塞亚暗示，在不关注具体事物（文化、历史、境况等）的情况下去寻求普遍性是毫无意义的。做出具体的个别承诺才是重要的。而对具体事物的承诺可以采取多种形式，包括信赖继承下来的智慧（「什么都不确定」）或重视自己的视角。

In his seminal essay 'Philosophy as Commitment' (1952), Zea says: 'Committing ourselves to the universal and the eternal, without making a single concrete commitment, does not commit us to anything.' While this was written decades before Gracia's critical remarks on Latin American philosophy, it could serve as a response to that critique. Here, Zea suggests that to seek universality without attending to the concrete (to culture, history, the circumstance and so on) is pointless. Making single concrete commitments is what's important. And committing oneself to the concrete can take many forms, including trusting in inherited wisdom ('nothing is certain') or valuing one's own perspective.

这就把我们带到了视角主义。墨西哥存在主义及其底层的历史主义都不应与我们通常所说的「视角主义」相混淆，即使我使用「视角」或「观点」这样的词来谈论它们。视角主义本身被墨西哥哲学家基于主体间性的理由所拒绝。也就是说，基于他者始终是我自身和我所知之事的一个条件。或者用奥尔特加著名格言（「我是我以及我的境况」）的意译来说，我是谁是我与我的境况互动的产物，而这些境况是由与我处于共融和共同体中的他者构成的。

This brings us to perspectivism. Neither Mexistentialism nor its underlying historicism is to be confused with what we normally call 'perspectivism', even if I use words like 'perspective' or 'viewpoint' to talk about them. Perspectivism itself is rejected by Mexican philosophers on intersubjective grounds. That is, on the grounds that _others_ are always a condition for myself and for what I know. Or that, in paraphrase of Ortega's famous dictum ('I am me and my circumstances'), _who I am is the product of my engagement with my circumstances_, and these are constituted by others in communion and community with myself.

不过，说墨西哥存在主义确立了一种稳健的视角主义（robust perspectivism）并不为错，这种视角主义之所以稳健，是因为它将我的视角（或聚焦点，_enfoque_）与一系列视角联系起来，这些视角共同呈现出「客观」真理的外观，但实际上是主体间性的真理（多个主体的真理）。主体间性的真理反映或播送的是文化或历史的观点；它不是托马斯·内格尔（Thomas Nagel）那种公正的或帝国的客观性的「无立场的视角」。

It wouldn't be wrong, however, to say that Mexistentialism assures itself on a _robust_ perspectivism, one that becomes robust by linking my perspective (or _enfoque_) to an array of perspectives that, together, give the _appearance_ of 'objective' truth, but that is actually intersubjective truth (truths of multiple subjects). Intersubjective truth reflects or broadcasts a cultural or historical viewpoint; it is not Thomas Nagel's 'view from nowhere' of impartial, or imperial, objectivity.

再次说明，当格拉西亚将墨西哥哲学称为「文化主义的」时，他是将其作为一种批评。哲学史上的文化主义路径假设哲学与文化及其危机存在某种密切关系——与如何应对这些危机、如何克服它们或如何处理它们有关。格拉西亚这样称呼它是为了凸显其缺乏客观性（并使其与真正的哲学保持距离）。但这种缺失并非墨西哥哲学的弱点，而是其力量。它是对主观视角的肯定。此外，如果视角矩阵（或文化主义立场）为我们提供了对自身所处现实或处境的忠实图景，或者它真实地反映了世界对我们的样貌，那么我看不出为什么它不应该成为驱动我们哲学的动力。

Again, when Gracia refers to Mexican philosophy as 'culturalist', he means it as a critique. A 'culturalist' approach to the history of philosophy assumes philosophy to be related in some intimate way to culture and its crises – with how to address these or how to overcome them or deal with them. Gracia calls it this to highlight its _lack of_ objectivity (and distance it from genuine philosophy). But this _lack_ is not a shortcoming of Mexican philosophy, but a strength. It is an affirmation of the _subjective point of view_. Moreover, if the perspectival array (or the culturalist standpoint) gives us a faithful picture of the reality or the situation in which we find ourselves, or if it truly reflects how the world is _for us_, then I don't see why it shouldn't be that which motivates our philosophies.

粗心的读者可能会得出结论：这里真正发生的事情是墨西哥哲学家根本不想费心将真理从语境中剥离出来，或者他们只是不了解客观性的哲学要求，因此也不了解什么是真正的哲学。当然，事实并非如此。墨西哥哲学家既非不关心、也非不了解西方所认为的「真正的哲学」；他们深知其历史和意识形态要求。相反，他们将主观视角连同文化和历史作为其哲学思考的主要成分，因为这样做，他们确认了那些要素、他们自身以及他们的哲学。

A careless reader may conclude that what's really going on here is that Mexican philosophers simply do not want to bother distancing truth from context, or that they are simply ignorant of the philosophical requirements of objectivity, and thus of genuine philosophy. This, of course, is not the case. Mexican philosophers are neither unbothered by nor ignorant of what the West has deemed 'genuine philosophy'; they know its history and ideological demands well. Rather, they make the subjective point of view together with culture and history the main ingredients in their philosophising because, in doing so, they validate those elements, themselves, and their philosophy.

这里有教训。墨西哥存在主义有东西可以教给我们。它教导我们，历史经验对于自我理解是必要的，更重要的是，历史意识对于构建那些应该对我们的哲学至关重要的根本问题是不可或缺的。带着这些教训，我们可以着手识别、阐述和捍卫全球各地的其他哲学干预。我们可以着手推进一项全球哲学的事业。

And there are lessons here. Mexistentialism has something to teach us. It teaches us that historical experience is necessary for self-understanding and, more importantly, that historical consciousness is essential for framing those questions that should matter to our philosophy. With these lessons we can get on with the task of recognising, articulating and vindicating other philosophical interventions throughout the globe. We can get on with the task of a global philosophy.

我这样说是因为我怀疑阻碍学术哲学家拥抱全球哲学传统的一种焦虑是：他们认为这样做就等于拥抱了哲学中的文化相对主义之类的东西。在涉及墨西哥哲学时，我逐渐认为，那种认为它仅仅提供一个特定的文化视角的焦虑，源于将历史主义混淆为视角主义——一种相对主义的、唯我论的教条，封闭了相互交流的道路。但对于墨西哥存在主义者来说，文化视角不是封闭的、相对主义的或唯我论的，而是对生活经验的历史性表达，每当它们被分享时就超越了自身。

I say this because I suspect that an anxiety keeping academic philosophers from embracing global philosophical traditions is the thought that, in doing so, they would be embracing something like _cultural relativism_ in philosophy. When it comes to Mexican philosophy, I've come to think that the anxiety that it offers _merely_ a determinate cultural perspective is grounded on confusing historicism for perspectivism as a relativistic, solipsistic doctrine that closes off roads to intercommunication. But for Mexistentialists in particular, cultural perspectives are not closed, relativistic or solipsistic, but historically informed articulations of lived experience that transcend themselves whenever they're shared.

乌兰加、塞亚和维列加斯所阐明的是，墨西哥哲学所提供的哲学观点（或视角、立场）不是相对主义的，而是历史的、文化的和即境的。如果一个视角同时是这些东西，那么它就值得被传达和分享。更重要的是，这些哲学家向我们保证，这些可分享的或超越性的真理没有什么抽象或神秘的地方。它们由历史和经验的压力塑造而成，任何认识到那些压力的人都能认识这些真理。当这些视角或真理汇聚在一起时，它们描绘出人类处境的轮廓——熟悉的，却又是粗粝的、永远不完整的。然而，只有当这些文化的、历史的或即境的真理在一个真理矩阵中相互关联时，我们才能谈论「全球哲学」——如果我们真的能谈论这种东西，而不说出超出我们有理由说的话的话。

What Uranga, Zea and Villegas make clear is that the philosophical point of view (or viewpoint, or perspective) offered by Mexican philosophy is not relativistic, but historical, cultural and circumstantial. And that if a perspective is all these things, then it's worth communicating and sharing. More importantly, these philosophers assure us that there's nothing abstract or mysterious about these shareable, or transcendental, truths. They are formed by the pressures of history and experience, and can be recognised by anyone who recognises those pressures. When these perspectives or truths are brought together, they paint an outline of the human condition that is familiar, yet rough and always incomplete. However, only when these cultural, historical or circumstantial truths are linked together in an array of truths can we talk of 'global philosophy' – _if_ we can talk about such things at all without saying more than what we are justified in saying.

但为什么不干脆按照西方教导我们的方式来做哲学——也就是说，不参照历史或境况——_sub specie aeternitatis_（从永恒的视角出发）。答案非常直接，与所谓的哲学「双重束缚」有关：墨西哥哲学家可以确认一种与墨西哥差异的特殊性相一致的本真的哲学身份，从而牢牢扎根于墨西哥历史作为意义和价值的源泉；或者坚持哲学作为人类共性的非历史表达，这要求超越那段历史和那些境况，从而超越差异。这是一个双重束缚，因为如果它选择保留其历史差异，那么它就有可能不符合西方意义上的哲学资格；如果它选择符合西方意义上的哲学资格，那么它就有可能抹去自身的差异。墨西哥哲学家选择了保留他们的差异，将哲学与他们的历史联系起来。

But why not just do philosophy as the West has taught us to do philosophy, that is, without reference to history or circumstance – _sub specie aeternitatis_ (from an eternal perspective). The answer to this is very straightforward and related to what is known as a philosophical 'double bind': Mexican philosophers can either affirm an authentic philosophical identity aligned with the particularity of Mexican difference, and thus firmly within the bounds of Mexican history as a source of meaning and value, or insist on philosophy as an ahistorical expression of a human sameness, something that requires the transcendence of that history and those circumstances and thus a transcendence of difference. It's a double bind because if it chooses to retain its historical difference, then it risks not being philosophical in the Western sense; if it chooses to be philosophical in the Western sense, then it risks erasing its difference. Mexican philosophers chose to retain their difference by linking their philosophy to their history.

我父亲对确定性的态度并非他独有。这是一种在历史和经验的熔炉中锻造的世界观，由墨西哥存在主义者用偶然性、_zozobra_、_relajo_ 和 _nepantla_ 等概念为我们阐述——这些概念捕捉了人类生命的偶然性和脆弱性。在宣称「什么都不确定」时，我父亲不过是在呼应经验的教诲，同时为可能发生的事情做好准备。他在关键时刻轻易地引用哲学家的思想，这并不让我惊讶。毕竟，他是一个历史境况的产物，与那些先于他的哲学家的境况并无不同：虽然充满灾难，却也充满力量和赋能。关于我们绝大多数人，无论是否墨西哥人，我们同样可以说：我们是一段历史的产物，这段历史虽然可能是创伤性的和痛苦的，但也可以是充满力量和赋能的。

My father's attitude to certainty is not his alone. It is a worldview forged in the crucible of history and experience, and articulated for us by Mexican existentialists with concepts like accidentality, _zozobra_, _relajo_ and _nepantla_ – concepts that capture the contingency and vulnerability of human life. In pronouncing that 'nothing is certain', my father is merely echoing the teachings of experience, while simultaneously preparing himself for what may come. That he easily channels philosophers in critical life moments does not surprise me. After all, he is a product of a historical circumstance that is not unlike those of the philosophers who came before him: although catastrophic, it is powerful and empowering. And we could say the same about the great majority of us, Mexican or not: we are products of a history that, while it may be traumatic and painful, can also be powerful and empowering.

我自己无法成为一个乐观主义者，原因与我父亲选择冒险面对移民执法部门的原因相同。最近的事件已经充分表明，我的公民身份提供的确定性和安全感微乎其微，尽管我出生在美国并且在这里度过了大半生。如果政治风向继续朝着目前的方向吹去，我最终可能不得不和父亲一起住在米却肯，被流放离开家园，成为历史及其诸多灾难的牺牲品。但我只知道历史教给我的东西。而历史教给我的是我父亲是对的：_nada es seguro_。于是我们继续前行。

I myself cannot be an optimist for the same reasons that my father chose to take his chances with immigration enforcement. Recent events have made it abundantly clear that my citizenship offers little certainty and security, even though I was born in the US and have lived here most of my life. If the political winds continue to blow in the direction in which they are currently blowing, I may end up living with my father in Michoacán, exiled from my home, a victim of history and its many catastrophes. But I know only what history has taught me. And history has taught me that my father's right: _nada es seguro_. And so we persist.
