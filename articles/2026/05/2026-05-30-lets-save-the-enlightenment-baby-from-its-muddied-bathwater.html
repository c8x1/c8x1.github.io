哲学

让我们把启蒙运动的婴儿从浑浊的洗澡水中救出来

Eliane Glaser·Aeon·2026年5月30日·14分钟阅读·原文

启蒙运动正在经历一段黑暗时期。大西洋两岸的批判种族理论家追随哲学家伊曼纽尔·埃泽（Emmanuel Eze）和查尔斯·W·米尔斯（Charles W Mills），认为启蒙运动应对现代种族主义负责。在《帝国时代》（The Age of Empire，2021）一书中，英国社会学家肯迪·安德鲁斯（Kehinde Andrews）表示，现在是停止崇敬康德（Kant）、洛克（Locke）和伏尔泰（Voltaire）等「死去的白人男性」的时候了。去年，爱丁堡大学（University of Edinburgh）——被广泛认为在传播种族主义科学理论方面发挥了「超乎寻常」的历史作用——进行了一次深刻的自我审视过程，发布了一份种族评估报告，承认苏格兰启蒙运动的领军人物对传播「人类历史上最具破坏性的观念」负有责任，包括认为人类社会存在于一个从「野蛮」到「文明」的等级「阶梯」上的观念，而欧洲人位于顶端。报告重点提到了大卫·休谟（David Hume）的角色，他在1753年版本的《论国民性格》（On National Characters，1748）论文中一个臭名昭著的脚注里声称，非白人种族「天生劣于白人」。该大学承认，它至今仍拥有来自与奴隶贸易和其他殖民征服有关的捐赠者的数百万英镑遗赠。与此同时，这座城市正陷入一场关于如何处理亨利·邓达斯（Henry Dundas）雕像的长期争议中，大多数历史学家认为他应对推迟英国议会废除奴隶制的进程负责。

The Enlightenment is going through a dark time. Critical race theorists on both sides of the Atlantic are following the philosophers Emmanuel Eze and Charles W Mills in holding the Enlightenment responsible for modern racism. In _The Age of Empire_ (2021), the British sociologist Kehinde Andrews says that it is time to stop revering 'dead white men' such as Kant, Locke and Voltaire. Last year, the University of Edinburgh, which is widely seen as having had an 'outsized' historic role in promulgating racist scientific theories, undertook an excoriating process of self-examination, publishing a Race Review that acknowledged that the leading thinkers of the Scottish Enlightenment were responsible for propagating 'some of the most damaging ideas in human history', including the idea that human societies exist on a hierarchical 'ladder', from 'savage' to 'civilised', with Europeans at the top. The Review highlighted the role of David Hume, who, in a notorious footnote to the 1753 version of his essay 'On National Characters' (1748), stated that non-white races are 'naturally inferior to the whites'. The university admits that it still has bequests totalling many millions of pounds from donors linked to the slave trade and other colonial conquests. At the same time, the city is embroiled in a long dispute over what to do with a statue of Henry Dundas, who most historians hold responsible for delaying the progress of abolition through UK Parliament.

启蒙运动因左翼指责其为帝国进行哲学粉饰而遭到谴责，同时它还受到民粹主义右翼的攻击，后者在其对我们既有政治制度以及代议制民主和专业知识的传统架构的影响力根基中看到了深远影响：那些为启蒙运动价值观挺身而出的人很容易被斥为「自满的自由精英」。2025年，威尔·赫顿（Will Hutton）在《观察家报》（The Observer）中哀叹道，在民粹主义专制时代，曾经被视为理所当然的美好事物——「正义、问责、社会公平、科学进步、国际秩序」——已经与一个「婆罗门阶层——即新的文明敌人」联系在一起。赫顿写道，对这个新敌人的攻击是由「对启蒙运动价值观旗手的报复需求」所推动的。启蒙运动的右翼批评者得到了硅谷技术大佬的支持。事实上，由极右翼软件开发者柯蒂斯·雅文（Curtis Yarvin）开创、受到J·D·万斯（J D Vance）和彼得·蒂尔（Peter Thiel）等人拥护的所谓「黑暗启蒙运动」，正试图抹除启蒙运动中平等和民主的价值观。

Censured by the Left for its philosophy-washing of Empire, the Enlightenment is further under fire from the populist Right who see the long arm of its influence in the foundations of our established political institutions and the traditional architecture of representative democracy and professional expertise: those who stand up for Enlightenment values are liable to find themselves castigated as members of a 'complacent liberal elite'. Writing in _The_ _Observer_ in 2025, Will Hutton bemoaned the fact that, in an era of populist autocracy, what were once taken-for-granted goods – 'justice, accountability, social fairness, scientific progress, international order' – have become associated with a 'Brahmin class – who are the new civilisational enemy.' Attacks on this new enemy are fuelled, Hutton wrote, by 'the need for vengeance on the standard-bearers of Enlightenment values.' Right-wing critics of the Enlightenment are supported by Silicon Valley tech bros. In fact, the so-called 'Dark Enlightenment' pioneered by the far-Right software developer Curtis Yarvin, championed by the likes of J D Vance and Peter Thiel, seeks to obliterate the Enlightenment values of equality and democracy.

著名语言学家和心理学家史蒂文·平克（Steven Pinker）已匆忙为启蒙运动辩护，他在2018年关于该主题的著作副标题是「理性、科学、人文主义与进步的论证」。平克认为，这些核心价值观带来了人类健康、繁荣和和平方面可衡量的改善。虽然看到杰出评论家为公认的事实和追求知识挺身而出是件好事，尤其是在学术、政治和媒体正被后真相、阴谋论和对专家的不信任所侵蚀的时代，但我无法全心全意地加入这一巨头行列。在 glaring 的经济不平等、气候崩溃和全球南方（Global South）的广泛贫困之中，平克对文明进步的信念至少可以说是过于乐观的。此外，我认为左翼对启蒙运动——无论其本质上还是情境上——的种族主义批评是有道理的。如果我们不正视启蒙运动的复杂性，它将继续被文化战争武器化，并被用于极端的论战目的。左翼可以全盘否定启蒙运动对智力严谨性和对真理承诺的提醒，而保守派则可以将其作为鼓吹西方优越性的工具，从而边缘化争取社会正义的重要运动。

Famously, the linguist and psychologist Steven Pinker has rushed to defend the Enlightenment, subtitling his 2018 book on the subject: 'The Case for Reason, Science, Humanism, and Progress'. These core values, says Pinker, have led to measurable improvements in human health, prosperity and peace. Although it's good to see prominent commentators stand up for agreed facts and the pursuit of knowledge, especially at a time when scholarship, politics and the media are being eroded by post-truth, conspiracy theories and a mistrust of experts, I cannot align myself wholeheartedly with this big-beast bandwagon. In the midst of glaring economic inequalities, climate breakdown and widespread poverty in the Global South, Pinker's faith in civilisational progress seems optimistic to say the least. Besides, I believe there is something to the Leftist critique of the Enlightenment as either intrinsically or circumstantially racist. If we don't engage with the Enlightenment's complexities, it will continue to be weaponised by the culture wars, and for extremist polemical ends. Leftists can cancel, wholesale, the Enlightenment's reminder of the need for intellectual rigour and a commitment to truth, while conservatives can use it as a tub-thumping defence of the West that marginalises the vital campaign for social justice.

由于这种两面夹击，启蒙运动的遗产在存在意义上岌岌可危。这让我深感忧虑，因为我的整个职业生涯都建立在试图理解和分析周围世界的基础上——尤其是一个仍然试图将思考中的女性局限于情感和「个人经验」领域的世界。

In consequence of this pincer-movement attack, the Enlightenment's legacy is existentially vulnerable. It makes me deeply worried as someone whose entire career has been built on trying to understand and analyse the world around me – especially a world that still tries to confine thinking women to the realms of emotion and 'personal experience'.

我认为启蒙运动的价值观是不可或缺的，但我们已经在很大程度上忘记了如何为其做出有力辩护：我们需要依靠经实验验证的共享事实；一个可以进行公开讨论的公共领域；以及讨论应建立在理性论证基础上的信念。此外，我们还需要珍视宽容、自由、人权和公共利益等更具政治性的价值观。人工智能的倡导者竟然厚颜无耻地声称大语言模型正在引领一场「第二次启蒙」，而事实上我们看到的是在启蒙运动之名下的对其遗产的摧毁。正如历史学家大卫·贝尔（David Bell）2025年在《纽约时报》（The New York Times）中所论证的，人工智能实际上正在「抛弃启蒙运动价值观」，因为它只是强化了「我们已有的认知」。在《卫报》（The Guardian）上，记者兼地缘政治风险顾问约瑟夫·德·韦克（Joseph de Weck）警告说，「人工智能正在把我们带回黑暗时代」，让我们变得懒惰，阻碍独立思考。

I believe that Enlightenment values are essential, but that we have largely forgotten how to make a good case for them: we need to rely on shared facts, tested by experiment; a public sphere where open discussion can take place; and the belief that discussion should be founded on reasoned argument. We need, moreover, to cherish the more political values of tolerance, freedom, human rights and the common good. Advocates for artificial intelligence have the temerity to claim that large language models are ushering in a 'second Enlightenment' when what we are in fact seeing is the destruction of the Enlightenment legacy under the false banner of its name. As the historian David Bell argued in _The New York Times_ in 2025, AI is actually 'shedding Enlightenment values' by simply reinforcing 'what we already think we know.' In _The Guardian_, the journalist and geopolitical risk consultant Joseph de Weck warned that 'AI is taking us back to the dark ages', making us lazy, and stymying independent thinking.

证据表明我们正在经历一场快速的去启蒙化。报纸发行量、注意力持续时间以及对公认知识形式的信任都在急剧下降。错误信息、虚假信息和深度伪造正在不断扩张。如果我们放弃启蒙运动计划中宝贵的方面，我们将暴露在一个充斥着人工智能胡言乱语、「我的真理」宣言、摇摆不定的情绪和不受约束的权力的世界中。

The evidence suggests that we are going through a rapid de-enlightenment. Newspaper circulations, attention spans and trust in forms of agreed knowledge are in freefall. Misinformation, disinformation and deepfakes are gaining ground. If we let go of the valuable aspects of the Enlightenment project, we open ourselves up to a world of AI blather, 'my truth' pronouncements, wobbly sentiment and unchecked power.

我对这种危险状况的不安促使我回过头来重新思考和评估启蒙运动及其所能提供的价值。但是，我不想不加思索地将其作为一个使命来收复，而是想梳理和权衡其优点，细致地辨别哪些东西仍然适合我们的时代。我想问的是，是否有可能挽救启蒙运动的号召力，以及是否值得捍卫左翼和右翼联合力量正在共同攻击的东西。启蒙运动的缺陷是附着在旧船上的藤壶，还是其设计本身的一部分？

My unease with this parlous state of affairs has provoked me to go back and rethink the Enlightenment and what it has to offer. But, rather than unthinkingly recouping it as a mission, I want instead to tease out and weigh up its merits, to discern with nuance what is still fit for our times. I want to ask if it is possible to rescue the Enlightenment's rallying power, and if it's worth defending what the combined forces of Left and Right are coming together to attack. Are the Enlightenment's deficiencies barnacles on an old ship, or integral to its design?

具有讽刺意味的是，后现代左翼在批判启蒙运动的同时，也解构了自身信仰体系的基础，如今自由派知识分子除了要求获得肯定或主张个人成为自己想要成为的人的权利之外，已经不知道该捍卫什么了。在 intolerance 的纯洁螺旋中，理性有被妖魔化为白人男性压迫工具的危险，而凯特·克兰奇（Kate Clanchy）或斯拉沃热·齐泽克（Slavoj Žižek）等作家和思想家遭到取消而非对话。然而，哪个更危险：坚持一套可能有缺陷的启蒙运动价值观，还是打开通向后真相世界的大门，在那个世界里任何威权主义议程都可能大行其道？我认为可以在保持政治激进性的同时维护知识标准。因为启蒙运动还有另一面，没有在文化战争中浮现，而且与中间派的自满截然相反：即知识上的谦逊和政治上的挑战。

Ironically, in critiquing the Enlightenment, the postmodern Left has deconstructed the basis of its own belief systems, and now liberal intellectuals no longer know what to defend outside of demands for affirmation, or the assertion of the individual right to be who you want to be. In the midst of intolerant purity spirals, reason is in danger of being demonised as a white man's oppressive tool, and writers and thinkers such as Kate Clanchy or Slavoj Žižek are cancelled rather than engaged with. Yet what is more dangerous: holding to a set of Enlightenment values that might be flawed, or opening the floodgates to a post-truth world in which any authoritarian agenda might well hold sway? I believe that it's possible to conserve intellectual standards and be politically radical at the same time. Because there is another side to the Enlightenment that is not surfacing in the culture wars, and which is the opposite of centrist complacency: namely, intellectual humility and political challenge.

讨论启蒙运动的困难在于，这个词的确切含义并不清晰。广义而言，它是从17世纪和18世纪科学方法的确立中发展出来的一个哲学项目：弗朗西斯·培根（Francis Bacon）及其圈子开创了对炼金术、神秘主义和迷信的摒弃，转而提倡对物理世界的经验研究，这成为了哲学实证主义的基础。如果你参观过国家美术馆（National Gallery）举办的约瑟夫·赖特·德比（Joseph Wright of Derby）展览，你可能见过《气泵中的鸟实验》（An Experiment on a Bird in the Air Pump，1768）——一幅致敬自然哲学的作品，通过明暗的强烈对比来戏剧化科学发现的影响力。

The difficulty with any discussion of the Enlightenment is that it's not clear what the word actually means. Broadly speaking, it was a philosophical project that grew out of the establishment of the scientific method in the 17th and 18th centuries: Francis Bacon and his circle had pioneered the rejection of alchemy, mysticism and superstition in favour of the empirical study of the physical world, and this became the basis of philosophical positivism. If you visited the National Gallery's exhibition devoted to Joseph Wright of Derby, you might have seen _An Experiment on a Bird in the Air Pump_ (1768) – a tribute to natural philosophy that offers stark contrasts of darkness and light to dramatise the impact of scientific discovery.

在18世纪的法国，一群被称为「哲人」（philosophes）的思想家出现了。以伏尔泰（Voltaire）、让·勒朗·达朗贝尔（Jean Le Rond d'Alembert）、德尼·狄德罗（Denis Diderot）和孟德斯鸠（Montesquieu）为首，他们倡导世俗的知识追求。在长达20年的时间里，狄德罗和达朗贝尔编纂了一部28卷的《百科全书》（Encyclopédie），一本旨在传给后代以促进人类进步的「系统性词典」。伏尔泰倡导数学逻辑的首要地位。孟德斯鸠则主张政府内部权力的分立，以确保没有任何一个集团能够实行暴政。启蒙运动也在苏格兰由亚当·斯密（Adam Smith）、大卫·休谟（David Hume）、詹姆斯·赫顿（James Hutton）和托马斯·里德（Thomas Reid）等人物推动，在德国则由摩西·门德尔松（Moses Mendelssohn）、戈特弗里德·威廉·莱布尼茨（Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz）和伊曼努尔·康德（Immanuel Kant）推进，他们试图将人类理性与对周围世界的探索结合起来。

In 18th-century France, a group of thinkers emerged known as the philosophes. Led by Voltaire, Jean Le Rond d'Alembert, Denis Diderot and Montesquieu, they championed the secular pursuit of knowledge. Over the course of 20 years, Diderot and d'Alembert produced a 28-volume Encyclopédie, a 'systematic dictionary' to be passed on to future generations for the improvement of mankind. Voltaire championed the primacy of mathematical logic. And Montesquieu argued for the division of power within government to ensure that no one group could exert tyranny. Enlightenment was also pursued in Scotland by figures such as Adam Smith, David Hume, James Hutton and Thomas Reid, and in Germany by Moses Mendelssohn, Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz and Immanuel Kant, who sought to bring together human rationality and an exploration of the world around us.

这些思想家的共同之处在于相信理性的力量能够将我们从继承的习俗和因袭的智慧中解放出来。《百科全书》将「哲人」定义为「践踏偏见、传统、普遍共识、权威——简言之，一切奴役大多数心灵的东西——敢于独立思考」的人。1784年，一份柏林期刊邀请读者回答「什么是启蒙运动？」的问题。康德提交了一篇文章，将启蒙运动定义为「人类摆脱自我招致的不成熟状态」，他将这种不成熟状态识别为「在没有他人指导的情况下无法运用自己的知性」。

What these thinkers had in common was a belief in the power of reason to liberate us from inherited custom and received wisdom. The Encyclopédie defined the philosophe as someone who, 'trampling on prejudice, tradition, universal consent, authority, in a word, all that enslaves most minds, dares to think for himself'. In 1784, a Berlin periodical invited readers to respond to the question 'What is Enlightenment?' Kant sent in an essay, defining it as 'man's release from his self-incurred immaturity', which he identified as 'the inability to use one's own understanding without the guidance of another.'

这种对教条的拒绝是鼓舞人心的。但启蒙运动是一个多样化的、甚至矛盾的运动，这也是它现在可以被占据截然不同立场的人当作政治足球来使用的原因之一。历史学家多琳达·奥特拉姆（Dorinda Outram）——启蒙运动多部著作的作者——告诉我：「我真的不太相信存在所谓的启蒙运动这种东西。」哲人们宣称自己「正在创造适用于全人类的普遍范畴，如普遍的人性」，但随后又「反复将人们排除在这些普遍范畴的庇护之外」，比如「黑人、女性和穷人」。在《为女权辩护》（A Vindication of the Rights of Woman，1792）中，玛丽·沃斯通克拉夫特（Mary Wollstonecraft）抨击启蒙运动声称具有普遍性，却将女性排斥在理性领域之外。「她真的拿起了启蒙运动的话柄，猛烈地摇撼它，说：看，这一切都很美好，但如果不把所有人都包括进来，它就不是真实的，」奥特拉姆说。启蒙运动的批评者不一定拒绝普遍主义作为一种理想，而是指出其对普遍主义的承诺是有选择性的。

This rejection of dogma is inspiring. But the Enlightenment was a diverse, even contradictory movement, which is partly why it can now be deployed as a political football by those who occupy very different positions. 'I'm really not at all convinced that there was such a thing as Enlightenment,' the historian Dorinda Outram, author of several books on the Enlightenment, told me. The philosophes declare that they're 'producing universal categories that apply to all human beings, like universal humanity', but then they 'repeatedly exclude people from being under the aegis of those universal categories', such as 'Black people, women and the poor'. In _A Vindication of the Rights of Woman_ (1792), Mary Wollstonecraft lambasted the Enlightenment for claiming universality while excluding women from the realm of rationality. 'She really takes it and shakes it and says, look, this is all wonderful, but it's not real unless you include everybody,' Outram said. Critics of the Enlightenment don't necessarily reject universalism as an ideal, but point out that the commitment to universalism was selective.

一些历史学家不仅指责启蒙运动虚伪，而且认为种族主义根植于其骨髓。安吉拉·塞尼（Angela Saini）是一位撰写过种族科学起源相关著作的科学记者。「我是启蒙运动的拥趸，」她告诉我。「它给了我们今天所知世界的大部分内容」，就「理性和科学方法」而言。然而，卡尔·林奈（Carl Linnaeus）的生物分类系统——启蒙运动科学的主要产物之一——按肤色划分人类，塞尼告诉我这是「极其武断的」：他「本质上是在用分类自然界的方式来对人类进行分类」。伏尔泰认为不同的族群有根本不同的起源——这一理论被称为多元发生论（polygenism）——并且认为理性时代只在西欧达到了成熟。康德在1802年写道：「人类在白人种族中达到最高程度的完善。」

Some historians not only charge the Enlightenment with hypocrisy, but believe that racism is in its bones. Angela Saini is a science journalist who has written about the origins of race science. 'I'm a big fan of the Enlightenment,' she told me. 'It gave us so much of the world as we know it today,' in terms of 'rationality and the scientific method'. Carl Linnaeus's system of biological classification, however, one of the main artefacts of Enlightenment science, divides people up by skin colour, which is 'incredibly arbitrary', Saini told me: he's 'essentially taxonomising humans in the same way that he's drawing up taxonomies of the natural world.' Voltaire believed that different ethnic groups had fundamentally different origins – a theory known as polygenism – and that the age of reason had arrived at maturity only in Western Europe. 'Humanity,' wrote Kant in 1802, 'has its highest degree of perfection in the white race.'

肯迪·安德鲁斯在一次开放大学（Open University）研讨会上说，对启蒙运动人物的标准辩护是，「是的，他们是种族主义者，但这与他们的道德哲学是分开的……所以我们为什么要因为洗澡水脏就把婴儿一起倒掉呢？」安德鲁斯说，这种想法是错误的，因为像洛克、让-雅克·卢梭（Jean-Jacques Rousseau）和康德这样的启蒙思想家「都持有欧洲优越、白人更好、黑人低劣的信念」，而且这「并非巧合」，实际上是他们「出发点的预设」。

The standard defence of Enlightenment figures, Kehinde Andrews told an Open University seminar, is that 'yes, they were racist, but that's separate from their moral philosophy … so why would we throw the baby out with the bathwater?' That is the wrong way to think about it, Andrews said, since Enlightenment thinkers like Locke, Jean-Jacques Rousseau and Kant 'all had these beliefs that Europe was superior, that whiteness was better, that Black people were inferior', and that this is 'not a coincidence', it's actually the 'premise of where they come from'.

塞尼指出，这些种族主义态度也是其时代的产物，而且出于经济和政治上的便利：启蒙运动所珍视的「自由、平等、理性和世俗主义」观念与「一些人劣于另一些人的观念」是同时锻造出来的。被描述为「自然的」等级制度是「自我服务的」，塞尼解释说，因为它们不仅为殖民扩张提供了正当性，还「巩固了社会秩序」。

Saini points out that these racist attitudes were also a product of their time, and were economically and politically expedient: the Enlightenment's cherished notions of 'freedom and equality and reason and secularism' were forged alongside 'these ideas about some people being inferior to others'. The hierarchies that were described as 'natural' were 'self-serving', Saini explained, because they not only justified colonial expansion but also 'reinforced the social order'.

尽管如此，殖民经历还是缓解了种族主义态度，因为西方人接触到了显然具有高度智慧和文化素养的文化。例如，「他们对中国越来越感兴趣，」奥特拉姆告诉我，「那里的碰撞显然不是理性与非理性之间的对抗。只是不同种类的理性。」启蒙运动之所以不一致，是因为它在随时间而变化。「18世纪初的启蒙运动与18世纪末的完全不同，」奥特拉姆说，「当时关注的是一种新的人道主义」和新的「利他主义」形式，例如「慈善性救生艇站的建立」，这是受到这样一个理念的启发：「人们愿意在危险的条件下出去营救与他们没有任何联系、完全陌生的人。」

The experience of colonisation nonetheless mitigated racist attitudes as Westerners came into contact with cultures that were evidently intellectually and culturally sophisticated. For example, 'they get more and more interested in China,' Outram told me, and 'the confrontation there is clearly not between reason and unreason. It's just different sorts of reason.' The Enlightenment was inconsistent because it was changing over time. 'It's a very different thing at the beginning of the 18th century from what it is at the end,' Outram said, 'when the focus is on a new sort of humanitarianism' and new forms of 'altruism', such as 'the philanthropic founding of lifeboat stations', which is inspired by the idea that 'people will willingly go out in dangerous conditions to try and rescue people who they have no links with, who are total strangers.'

正如历史学家乔纳森·伊斯雷尔（Jonathan Israel）在《激进启蒙运动》（Radical Enlightenment，2001）一书中所论证的，与那些拥有直接或间接奴隶制联系的苍白、男性、富有的精英阶层的负面形象相反，杰出的启蒙思想家如狄德罗和尼古拉·德·孔多塞（Nicolas de Condorcet）实际上是热情的废奴主义者：狄德罗在反对殖民统治的论战性著作《两个印度群岛的历史》（Histoire des deux Indes，1770）中贡献了一些尖锐的反奴隶制论点，并在《百科全书》中对「黑人」的遭遇表示痛惜，写道：「我们把他们降低了，我不会说是降到奴隶的境地，而是降到驮畜的境地；而我们是理性的！而我们是基督徒！」孔多塞在1795年写道，「发现时代」的成果毫无意义，除非欧洲人「承认其他气候条件下的人，在自然的意志下是平等的和兄弟般的，从来就不是为了滋养少数特权国家的骄傲和贪婪而生的」。启蒙运动被后来的反奴隶制起义所援引，例如19世纪初海地的起义，以及后来的反殖民思想家如W·E·B·杜波依斯（W E B Du Bois），他借鉴了启蒙运动关于共同人性和通过理性获得解放的主张。

As the historian Jonathan Israel argued in _Radical Enlightenment_ (2001), in contrast to negative images of pale, male, wealthy elites with direct or indirect links to slavery, prominent Enlightenment thinkers such as Diderot and Nicolas de Condorcet were in fact passionate abolitionists: Diderot contributed some stridently anti-slavery arguments to the _Histoire des deux Indes_ (1770), a polemic against colonial rule, and bemoaned the treatment of 'Negroes' in the Encyclopédie, writing that 'We have reduced them, I wouldn't say to the conditions of slaves, but to that of beasts of burden; and we are reasonable! And we are Christians!' Condorcet wrote in 1795 that the gains of the 'Age of Discovery' counted for nothing unless Europeans 'acknowledge men of other climates, equals and brothers by the will of nature, have never been formed to nourish the pride and avarice of a few privileged nations'. Enlightenment was invoked by later anti-slavery revolts, such as in Haiti at the turn of the 19th century, and by later anticolonial thinkers such as W E B Du Bois, who drew on its assertion of a common humanity and emancipation through reason.

我与历史学家理查德·沃特莫尔（Richard Whatmore）——《启蒙运动的终结：帝国、商业、危机》（The End of Enlightenment: Empire, Commerce, Crisis，2023）的作者——进行了交谈，他引用了伏尔泰1764年的话：「不和是人类的大恶；宽容是唯一的解药。」对沃特莫尔来说，启蒙运动是「任何防止宗教战争爆发的策略」。在18世纪，「多样性意味着持有不同意见而不想碾平你的对手。一旦你想要排斥和流放，那就是启蒙运动的终结。那就是狂热主义。」他说，困难的事情是「与那些想法和你完全不同的人生活在一起」。沃特莫尔认为启蒙运动随法国革命及其创造「排他性共同体」的愿望而终结，他将其比作「我们的文化战争」。一旦你陷入文化战争，「那就表明你已经输给了18世纪的心智」。沃特莫尔在特朗普式的右翼和认同政治的左翼中都看到了这种倾向。反对偏见却取消那些与你意见不同的人——可以说——是一种特别现代的虚伪形式。

I spoke to Richard Whatmore, a historian and author of _The End of Enlightenment: Empire, Commerce, Crisis_ (2023), who cited Voltaire writing in 1764 that 'Discord is the great ill of mankind; and tolerance is the only remedy for it.' For Whatmore, Enlightenment is 'any strategy that prevents wars of religion from breaking out.' In the 18th century, 'diversity means disagreeing without wanting to flatten your rivals. As soon as you want to ostracise and exile, that's the end of Enlightenment. That's fanaticism.' The difficult thing, he said, 'is living together with people who think completely differently to the way that you do.' Whatmore believes the Enlightenment ended with the French Revolution and its desire to create 'an exclusive community', which he likens to 'our culture wars'. As soon as you descend into culture war, 'that shows you've lost to an 18th-century mind'. Whatmore identifies this tendency on both the Trumpian Right and the identitarian Left. To oppose prejudice but cancel those who disagree with you is – arguably – a particularly modern form of hypocrisy.

宽容——适用于人和观点——是一种难以捉摸的美德。我能看到普遍主义与特殊性之争的双方观点。所以我不信任圣保罗向犹太人伸出的温暖拥抱——邀请他们加入基督教的怀抱，同时抹除犹太身份的边界——也不信任法国虚伪的共和主义，在那里，宗教群体之间公平竞争的说法受到穆斯林社区成员的质疑。但我确实认同一种真正平等的普遍主义，它优先考虑和平异议的能力，而不是认同政治者或民族主义者狭隘的承认要求。

Tolerance – applied to both people and opinions – is a slippery virtue. I can see both sides of the debate between universalism and particularity. So I mistrust the warm embrace St Paul extends to the Jews, inviting them into the Christian fold while simultaneously erasing the boundaries of Jewish identity, and also the disingenuous republicanism of France, where claims to a level playing field between religious groups are disputed by members of the Muslim community. But I do subscribe to a genuinely egalitarian universalism that prioritises the capacity for peaceable disagreement over narrow demands for recognition by either identitarians or nationalists.

普遍主义在犹太启蒙运动的成员中引发了激烈辩论，包括摩西·门德尔松，他——与康德一样——向柏林杂志的征文活动投了一篇文章。门德尔松赞成犹太人的同化，但犹太身份的保存仍然是一个充满争议的问题——这一问题随着纳粹大屠杀和广泛的结论（即同化的「实验」已经失败）而达到顶峰。也许并不令人意外，许多最重要的启蒙运动历史学家都是战后犹太学者，其中包括彼得·盖伊（Peter Gay），他回顾启蒙运动以寻求如何避免法西斯主义的模式。德国哲学家尤尔根·哈贝马斯（Jürgen Habermas）也反对民族中心主义，倡导超越民族或文化特殊性的普遍道德。

Universalism was hotly debated by members of the Jewish Enlightenment, including Moses Mendelssohn, who – like Kant – contributed an essay to the Berlin magazine essay call. Mendelssohn was in favour of Jewish assimilation, but the preservation of Jewish identity remained a fraught issue – coming to a head with the Nazi Holocaust and the widespread conclusion that the assimilationist 'experiment' had failed. Unsurprisingly, perhaps, many of the foremost historians of the Enlightenment were postwar Jewish scholars, among them Peter Gay, who looked back to the Enlightenment to provide a model for how to avoid fascism. The German philosopher Jürgen Habermas has also argued against ethnocentrism and in favour of a universal morality that transcends national or cultural particularity.

启蒙运动普遍主义的批评者揭示了经常隐藏在「客观性」主张背后的权力动态，这是正确的。但对我来说，关键的一点是，大多数对启蒙运动价值观的攻击都没有考虑到实际运作中的权力失衡：所谓的「自由精英」实际上正处于畏缩退却之中。瞄准「主流媒体」、专家、专业人士，特别是那个虚构的名为「光明会」（illuminati）的排他性小集团的民粹主义右翼，本质上是在攻击那些四面楚歌的残余力量或幽灵。与此同时，左翼将准星对准了文化「守门人」，而他们的影响力要么是虚构的，要么正在衰退。与社会中最受践踏的成员相比，策展人、图书编辑和终身教授确实拥有一些权力，但与那些正在无情地削弱他们及其所代表的一切的科技公司、亿万富翁和政治强人相比，他们其实没有多少力量。

Critics of Enlightenment universalism are right to uncover the power dynamics that too often underlie claims to 'objectivity'. But the key point for me is that most attacks on Enlightenment values fail to take account of the power disparity actually in play: the fact that the so-called 'liberal elite' is actually in timid retreat. The populist Right taking aim at the 'mainstream media', experts, professionals and especially the apocryphal exclusive cabal called the 'illuminati' is essentially targeting embattled remnants or phantom ghouls. Meanwhile, the Left trains its sights on cultural 'gatekeepers' whose influence is also either fictive or on the wane. Relative to the most downtrodden members of society, curators, book editors and tenured academics hold some power but, compared with the tech corporations, billionaires and political strongmen who are ruthlessly undermining them and everything they stand for, they really don't.

当埃莉诺·罗斯福（Eleanor Roosevelt）在1948年起草《世界人权宣言》（Universal Declaration of Human Rights）时，她非常注意纳入来自其他国家和文化的代表，包括中国哲学家和外交官张彭春（P C Chang）。张彭春是一位多元主义者，支持该宣言，但仍存在一个特殊主义的挑战：即该宣言强调个人权利和自由，而根据儒家传统，自我是由社会关系来定义的。我们应该记住这样的挑战，但我们绝不能让它们加速那些建立在启蒙运动哲学基础上的普遍主义制度的毁灭。整个国际秩序目前正岌岌可危，受到新极权主义领导人的威胁，同时又因其左翼前盟友指责其西方中心主义和排他性而遭到削弱。我们让其消亡将自食其果，因为正是这些制度在支持弱势群体并让真正的精英承担责任。

When Eleanor Roosevelt drew up the Universal Declaration of Human Rights in 1948, she took great care to include representatives from other countries and cultures, including the Chinese philosopher and diplomat P C Chang. Chang was a pluralist and supported the Declaration, but there remained a particularist challenge: namely, that the Declaration emphasises individual rights and liberties whereas, according to the Confucian tradition, the self is defined by communal ties. We should remember challenges like these, but we must not let them contribute to the destruction of those universalist institutions that were built on the philosophical foundations of the Enlightenment. The entire international order is currently teetering on the edge, threatened by neo-totalitarian leaders and undermined in turn by its former allies on the Left for being Western-centric and exclusionary. We let them perish at our peril, as it is these institutions that support the less powerful and hold the real elites to account.

理性是我在工作和与他人互动中——尤其是在需要解决冲突时——所倚重的指南针。但我们也应该认识到它的局限性：它有时会生硬地边缘化习惯、认知失调和难以言传的体验等特质。对理性能力的过度坚持如果不加以内在约束，就会显得傲慢，而攻击专家的一个不幸后果是，他们在自我防御中可能变得更加教条。在《启蒙辩证法》（Dialectic of Enlightenment，1947）中，西奥多·阿多诺（Theodor Adorno）和马克斯·霍克海默（Max Horkheimer）认为，启蒙运动对理性的僵硬坚持不可避免地导致极权主义。他们认为，对宗教或传统等替代性价值体系的压制是一种「祛魅」，其背后始终有暴力的威胁。「完全启蒙的大地，」他们写道，「在灾难中放射着胜利的光辉。」奥特拉姆认为阿多诺和霍克海默触及了某些重要的东西：「启蒙运动价值观和启蒙思想非常容易走向其对立面的方式。」

Reason is the compass I look to in my work and in my interactions with others – especially when there is conflict to resolve. But we should also recognise its limitations: its sometimes stolid sidelining of qualities like habit, cognitive dissonance and ineffable experience. An over-insistence on rationality can look like arrogance if it is not checked internally, and an unfortunate consequence of the attack on experts is that they can become more dogmatic in self-defence. In the _Dialectic of Enlightenment_ (1947), Theodor Adorno and Max Horkheimer argued that the Enlightenment's rigid insistence on reason led inevitably to totalitarianism. They believed that the suppression of alternative value systems such as religion or tradition was a 'disenchantment' that was always underpinned by the threat of violence. 'The fully enlightened earth,' they wrote, 'radiates disaster triumphant.' Outram thinks that Adorno and Horkheimer put their finger on something important: 'the way in which Enlightenment values and Enlightenment thinking were very likely to tip over into their opposites.'

启蒙运动理性一直都有其批评者。卢梭认为它腐蚀了纯真的人性。米歇尔·福柯（Michel Foucault）不喜欢它倾向于污名化那些不符合社会「理性」定义的人：流浪者、罪犯和精神病患者。哲学家约翰·格雷（John Gray）认为其对进步的信仰是不切实际的幻想。塞尼告诉我，作为一名记者，她的首要目标是改变人们的想法使之向善，而简单地将「合理的」东西作为不证自明的真理来陈述并不总是实现这一目标的最佳方式。「如果我试图说服一个对接种疫苗犹豫不决的人去接种，用疫苗是安全的、科学家已经证明了这一点这种想法来敲打他们是没有用的，」她说。毕竟，他们的恐惧往往「来自一个理性的地方。可能是对身体自主权丧失的恐惧，对疫苗伤害的真正恐惧，或者是由他们读到的一些信息所植入的疑虑。」将某人的错误立场归类为非理性往往会使他们更加固执己见，「因为他们觉得自己受到了攻击。」

Enlightenment reason has always had its critics. Rousseau believed that it corrupted innocent human nature. Michel Foucault didn't like its tendency to stigmatise those who don't fit society's definition of the 'reasonable': vagabonds, criminals and the mentally ill. The philosopher John Gray thinks that its faith in progress is quixotic. Saini told me that, as a journalist, she is focused on the end goal of changing people's minds for the good, and simply stating what is 'reasonable' as self-evident truth is not always the best way of achieving that. 'If I'm trying to convince someone who is hesitant about taking a vaccine to take it, it doesn't work to hit them over the head with this idea that vaccines are safe and scientists have proven this,' she said. After all, their fears often 'come from a rational place. It could be a fear of losing bodily autonomy, a genuine fear of vaccine injury, or doubts that have been seeded by some information that they've read.' Categorising someone's mistaken position as irrational tends to entrench them in it further, 'because they feel they're under attack.'

塞尼还告诫不要将我们政治中的民粹主义转向视为完全非理性的。「政治上长期以来存在一种僵局，人们觉得什么都改变不了；无论选谁都一样。」当民粹主义候选人承诺全面的变革时，「这对人们来说非常有吸引力，」她说。「这并不意味着他们必然在背离理性。这只意味着他们感到沮丧。」奥特拉姆同样强调保持对理性和知识含义的多元观点的重要性。「对专业知识的攻击实际上是不同知识形式之间的斗争……我是专家，」她承认，「所以我在其中有利害关系，」但「剑桥历史学院所赋予的专业知识，与比如说啤酒酿造者的默会知识一样有效。」

Saini also cautions against dismissing the populist turn in our politics as wholly irrational. 'There's been a kind of block in politics for a long time, where people felt that nothing was happening; it didn't matter who they elected.' When populist candidates promise wholesale change, 'it is very appealing to people,' she said. 'That doesn't mean they're necessarily retreating from reason. It just means that they're frustrated.' Outram likewise stresses the importance of maintaining a pluralistic view of what reason and knowledge mean. 'The attack on expertise is, in fact, a struggle between different forms of knowledge … I am an expert,' she acknowledges, 'so I have some skin in the game,' but the expertise that 'going through the Cambridge History School gives you is as valid as the tacit knowledge of, say, brewers of beer.'

对奥特拉姆来说，倡导道德比捍卫理性是更为紧迫的优先事项，因为「非理性、错误信息和虚假信息都被用来服务于残忍……如果你对某人撒谎，你就表明你不尊重他们。」撒谎——比如政府或企业的撒谎——无例外地是「在为道德上站不住脚的项目辩护，否则你就不必为此撒谎。」奥特拉姆认为我们现在应该记住的是启蒙运动后来的关怀他人的价值观——由亚当·斯密和埃德蒙·伯克（Edmund Burke）所阐发：「我们通过同情和共情与所有其他人联系在一起的理念」，「我们不可能看到他人受苦而自己不感到痛苦。」（这让人想起康德在倡导世界主义的国际主义语境下所作的动人陈述：「世界一个地方的权利受到侵犯，在各处都能被感受到。」）道德正是阻止理性沦为虚伪的东西。

For Outram, promoting morality is a more pressing priority than defending reason, because 'unreason, misinformation and disinformation are all used in the service of cruelty … If you lie to somebody, you show that you don't respect them.' Lying – by governments or corporations, say – is invariably carried out 'in defence of projects which are morally indefensible, otherwise you wouldn't have to lie about them.' It is the later Enlightenment value of care for others – articulated by Adam Smith and Edmund Burke – that Outram believes we should remember now: 'the idea that we're linked to all other human beings by sympathy and empathy,' that 'we can't see other people suffer without feeling pain ourselves.' (This recalls Kant's moving statement, made in the context of advocating a cosmopolitan internationalism, that 'a violation of rights in one part of the world is felt everywhere.') Morality is what stops reason succumbing to hypocrisy.

当然，知识的积累并不自动带来人类的进步。但还有一个替代性的、反启蒙运动的叙事也并不更好：潘多拉打开她的盒子，或夏娃吃下苹果的故事。这些故事表达了对知识及其所带来混乱的恐惧，然而我们发现混乱实际上源于无知。我们必须保持认识论上的谦逊，但我们也必须在周围现实溶解为白宫制造的梗图、点击诱饵广告和人工智能垃圾时保持认知方向感。

Of course, the accumulation of knowledge doesn't automatically lead to human progress. But there is an alternative, counter-Enlightenment narrative that is hardly preferable: the narrative of Pandora opening her box, or Eve eating the apple. These stories express a fear of knowledge and the chaos it brings, yet we find that chaos actually comes from ignorance. We must maintain epistemological humility, but we must also keep our cognitive bearings as the reality around us dissolves into White House-generated memes, clickbait advertising and AI slop.

与启蒙运动以高高在上的特权姿态著称相反，其拥护者首先追求的是推广一种既谦逊又不安分的批判精神。在《人类理解研究》（An Enquiry Concerning Human Understanding，1748）中，休谟写道，如果那些「在观点上教条主义」的人能够意识到「人类理解力即使在最完善的状态下也有奇怪的弱点」，这种认识将「自然地激发他们更多的谦虚和克制」，并「减少他们对自己的偏爱以及对反对者的偏见」。知识上的谦逊与对他人的宽容密切相关：既然「我们都由脆弱和错误构成，」伏尔泰写道，「让我们互相宽恕彼此的愚蠢——这就是自然的第一法则」。康德强调人类知识不可逃避的局限性。我不是第一个指出，在我们这个日益无知的时代，具有讽刺意味的是人们顽固地坚持他们自以为知道的东西。

In contrast to the Enlightenment's reputation for high-minded entitlement, its proponents aimed above all to promote critique that was both unassuming and restless. In _An Enquiry Concerning Human Understanding_ (1748), Hume wrote that if those who are 'dogmatical in their opinions' could become 'sensible of the strange infirmities of human understanding, even in its most perfect state', this realisation would 'naturally inspire them with more modesty and reserve' and 'diminish their fond opinion of themselves, and their prejudice against antagonists'. Intellectual modesty was intimately linked to tolerance of others: since 'we are all formed of frailty and error,' Voltaire wrote, 'let us pardon reciprocally each other's folly – that is the first law of nature'. Kant emphasised the inescapable limits of human knowledge. I am not the first to note that our own age of increasing ignorance is characterised, ironically, by people holding trenchantly to what they think they know.

与康德一样，福柯也追问了「什么是启蒙运动？」——这是他1984年一篇文章的标题。他将其定义为「一种态度的永久再激活——即一种可以描述为对我们所处历史时代的永久批判的哲学精神气质」。从这个角度看，启蒙运动为社会科学奠定了基础：即我们的「历史时代」是无限可读的信念。这是我最喜欢的启蒙运动定义：一种对「永久批判」的承诺：对我们的世界，同样也——关键地——对我们自身的批判。这是对抗操纵和自我欺骗的最佳良方。

Like Kant, Foucault also asked 'What is Enlightenment?' – the title of a 1984 essay. He defined it as 'the permanent reactivation of an attitude – that is, of a philosophical ethos that could be described as a permanent critique of our historical era'. Seen in this light, the Enlightenment laid the foundations for the social sciences: the conviction that our 'historical era' is endlessly readable. This is my favourite definition of the Enlightenment: a commitment to 'permanent critique': of our world, and also – crucially – of ourselves. It is the best remedy for both manipulation and self-delusion.

无论是认同政治的左翼还是摇旗呐喊的右翼，目前在自我批判方面都不太擅长。左翼过于自以为是，右翼过于执着于「平衡」的幻象而不愿承认自己的意识形态议程。至于政治激进主义，左翼对身份的狭隘关注使其无法审视宏大的社会经济权力动态，而右翼对西方伟大之处玫瑰色的怀旧导致它去捍卫现状。双方都远远谈不上「开明」。我们确实需要谦逊和自我质疑，但这不应阻止我们向权力说真话，挑战金融和技术精英。随着技术大佬们声称引领一场新的启蒙运动（实际上，不过是同样的白人、男性和英语中心的旧势力换上了新装），我们更有必要巩固我们知识遗产中的优良部分。

Neither the identitarian Left nor the flag-waving Right is currently very good at self-critique. The Left is too self-righteous and the Right too wedded to an illusion of 'balance' to acknowledge its own ideological agenda. As for political radicalism, the Left's narrow focus on identity prevents it from examining big-picture, socioeconomic power dynamics, and the Right's rose-tinted nostalgia for Western greatness leads it to defend the status quo. Neither side is remotely 'enlightened'. We need to be humble and self-questioning, to be sure, but that should not stop us speaking truth to power, and challenging financial and technological elites. With the tech bros laying claim to a new Enlightenment (when, really, it's the same white, male and Anglophone-centric old guard in new clothes), there's all the more need to bolster the good in our intellectual inheritance.

启蒙运动可能是一个混合的、矛盾的项目，但其批判精神与革命意图紧密相连：为一个更美好的世界奠定知识基础。我们必须通过记住启蒙运动自身关于辨别力的教导来拯救其婴儿于浑浊的洗澡水之中；倡导将理性作为一种追求而非一种绝对；最重要的是，在专业知识、文化资本和高水平知识标准正面临严重威胁的时刻，拒绝将它们与权力和特权联系起来的企图。

The Enlightenment may have been a mixed, paradoxical project, but its critical attitude was bound up with revolutionary intent: to lay the intellectual foundations for a better world. We must save the Enlightenment baby from its muddied bathwater by remembering its own teaching of discernment; advocating for rationality as an ambition rather than an absolute; and above all rejecting attempts to link expertise, cultural capital and high intellectual standards with power and privilege at a time when they are so gravely imperilled.

在我们这个二元对立的时代，启蒙运动包含多样性这一事实其实是有益的。「当时人们的思考方式就存在很大的多元性，」塞尼说。「这就是为什么我认为，当我们回顾过去时，应该避免过于武断的评判，因为我们自己也将被后代根据我们现在的生活方式来评判。」拒绝将历史中的人或思想简单地归入好坏的整体类别，这使我们能够说，她告诉我：「我们知道我们想要什么样的世界，而且如果一切都是可能的——我认为这就是历史的教训——那么我们就可以取走我们知道有用的东西。」关于其余的部分，塞尼总结道，「我们可以说那对你们来说是可以的，对我们来说不行。这没问题。」

In our age of binaries, the fact that the Enlightenment contains multitudes is actually helpful. 'There was a great deal of plurality in the way that people thought, even at that time,' Saini said. 'And that's why I think, when we look at the past, we should hesitate from being too judgmental, because we ourselves will be judged by future generations for the way we live now.' Refusing to consign people or ideas in history to wholesale categories of good or bad allows us to say, she told me: 'We know the kind of world that we want and, if anything is possible, which is I think the lesson of history, then we can take what we know is useful.' About the rest of it, Saini concludes, 'We can say that was fine for you, it's not fine for us. And that's OK.'
