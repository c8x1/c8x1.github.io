哲学

辩护的内外之分

Adam Carter·Aeon·2026年5月26日·10分钟阅读·原文

你不可能故意犯错误。要明白这一点，试试我最喜欢的哲学小把戏。现在，去相信一件你认为错误的事情：比如太阳只不过是一个大灯泡。不要想象你相信它——而是真正去相信它。要对它如此确信，以至于你愿意拿真金白银打赌它是真的。当我尝试这样做的时候，我会感到一种奇特的认知障碍，仿佛内心深处有一种对按命令去相信的天然排斥，尤其排斥那些我已经认为是错误的东西。不幸的是，这并不意味着只相信真理是一件容易的事。如果学习和思考就像决定只让事实进入头脑那样简单，我们永远不会犯错。然而我们却不断跌入谬误之中。我们都能想起那些自己深信不疑后来却被证明是错误的时候，我们都有自己最喜欢的知觉错觉、歧义图像和不可能场景的图片，这些都一再提醒我们：事情并不总是如表面所见。

To riff on the opening lines of Steven Shapin's book The Scientific Revolution (1996), there is no such thing as a science-religion conflict, and this is an essay about it. It is not, however, another rebuttal of the 'conflict narrative' – there is already an abundance of good, recent writing in that vein from historians, sociologists and philosophers as well as scientists themselves. Readers still under the misapprehension that the history of science can be accurately characterised by a continuous struggle to escape from the shackles of religious oppression into a sunny secular upland of free thought (loudly expressed by a few scientists but no historians) can consult Peter Harrison's masterly book The Territories of Science and Religion (2015), or dip into Ronald Numbers's delightful edited volume Galileo Goes to Jail and Other Myths about Science and Religion (2009).

有这样一份记录在案的前科，我们有理由反思为什么我们的思维有时会出错，有时又能走对。认识论（epistemology）——对知识、信念和证据的哲学研究——正是从这里出发，从我们的可错性开始。从这个起点出发，认识论有许多路径可走，有许多问题可以将我们引上不同的探索之路。

Likewise, assumptions that theological and scientific methodologies and truth-claims are necessarily in philosophical or rational conflict might be challenged by Alister McGrath's book The Territories of Human Reason (2019) or Andrew Torrance and Thomas McCall's edited Knowing Creation (2018). The late-Victorian origin of the 'alternative history' of unavoidable conflict is fascinating in its own right, but also damaging in that it has multiplied through so much public and educational discourse in the 20th century in both secular and religious communities. That is the topic of a new and fascinating study by the historian James Ungureanu, Science, Religion, and the Protestant Tradition (2019). Finally, the concomitant assumption that scientists must, by logical force, adopt non-theistic worldviews is roundly rebutted by recent and global social science, such as Elaine Eklund's major survey, also published in a new book, Secularity and Science (2019).

例如，我们可以继续追问思考本身的本质。思考是否仅仅等同于形成和重新形成信念？还是完全是另一回事？另一个选择是追问在相信中什么算作「成功」或「正确」。这第二条路径关乎认识论者所谓的「辩护」（justification）。既然真实的思想并不会自带一种特殊的光芒来宣布自身为真，我们就不能把真理作为良好形成的、有价值的信念的标记。相反，我们可能需要寻找其他东西来区分好的信念和意见与坏的——某种能够辩护某些信念而非另一些的东西，某种能解释为什么某些信念可信而某些不可信的东西。

All well and good – so the history, philosophy and sociology of science and religion are richer and more interesting than the media-tales and high-school stories of opposition we were all brought up on. It seems a good time to ask the 'so what?' questions, however, especially since there has been less work in that direction. If Islamic, Jewish and Christian theologies were demonstrably central in the construction of our current scientific methodologies, for example, then what might such a reassessment imply for fruitful development of the role that science plays in our modern world? In what ways might religious communities support science especially under the shadow of a 'post-truth' political order? What implications and resources might a rethink of science and religion offer for the anguished science-educational discussion on both sides of the Atlantic, and for the emerging international discussions on 'science-literacy'?

确实，这对许多认识论者来说是一个大问题：辩护和可信度到底是什么。但在探索的路上，道路变窄了，一个关于辩护在求知过程中所扮演角色的新问题浮现出来。虽然一个只保留事实的过滤器无法解决避免谬误和获得真理的问题，但一个思想的可信度是否需要以一种真理所不能的方式对你变得显而易见？还是说你的意见只要可信就够了，无需其他？这些问题引向了认识论中令人生畏的所谓内在论与外在论（internalism/externalism）之争，但别让这些主义吓到你。正如我们将看到的，关于辩护的学术问题最终引向关于认识论本身研究对象的更深层次问题。

I want to explore here directions in which we could take those consequential questions. Three perspectives will suggest lines of new resources for thinking: the critical tools offered by the discipline of theology itself (even in an entirely secular context), a reappraisal of ancient and premodern texts, and a new way of looking at the unanswered questions and predicament of some postmodern philosophy and sociology. I'll finish by suggesting how these in turn suggest new configurations of religious communities in regard to science and technology.

要理解什么是辩护，考虑这个类比。我拥有一个迷你美式橄榄球头盔，上面有四分卫布雷特·法弗（Brett Favre）在他带领绿湾包装工队（Green Bay Packers）首次进入超级碗并当选MVP（美国国家橄榄球联盟最有价值球员）那一年的亲笔签名。（事实上，我亲手把头盔递给他请他签名的。）如果我想把这件体育纪念品卖给你，你自然会想要确认这个签名不是伪造的，因为迷你头盔、马克笔和法弗签名的照片并不难获得，而你自己也分辨不出真伪。（真正的签名同样不会自带特殊光芒。）因此，一位专家鉴定师的真品证书——一个能够检查签名并判断该签名是否出自法弗本人之手的鉴定师——可能会为法弗在该头盔上签名这一声称增添可信度。一旦鉴定师认证签名为真品，你就可以放心地、有理由地购买这个头盔。我们甚至可以进一步说，如果有人在没有证书的情况下购买了头盔，那就是一个草率的决定。（你可能会质疑花钱买体育纪念品本身就是个草率的决定，但我们不得不把这种顾虑搁置一边。）

The humble conjunction 'and' does much more work in framing discussions of 'theology and science' than at first apparent. It tacitly assumes that its referents belong to the same category ('red' and 'blue'), implying a limited overlap between them ('north' and 'south'), and it might already bias the discussion into oppositional mode ('liberal' and 'conservative'). Yet both science and theology resist boundaries – each has something to say about everything. Other conjunctions are possible that do much greater justice to the history and philosophy of science, and also to the cultural narratives of theology. A strong candidate is 'of', when the appropriate question now becomes: 'What is a theology of science?' and its complement, 'What is a science of theology?'

当我们在追踪一件重要的事情时，我们经常依赖其他东西来提示我们它的存在或不存在——类似于真品证书那样的东西——我们的信念、思想和意见也是如此。过一种人的生活，就是在由各种渠道汇成的信息海洋中游泳：我们的感官、我们的记忆、我们的社交网络。但是，正如塑料橄榄球头盔上的弯弯曲曲不会向未经训练的眼睛宣告自己是真迹一样，信息也并不总是带着真理的明亮光环。使某件事值得相信的东西——使一个信念可信或有辩护的东西——必须是别的什么。但如果信念需要自己的「真品证书」，那些证书应该是什么样的？也就是说，辩护是如何运作的？

A 'theology of…' delivers a narrative of teleology, a story of purpose. A 'theology of science' will describe, within the religious narrative of one or more traditions, what the work of science is for. There have been examples of the 'theology of…' genre addressing, for example, music – see James Begbie's Theology, Music and Time (2000) – and art – see Nicholas Wolterstorff's Art in Action (1997). Note that working through a teleology of a cultural art by calling on theological resources does not imply a personal commitment to that theology – it might simply respond to a need for academic thinking about purpose. For example, Begbie explores the role that music plays in accommodating human experience to time, while Wolterstorff discovers a responsibility toward the visual aesthetics of public spaces. In both cases, we find that theology has retained a set of critical tools that address the essential human experience of purpose, value and ethics in regard to a capacity or endeavour.

假设你有兴趣购买这个头盔。如果一位专家检查了它并出具了真正的真品证书，然后你买了它，你买到的是一个经过认证的真正签名。但为了让你负责任地购买，为了让这次购买是有辩护的，那个真品证书需要以什么方式纳入你的决策？

Intriguingly, it appears that some of the social frustrations that science now experiences result from missing, inadequate or even damaging cultural narratives of science. Absence of a narrative that delineates what science is for leave it open to hijacking by personal or corporate sectarian interests alone, such as the purely economic framings of much government policy. It also muddies educational waters, resulting in an over-instrumental approach to science formation. I have elsewhere attempted to tease out a longer argument for what a 'theology of science' might look like, but even a summary must begin with examples of the fresh (though ancient) sources that a late-modern theological project of this kind requires.

有一种思想流派认为：除非你首先知道真品证书的存在，否则你不能负责任地购买。这引出了一大堆问题。以什么方式知道，知道什么？谁来认证认证者？证书不是和签名一样容易被伪造吗？这些都是合理的顾虑，但这种想法的基本吸引力似乎足够清楚。如果你分辨不出伪造的笔迹和法弗的笔迹，自然会想要一个你自己能够鉴赏的替代标志。毕竟，我们之所以想要一个更清晰的可信度标记，不正是因为签名的真实性对我们来说不够明显吗？如果你不能举起证书说：「这就是我知道它是真品的原因」，那证书有什么意义呢？

The cue for a first wellspring of raw material comes from the neo-Kantian Berlin philosopher Susan Neiman. In a remarkable essay, she urges that Western philosophy acknowledge, for a number of reasons, a second foundational source alongside Plato – that of the Biblical Book of Job. The ancient Semitic text offers a matchless starting point for a narratology of the human relationship of the mind, and the experience of human suffering, with the material world. Long recognised as a masterpiece of ancient literature, Job has attracted and perplexed scholars in equal measures for centuries, and is still a vibrant field of study. David Clines, a leading and lifelong scholar of the text, calls Job 'the most intense book theologically and intellectually of the Old Testament'. Inspiring commentators across vistas of centuries and philosophies, from Basil the Great to Emmanuel Levinas, its relevance to a theology of science is immediately apparent from the poetic 'Lord's Answer' to Job's complaints late in the book.

另一种思想流派认为：你根本不需要意识到证书的存在就可以心安理得地购买头盔。虽然你可以看一眼证书，但你不是必须这样做才能让它发挥作用。证书是专家认定签名为真品这一事实的记录，只要这一可靠判断者发现的记录存在，你就可以毫无顾虑地购买头盔。一旦认证完成，你负责任地购买的条件就具备了。毕竟，这不就是认证专家的工作吗？

The writer develops material from the core creation narrative in Hebrew wisdom poetry – as found in Psalms, Proverbs and Prophets – that speaks of creation through 'ordering', as well as bounding and setting foundations. The questing survey next sweeps over the animal kingdom, then finishes with a celebrated 'de-centralising' text that places humans at the periphery of the world, looking on in wonder and terror at the 'other' – the great beasts Behemoth and Leviathan.

这两种思想流派在真品证书实际如何纳入你的决策上存在分歧。既然第一种流派认为证书必须在作为买方的你的视角之内，我们可以称之为「内在论」。第二种流派说证书只需要以适当的方式为商品出具即可，所以我们可以称之为「外在论」。买方并不真的需要查阅或检查它们。

The text is an ancient recognition of the unpredictable aspects of the world: the whirlwind, the earthquake, the flood, unknown great beasts. In today's terms, we have in the Lord's Answer to Job a foundational framing for the primary questions of the fields we now call cosmology, geology, meteorology, astronomy, zoology… We recognise an ancient and questioning view into nature unsurpassed in its astute attention to detail and sensibility towards the tensions of humanity in confrontation with materiality. The call to a questioning relationship of the mind from this ancient and enigmatic source feeds questions of purpose in the human engagement with nature from a cultural depth that a restriction to contemporary discourse does not touch.

这种流派之间的区分类似于认识论者之间关于辩护本质的实际争论。更大的问题是：什么为我们的信念提供辩护？更窄的问题是：辩护如何纳入一个思考着、探索着的人的生活。对于认识论中的内在论者来说，我的信念不可能得到辩护，除非其辩护者能够以某种方式被我鉴赏。外在论者否认这一点；他们说即使我无法检验使信念可信的因素，我也可以拥有一个有辩护的信念。

Drawing on historical sources is helpful in another way. The philosophy of every age contains its tacit assumptions, taken as evident so not critically examined. A project on the human purpose for science that draws on theological thinking might, in this light, draw on writing from periods when this was an academically developed topic, such as the scientific renaissances of the 13th and 17th centuries. Both saw considerable scientific progress (such as, respectively, the development of geometric optics to explain the rainbow phenomenon, and the establishment of heliocentricity). Furthermore, both periods, while perfectly distinguishing 'natural philosophy' from theology, worked in an intellectual atmosphere that encouraged a fluidity of thought between them.

这是一场学术争论吗？当然是，但我认为它不仅仅是学术性的。诚然，除非你修读高阶哲学课程，否则不太可能了解到内在论和外在论。然而，真正利害攸关的是关于我们作为认知者和探索者的竞争性构想，以及哪一种构想将在知识论中占上风。当我们追问关于可犯错理智的艰难问题时，应该从哪里开始？我们的基础图景是什么？

An instructive and insightful thinker from the first is the polymath Robert Grosseteste. Master to the Oxford Franciscans in the 1220s, and Bishop of Lincoln from 1235 to his death in 1253, Grosseteste wrote in highly mathematical ways about light, colour, sound and the heavens. He drew on the earlier Arab transmission of and commentaries on Aristotle, yet developed many topics well beyond the legacy of the ancient philosopher (he was the first, for example, to identify the phenomenon of refraction to be responsible for rainbows). He also brought a developed Christian philosophy to bear upon the reawakening of natural philosophy in Europe, whose programmes of astronomy, mechanics and above all optics would lead to early modern science.

在很长一段时间里，大多数哲学家把内在论视为理所当然的常识。如果你知道某件事，或者有充分的理由相信它，那么你就可以用一个你能引用的理由来为自己辩护。以苏格拉底（Socrates）为例。他的看家本领就是向当地的专家提出尖锐的问题，直到很清楚专家们其实并不知道自己说的是什么。例如，在柏拉图（Plato）的《游叙弗伦篇》（Euthyphro）对话中，苏格拉底遇到了当地的祭司和众神专家——正是游叙弗伦（Euthyphro）本人——他计划做一件按当地标准不敬的事情：起诉自己的父亲。苏格拉底问他，一个精通神明之道的智者，怎么能做一件众神显然会谴责的事？

In his Commentary on the Posterior Analytics (Aristotle's most detailed exposition of his scientific method), Grosseteste places a sophisticated theological philosophy of science within an overarching Christian narrative of Creation, Fall and Redemption. Employing an ancient metaphor for the effect of the Fall on the higher intellectual powers as a 'lulling to sleep', he maintains that the lower faculties, including critically the senses, are less affected by fallen human nature than the higher. So, re-illumination must start there: Since sense perception, the weakest of all human powers, apprehending only corruptible individual things, survives, imagination stands, memory stands, and finally understanding, which is the noblest of human powers capable of apprehending the incorruptible, universal, first essences, stands!

游叙弗伦回答说，事实上他的计划符合众神的标准。这只会引发苏格拉底更多的问题，他继续追问游叙弗伦澄清他所谓的虔敬是什么意思。如果游叙弗伦的计划如此虔敬，他当然能解释自己的理由并阐明虔敬的本质？然而在追问之下，游叙弗伦把自己逼入了困境，无法给出关于虔敬的清晰说明。对话到此结束，雅典首屈一指的神学家以一句「我实际上还有事要先走了」告辞。

Human re-engagement with the external world through the senses, recovering a potential knowledge of it, becomes a participation in the theological project of healing. Furthermore, the reason that this is possible is because this relationship with the created world is also the nexus at which human seeking is met by divine illumination.

雅典人尊敬游叙弗伦在众神之道上的智慧，但他却无法回答关于自己专业领域的一些基本问题。他的「知识」似乎颇为欠缺。观察苏格拉底，我们得到的印象是：如果你真正知道某件事，那么你必须能够给出说明。就像在数学课上，你必须能够展示你的推理过程。在理论上，你的说明应该是能够为信念辩护的真品证书，而且这应该满足内在论者，因为你是从自己的思想中「从内部」产生说明的。这也使它适合回应苏格拉底式的那种挑战。

The rise of experimentation in science as we now know it is itself a counterintuitive turn, in spite the hindsight-fuelled criticism of ancient, renaissance and medieval natural philosophers for their failure to adopt it. Yet the notion that one could learn anything general about the workings of nature by acts as specific and as artificial as those constituting an experiment was not at all evident, even after the foundation of the Royal Society. The 17th-century philosopher Margaret Cavendish was among the clearest of critics in her Observations upon Experimental Philosophy (1668): For as much as a natural man differs from an artificial statue or picture of a man, so much differs a natural effect from an artificial…

虽然苏格拉底式的说明在某些领域看起来是恰当的（例如在几何学中，有着明确的逐步证明），但在其他地方我们并不总是期望或要求这种说明。许多冷知识达人说不出自己是怎么知道每一个答案的——答案就是跳进了脑海。他们的回答似乎比侥幸猜中要好，但远远达不到苏格拉底式给出理由的理想。

Paradoxically perhaps, it was the theologically informed imagination of the medieval and early modern teleology of science that motivated the counterintuitive step that won against Cavendish's critique.

其他常见的辩护来源也是如此，比如他人的证言或我们自己的感官。尽管如此，这些来源似乎仍然满足内在论的基本理念：辩护必须在我们经验之内。一种观点认为，拥有某种类型的经验为你的信念提供了一种较弱但足够实质性的辩护。假设我有一种感官经验：我似乎听到了我的狗在地板上走动时爪子发出的咔嗒声。这种经验使我相信我的狗正在我身后走动。听觉经验辩护了我的信念。它并不能证明我的狗就在附近（她甚至可能不在那里），但这足以让我有理由相信她在。

Much of 'postmodern' philosophical thinking and its antecedents through the 20th century appear at best to have no contact with science at all, and at worst to strike at the very root-assumptions on which natural science is built, such as the existence of a real world, and the human ability to speak representationally of it. The occasional explicit skirmishes in the 1990s 'science wars' between philosophers and scientists (such as the 'Sokal-affair' and the subsequent public acrimony between the physicist Alan Sokal and the philosopher Jacques Derrida) have suggested an irreconcilable conflict. A superficial evaluation might conclude that the charges of 'intellectual imposture' and 'uncritical naivety' levied from either side are simply the millennial manifestation of the earlier 'two cultures' conflict of F R Leavis and C P Snow, between the late-modern divided intellectual world of the sciences and the humanities. Yet in light of the long and theologically informed perspective on the story that we have sketched, the relationship of science to the major postmodern philosophical themes looks rather different.

与苏格拉底式的观点一样，这种观点通过把你为辩护所需的一切都放在你的视角之内来体现内在论理念——这些东西对你的推理而言是充分可用的。我对自己狗在哪里的信念是有辩护的，因为我有一种让我联想到她脚步声的经验。而这些经验本身不需要辩护，因为正如哲学家罗德里克·奇泽姆（Roderick Chisholm）在1966年所说，它们是「自我呈现的」。它们使自身为人所知。它们怎么能不为人所知呢？还有什么比意识经验更明亮、更显而易见的呢？

Søren Kierkegaard and Albert Camus wrote of the 'absurd' – a gulf between the human quest for meaning and its absence in the world. Levinas and Jean-Paul Sartre wrote of the 'nausea' that arises from a human confrontation with sheer, basic existence. Derrida and Ferdinand de Saussure framed the human predicament of desire to represent the unrepresentable as différance. Hannah Arendt introduces The Human Condition (1958) with a meditation on the iconic value of human spaceflight, and concludes that the history of modernism has been a turning away from the world that has increased its inhospitality, so that we are suffering from 'world alienation'. The first modern articulation of what these thinkers have in common, an irreconcilable aspect of the human condition in respect of the world, comes from Immanuel Kant's Critique of Judgment (1790): Between the realm of the natural concept, as the sensible, and the realm of the concept of freedom, as the supersensible, there is a great gulf fixed, so that it is not possible to pass from the former to the latter by means of the theoretical employment of reason.

嗯，意见并不一致。威尔弗里德·塞拉斯（Wilfrid Sellars）明确阐述了自我呈现观念的一个持久困难：原始经验不适合用来辩护。它根本就是错误的工具。如果游叙弗伦用一段惟妙惟肖的家燕叫声来回应苏格拉底的提问，这位哲学家可能会留下深刻印象，但会正确地认为他的问题不仅得到了不充分的回答，而且根本没有得到正确类型的回应。同样，如果你问我：「你有什么理由认为你的狗在附近？」指出我那不可重复的、难以言表的内在片段有什么用呢？一份不能出示的真品证书有什么用呢？我甚至不能向自己引用我的经验，因为一旦我有了那个经验，它就已经过去了。

Kant's recognition that more than reason alone is required for human re-engagement with the world is echoed by George Steiner. Real Presences (1989), his short but plangent lament over late-modern literary disengagement with reference and meaning, looks from predicament to possible solution: Only art can go some way towards making accessible, towards waking into some measure of communicability, the sheer inhuman otherness of matter…

每一种内在论观点，甚至较弱的变体，都说辩护必须从你的视角出发是可获取的。辩护总是来自内部。但这种可获取性是什么？它通常被想象为你可以从扶手椅上获得的东西——通过反思自己的思想——所以如果你在相信任何事情上是有辩护的，你可以此时此地通过内省找到那种辩护。反思成了为你的信念提供辩护的手段，这就把巨大的负担压在了反思的肩上。

Steiner's relational language is full of religious resonance – for re-ligio is simply at source the re-connection of the broken. Yet, once we are prepared to situate science within the same relationship to the humanities as enjoyed by the arts, then it also fits rather snugly into a framing of 'making accessible the sheer inhuman otherness of matter'. What else, on reflection, does science do?

这也意味着任何不够反思的人都不可能拥有有辩护的信念。我们经常谈论很小的孩子——甚至在他们学会说话之前——知道什么，我们也说我们的同种生物也知道一些事情。我可能会告诉你有一只东菲比霸鹟（eastern phoebe）知道并记得她的巢在哪里，但分辨不出她的一只雏鸟是褐头牛鹂（cowbird）。虽然有些哲学家乐意将这些例子当作比喻性的而一笔带过，但其他人发现成熟成年人类与其他动物——无论是人类还是非人类——之间的连续性太过显著而无法忽视。毕竟，我们都面临同样的基本问题。我们都必须在不断变化的、并不配合我们的环境中找到自己的路。

The modernist hope of controlling nature through technology is dashed on the rocks of the same increasingly deep and problematic entangling with the world that prevents our withdrawal from it. The philosopher Jean-Pierre Dupuy, commenting in 2010 on a Europe-wide project using narrative analysis of public debates around nanotechnology, shows that they draw instead on both ancient and modern 'narratives of despair', creating an undertow to any discussion of 'troubled technologies' that, if unrecognised, renders effective public consultation impossible.

因此，要理解我们的认知处境，我们可能需要寻找内在论的替代方案。内在论对手扶椅式反思的聚焦使辩护过于智识化了。这听起来可能是一个奇怪的抱怨。它让人想起据说哲学家西德尼·莫根贝塞尔（Sidney Morgenbesser）对行为主义者 B·F·斯金纳（B F Skinner）说的话：「你是告诉我把人拟人化是错的吗？」一旦我们想起那些从内在论观点看似乎有问题的例子，这种悖论感就消失了。内在论捕捉了哲学和科学的严谨精神——在那里证明和论证是流通的硬通货——但当我们开始认真对待儿童和非人类动物信念的可信度时，它就显得力不从心了。它们通常不会或不能反思，所以内在论会让我们否认它们能拥有有辩护的信念。外在论者说：「那就更糟糕的是内在论了。」

The research team labelled the narratives: (1) Be careful what you wish for – the narrative of desire; (2) Pandora's Box – the narrative of evil and hope; (3) Messing with nature – the narrative of the sacred; (4) Kept in the dark – the narrative of alienation; and (5) The rich get richer and the poor get poorer – the narrative of exploitation. These dark and alienated stories turn up again and again below the surface of public framings of science, yet driving opinion and policy. The continuously complex case of genetically modified organisms is another example. None of these underlying and framing stories draws on the theological resources within the history of science itself, but all do illustrate the absurd, the alienation and the irreconcilable of postmodern thinking.

外在论作为可靠论（reliabilism）在认识论中崭露头角，该理论认为由可靠过程产生的信念是有辩护的。这种观点的吸引力不难理解。正如我们已经注意到的，我们依赖多种多样的信息渠道——知觉、记忆等等——我们之所以依赖这些特定的渠道，是因为它们足够经常地给出正确答案。当然，我们偶尔也会依赖它们而犯错（有一次我以为是提前到达，结果比一个半小时迟到地出现在研究生研讨会上），但这些过程本身成功的次数足够多，以至于即使它们给了你一个虚假的信念，那些信念仍然是有辩护的。一个可靠的认知过程就像一位优秀的气象学家。即使他们的记录中有一些错误，你也可以信任他们的话。

Small wonder, perhaps, that Bruno Latour, writing in 2007 on environmentalism, revisits the narrative of Pandora's Box, showing that the modernist hope of controlling nature through technology is dashed on the rocks of the same increasingly deep and problematic entangling with the world that prevents our withdrawal from it. But Latour then makes a surprising move: he calls for a re-examination of the connection between mastery, technology and theology as a route out of the environmental impasse.

而且，也像一位优秀的气象学家一样，可靠认知过程的内部运作不需要对你透明就能交付成果。你可以在完全不了解视觉如何运作、甚至不了解你视觉系统整体可靠性的情况下，根据你所看到的形成有辩护的信念。对可靠论来说，辩护源自过程的可靠性，而非其对意识的可获取性。因此，可靠论是一种外在论理论，而非内在论。

What forms would an answer to Latour's call take? One is simply the strong yet gentle repeating of truth to power that a confessional voice for science, and evidence-based thinking, can have when it is resting on deep foundations of a theology that understands science as a gift rather than a threat. One reason that Katharine Hayhoe, the Texan climate scientist, is such a powerful advocate in the United States for taking climate change seriously is that she is able to explicitly work through a theological argument for environmental care with those who resonate with that, but whose ideological commitments are impervious to secular voices.

没有了可获取性的负担，外在论能够解释非反思性思考者——如鸟类、狗和幼儿——的信念可信度。弗兰克·拉姆齐（Frank Ramsey）曾将信念比作地图，所以如果我们以制作和利用内部地图来模拟思考，把我们的同类生物纳入视野就很有道理。每一个思考着的事物都需要在不断变化的环境中找到自己的路——食物、朋友和敌人的位置都可能改变。因此，当我们思考这些生物信念的可信度和辩护时，我们关注的是这些生物成功所需什么。它们需要将自己与世界接触的感官。它们需要可以依赖的可靠过程。

There are more grassroots-level examples that demonstrate how religious communities can support a healthy lay engagement with science. Local movements can dissolve some of the alienation and fear that characterises science for many people. In 2010, a group of local churches in Leeds in the UK, decided to hold a community science festival that encouraged people to share their own and their families' stories, together with the objects that went with them (from an ancient telescope to a circuit board from an early colour TV set that was constructed by a resident's grandfather). A diverse movement under the general title 'Equipping Christian Leadership in an Age of Science' in the UK has discovered a natural empathy for science as a creative gift, rather than a threat to belief, within local churches.

一个内在论者可能会抱怨：「那苏格拉底式的给出和索取理由的实践呢？」可靠论者——以及其他类型的外在论者——通过简单地将理性反思外在化来回应这一点。我关于自己信念的信念来自另一种可靠过程：内省。成熟人类合理化的能力令人敬畏，对于必须平衡竞争性优先事项和自我意识的社交生物来说完全恰当。但这不是追随内在论者将其树立为辩护的来源或我们唯一的辩护手段的理由。

At a national level, the past five years have seen a remarkable project engaging senior church leaders in the UK with current scientific issues and their researchers. In a country with an established Church, it is essential that its voices in the national political process are scientifically informed and connected. Workshop participants, including scientists with no religious background or practice, have found the combination of science, theology and community leadership to be uniquely powerful in resourcing discussions of ethical ways forward, in issues from fracking to artificial intelligence.

另一个内在论的论证给外在论者带来了更多麻烦。让我们回到签名的例子。在故事的一个版本中，你基于真品证书决定购买头盔，一切顺利，你成为法弗亲笔签名的骄傲拥有者。但想象另一个结局不太愉快的版本：签名实际上是一份极其逼真的伪造品，以至于鉴定师为它出具了真品证书，而你基于证书购买了头盔。在第一个故事中，你利用证书做出决定，它让你得到了你想要的。在不愉快的版本中，你像之前一样利用证书做出决定，但由于并非你自己的过错，事情出了差错。

A relational narrative for science that speaks to the need to reconcile the human with the material, and that draws on ancient wisdom, contributes to the construction of new pathways to a healthier public discourse, and an interdisciplinary educational project that is faithful to the story of human engagement with the apparently chaotic, inhuman materiality of nature, yet one whose future must be negotiated alongside our own. Without new thinking on 'science and religion', we risk forfeiting an essential source for wisdom today.

内在论者说，尽管结局不同，我们可以看到一条共同的线索贯穿两个版本的故事。在两种情况下，你基于同样的理由进行了推理和决定。就你所能分辨的而言，两个版本中「从内部」看来一切都是一样的。不愉快结局中签名是伪造的这一事实对购买决策是否负责任没有任何影响，因为你的决定是基于一个可信来源做出的。如果在好的情况下你做出了正确的判断，那么在坏的情况下你也做出了正确的判断。

我们可以对信念得出同样的结论。想想你读到这里时你所在地方的天气如何。对我来说，天气非常闷热多云。假设我是对的；那就是好的情况。但在坏的情况下，虽然一切看起来和好的情况完全一样——感觉潮湿、看起来多云——我实际上在做梦，或者不知不觉被困在模拟中，或者被以其他方式欺骗了，所以我关于几乎一切的信念，包括天气，都是错误的。

就像签名故事中愉快的和不愉快的结局一样，我信念的结果——好的情况中的真理和坏的情况中的谬误——似乎与我是否以正确的方式形成信念并不真正相关。好的情况中我所能依据的一切在坏的情况中也都存在，反之亦然。信念的外在结果显然不同，但辩护并不不同。

外在论者在解释为什么我在好的和坏的情况下的信念似乎处于同等地位方面一直感到困难。可靠论者可以指向我的感官的可靠性来解释我在好的情况中的辩护，但当我在做梦或被困在母体（Matrix）中时呢？我的信念形成过程在坏的情况中不会是可靠的，所以我在其中不会有有辩护的信念。这与我们从考虑这些故事中得到的印象相抵触。你完全可以做出正确的判断却仍然失败，即使在被持续欺骗的情况下也是如此。

看起来我们是从一个简单的问题开始的：无论辩护到底是什么，它是内在的还是外在的？我们看到事情没那么简单。虽然表面上看这是一个关于单一主张的争论，但它实际上是不同的研究纲领之间的碰撞。争论的一方认为，当我们谈论一个信念的辩护时，我们谈论的是一个反思性的、自主的存在者的信念——一个我们可以追究责任的、对他人负责的人。相信者能够（而且必须）用好的理由来解释自己。

但另一方不仅仅不同意这最后一点。他们不同意将认知视为反思性清算的整个图景。思考不仅仅是为了理论化，而是为了行动和生活。在他们看来，我们必须以认知在动物生命中的角色为中心来理解可信度。给出和索取理由是后来才出现的，是从对血肉之躯的生物更为基本的关切中衍生出来的。

这些是关于认知者是什么的不相容愿景。当我们把关于自身可错性的问题框架化时，我们必须追问：「可错性究竟是在做什么方面的可错性？」但内在论与外在论之争，按照它通常的展开方式，是从这一点的下游开始的，仿佛我们已经有了一个关于我们在探索时在做什么的令人满意的解释：「我们应该这样假设吗？」

考虑勒内·笛卡尔（Ren Descartes）之后的心身问题。作为对笛卡尔关于心灵是一种非物质事物的结论的回应，一个唯物主义者可能会说：「不对，心灵就是大脑！它是一种物质的东西。」但等等——通过将心灵等同于物质，我们不经意间认可了一个核心的笛卡尔观念：心灵属于事物或质料的一般范畴。然而心灵可能根本不是那样的。如果我们把「心身问题」当作在非物质实体和物质实体之间做选择，我们可能会错过第三种可能性——「心灵」可能更像是一组能力的集合，而不是一个对象。它可能是一种活动，而不是另一个身体部分。

同样，内在论与外在论之争可能掩盖了更深层次的问题和有前景的替代方案。并不是「辩护是内在的吗」这个问题问错了方向或形式不当。而是它在逻辑空间应当完全敞开的节点上限制了我们的选项。毕竟，根据内在论者和外在论者的看法，一个认知者是什么？认知者就是相信者——他们通过形成句子大小的意见来对一个预先包装好的世界做出反应。但这一主张并不是一个给定，就像心灵是一种实体这一观念不是一个给定一样。

让我们重新追溯我们的步伐。我们从追问辩护及其是否是内在的中学到了一些东西。我们看到了不同的辩护概念如何体现不同的关于认知者是什么的理论。我们有机会走上我们在开头注意到的那条路，去探索一个更基本的问题。反思我们的记录，我们的成功和失败，我们可以问：「什么是探索？当我们试图去认知时，我们在做什么？」
