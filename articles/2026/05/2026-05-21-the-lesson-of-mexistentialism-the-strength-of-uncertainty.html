墨西哥存在主义的启示：不确定性的力量

Carlos Alberto Sánchez·Aeon·2026年5月21日·11分钟阅读·原文

最近一个晚上，我费尽口舌试图说服父亲_不要_回米却肯（Michoacán）。我告诉他现在不是好时机。他提醒我，自从老布什（George H W Bush）政府时期起，他就是归化入籍的美国公民了。我把新闻上看到的情况告诉了他：那些在美国居住了几十年、持有绿卡且有良好公民记录的人正在被驱逐出境，而理由似乎与过去的犯罪行为有关，比如顺手牵羊或违章停车。我提醒他，早在1990年代，他就曾因工作场所的一起事件被逮捕并以袭击罪起诉。我说：「我担心，」这就足以拘留你、剥夺你的公民身份、然后将你驱逐出境！他耸了耸肩。「更糟的事都发生过。再说了，我已经七十四岁了。我可能会在路上死去。_Nada es seguro_（世事无定）。没有什么事是确定的。」

I recently spent an evening trying to convince my father _not_ to go back to Michoacán. I told him it wasn't a good time. He reminded me that he's been a naturalised US citizen since the George H W Bush administration. I reported what I've seen on the news: people with decades of US residency, with Green Cards and outstanding civic engagement, are being deported, and the rationale seems to have something to do with previous acts of criminality, like shoplifting or parking tickets. I reminded him that, back in the 1990s, he was arrested and charged with assault for an incident at work. 'I'm afraid,' I said, that 'that's enough to detain you, strip you of your citizenship, and deport you!' He shrugged. 'Worse things have happened. Besides, I'm 74 years old. I may die on the way. _Nada es seguro_. Nothing is certain.'

我只好作罢。我妹妹惊慌地打电话给我：「告诉他别去！」

I let it be. My sister called me in a panic: 'Tell him not to go!'

「没用，」我说，「他要去。明年他试图回来的时候，我们就会知道他的命运了。」于是等待的游戏开始了。

'It's no use,' I said. 'He's going. We'll find out his fate when he tries to come back sometime next year.' And the waiting game began.

父亲那种不以为意的态度曾让我困惑不已。成长过程中，我以为他过于多愁善感、忧郁消沉。我确信他有什么不对劲。我想，也许他患上了抑郁症。他度过每一天的样子，就像一个被背叛了太多次的人，已经认命接受了一种令人失望的生活。

My father's dismissive attitude used to confuse me. Growing up, I thought he was overly sentimental and melancholic. I was sure that there was something wrong with him. I thought that, maybe, he was depressed. He lived each day like a man who had been betrayed one too many times and was now resigned to a disappointing life.

现在我明白自己错了。我现在看到，他不过是在反映命运的磨难与考验——他是一个历史的产物，这段历史无情地将他暴露在一个真相面前：「更糟的事」随时可能发生，而且，真正地、确确实实地，_没有任何事是确定的_。我现在明白，我曾以为的不以为意，其实是对一种根本性的脆弱性和偶然性的觉察与接受。

Now I see how wrong I was. I see now that he was merely reflecting the trials and tribulations of circumstance, that he was a product of a history that had mercilessly exposed him to the truth that a 'worse thing' can always happen and that, really and truly, _nothing is certain_. Now I see that what I thought was dismissiveness was awareness and acceptance of a fundamental fragility and accidentality.

在阅读了墨西哥哲学家关于偶然性和脆弱性的论述之后，我更加清晰地理解了父亲。墨西哥哲学家，尤其是存在主义传统中的那些——我在别处称之为（墨）存在主义（(M)existentialism）——在谈论_做墨西哥人意味着什么_时，似乎脑海中就有一个像我父亲这样的人。他们谈论的是一种理解或认识到「没有什么事是确定的」这句话含义的人。这种人理解或认识到自己的_nepantla_——他们处于空间、时间、目的地、生死之间的居间地带——因此他们认识到自己的不确定性、不稳定性以及根本性的不确定感，也就是墨西哥存在主义者所称的_zozobra_。简而言之，他们确信一件事：「_nada es seguro_（世事无定）」。那就是我父亲。

I came to see my father with even more clarity after reading what Mexican philosophers had to say about accidentality and fragility. Mexican philosophers, especially those of the existentialist tradition – what I've elsewhere referred to as (M)existentialism – seem to have a person like my father in mind when they talk about _what it means to be Mexican_. They talk about a type of person who understands or recognises the meaning of the phrase 'nothing is certain'. This person understands or recognises their _nepantla –_ that they are in between spaces, times, destinations, life and death – and so they recognise their indeterminacy, instability and radical uncertainty, what Mexistentialists call _zozobra_.In short, they are sure of one thing: that '_nada es seguro_'. That's my father.

想到我父亲是我认为_现在_阅读墨西哥存在主义很重要——或者说在_危机时刻_阅读它——的原因之一。当我们这样做时，我们不仅获得了可以帮助我们表达当前危机的词汇——如偶然性、_zozobra_、_nepantla_和_relajo_等词语——而且它还帮助我们理解我们的危机与我们的哲学如何紧密相连，历史创伤如何塑造或影响我们的视角，以及我们的视角为何在根本上重要。毕竟，父亲认为「没有什么事是确定的」是有原因的，这与他读过的什么或别人告诉他的什么毫无关系，而与他所经历的一切息息相关。

Thinking of my father is one reason why I think it's important to read Mexistentialism _now_, or to read it _in times of crisis_. When we do, not only are we gifted with vocabulary that can help us articulate our current crisis – words like accidentality, _zozobra_, _nepantla_ and _relajo_ – but it helps us understand how our crises and our philosophies are intimately tied to one another, how historical trauma shapes or informs our perspectives, and why our perspectives matter in the first place. After all, there is a reason why my father thinks that 'nothing is certain', and it has nothing to do with something he's read or something someone's told him. It has everything to do with the life he's lived.

一些哲学家在谈论视角或历史事件的塑造力量时会感到不安，但在墨西哥存在主义中，这些正是理解我们自身和世界——尤其是在危机世界中的我们——的核心。不可否认，这正是我们今天所处的位置：一个处于危机中的世界。我所说的「我们」，指的是那些身处危机之中或时刻面临危机威胁的人。在此，我遵循墨西哥哲学家埃米利奥·乌兰加（Emilio Uranga，1921-1988）在指称阅读和欣赏其哲学分析的「我们」时的含义：他们是「那些由于千百次历史、文化或社会事件的偶然，而被灾祸所框定的人」。对乌兰加以及他的同时代人来说，那些「被灾祸所框定的人」是墨西哥人以及所有像墨西哥人一样、能够认同一段受压迫、边缘化和历史暴力之历史的「他者」。

Some philosophers get anxious with talk of perspectives or the shaping power of historical events, but in Mexistentialism these are central to understanding ourselves and our world, and especially ourselves in a world in crisis. Undeniably, this is where we find ourselves today: a world in crisis. And by 'we', I mean those of us who exist in crisis or under the constant threat of crisis. In this, I follow what the Mexican philosopher Emilio Uranga (1921-88) means when he refers to the 'we' that will read and appreciate his philosophical analyses: they are 'those others that through a thousand accidents of history, of culture or society, have been framed by the catastrophic'. For Uranga, as well as for his contemporaries, those 'framed by the catastrophic' are Mexicans and all 'others' who, like Mexicans, can identify with a history of oppression, marginalisation and historical violence.

乌兰加不可能预见到的是，自从他写下这些话大约七十五年以来，「被灾祸所框定的人」这一「我们」已经扩大了。那些视角被创伤、暴力、迫害和恐惧所塑造的_我们_，已经扩展到包括来自世界各地的许多其他民族。在离家更近的美国，移民或那些_看起来像_移民的人，每天都在被背叛、不安全感和恐惧所框定——被塑造、被影响、被留下创伤——而对这些人来说，「没有什么事是确定的」是唯一的确定性。

What Uranga couldn't have foreseen is that the 'we' that has been framed by the catastrophic has grown since he wrote those words some 75 years ago. The _we_ whose perspectives are shaped by trauma, violence, persecution and fear has expanded to include many other peoples from across the globe. Closer to home, in the US, it is immigrants or those who _look like_ immigrants who, every day, are framed – shaped, informed, scarred – by betrayal, insecurity and terror, and for whom 'nothing is certain' is the only certainty.

对我来说，在危机时刻阅读墨西哥存在主义是一种揭示和赋权的体验。它对你来说也可以是揭示和赋权的。它在提醒我们人类是脆弱和偶然的（因此没有什么事是确定的）同时，教会我们：我们的危机，即使被灾祸所框定，也只是表面上的灾难。我们的危机不会摧毁我们！我们之所以知道危机不会摧毁我们，是因为这些危机铭刻在历史之中，而正是历史_框定_了我们是谁。至少，这是墨西哥存在主义及其底层历史主义的启示。

For me, reading Mexistentialism in times of crisis has been revealing and empowering. And it can be revealing and empowering for you too. While reminding us that our humanity is fragile and accidental (thus, that nothing is certain), it teaches us that our crises, even if they are framed by the catastrophic, are that only in appearance. Our crises will not destroy us! And we know that our crises will not destroy us because these crises are inscribed in history, and it is history that _frames_ who we are. This is, at least, the lesson of Mexistentialism and its underlying historicism.

借鉴何塞·奥尔特加·伊·加塞特（José Ortega y Gasset）、威廉·狄尔泰（Wilhelm Dilthey）、马丁·海德格尔（Martin Heidegger）等人的思想，20世纪中叶的墨西哥哲学家开始接受「历史主义」，西班牙哲学家胡利安·马里亚斯（Julián Marías）于1967年将其概括为以下主张：

Taking a cue from José Ortega y Gasset, Wilhelm Dilthey, Martin Heidegger and others, Mexican philosophers of the mid-20th century come to appropriate 'historicism', summarised by the Spanish philosopher Julián Marías in 1967 as the claim that:

人类生活的视界是历史的；人是由他所碰巧生活的历史层面所定义的；人曾经是什么，是人现在是什么的本质组成部分；他今天之所以是他，正是因为他过去是其他的东西；人类生活的领域包括历史。

the horizon of human life is historical; man is defined by the historical level at which it has been his lot to live; what man has been is an essential component of what he is; he is what he is today precisely because he was other things formerly; the realm of human life includes history.

对墨西哥存在主义者来说，历史主义的真理是显而易见的：殖民主义、独立、革命和其他历史经验创造了条件，形成了他们在奥尔特加之后所理解的「人类生活的视界」，或称「墨西哥处境」——根据莱奥波尔多·塞亚（Leopoldo Zea）的开创性著作《走向[拉丁]美洲哲学》（_Toward a [Latin] American Philosophy_，1942），这一处境「总是作为一个问题呈现自身」。哲学思考就是试图解决那个问题。

For Mexistentialists, the truth of historicism is obvious: colonialism, independence, revolution and other historical experiences created the conditions for what they understood, after Ortega, as 'the horizon of human life', or as the 'Mexican circumstance' – one that, according to Leopoldo Zea's pioneering work _Toward a [Latin] American Philosophy_ (1942), 'presents itself always as a problem'. To philosophise is to attempt to solve that problem.

最终，使墨西哥存在主义作为一种哲学世界观具有吸引力的是，它捍卫或证实了处境化的、即情即境的历史观点或视角——它赋予了这种视角力量。

Ultimately, what makes Mexistentialism attractive as a philosophical worldview is that it vindicates or validates the situated, or circumstantial, historical viewpoint or perspective – it empowers it.

墨西哥哲学，特别是在20世纪中叶，为自己设定了一项艰巨的任务：概括来说，其哲学家们试图在后殖民的景观中为自己破解存在、生存和自由的意义——这一景观上层层叠叠地覆盖着数百年来最初为剥夺权力、压迫和边缘化而构建的宗教、政治和本体论诠释。超过四百年间，西方尽其所能沉淀了一种特定的欧洲中心主义世界观。这种沉淀体现在存在方式和行为方式上，它们反映的是一种非墨西哥式的存在和作为；这就成了一个需要哲学干预的问题。

Mexican philosophy, particularly in the mid-20th century, set for itself a difficult task: broadly speaking, its philosophers sought to decipher, for themselves, the meaning of being, existence and freedom within a postcolonial landscape layered with centuries of religious, political and ontological interpretations originally constructed to disempower, oppress and marginalise. For more than 400 years, the West did its best to sediment a certain Eurocentric worldview. This sedimentation manifested in ways of being and doing that were reflections of a non-Mexican being and doing; this became a problem needing a philosophical intervention.

我说这些是为了强调一个观点：墨西哥哲学总体上，尤其是墨西哥存在主义，倾向于将自身扎根于历史、文化或「处境」之中。对概念、思想或现象的分析往往是由真实事件、特定时刻、运动和动荡所推动的。对现象或概念的抽象考量虽然并非这一传统所排斥，但并非其定义特征。因此，墨西哥存在主义哲学家声称，可以说，要「把哲学带到街头」，「给亚里士多德穿上瓦拉切（huaraches，墨西哥传统凉鞋）」，这是一种戏剧性的说法，意思是他们的目标是将哲学拉到日常生活中，拉到熟悉和具体的事物中，拉到墨西哥人生活的世界和生活经验中。

I say this to highlight the idea that Mexican philosophy in general, and Mexistentialism in particular, tends to root itself in history, or culture, or in the 'circumstance'. Analyses of concepts, ideas or phenomena are often motivated by actual events, by moments in time, by movements and upheavals. The abstract consideration of phenomena or concepts, while not alien to this tradition, is not what defines it. Thus, Mexistentialist philosophers claim to, as it were, 'take philosophy out to the streets' and 'put huaraches on Aristotle', a dramatic way of saying that their goal is to pull philosophy down to the everyday, to the familiar and concrete, to the lived world and to the lived experience of Mexicans.

移民、迫害、死亡和背弃承诺的历史促成了父亲的悲观态度

A history of immigration, persecution, death and broken promises motivates my father's pessimistic outlook

墨西哥存在主义的历史主义坚持认为历史事件塑造了我们如何思考以及我们思考什么。墨西哥历史本身——一部暴力、创伤和灾难的历史——要求哲学家以非理想化的方式解释世界，而给亚里士多德穿上瓦拉切、使哲学对普通人变得可理解的努力，正是对这一要求的回应。哲学家阿韦拉多·比列加斯（Abelardo Villegas）将这种要求归因于事件的「结构」。事件的结构是历史学家在穷尽一切解读之后仍然完整的东西。这种结构是无法被解读消解的，而正是对其的聚焦，最终赋予了墨西哥历史其独特性，赋予墨西哥哲学洞见以价值。

Mexistentialist historicism insists that historical events shape how we think and what we think. Mexican history itself – one that is violent, traumatic and catastrophic – demands that philosophers interpret the world in a non-idealistic way, and the effort to put huaraches on Aristotle, or make philosophy intelligible to the common person, is answering that demand. The philosopher Abelardo Villegas attributes such demands to the 'structure' of events. The structure of an event is what remains intact after historians exhaust themselves trying to interpret it. This structure is what cannot be interpreted away and the centring of which, ultimately, lends Mexican history its uniqueness and Mexican philosophical insights their value.

历史力量将影响哲学所说的内容以及像父亲这样的人所认为的真理。暴力、征服、殖民主义和革命的历史推动了乌兰加关于偶然性的哲学；移民、迫害、死亡和背弃承诺的历史推动了父亲「没有什么事是确定的」这一悲观态度。虽然这些思想或态度是在独特的历史经验中锻造出来的，但它们作为对所有人都有价值的教训被传达。比列加斯说，墨西哥哲学家将「向其他民族提供一种他们可能不具备的历史和文化经验」。这与乌兰加在其《墨西哥人的存在分析》（_Analysis of Mexican Being_，1952）中的主张相呼应，他坚持认为墨西哥哲学家「有教训可以传授」，而且这个教训绝不能是「不可传达的」。它将是从经验的语言翻译为哲学语言的教训，这意味着它将是可分享的、可传递的，并且可能是赋权的。这种赋权是必要的，尤其是对那些被灾祸所框定的人来说。对他们而言，从自己的视角谈论自己的经验，可能是从未被授权过的事；即使被授权了，也可能被认为毫无价值。

Historical forces will inform what is said by philosophy and what is thought as true by folks like my father. A history of violence, conquest, colonialism and revolution motivates Uranga's philosophy of accidentality, for instance; a history of immigration, persecution, death and broken promises motivates my father's pessimistic outlook that 'nothing is certain'. And while these ideas or outlooks are forged in a unique historical experience, they are communicated as valuable lessons for all.Villegas says that the Mexican philosopher will come to 'offer other peoples … a historical and cultural experience that they may not have.' This echoes Uranga's claim in his _Analysis of Mexican Being_ (1952), where he insists that Mexican philosophers 'have a lesson to teach', and that this lesson must not be 'incommunicable'. It will be a lesson translated from the language of experience to the language of philosophy, which means it will be shareable, transferable and potentially empowering. Such empowerment is necessary, especially for those framed by the catastrophic. For them, speaking from their own perspective about their own experience may not be something that has ever been authorised, and, if authorised, deemed of any value.

然而，如果哲学是由历史（由时间的、可变的事物）所提供的，那么客观性呢？我们中最理性的人坚持认为客观观点具有至高无上的地位，客观观点被定义为非个人的、超然于历史的视角，因为它为我们的知识提供了正当性，为我们的科学赋予了信心。历史化的视角威胁到了这种至高地位。

Yet, if philosophy is informed by history (by the temporal and changeable), then what about objectivity? The most rational among us insist on the supremacy of the objective point of view, defined as the impersonal, detached and ahistorical perspective, because it lends justification to our knowledge, and confidence to our science. A historically informed perspective threatens this supremacy.

但在墨西哥存在主义者看来，客观性可能并不像人们所说的那么了不起。根据莱奥波尔多·塞亚（Leopoldo Zea，1912-2004）的观点，客观性不过是殖民世界观的又一个遗留物。塞亚将坚持客观性称为一种「帝国激情」，即一种追求超然、非个人化和匿名性的帝国主义欲望。在这种观点下，客观性是一种意识形态建构，对掌权者（帝国）有利，他们将其设定为衡量什么算作「真理」和值得传播的尺度。如果一个「真理」在这个严格意义上不是「客观的」，那么它就被贬低为文化或历史独特性的产物——被视为习俗、神话或迷信。

For Mexistentialists though, objectivity may not be all that it's cracked up to be. According to Leopoldo Zea (1912-2004), objectivity is but another remnant of a colonial worldview. Zea referred to the insistence on objectivity as an 'imperial passion', that is, an imperialist desire for detachment, depersonalisation and anonymity. On this view, objectivity is an ideological construct useful for those in power (the Imperium), who set it as a metric over what counts as 'true' and worth communicating. If a 'truth' is not 'objective' in this strict sense of the term, then it is discounted as a product of cultural or historical idiosyncrasy – as custom, myth or superstition.

我们无法触及「无立场的观点」。我们能触及的只有自己的生活、历史和文化

We cannot access the 'view from nowhere'. We have access only to our own lives, our history and our culture

但将真理或知识贬低为文化和历史的独特性，是一种使处境化的、历史的视角或观点失效的方式。然而，根据塞亚和乌兰加等哲学家的观点，处境化的、历史的视角是我们唯一能够获得的视角，因为我们自身就是处境化和历史性的存在（我们不可能不是如此）。我们无法触及欧洲中心主义的客观性（以及哲学）所承诺的「无立场的观点」。我们能触及的只有自己的生活、历史和文化。

But discounting truth or knowledge as a cultural and historical idiosyncrasy is a way to invalidate situated, historical perspectives or viewpoints. However, according to philosophers like Zea and Uranga, the situated, historical perspective is the only one available to us, since we are situated and historical ourselves (we can't be otherwise). We cannot access the 'view from nowhere' that Eurocentric views of objectivity (and philosophy) promise. We have access only to our own lives, our history and our culture.

批评者指出，在最好的情况下，历史主义哲学方法仅仅反映了在特定时期构成一种文化的价值观、信仰和习俗。这一观点使得古巴裔美国哲学家豪尔赫·格拉西亚（Jorge Gracia，1942-2021）质疑了存在一种原创的、自主的、真正的拉丁美洲哲学的可能性，理由是哲学的文化基础没有普适性，因此缺乏哲学价值。格拉西亚将此称为文化主义方法，并论证说它部分地导致了拉丁美洲真正的哲学传统的迟到。在格拉西亚看来，即使文化在知识和价值观的产生中起着形成性作用，其时间维度也使其不符合真正哲学的标准。历史主义面临同样的指控。

Critics point out that, in the best-case scenario, the historicist approach to philosophy merely reflects those values, beliefs and customs that constitute a culture at a particular time. This view leads the Cuban-born American philosopher Jorge Gracia (1942-2021) to question the possibility of an original, autonomous and genuine Latin American philosophy on the grounds that a cultural foundation to philosophy has no universal applicability and thus lacks philosophical value. Gracia calls this the culturalist approach and argues that it is partly responsible for the delayed arrival of a proper philosophical tradition in Latin America. In Gracia's estimation, even if culture plays a formative role in the production of knowledge and values, its temporal dimension disqualifies it from being properly philosophical. Historicism is subject to the same indictment.

塞亚在其里程碑式的文章《作为承诺的哲学》（_Philosophy as Commitment_，1952）中写道：「承诺于普遍和永恒的事物，而不做出任何具体的承诺，并不构成真正的承诺。」虽然这写于格拉西亚对拉丁美洲哲学的批评几十年之前，但它可以作为对那种批评的回应。在这里，塞亚指出，在不关注具体事物（文化、历史、处境等等）的情况下追求普遍性是毫无意义的。做出具体的、个别的承诺才是重要的。而对具体事物的承诺可以采取多种形式，包括信赖传承的智慧（「没有什么事是确定的」）或重视自己的视角。

In his seminal essay 'Philosophy as Commitment' (1952), Zea says: 'Committing ourselves to the universal and the eternal, without making a single concrete commitment, does not commit us to anything.' While this was written decades before Gracia's critical remarks on Latin American philosophy, it could serve as a response to that critique. Here, Zea suggests that to seek universality without attending to the concrete (to culture, history, the circumstance and so on) is pointless. Making single concrete commitments is what's important. And committing oneself to the concrete can take many forms, including trusting in inherited wisdom ('nothing is certain') or valuing one's own perspective.

这把我们带到了视角主义。墨西哥存在主义及其底层的历史主义都不应与我们通常所说的「视角主义」相混淆，即使我在谈论它们时使用了「视角」或「观点」等词语。视角主义本身被墨西哥哲学家以主体间性的理由所拒绝。也就是说，基于以下理由：他者始终是我自身和我所知之事的条件。或者，转述奥尔特加的名言（「我是我以及我的处境」），我是谁是我与我的处境互动的产物，而这些处境是由与我共在的共同体中的他者所构成的。

This brings us to perspectivism. Neither Mexistentialism nor its underlying historicism is to be confused with what we normally call 'perspectivism', even if I use words like 'perspective' or 'viewpoint' to talk about them. Perspectivism itself is rejected by Mexican philosophers on intersubjective grounds. That is, on the grounds that others are always a condition for myself and for what I know. Or that, in paraphrase of Ortega's famous dictum ('I am me and my circumstances'), who I am is the product of my engagement with my circumstances, and these are constituted by others in communion and community with myself.

这种客观性的缺失不是墨西哥哲学的缺陷，而是其优势

This lack of objectivity is not a shortcoming of Mexican philosophy, but a strength

然而，说墨西哥存在主义建立在一种扎实的视角主义之上并不为过——它之所以扎实，是因为它将我的视角（或称_enfoque_）与一系列视角联系起来，这些视角共同呈现出「客观」真理的外观，但实际上是主体间性的真理（多个主体的真理）。主体间性的真理反映或传播的是一种文化或历史的观点；它不是托马斯·内格尔（Thomas Nagel）那种公正的或帝国式客观性的「无立场的观点」。

It wouldn't be wrong, however, to say that Mexistentialism assures itself on a robust perspectivism, one that becomes robust by linking my perspective (or enfoque) to an array of perspectives that, together, give the appearance of 'objective' truth, but that is actually intersubjective truth (truths of multiple subjects). Intersubjective truth reflects or broadcasts a cultural or historical viewpoint; it is not Thomas Nagel's 'view from nowhere' of impartial, or imperial, objectivity.

再次说明，当格拉西亚将墨西哥哲学称为「文化主义的」时，他是将其作为一种批评来使用的。「文化主义的」哲学史研究方法假定哲学以某种密切的方式与文化及其危机相关——涉及如何应对这些危机、如何克服它们或如何处理它们。格拉西亚这样称呼它是为了突出其客观性的缺失（并将其与真正的哲学拉开距离）。但这种缺失不是墨西哥哲学的缺陷，而是其优势。它是对主观观点的肯定。此外，如果视角集合（或文化主义立场）为我们提供了一幅关于我们所处现实或处境的忠实图景，或者它真实地反映了世界对我们的面貌，那么我看不出为什么它不应该成为推动我们哲学的动力。

Again, when Gracia refers to Mexican philosophy as 'culturalist', he means it as a critique. A 'culturalist' approach to the history of philosophy assumes philosophy to be related in some intimate way to culture and its crises – with how to address these or how to overcome them or deal with them. Gracia calls it this to highlight its lack of objectivity (and distance it from genuine philosophy). But this lack is not a shortcoming of Mexican philosophy, but a strength. It is an affirmation of the subjective point of view. Moreover, if the perspectival array (or the culturalist standpoint) gives us a faithful picture of the reality or the situation in which we find ourselves, or if it truly reflects how the world is for us, then I don't see why it shouldn't be that which motivates our philosophies.

粗心的读者可能会得出结论，认为这里真正发生的事情不过是墨西哥哲学家不愿意将真理从语境中分离出来，或者他们只不过是对客观性的哲学要求——因此也是对真正哲学的要求——一无所知。当然，事实并非如此。墨西哥哲学家既非不关心，也非不了解西方所认为的「真正哲学」；他们熟知其历史和意识形态要求。恰恰相反，他们将主观观点连同文化和历史一起作为其哲学思考的主要成分，因为这样做，他们才能确认那些元素、他们自身以及他们的哲学的价值。

A careless reader may conclude that what's really going on here is that Mexican philosophers simply do not want to bother distancing truth from context, or that they are simply ignorant of the philosophical requirements of objectivity, and thus of genuine philosophy. This, of course, is not the case. Mexican philosophers are neither unbothered by nor ignorant of what the West has deemed 'genuine philosophy'; they know its history and ideological demands well. Rather, they make the subjective point of view together with culture and history the main ingredients in their philosophising because, in doing so, they validate those elements, themselves, and their philosophy.

这里蕴含着教训。墨西哥存在主义有东西可以教给我们。它教会我们，历史经验对于自我理解是必要的，更重要的是，历史意识对于框定那些对哲学来说应该重要的问题是不可或缺的。凭借这些教训，我们可以着手识别、阐述和捍卫全球各地的其他哲学干预。我们可以着手推进全球哲学的事业。

And there are lessons here. Mexistentialism has something to teach us. It teaches us that historical experience is necessary for self-understanding and, more importantly, that historical consciousness is essential for framing those questions that should matter to our philosophy. With these lessons we can get on with the task of recognising, articulating and vindicating other philosophical interventions throughout the globe. We can get on with the task of a global philosophy.

我这样说是因为我怀疑阻碍学术哲学家拥抱全球哲学传统的一种焦虑是，他们认为这样做就等于在哲学中拥抱了类似于文化相对主义的东西。就墨西哥哲学而言，我逐渐认为那种认为它只提供某种特定文化视角的焦虑，建立在将历史主义与视角主义相混淆的基础上——后者被视为一种相对主义的、唯我论的学说，封闭了交流的道路。但对于墨西哥存在主义者来说，文化视角并非封闭的、相对主义的或唯我论的，而是对生活经验的历史化表达，每当它们被分享时就会超越自身。

I say this because I suspect that an anxiety keeping academic philosophers from embracing global philosophical traditions is the thought that, in doing so, they would be embracing something like cultural relativism in philosophy. When it comes to Mexican philosophy, I've come to think that the anxiety that it offers merely a determinate cultural perspective is grounded on confusing historicism for perspectivism as a relativistic, solipsistic doctrine that closes off roads to intercommunication. But for Mexistentialists in particular, cultural perspectives are not closed, relativistic or solipsistic, but historically informed articulations of lived experience that transcend themselves whenever they're shared.

墨西哥哲学家选择保留自身的差异，将哲学与历史联系在一起

Mexican philosophers chose to retain their difference by linking their philosophy to their history

乌兰加、塞亚和比列加斯阐明的是，墨西哥哲学所提供的哲学观点（或观点、视角）并非相对主义的，而是历史的、文化的和处境化的。如果一个视角具备所有这些特征，那么它就值得传播和分享。更重要的是，这些哲学家向我们保证，这些可分享的或超越性的真理没有什么抽象或神秘的地方。它们由历史和经验的压力所塑造，任何认识到这些压力的人都能识别它们。当这些视角或真理被汇集在一起时，它们描绘出人类境况的轮廓——熟悉的、却又粗粝且永远不完整的轮廓。然而，只有当这些文化的、历史的或处境化的真理在一个真理序列中被连接起来时，我们才能谈论「全球哲学」——如果我们有资格谈论这类事情的话，而且不超过我们有理由说的范围。

What Uranga, Zea and Villegas make clear is that the philosophical point of view (or viewpoint, or perspective) offered by Mexican philosophy is not relativistic, but historical, cultural and circumstantial. And that if a perspective is all these things, then it's worth communicating and sharing. More importantly, these philosophers assure us that there's nothing abstract or mysterious about these shareable, or transcendental, truths. They are formed by the pressures of history and experience, and can be recognised by anyone who recognises those pressures. When these perspectives or truths are brought together, they paint an outline of the human condition that is familiar, yet rough and always incomplete. However, only when these cultural, historical or circumstantial truths are linked together in an array of truths can we talk of 'global philosophy' – if we can talk about such things at all without saying more than what we are justified in saying.

但为什么不干脆像西方教我们做哲学那样去做哲学——即不参照历史或处境，sub specie aeternitatis（从永恒的视角）。答案非常直接，与所谓的哲学「双重束缚」有关：墨西哥哲学家要么肯定一种与墨西哥差异的独特性相一致的真实哲学身份，从而牢牢地扎根于作为意义和价值来源的墨西哥历史之中；要么坚持将哲学视为人类共通性的一种超历史表达——这需要超越那段历史和那些处境，从而超越差异。这是一个双重束缚，因为如果它选择保留其历史差异，就有可能不符合西方意义上的哲学；如果它选择成为西方意义上的哲学，就有可能抹除其差异。墨西哥哲学家选择了保留差异——将哲学与历史联系在一起。

But why not just do philosophy as the West has taught us to do philosophy, that is, without reference to history or circumstance – sub specie aeternitatis (from an eternal perspective). The answer to this is very straightforward and related to what is known as a philosophical 'double bind': Mexican philosophers can either affirm an authentic philosophical identity aligned with the particularity of Mexican difference, and thus firmly within the bounds of Mexican history as a source of meaning and value, or insist on philosophy as an ahistorical expression of a human sameness, something that requires the transcendence of that history and those circumstances and thus a transcendence of difference. It's a double bind because if it chooses to retain its historical difference, then it risks not being philosophical in the Western sense; if it chooses to be philosophical in the Western sense, then it risks erasing its difference. Mexican philosophers chose to retain their difference by linking their philosophy to their history.

父亲对确定性的态度并非他独有。这是一种在历史和经验的熔炉中锻造出来的世界观，由墨西哥存在主义者用偶然性、zozobra、relajo和nepantla等概念为我们阐述——这些概念捕捉了人类生活的偶然性和脆弱性。在宣称「没有什么事是确定的」时，父亲不过是在回应经验的教诲，同时为可能到来的一切做好准备。他在关键时刻自然而然地表现出哲学家的气质，这并不让我惊讶。毕竟，他是一个历史处境的产物，这种处境与在他之前的哲学家们所经历的并无不同：虽然是灾难性的，但却是强大的、赋权的。关于我们绝大多数人——无论是否是墨西哥人——我们也可以说同样的话：我们是历史的产物，而历史虽然可能充满创伤和痛苦，却也可以是强大的、赋权的。

My father's attitude to certainty is not his alone. It is a worldview forged in the crucible of history and experience, and articulated for us by Mexican existentialists with concepts like accidentality, zozobra, relajo and nepantla – concepts that capture the contingency and vulnerability of human life. In pronouncing that 'nothing is certain', my father is merely echoing the teachings of experience, while simultaneously preparing himself for what may come. That he easily channels philosophers in critical life moments does not surprise me. After all, he is a product of a historical circumstance that is not unlike those of the philosophers who came before him: although catastrophic, it is powerful and empowering. And we could say the same about the great majority of us, Mexican or not: we are products of a history that, while it may be traumatic and painful, can also be powerful and empowering.

我自己无法成为一个乐观主义者，原因与父亲选择赌一把移民执法一样。最近的事件已经清楚地表明，我的公民身份几乎没有提供什么确定性和安全感，尽管我出生在美国并且在这里度过了大半生。如果政治风向继续朝着目前的刮去，我可能最终会和父亲一起住在米却肯，被流放于家园之外，成为历史及其众多灾祸的受害者。但我只知道历史教给我的东西。而历史教会我，父亲是对的：nada es seguro（世事无定）。于是我们坚持下去。

I myself cannot be an optimist for the same reasons that my father chose to take his chances with immigration enforcement. Recent events have made it abundantly clear that my citizenship offers little certainty and security, even though I was born in the US and have lived here most of my life. If the political winds continue to blow in the direction in which they are currently blowing, I may end up living with my father in Michoacán, exiled from my home, a victim of history and its many catastrophes. But I know only what history has taught me. And history has taught me that my father's right: nada es seguro. And so we persist.
