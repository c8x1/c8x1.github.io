盲视如何解答意识的难问题

Nicholas Humphrey·Aeon·2026年5月29日·12分钟阅读·原文

50年前《新科学家》（_New Scientist_）杂志的封面展示了一只恒河猴的照片，标题是「一只看见一切的盲猴」。这只名叫海伦（Helen）的猴子是一项视觉神经心理学研究的一部分，由劳伦斯（拉里）·韦斯克兰茨（Lawrence (Larry) Weiskrantz）在剑桥大学（University of Cambridge）心理学实验室主持。1965年，他手术切除了海伦大脑后部的主要视觉皮层。手术后，海伦看起来完全失明了。一年后，作为博士研究生的我见到她时，一切似乎毫无改变。

The cover of _New Scientist_ magazine 50 years ago showed a picture of a rhesus monkey, with the headline 'A Blind Monkey That Sees Everything'. The monkey, named Helen, was part of a study into the neuropsychology of vision, led by Lawrence (Larry) Weiskrantz in the psychology laboratory at the University of Cambridge. In 1965, he had surgically removed the primary visual cortex at the back of Helen's brain. Following the operation, Helen appeared to be quite blind. When, as a PhD student, I met her a year later, it seemed nothing had changed.

但有一件事让我困惑。在哺乳动物中，从眼睛到大脑有两条主要通路：一条是进化上古老的——源自鱼类、青蛙和爬行动物所使用的视觉系统的后裔——通往中脑的视顶盖（optic tectum）；另一条是较新的，向上通往皮层。在海伦身上，较古老的视觉系统仍然完好无损。如果青蛙能利用视顶盖看东西，海伦为什么不能？

But something puzzled me. In mammals, there are two main pathways from the eye to the brain: an evolutionarily ancient one – the descendant of the visual system used by fish, frogs and reptiles – that goes to the optic tectum in the mid-brain, and a newer one that goes up to the cortex. In Helen, the older visual system was still intact. If a frog can see using the optic tectum, why not Helen?

趁韦斯克兰茨去开会不在的时候，我抓住机会进一步研究。我和海伦坐在一起，陪她玩耍，只要她试图通过视觉与我互动，就给予她食物奖励。令我欣喜的是，她开始做出回应了。几个小时之内，她就能伸手从我的手中拿走苹果片；一周之内，她能伸手去触碰一个闪烁的小灯……七年后（如下方视频所示），她能在复杂的场地中奔跑，灵活地避开障碍物，从地上捡起花生。

While Weiskrantz was away at a conference, I took the chance to investigate further. I sat with Helen and played with her, offering her treats for any attempt to engage with me by sight. To my delight, she began to respond. Within a few hours, I had her reaching out to take pieces of apple from my hand; within a week, she was reaching out to touch a small flashing light… Seven years later (as shown in the video below), she was running round a complex arena, deftly avoiding obstacles, picking up peanuts from the floor.

任何在1972年观察海伦——而不知道她过去经历——的人都会觉得她的视力现在已经相当正常了。然而，她真的能「看见一切」，正如《新科学家》封面所暗示的那样吗？我不这么认为。我很难说清缺了什么。但我的直觉是，海伦自己仍然怀疑她能看见。她似乎对自己的能力奇怪地缺乏信心。如果她情绪不佳或受到惊吓，她的信心就会消失，她会像在黑暗中一样踉踉跄跄。我在杂志封面内文章中用的标题是「看见与虚无」（Seeing and Nothingness）。

To anyone who'd observed Helen in 1972 – and didn't know the history – it would have seemed that her eyesight was now quite normal. Yet, could she really 'see everything', as the _New Scientist_'s cover implied? I didn't think so. I found it hard to put my finger on what was missing. But my hunch was that Helen herself _still doubted_ she could see. She seemed strangely unsure of herself. If she was upset or frightened, her confidence would desert her, and she would stumble about as if in the dark again. The title I gave to my article inside the covers of the magazine was 'Seeing and Nothingness'.

我们正处于一项非凡发现的边缘。在海伦的研究发现之后，韦斯克兰茨对一位人类患者采取了新的方法。这位患者以姓名首字母 DB 为代称，在接受了移除影响大脑左侧视觉皮层的肿瘤手术后，右半视野失明。在失明区域，DB 本人坚称自己没有任何视觉意识。尽管如此，韦斯克兰茨还是要求他猜测位于该区域的物体的位置和形状。令所有人惊讶的是，他持续猜对。对 DB 本人来说，他猜测的成功似乎完全不合情理。就他而言，他自己不是其感知判断的来源，他的视觉与他毫无关系。韦斯克兰茨将这种能力命名为「盲视」（blindsight）：在没有任何视觉感觉的情况下的视觉感知。

We were on the brink of a remarkable discovery. Following on from the findings with Helen, Weiskrantz took a new approach with a human patient, known by the initials DB, who, after surgery to remove a growth affecting the visual cortex on the left side of his brain, was blind across the right-half field of vision. In the blind area, DB himself maintained that he had no visual awareness. Nonetheless, Weiskrantz asked him to _guess_ the location and shape of an object that lay in this area. To everyone's surprise, he consistently guessed correctly. To DB himself, his success in guessing seemed quite _unreasonable_. So far as he was concerned, _he_ wasn't the source of his perceptual judgments, his sight had _nothing to do with him_. Weiskrantz named this capacity 'blindsight': visual perception in the absence of any felt visual sensations.

盲视如今已是公认的临床现象。最初被发现时，它在理论上令人震惊。没有人预料到感知和感觉之间可能存在这样的分离。然而，当我在思考其对理解意识的启示时，我发现自己在重新审视。也许真正的谜题不是盲视中感觉的缺失，而是正常视觉中感觉的存在？如果盲视是看见与虚无，正常视觉就是看见与某物。而 surely 正是这种「某物」需要被解释。

Blindsight is now a well-established clinical phenomenon. When first discovered, it seemed theoretically shocking. No one had expected there could possibly be any such dissociation between perception and sensation. Yet, as I ruminated on the implications of it for understanding consciousness, I found myself doing a double-take. Perhaps the real puzzle is not so much the _absence_ of sensation in blindsight as its _presence_ in normal sight? If blindsight is seeing and nothingness, normal sight is _seeing and somethingness_. And surely it's this _something_ that stands in need of explanation.

为什么视觉感觉——如正常视觉中所体验的——具有那种神秘的感觉？为什么存在哲学家所谓的「现象体验」（phenomenal experience）或感受质（qualia）——我们通过感官接收刺激时产生的主观的、个人的感受？不仅限于视觉，而是所有感觉模态：红色的红、咸味的咸、疼痛的痛——这种额外的体验维度究竟意味着什么？它的功能是什么？关键是，除了我们自己之外，还有哪些动物体验了它，哪些是有感知力的（sentient）？

Why do visual sensations, as experienced in normal vision, have the mysterious feel they do? Why is there any such thing as what philosophers call 'phenomenal experience' or qualia – our subjective, personal sense of interacting with stimuli arriving via our sense organs? Not only in the case of vision, but across all sense modalities: the redness of red; the saltiness of salt; the paininess of pain – what does this extra dimension of experience amount to? What's it for? And, crucially, which animals besides ourselves experience it, which are _sentient_?

需要明确的是，感觉（sensation）的功能不同于感知（perception）。两者都是心理表征的形式：大脑产生的观念（ideas）。但它们所表征的——它们所关于的——是截然不同的事物。感知——在盲视中仍然部分完好——是关于「外部世界正在发生什么」：苹果是红的；石头是硬的；鸟在唱歌。相比之下，感觉更加个人化，它关于「正在发生在我身上的事以及作为主体的我如何评估它」：疼痛在我的脚趾里而且很可怕；甜味在我的舌头上而且发腻；红光在我眼前而且让我心潮澎湃。

Sensation, let's be clear, has a different function from perception. Both are forms of mental representation: _ideas_ generated by the brain. But they represent – they are _about_ – very different kinds of things. Perception – which is still partly intact in blindsight – is about 'what's happening out there in the external world': the apple is red; the rock is hard; the bird is singing. By contrast, sensation is more personal, it's about 'what's happening to me and how I as a subject evaluate it': the pain is in my toe and horrible; the sweet taste is on my tongue and sickly; the red light is before my eyes and stirs me up.

仿佛在拥有感觉时，我们既在记录刺激的客观事实，又在表达我们关于它的个人身体意见。但这些额外的定性维度从何而来？是什么使感觉创造的主观当下显得如此丰富而深邃，仿佛我们生活在浓稠的时间中？艺术家瓦西里·康定斯基（Wassily Kandinsky）写下面这段话时是什么意思：「色彩是一种直接影响力量的灵魂力量。色彩是键盘，眼睛是琴槌，灵魂是有许多弦的钢琴」？为什么人们确实会使用「体验感觉就像是某种东西」（it's like something to）这种奇怪的表达？是否因为意识感觉就像是某种它们实际上不是的东西？

It's as if, in having sensations, we're both registering the objective fact of stimulation and expressing our personal bodily opinion about it. But where do those extra qualitative dimensions come from? What can make the subjective present created by sensations seem so rich and deep, as if we're living in thick time? What can the artist Wassily Kandinsky mean when he writes: 'Colour is a power which directly influences the soul. Colour is the keyboard, the eyes are the hammers, the soul is the piano with many strings'? Why indeed do people use the strange expression 'it's like something to' experience sensations? Is it because conscious sensations are like something they cannot really be?

在试图回答这些问题时，我们面对的是所谓的「意识的难问题」（hard problem of consciousness）：一个物理大脑如何能够支撑现象体验的超物理属性。而且我不需要告诉你，我们一些最优秀的哲学家已经说过这个问题永远无法解决。正如哲学家杰里·福多（Jerry Fodor）所言：

In attempting to answer these questions, we're up against the so-called 'hard problem of consciousness': how a physical brain could underwrite the extra-physical properties of phenomenal experience. And I don't need to tell you this is a problem that some of our best philosophers have said we're never going to solve. As the philosopher Jerry Fodor has put it:

「即使有一丝一毫的线索，我们也不知道一个大脑（或任何其他物理的东西）如何能够成为意识经验的场所。这……毫无疑问是最终极的形而上学奥秘之一；别押注任何人能解决它。」

[We don't know], even to a first glimmer, how a brain (or anything else that is physical) could manage to be a locus of conscious experience. This … is, surely, among the ultimate metaphysical mysteries; don't bet on anybody ever solving it.

过去50年来，我一直在缓慢地寻找这些问题的答案。从一开始，我就认为不应该正面硬刚难问题。不仅仅因为它难，而是因为它是错误的问题。

Over the past 50 years, I've been inching my way towards answers to these questions. From the start, I thought better than to confront the hard problem head-on. Not just because it's hard, but because it's the wrong problem.

许多理论家确实相信意识状态与大脑状态是同一的（identical）。对他们来说，理解意识体验的显而易见的最佳方法就是寻找在其物理结构中内置了现象属性的大脑事件。这其实是一个相当古老的想法。追溯到1929年的《大英百科全书》（_Encyclopaedia Britannica_），你可以读到：

Many theorists do indeed believe that conscious states are _identical_ to brain states. And, for them, the obvious and best approach to understanding conscious experience is to search for brain events that have _phenomenal properties built into their physical structure_. This is actually quite an old idea. Going back to the _Encyclopaedia Britannica_ of 1929, for example, you could read:

「一种理论认为，物理身体的每个原子都拥有意识的固有属性。如果每个原子，或者在这种理论的后续版本中，身体的每个细胞都散发自己的意识，那么『自我』实际上必须由所有这些微小的意识单元的集合构成……第二种理论假设大脑中存在特殊的神经细胞，只要被激活就能产生意识……心理子理论（psychonic theory）基于意识与神经元间现象的对应关系，认为每当任何神经元之间的接合组织单位被激活时，意识就会产生。」

One theory holds that each atom of the physical body possesses an inherent attribute of consciousness. If each atom, or, in later forms of this theory, each cell of the body emanates its own consciousness, then the 'self' must actually consist of an amalgam of all these tiny units of awareness … A second theory assumes that there exist, in the brain, special nerve cells capable of producing consciousness whenever activated … The psychonic theory, based on the correspondences between consciousness and inter-neuronic phenomena, suggests that consciousness occurs each time any unit of junctional tissue between individual neurones is energised.

如今，语言已经更新。例如，目前很流行的「整合信息理论」（integrated information theory）假设现象体验直接产生于信息在大脑中被保留和与其他信息并列的方式。

Today, the language has moved on. For example, 'integrated information theory', which is currently fashionable, postulates that phenomenal experience arises directly from the way information is retained and juxtaposed with other information within the brain.

然而，正如我逐渐认识到的，所有这类物理同一性理论都走错了方向。它们过去是——现在也是——试图解释现象属性如何能够是大脑过程的属性。但这建立在对需要解释的事物类型的一个根本性误解之上。让我强调：感觉是观念（ideas）。它们是大脑表征感官正在发生什么以及我们对此感受如何的方式。因此，它们的属性不应该被解释为大脑状态的属性，而应该被解释为大脑构想出来的心理状态的属性。

However, as I came to realise, all such physical-identity theories have got off on the wrong foot. They were – and are – attempts to explain how phenomenal properties could be properties _of_ a brain process. But this rests on a fundamental misunderstanding of the kind of thing that needs explaining. Let me emphasise: sensations are _ideas_. They are the way our brains represent what's happening at our sense organs and how we feel about it. Their properties are to be explained, therefore, not literally as the properties of _brain-states_, but rather as the properties of _mind-states dreamed up by the brain_.

而一旦我以这种方式看待它，迷雾就消散了。现象意识是大脑执行的一种认知操作的结果：以一种特定方式表征感官体验。很可能，它涉及大脑生成某种类似内部文本（internal text）的东西，然后将其解读为关于现象属性的描述。然而，我们不应该期望这段大脑文本本身具有现象属性，就像我们不应该期望《白鲸》（_Moby Dick_）的文本是白色的或像鲸鱼一样。

And, once I came to see it this way, the shades fell away. Phenomenal consciousness is the result of a cognitive operation performed by the brain: _representing_ sensory experience in a certain way. Quite possibly, it involves the brain generating something like an _internal text,_ that it _interprets as being about phenomenal properties_. However, we should no more expect this brain text to have phenomenal properties in its own right than we should expect the text of _Moby Dick_ to be white or whale-like.

我的计划变成了弄清楚像大脑这样的生物机器如何能够完成这种表征壮举。而我采用的策略是「正向工程」（forward engineering）。也就是说，我从最终产物出发——即人类今天所体验的感觉；但与其像科学家通常做的那样将其视为需要拆解的东西，我将其视为需要发明（invent）的东西。我试图构建一个进化序列，使我们从虚无走向某物：从我们远古祖先的盲视发展到我们人类今天所享受的完全现象性视觉。

My project became to work out how a biological machine like the brain could carry out this feat of representation. And the strategy I've adopted has been 'forward engineering'. That's to say, I've begun with the end product – sensations as humans experience them today; but, rather than treating this, as scientists typically do, as something to deconstruct, I've treated it as something to _invent._ I've tried to come up with an _evolutionary_ sequence that will get us from nothingness to somethingness: from the blindsight of our remote ancestors to the fully phenomenal sight we humans enjoy today.

我认为感觉最初是作为对感官刺激的一种主动行为反应：动物对刺激所做的某事（did），而不是它对此感受到的某事（felt）。

I believe sensations originated as an active behavioural response to sensory stimulation: something the animal _did_ about the stimulus rather than something it _felt_ about it.

想象一种原始的变形虫般的动物漂浮在远古海洋中。各种事情发生在它身上。光落在它的身体表面，压力波压迫着它，化学物质附着在它身上。其中一些事件对动物有益，其他的则有害。如果要生存下去，它必须进化出分辨好坏的能力，并做出不同的反应——对这个刺激做出「哎哟！」的反应，对那个刺激做出「太好了！」的反应。

Imagine a primitive amoeba-like animal floating in the ancient seas. Stuff happens to it. Light falls on its body surface, pressure waves press against it, chemicals stick to it. Some of these events are going to be good for the animal, others bad. If it is to survive, it must evolve the ability to sort out the good from the bad, and to respond differently – reacting to this stimulus with an _Ouch!_, and to that with a _Whoopee!_

我称这些评估输入的表达性反应为「感受表达」（sentition）。最初，它们完全是局部反应——在刺激部位周围组织的接受或拒绝的扭动。比如，当红光照射到动物身上时，它会做出一种特征性的扭动——它以「红色」的方式扭动。当盐分到达时，它会做出不同类型的扭动——它以「咸味」的方式扭动。但不久之后，这些反应开始由一个中央神经节或原脑来中介。

I call these expressive responses that evaluate the input 'sentition'. To start with, they are entirely local responses – wriggles of acceptance or rejection – organised around the site of stimulation. When, say, red light falls on the animal, it makes a characteristic wriggle of activity – it wriggles 'redly'. When salt arrives, it makes a different kind of wriggle – it wriggles 'saltily'. But, before long, the responses come to be mediated by a central ganglion, or proto-brain.

从一开始，感受表达就被自然选择设计为具有适应性，每个反应都考虑到什么类型的刺激正在到达身体表面，以及它对动物的健康有什么意义。因此，即使从最早的阶段起，我们就可以说动物正在表现刺激对它意味着什么。然而，到目前为止，这些反应完全是反射性的，这些意义中没有任何部分被保存在「心中」。

From the beginning, sentition has been designed by natural selection to be adaptive, with each response taking account of what kind of stimulus is reaching the body surface, and what import it has for the animal's wellbeing. Thus, even from the earliest stage, we could say the animal is _enacting_ what the stimulation _means_ to it. Yet, so far, the responses are entirely reflex, and none of this meaning is being held 'in mind'.

但是让我们想象，随着动物的生活变得更加复杂，它到了一个阶段，需要保留某种关于影响它的事物的「心理记录」：一种可以用作计划和决策基础的刺激表征。事实证明，有一种巧妙的方法可以实现这一点。要发现「正在发生在我身上的事」，动物只需监控「我对此正在做什么」。它可以通过创建反应命令信号的副本——一种可以反向读取以重建刺激含义的「传出副本」（efference copy）——来做到这一点。

Let's imagine, however, that as the animal's life becomes more complex, it reaches a stage where it would benefit from retaining some kind of 'mental record' of what's affecting it: a representation of the stimulus that can serve as a basis for planning and decision-making. And, as it turns out, there's a neat way of achieving this. To discover 'what's happening to me', the animal has only to monitor 'what I'm doing about it'. And it can do this by the simple trick of creating a copy of the command signals for the responses – an 'efference copy' that can be read in reverse to recreate the meaning of the stimulation.

简而言之，动物可以通过访问其自身反应中已经隐含的信息来开始获得对刺激的感受（get a feel）。我认为这是主观感觉的前体。当然，它最初不会是我们人类所知的感觉：它不会有任何特殊的现象性质。

In short, the animal can begin to _get a feel_ for the stimulus by accessing the information already implicit in its own response. This, I believe, is the precursor of subjective sensation. But, of course, it will not at first be sensation as we humans know it: it will not have any special phenomenal quality.

获得现象属性的关键在于感受表达如何继续进化。在早期，它涉及外显的身体行为。但必然会有一个阶段，这种外显行为不再合适。比如，动物不再想对红光做出反射性的退缩反应。但它仍然想记录红光正在照射它的身体并且它感到威胁。那么该怎么办？

The key to acquiring phenomenal properties lies in how sentition goes on evolving. In the early days, it involves bodily behaviour, out in the open. But there must come a time when such overt behaviour is no longer appropriate. The animal no longer wants to recoil reflexly from red light, for example. But it still wants to register that red light is falling on its body and that it feels menacing. So what to do?

答案是让这些反应变得内化或「私有化」。发生的事情是，命令信号不再引发实际的运动行为，而是开始靶向感官器官投射到大脑的内部身体图谱。通过这种方式，感受表达进化为一种虚拟的身体表达形式——但仍然是一种可以被读取以提供引发它的刺激的心理表征的活动。

The answer is for the responses to become internalised or 'privatised'. What occurs is that the command signals, rather than bringing about actual motor behaviour, begin to target the internal body-map where the sense organs project to the brain. In this way, sentition evolves to be a _virtual_ form of bodily expression – yet still an activity that can be read to provide a mental representation of the stimulation that elicits it.

但运气好的是，私有化产生了一个非凡的结果。它导致了大脑运动区和感觉区之间反馈环路（feedback loops）的创建。这些环路具有维持递归活动（recursive activity）的潜力，一圈又一圈，抓住自己的尾巴。我认为这一发展是改变游戏规则的。关键是，它意味着活动可以在时间上被拉伸，从而创造感觉的「浓密时刻」（thick moment）。但不仅如此，活动可以被引导和稳定，从而创造一个数学上复杂的吸引子状态（attractor state）——一种自我重建的动态活动模式。

But, as luck would have it, the privatisation has a remarkable result. It leads to the creation of _feedback loops_ between motor and sensory regions of the brain. These loops have the potential to sustain _recursive activity_, going round and round, catching its own tail. And, I suggest, this development is game-changing. Crucially, it means the activity can be drawn out in time, so as to create the 'thick moment' of sensation. But, more than that, the activity can be channelled and stabilised, so as to create a mathematically complex _attractor state_ – a dynamic pattern of activity that recreates itself.

这样的吸引子在理论上可以拥有非凡的超维属性。真实的、非真实的、还是神奇的？答案取决于观察者的眼光。这意味着，从进化的这一点开始，每当改善感觉质量对动物有益时，自然选择就有了一个全新的设计空间可以探索。对电路的微小调整可以导致吸引子形状的剧烈变化，伴随主观体验的相应变化。

Such an attractor can, in theory, have remarkable hyper-dimensional properties. Real, unreal, magical? The answer will be in the eye of the beholder. It means that, from this point on in evolution, whenever it would be beneficial to the animal to 'improve' the quality of its sensations, natural selection has a whole new design space to explore. Small adjustments to the circuitry can result in dramatic changes to the attractor's shape, with corresponding changes in the subjective experience.

我认为最终的结果——在通向人类和其他像我们一样体验事物的动物的谱系中——是创造了一种非常特殊的吸引子，主体将其解读为具有不可解释的现象感受质感觉的感觉。这种吸引子仍然是一种感受表达，它起源于对感官刺激的反应，仍然携带着关于「正在发生在我身上的事」的信息。但现在这些信息以一种非凡的新包装出现。可以说，它是作为写在大脑上的谜题的一部分出现的。

I believe the upshot – in the line of animals that led to humans and others that experience things as we do – has been the creation of a very special kind of attractor, which the subject reads as a sensation with the unaccountable feel of phenomenal qualia. This attractor is still a type of sentition, which originates as a response to sensory stimulation, and still carries information about 'what's happening to me'. But this information now comes in a remarkable new package. It comes, if you like, as part of a riddle written on the brain.

这只是故事的粗略轮廓，毫无疑问最多只是部分正确。但有所保留地说，我认为它可以胜任这一任务。每一步作为进化发展都是合理的。而且它导向了一个原则上可以产生我们人类所拥有的那种现象意识的终态。

This is the story only in bare outline, and no doubt it's only partly right at best. But, with due reservation, I submit that it's up to the job. Every step is plausible as an evolutionary development. And it leads to an end state that could in principle deliver the kind of phenomenal consciousness we humans possess.

但我们仍然必须回答一个关键问题：为什么？对我们的祖先——以及今天仍然对我们——来说，拥有以这种奇妙的——一些哲学家可能会说完全不必要地花哨的——方式包装的意识体验，究竟有什么生物学优势？再次引用福多的话：

But we still have to address the crucial question: _Why_? Whatever could have been the biological advantage to our ancestors, and still to us today, of having conscious experience dressed up in this wonderful – and, some philosophers would say, quite unnecessarily exotic – fashion? To quote Fodor again:

「意识……似乎属于长期失业的那一类……就任何人所知，我们有意识的心智能做的任何事情，如果它们没有意识的话也一样能做到。那么，上帝为什么还要费心创造意识呢？他到底在想什么？」

Consciousness … seems to be among the chronically unemployed … As far as anybody knows, anything that our conscious minds can do they could do just as well if they weren't conscious. Why, then, did God bother to make consciousness? What on earth could he have had in mind?

我无法代替上帝回答。但在代表自然选择回答时，我认为我们可以而且应该让第一人称直觉来引导我们。所以，问问自己：如果你缺乏现象意识，你的生活中会缺少什么？如果你有盲视、盲触、盲听、一切皆盲？尽管福多有所质疑，我确信有一个显而易见的答案，就是我们在讨论盲视时触及的那个。缺少的将不是别的，正是你（you），你的有意识的自我（conscious self）。

I can't answer for God. But in answering for natural selection, I think we can and should let first-person intuition be our guide. So, ask yourself: what would be missing from _your_ life if you lacked phenomenal consciousness? If you had blindsight, blind-touch, blind-hearing, blind-everything? _Pace_ Fodor, I'm sure there's an obvious answer, and it's the one we touched on when discussing blindsight. It's that what would be missing would be nothing less than _you,_ your _conscious self_.

关于患有盲视的人类患者，最引人注目的事实之一是，他们不对自己的视觉能力主张所有权（ownership）。缺乏视觉感受质——看见的「某物性」——他们相信视觉感知与自己毫无关系。那么想象一下，如果你完全缺乏任何类型的感受质，发现你所有的感官体验都不属于你？我确信你的自我（self）将会消失。

One of the most striking facts about human patients with blindsight is that they don't take _ownership_ of their capacity to see. Lacking visual qualia – the 'somethingness' of seeing – they believe that visual perception has nothing to do with _them_. Then, imagine if you were to lack qualia of any kind at all, and to find that none of your sensory experience was owned by you? I'm sure your _self_ would disappear.

所以，回想一下当你的祖先第一次体验到充满感受质的感觉，并在虚无之中，现象自我（phenomenal self）出现时，一定发生了的那种转变。当然，这不是一夜之间发生的。但它也不一定是一个渐进的过程。因为事实是，反馈环路中复杂的活动模式容易发生突然的阶跃式变化；吸引子具有全有或全无的特征。我认为，一旦负责产生现象体验的大脑电路重组开始，它可能在相当短的时间内完成，也许只需几百代。

So, think back to the transformation that must have taken place when your ancestors first woke up to the experience of sensations imbued with qualia, and – out of nothing – the phenomenal self _appeared._ Of course, it won't have happened overnight. But nor need it have been a gradual process either. For the fact is that complex patterns of activity in feedback loops are liable to undergo sudden stepwise changes; attractors have an all-or-nothing character. I believe the reorganisation of the brain circuits responsible for generating phenomenal experience, once started, could have come to fruition quite quickly, perhaps within a few hundred generations.

无论何时发生的，它必然是一个心理和社会的分水岭。有了这一奇妙的新现象作为你存在的核心，你将开始以一种新的、更深刻的方式对自己重要（matter）。你将前所未有地相信自己独特的意义（singular significance）。而且，不仅是你。因为你很快会意识到，你的同类中其他成员也拥有像你一样的有意识的自我。你也会被引导去尊重他们的个人价值。

Whenever it happened, it's bound to have been a psychological and social watershed. With this marvellous new phenomenon at the core of your being, you'll start to _matter_ to yourself in a new and deeper way. You'll come to believe, as never before, in your own _singular significance._ What's more, it will not just be _you_. For you'll soon realise that other members of your species possess conscious selves like yours. You'll be led to respect _their_ individual worth as well.

「我感受，故我在。」「你感受，故你也在。」

'I feel, therefore I am.' 'You feel, therefore you are too.'

在此基础上，你很快会发现，当你通过想象力的跳跃将自己置于同伴的位置时，你可以在你的自我中模拟他们正在感受什么。简言之，现象意识将成为你进入我所谓的「自我社会」（the society of selves）的入场券。

To cap this, you'll soon discover that when, by a leap of imagination you put yourself in your fellow creature's place, you can model, in _your_ self, what _they_ are feeling. In short, phenomenal consciousness will become your ticket to living in what I've called 'the society of selves'.

对大脑来说是一小步，对心智来说却是一大飞跃。

One small step for the brain, one giant leap for the mind.

如果这些想法是正确的，它们对现象意识——即感知力（sentience）——在动物界的分布有着不可回避的启示。那么，我们如何判断一个动物物种是否越过了那条线？

These ideas, if right, have inescapable implications for the distribution of phenomenal consciousness – which is to say, sentience – across the animal kingdom. So, how can we tell whether an animal species has crossed the line?

有两个关键的考量因素。这将取决于动物拥有的脑的类型，以及它过的生活类型。

There are two crucial considerations. It's going to depend on the kind of brain the animal has, and the kind of life it leads.

首先，除非动物拥有一个能够在感觉-运动振荡环路基础上创造我们所识别的那种吸引子的大脑，否则就不存在产生现象体验的生理手段（physiological means）。其次，除非动物拥有一种生活方式，在其中拥有现象丰富的自我意识可以提升其个人和社会生存能力，否则其祖先就没有进化激励（evolutionary incentive）去获得这样的大脑。

First, there will be no _physiological means_ for generating phenomenal experience unless the animal has a brain that, building on reverberatory sensory-motor loops, can create attractors of the kind we've identified. Second, there can have been no _evolutionary incentive_ for the animal's ancestors to acquire such a brain unless it has a _lifestyle_ in which possession of a phenomenally enriched sense of self can enhance its personal and social survival.

综合考虑这些因素，我得出了一个令人惊讶——且可能不受欢迎——的结论。我认为感知力一定是一种相当晚近的进化创新。地球上的绝大多数动物既没有产生它的脑，也没有使用它的需要。让我大胆地说得更具体一些：我怀疑感知力可能直到温血动物——哺乳动物和鸟类——进化出来才出现，大约在两亿年前。

Taking all this into account, I'm led to a surprising – and possibly unwelcome – conclusion. I believe sentience must be a rather recent evolutionary innovation. By far the majority of animals on Earth have neither the brains nor the use for it. To stick my neck out, I'll be more specific: I suspect that sentience may not have arrived until the evolution of warm-blooded animals, mammals and birds, around 200 million years ago.

我在那里划线有两个原因。温血性的发明对动物的生活方式和大脑都产生了重大影响。一方面，变温血使动物相对独立于环境条件，赋予它们新的行为和心理自主性程度。这意味着它们会有更大的动力将自己和同类视为拥有自我和心智的个体。另一方面，变温血会对大脑活动产生巨大影响。因为神经细胞的传导速度随温度升高而增加，大脑中神经细胞的速度将增加一倍以上。我认为这可能对建立激发吸引子状态所需的反馈产生了决定性的作用。

I draw the line there for two reasons. The invention of warm-bloodedness had major effects both on animals' lifestyles and on their brains. On the one hand, becoming warm-blooded will have made animals relatively independent of environmental conditions, giving them a new degree of behavioural and psychological autonomy. This means they will have had more of an incentive to think of themselves and others of their kind as individuals with selves and minds of their own. On the other hand, becoming warm-blooded will have had a dramatic effect on brain activity. Because the conduction speed of nerve cells increases with temperature, the speed of nerve cells in the brain will have more than doubled. I believe this could have made all the difference to establishing the feedback required to fire up the attractor states.

黑猩猩、狗、鹦鹉，我们可以确定。龙虾、蜥蜴、青蛙，我们可以排除。

Chimpanzees, dogs, parrots we can be sure of. Lobsters, lizards, frogs we can rule out.

将这些想法引入动物行为学领域，我考察了一系列可能适用的诊断标准。动物是否：

Bringing these ideas into the field of animal behaviour, I've looked at a range of diagnostic criteria that might apply. Does the animal:

拥有以感觉为中心的稳固自我意识？从事自我愉悦活动——无论是听音乐还是自慰？具有「我」和「你」的概念？将自己的身份认同感向前延续？将自我归因于他者？借出自己的心智以理解他人的感受？

Have a robust sense of self, centred on sensations?
Engage in self-pleasuring activities – be it listening to music or masturbation?
Have notions of 'I' and 'you'?
Carry their sense of their own identity forward?
Attribute selfhood to others?
Lend out their minds so as to understand others' feelings?

总的来说，这些测试证实了我的直觉：只有哺乳动物和鸟类达标。黑猩猩、狗、鹦鹉，我们可以确定。龙虾、蜥蜴、青蛙，我们可以排除。

Broadly, these tests confirm my hunch that it's only mammals and birds who make the cut. Chimpanzees, dogs, parrots we can be sure of. Lobsters, lizards, frogs we can rule out.

章鱼呢？它们是大家最喜欢的有感知力的候选物种。但行为证据并不支持这一点。表面上看，章鱼不因感觉本身而寻求感觉的乐趣；它们没有作为个体的强烈自我意识；它们不将自我归因于他者；它们也不在乎。

Octopuses? They are everybody's favourite candidate for an outlying species that is sentient. But the behavioural evidence belies this. On the face of it, octopuses don't find pleasure in sensation-seeking for sensation's sake; they don't have a strong sense of themselves as individuals; they don't attribute selfhood to others; nor do they care.

人造机器呢？当然，已经存在能够以自己的水平看、听和闻的机器。但就像龙虾和青蛙一样，这大概是盲视的看、盲视的听、盲视的闻。鉴于无感知力动物和机器被设计来完成的生活任务，我们可以假设现象性的盲目并没有让它们更差。

What about man-made machines? There are, of course, already machines in existence that see and hear and smell at their own level. But, as with lobsters and frogs, it's presumably blind-seeing, blind-hearing, blind-smelling. Given the life-tasks that nonsentient animals and machines have been designed to accomplish, we can assume that phenomenal blindness leaves them none the worse off.

尽管如此，让我们假设在不远的将来，人类工程师想要建造机器人来执行一项感知力和自我真的可能发挥作用的任务：即在一个充满其他现象性自我的世界中作为有意义的个体生存下去。那么我可以想象，有朝一日工程师们可以从自然之书中借鉴一页，通过复制人类中负责产生现象意识的专门大脑电路，将感知力建造到机器中。

Nevertheless, let's suppose that, not so far in the future, human engineers were to want to build robots to undertake a task where sentience and selfhood really might play a role: namely, to survive as significant individuals in a world of other phenomenal selves. Then I can imagine that one day the engineers could in fact take a leaf from Nature's book and, by duplicating the specialised brain circuits responsible for phenomenal consciousness in humans, build sentience into a machine.

感知力是否已经在地外生物中独立进化出来了？遥远的宇宙中无疑已经存在许多拥有发达感官器官的生命形式。但肯定地说，它们中的大多数，就像在地球上一样，可能仍然处于现象性盲目状态。会不会它们全部都是如此？在无限的宇宙中任何事都有可能。但这并不意味着感知力——如我们地球人所知的那种——一定在别处也进化出来了。

Could it be that sentience has already evolved, independently, in extraterrestrial beings? The distant Universe no doubt already contains many life forms with well-developed sense organs. But surely _most_ of them, as here on Earth, are likely to have remained phenomenally blind. Could that be _all_ of them? Anything is possible in an infinite universe. But that doesn't mean that sentience, as we Earthlings know it, _must_ have evolved elsewhere.

宇航员弗兰克·博尔曼（Frank Borman）从阿波罗8号的舷窗向外望去时评论道：「地球是整个宇宙中唯一有颜色的东西。」严格来说这不可能是真的。但地球可能是唯一一个存在能够像我们人类——以及狗和鸽子——那样体验颜色感受质的生物的地方：在这里看是「像某种东西」，像某种东西（something），像这样（this）地去看见。

The astronaut Frank Borman, looking from the window of Apollo 8, remarked that 'the Earth was the only thing in the entire Universe that had any colour'. That can't, strictly, be true. But it could still be true that Earth is the only place where beings are capable of experiencing colour qualia in the way we humans – and dogs and pigeons – do: where it's 'like something', like _something,_ like _this_ to see.
