两位钟表匠

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年5月24日·2分钟阅读

在一个以信任为通货、以沉默为法则的国度里，两位钟表匠——阿尔德里克（Aldric）和布莱思（Blythe）——为皇帝打造精密的钟表。他们的手艺堪称传奇，竞争也异常激烈。每个人都认为自己才配得上「皇家钟表大师」的头衔。

In a country where trust was currency and silence was law, two watchmakers — Aldric and Blythe — built intricate timepieces for the Emperor. Their craft was legendary, their rivalry fierce. Each believed they alone deserved the title of Master Horologist.

一个秋日的傍晚，皇帝最珍爱的精密计时器从金库中不翼而飞。卫兵从床上将两位钟表匠一并捉拿。他们被分别押往不同的塔楼——彼此看不见、听不着，也无法传递任何消息——然后被告知了同样一番话。

One autumn evening, the Emperor's most treasured chronograph vanished from the vault. Guards seized both watchmakers from their beds. They were dragged to separate towers — out of sight, out of earshot, beyond the reach of any message — and told the same thing.

「我们知道你们中有一人偷了计时器。也许两人都参与了，也许只有一个。条件如下：如果你坦白而你的同伴保持沉默，你将获得自由并被授予「皇家钟表大师」的头衔，而沉默者将在黑曜石矿场服刑二十年。如果你们都坦白，各服刑五年。如果你们都保持沉默，我们将以玩忽职守的轻罪关押你们一年，然后释放。」

"We know one of you stole the chronograph. Perhaps both of you did, perhaps only one. Here is the offer. If you confess and your partner remains silent, you go free and receive the title of Master Horologist, while the silent one serves twenty years in the obsidian mines. If you both confess, you each serve five years. If you both remain silent, we will hold you for one year on a lesser charge of negligence, and then release you."

阿尔德里克独自坐在冰冷的塔楼里，仔细地盘算着。「如果布莱思保持沉默，那我应该坦白——我可以获得自由，而不必服刑一年。如果布莱思坦白了，那我也应该坦白——五年总比二十年好。无论哪种情况，坦白都是更明智的选择。」他的逻辑天衣无缝、无懈可击。

Aldric sat alone in his cold tower, reasoning carefully. "If Blythe stays silent, then I should confess — I go free instead of serving one year. If Blythe confesses, then I should also confess — five years is better than twenty. Either way, confessing is the wiser choice." His logic was airtight, flawless, impeccable.

在另一座塔楼里，布莱思背靠着石墙，以同样冷静的精密逻辑盘算着。「如果阿尔德里克保持沉默，我应该坦白——自由胜过一年刑期。如果阿尔德里克坦白了，我也应该坦白——五年好过二十年。无论他怎么选，坦白都对我最有利。」她的逻辑同样天衣无缝、同样无懈可击、同样完美无缺。

In the other tower, Blythe sat with her back against the stone wall, reasoning with the same cold precision. "If Aldric stays silent, I should confess — freedom is better than one year. If Aldric confesses, I should confess too — five years beats twenty. No matter what he does, confessing serves me best." Her logic was equally airtight, equally flawless, equally impeccable.

两人都坦白了。两人都在黑曜石矿场服了五年苦役。而那枚计时器，从头到尾不过是一个粗心的仆人放错了地方——根本没有人偷。

Both confessed. Both served five years in the obsidian mines. And the chronograph had been misplaced by a careless servant all along — stolen by no one.

在各自的牢房里，每个人都做出了理性的选择。每个人都沿着逻辑走向了必然的结论。然而，他们共同抵达的结局，却比本可以通过互相信任、保持沉默而获得的那个更糟糕。在孤立中追求理性，将他们引离了他们本可以共同找到的最优答案。两座塔楼没有一扇窗户朝向彼此，而这才是最残酷的建筑设计。

From their separate cells, each had made the rational choice. Each had followed reason to its inevitable conclusion. Yet together, they had arrived at an outcome worse than the one they could have reached had they trusted each other and stayed silent. Rationality, pursued in isolation, had led them away from the best answer they could have found together. The towers had no windows facing each other, and that was the cruelest architecture of all.

这就是博弈论家们所称的「囚徒困境」（Prisoner's Dilemma）。两方无法沟通，各自面临合作与背叛之间的抉择。无论对方如何选择，背叛总能带来更好的个人结果——这就是所谓的「占优策略」（dominant strategy）。然而，当双方都遵循这一逻辑时，他们各自得到的结果却比双方都合作时更糟。这个困境揭示了人类事务中一种深刻的张力：个体的理性并不总能通向集体的福祉。它浮现于军备竞赛、价格战、环境退化之中，以及任何信任缺席、合作的代价是即时的而收益却是共享的处境之中。钟表匠的塔楼当然是隐喻——但矿场却是足够真实的。

This is the structure that game theorists call the Prisoner's Dilemma. Two parties, unable to communicate, each face a choice between cooperation and defection. Regardless of what the other does, defecting always yields a better individual outcome — it is the "dominant strategy." Yet when both follow this logic, they arrive at a result worse for each of them than if both had cooperated. The dilemma reveals a deep tension in human affairs: individual rationality does not always lead to collective well-being. It surfaces in arms races, price wars, environmental degradation, and any situation where trust is absent and the costs of cooperation are immediate while the rewards are shared. The watchmakers' towers, of course, are metaphor — but the mines are real enough.
