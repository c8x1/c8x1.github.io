意识的「难问题」不过是对真正问题的干扰

Anil Seth·Aeon·2026年5月27日·12分钟阅读·原文

理解意识的最佳方式是什么？在哲学领域，围绕宇宙是否如勒内·笛卡尔（René Descartes）所言被划分为「心灵之物」与「物质之物」的争论，已持续数百年且至今未歇。但现代神经科学的兴起让一种更务实的路径逐渐占据主流：这种路径以哲学为指导，却不依赖哲学研究来提供答案。其关键在于认识到，要揭示意识的物质基础——开始从主观的、现象的层面通往客观的、可测量的层面搭建解释性的桥梁——并不需要先解释「意识为何存在」。

What is the best way to understand consciousness? In philosophy, centuries-old debates continue to rage over whether the Universe is divided, following René Descartes, into 'mind stuff' and 'matter stuff'. But the rise of modern neuroscience has seen a more pragmatic approach gain ground: an approach that is guided by philosophy but doesn't rely on philosophical research to provide the answers. Its key is to recognise that explaining _why consciousness exists at all_ is not necessary in order to make progress in revealing its material basis – to start building explanatory bridges from the subjective and phenomenal to the objective and measurable.

我在布莱顿的萨塞克斯大学（University of Sussex）意识科学中心（Sussex Centre for Consciousness Science）工作，与认知科学家、神经科学家、精神科医生、脑成像专家、虚拟现实技术高手和数学家——当然还有哲学家——合作，致力于实现这一目标。与其他实验室一道，我们正在获得关于意识的令人振奋的新见解——这些见解正在医学领域产生实实在在的影响，并反过来提出新的智识和伦理挑战。在我自己的研究中，一幅新的图景正在成形：意识经验被视为深深植根于大脑与身体如何协同工作以维持生理完整性——即维持生存——的过程之中。按照这个叙事，我们是有意识的「野兽机器」，我希望向你解释为什么。

In my work at the Sussex Centre for Consciousness Science at the University of Sussex in Brighton, I collaborate with cognitive scientists, neuroscientists, psychiatrists, brain imagers, virtual reality wizards and mathematicians – and philosophers too – trying to do just this. And together with other laboratories, we are gaining exciting new insights into consciousness – insights that are making real differences in medicine, and that in turn raise new intellectual and ethical challenges. In my own research, a new picture is taking shape in which conscious experience is seen as deeply grounded in how brains and bodies work together to maintain physiological integrity – to stay alive. In this story, we are conscious 'beast-machines', and I hope to show you why.

让我们从大卫·查尔默斯（David Chalmers）继承自笛卡尔的有影响力的区分开始：「简单问题」与「难问题」。「简单问题」是理解大脑（和身体）如何产生感知、认知、学习和行为。「难问题」则是理解为什么以及如何这一切会与意识相关联：为什么我们不只是机器人，或哲学意义上的僵尸，而没有任何内在的宇宙？人们很容易认为，解决简单问题（无论这意味着什么）对解决难问题毫无帮助，让意识的脑基础仍是一个完全的谜。

Let's begin with David Chalmers's influential distinction, inherited from Descartes, between the 'easy problem' and the 'hard problem'. The 'easy problem' is to understand how the brain (and body) gives rise to perception, cognition, learning and behaviour. The 'hard' problem is to understand why and how any of this should be associated with consciousness at all: why aren't we just robots, or philosophical zombies, without any inner universe? It's tempting to think that solving the easy problem (whatever this might mean) would get us nowhere in solving the hard problem, leaving the brain basis of consciousness a total mystery.

但还有另一种选择，我称之为「真正的问题」：如何用生物机制来解释意识的各种属性；既不假装它不存在（简单问题），也不过分执着于解释它为何首先存在（难问题）。（熟悉「神经现象学」的人会看到这种表述与其有一些相似之处——但也有差异，后文将会看到。）

But there is an alternative, which I like to call the _real problem_: how to account for the various properties of consciousness in terms of biological mechanisms; without pretending it doesn't exist (easy problem) and without worrying too much about explaining its existence in the first place (hard problem). (People familiar with 'neurophenomenology' will see some similarities with this way of putting things – but there are differences too, as we will see.)

这种方法有历史的先例，例如在生命研究中。曾经，生化学家怀疑生物机制能否解释「活着」这一属性。如今，尽管我们的理解仍不完整，但这种最初的神秘感已在很大程度上消散了。生物学家只是踏踏实实地致力于用底层机制解释生命系统的各种属性：代谢、稳态、繁殖等等。一个重要的启示是，生命并非「单一的事物」——相反，它有许多可能分离的面向。

There are some historical parallels for this approach, for example in the study of life. Once, biochemists doubted that biological mechanisms could ever explain the property of being alive. Today, although our understanding remains incomplete, this initial sense of mystery has largely dissolved. Biologists have simply gotten on with the business of explaining the various properties of living systems in terms of underlying mechanisms: metabolism, homeostasis, reproduction and so on. An important lesson here is that life is not 'one thing' – rather, it has many potentially separable aspects.

同理，解决意识的真正问题，需要区分意识的不同面向，并将其现象学属性（对意识经验是什么样的主观的、第一人称的描述）映射到底层生物机制（客观的、第三人称的描述）。一个好的出发点是区分意识「水平」、意识「内容」和意识「自我」。意识水平关乎是否具有意识——处于无梦睡眠（或全身麻醉下）与清醒而敏锐地觉知之间的区别。意识内容是当你有意识时填充你意识经验的东西——构成你内在宇宙的视觉、声音、气味、情感、思想和信念。而在这些意识内容之中，有一个特定的「成为你」的体验。这就是意识自我，可能也是我们最为紧紧依恋的意识面向。

In the same way, tackling the real problem of consciousness depends on distinguishing different aspects of consciousness, and mapping their phenomenological properties (subjective first-person descriptions of what conscious experiences are like) onto underlying biological mechanisms (objective third-person descriptions). A good starting point is to distinguish between conscious _level_, conscious _content_, and conscious _self_. Conscious level has to do with being conscious at all – the difference between being in a dreamless sleep (or under general anaesthesia) and being vividly awake and aware. Conscious contents are what populate your conscious experiences when you are conscious – the sights, sounds, smells, emotions, thoughts and beliefs that make up your inner universe. And among these conscious contents is the specific experience of _being you_. This is conscious self, and is probably the aspect of consciousness that we cling to most tightly.

支撑我们拥有意识能力的根本脑机制是什么？重要的是，意识水平并不等同于清醒。当你做梦时，虽然睡着了，却仍有意识经验。而在某些病理情况下，如植物人状态（有时被称为「清醒的无觉知」），你可能完全没有意识，但仍然经历睡眠和清醒的循环。

What are the fundamental brain mechanisms that underlie our ability to be conscious at all? Importantly, conscious level is not the same as wakefulness. When you dream, you have conscious experiences even though you're asleep. And in some pathological cases, such as the vegetative state (sometimes called 'wakeful unawareness'), you can be altogether without consciousness, but still go through cycles of sleep and waking.

那么，究竟是什么支撑着「有意识」——而不仅仅是「醒着」？我们知道这不仅仅取决于神经元的数量。小脑（挂在大脑皮层后方的所谓「小脑」）拥有的神经元数量约为大脑其余部分的四倍，但在维持意识水平方面似乎几乎没有参与。它甚至不是神经活动的整体水平——在无梦睡眠期间，你的大脑几乎与清醒意识状态下一样活跃。相反，意识似乎取决于大脑不同部分以特定方式相互「对话」。

So what underlies being conscious specifically, as opposed to just being awake? We know it's not just the number of neurons involved. The cerebellum (the so-called 'little brain' hanging off the back of the cortex) has about four times as many neurons as the rest of the brain, but seems barely involved in maintaining conscious level. It's not even the overall level of neural activity – your brain is almost as active during dreamless sleep as it is during conscious wakefulness. Rather, consciousness seems to depend on how different parts of the brain speak to each other, in specific ways.

米兰大学（University of Milan）的神经科学家马塞洛·马西米尼（Marcello Massimini）的一系列研究为这一观点提供了有力的证据。在这些研究中，大脑受到短暂能量脉冲的刺激——使用一种叫做经颅磁刺激（transcranial magnetic stimulation, TMS）的技术——并使用脑电图（EEG）记录其电「回波」。在无梦睡眠和全身麻醉下，这些回波非常简单，就像向静水中投掷石块所产生的波浪。但在意识状态下，典型的回波在皮层表面广泛传播，以复杂的模式消失又重现。令人兴奋的是，我们现在可以通过计算这些回波的「可压缩性」来量化其复杂性，类似于简单的算法将数字照片压缩为 JPEG 文件的方式。这项能力代表了我们朝着制造「意识计」迈出了第一步——它既具有实用价值，又有理论依据。

A series of studies by the neuroscientist Marcello Massimini at the University of Milan provides powerful evidence for this view. In these studies, the brain is stimulated by brief pulses of energy – using a technique called transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS) – and its electrical 'echoes' are recorded using EEG. In dreamless sleep and general anaesthesia, these echoes are very simple, like the waves generated by throwing a stone into still water. But during conscious states, a typical echo ranges widely over the cortical surface, disappearing and reappearing in complex patterns. Excitingly, we can now quantify the complexity of these echoes by working out how compressible they are, similar to how simple algorithms compress digital photos into JPEG files. The ability to do this represents a first step towards a 'consciousness-meter' that is both practically useful and theoretically motivated.

意识的复杂性测量方法已被用于追踪睡眠和麻醉状态下觉知水平的变化。它们甚至可以用于检测脑损伤后是否仍有意识残留——在这种情况下，仅依据患者行为做出的诊断有时会产生误导。在萨塞克斯意识科学中心，我们正在通过基于自发神经活动——大脑持续的「回声」——来计算「脑复杂性」，以提高这些测量方法的实用性，而无需进行脑刺激。其前景在于，能够「测量」意识、量化它的来去，将如同我们对热的物理理解（即平均分子动能）依赖于18世纪第一批可靠温度计的发展一样，彻底改变我们的科学认知。开尔文勋爵（Lord Kelvin）这样说道：「在物理科学中，学习任何学科的第一步，就是找到数值计算的原理和测量与之相关的某种性质的可行方法。」更简单地说：「能测量才能认知。」

Complexity measures of consciousness have already been used to track changing levels of awareness across states of sleep and anaesthesia. They can even be used to check for any persistence of consciousness following brain injury, where diagnoses based on a patient's behaviour are sometimes misleading. At the Sussex Centre, we are working to improve the practicality of these measures by computing 'brain complexity' on the basis of spontaneous neural activity – the brain's ongoing 'echo' – without the need for brain stimulation. The promise is that the ability to _measure_ consciousness, to quantify its comings and goings, will transform our scientific understanding in the same way that our physical understanding of heat (as average molecular kinetic energy) depended on the development, in the 18th century, of the first reliable thermometers. Lord Kelvin put it this way: 'In physical science the first essential step in the direction of learning any subject is to find principles of numerical reckoning and practicable methods for measuring some quality connected with it.' More simply: 'To measure is to know.'

但脑复杂性测量所测量的「性质」究竟是什么？这就涉及到关于意识的新理论思想了。这些思想始于1990年代末，当时杰拉尔德·埃德尔曼（Gerald Edelman）——我在圣迭戈神经科学研究所（Neurosciences Institute in San Diego）的前导师——和朱利奥·托诺尼（Giulio Tononi）——现在在麦迪逊的威斯康星大学（University of Wisconsin in Madison）——提出，意识经验的独特之处在于同时具有高度的「信息性」和高度的「整合性」。

But what is the 'quality' that brain-complexity measures are measuring? This is where new theoretical ideas about consciousness come into play. These start in the late 1990s, when Gerald Edelman (my former mentor at the Neurosciences Institute in San Diego) and Giulio Tononi – now at the University of Wisconsin in Madison – argued that conscious experiences were unique in being simultaneously highly 'informative' and highly 'integrated'.

捕捉信息与整合共存的数学框架，正好映射到新兴的脑复杂性测量方法上。

The maths that captures the co-existence of information and integration maps onto the emerging measures of brain complexity.

意识具有「信息性」，是指每一次经验都与你曾有过或可能有的每一次其他经验不同。越过面前的书桌望向窗外的远处，我以前从未精确地经历过咖啡杯、电脑和云彩的这特定组合——当它与同时呈现的所有其他感知、情感和思想结合时，这种经验就更加独特了。每一次意识经验都涉及对不确定性的极大缩减——在任何时刻，我们都从众多可能的经验中经历其中之一——而不确定性的缩减正是数学上我们所说的「信息」。

Consciousness is _informative_ in the sense that every experience is different from every other experience you have ever had, or ever could have. Looking past the desk in front of me through the window beyond, I have never before experienced precisely _this_ configuration of coffee cups, computers and clouds – an experience that is even more distinctive when combined with all the other perceptions, emotions and thoughts simultaneously present. Every conscious experience involves a very large reduction of uncertainty – at any time, we have one experience out of vastly many possible experiences – and reduction of uncertainty is what mathematically we mean by 'information'.

意识具有「整合性」，是指每一次意识经验都呈现为一个统一的场景。我们不会将颜色与形状分开体验，也不会将物体与背景独立感知。我此刻意识经验的众多不同元素——电脑和咖啡杯，以及巴赫柔和的旋律和关于接下来写什么的忧虑——似乎以一种深刻的方式联系在一起，作为一个单一包容的意识状态的各个方面。

Consciousness is _integrated_ in the sense that every conscious experience appears as a unified scene. We do not experience colours separately from their shapes, nor objects independently of their background. The many different elements of my conscious experience right now – computers and coffee cups, as well as the gentle sounds of Bach and my worries about what to write next – seem tied together in a deep way, as aspects of a single encompassing state of consciousness.

事实证明，捕捉这种信息与整合共存的数学框架，正好映射到我上文描述的新兴脑复杂性测量方法上。这并非巧合——这是「真正的问题」策略的应用。我们正在从主观经验的层面对意识进行描述，并将其映射到脑机制的客观描述。

It turns out that the maths that captures this co-existence of information and integration maps onto the emerging measures of brain complexity I described above. This is no accident – it is an application of the 'real problem' strategy. We're taking a description of consciousness at the level of subjective experience, and mapping it to objective descriptions of brain mechanisms.

一些研究者将这些思想推得更远，试图直面难问题本身。开创这一路径的托诺尼认为，意识就是整合信息。这是一个引人入胜且有力的提议，但代价是必须承认意识可能存在于任何地方、任何事物之中——这是一种被称为泛心论（panpsychism）的哲学观点。所需的额外数学扭曲也意味着，在实践中，整合信息对任何真实的复杂系统都变得无法测量。这是一个富有教益的例子，说明针对难问题而非真正问题会如何减缓甚至阻止实验进展。

Some researchers take these ideas much further, to grapple with the hard problem itself. Tononi, who pioneered this approach, argues that consciousness simply _is_ integrated information. This is an intriguing and powerful proposal, but it comes at the cost of admitting that consciousness could be present everywhere and in everything, a philosophical view known as panpsychism. The additional mathematical contortions needed also mean that, in practice, integrated information becomes impossible to measure for any real complex system. This is an instructive example of how targeting the hard problem, rather than the real problem, can slow down or even stop experimental progress.

当我们有意识时，我们总是「意识到了」某些东西。大脑中是什么决定了意识的内容？对这个问题的标准方法是寻找所谓的「意识的神经相关物」（neural correlates of consciousness, NCCs）。在1990年代，弗朗西斯·克里克（Francis Crick）和克里斯托夫·科赫（Christof Koch）将NCC定义为「共同足以产生特定意识感知的最小神经元事件和机制集合」。这一定义在过去四分之一世纪中发挥了很好的作用，因为它直接导向了实验。我们可以比较有意识感知与无意识感知，寻找脑活动的差异，使用（例如）脑电图和功能性磁共振成像。有许多方法可以做到这一点。最流行的一种是「双眼竞争」（binocular rivalry），即将不同的图像分别呈现给每只眼睛，使意识感知在两者之间翻转（而感觉输入保持不变）。另一种是「遮蔽」（masking），即一个短暂闪现的图像之后紧随一个无意义图案。在这里，第一个图像是否被有意识地感知，取决于图像与遮蔽物之间的时间间隔。

When we are conscious, we are conscious _of something._ What in the brain determines the contents of consciousness? The standard approach to this question has been to look for so-called 'neural correlates of consciousness' (NCCs). In the 1990s, Francis Crick and Christof Koch defined an NCC as 'the minimal set of neuronal events and mechanisms jointly sufficient for a specific conscious percept'. This definition has served very well over the past quarter century because it leads directly to experiments. We can compare conscious perception with unconscious perception and look for the difference in brain activity, using (for example) EEG and functional MRI. There are many ways of doing this. One of the most popular is _binocular rivalry_, in which different images are presented to each eye so that conscious perception flips from one to the other (while sensory input remains constant). Another is _masking_, in which a briefly flashed image is rapidly followed by a meaningless pattern. Here, whether the first image is consciously perceived depends on the delay between the image and the mask.

诸如此类的实验已经识别出与意识感知一致相关的大脑区域，且不受该感知是视觉、听觉还是其他感觉模式的影响。这个故事的最新篇章涉及一些实验，试图区分参与「报告」意识感知（例如说：「我看到一张脸！」）的脑区域与参与「生成」意识感知本身的脑区域。但尽管这些实验很有力，它们并没有真正解决意识的「真正」问题。说大脑后部皮层的某个「热点」（例如）在意识感知期间被可靠激活，并不能解释为什么该区域的活动应该与意识相关。为此，我们需要一种描述大脑「做什么」而不只是「在哪里做」的一般感知理论。

Experiments such as these have identified brain regions that are consistently associated with conscious perception, independently of whether that perception is visual, auditory or in some other sensory modality. The most recent chapter in this story involves experiments that try to distinguish between those brain regions involved in _reporting about_ a conscious percept (eg, saying: 'I see a face!') from those involved in _generating_ the conscious percept itself. But as powerful as these experiments are, they do not really address the 'real' problem of consciousness. To say that a posterior cortical 'hot-spot' (for instance) is reliably activated during conscious perception does not explain _why_ activity in that region should be associated with consciousness. For this, we need a general theory of perception that describes what brains do, not just where they do it.

在19世纪，德国博学者赫尔曼·冯·亥姆霍兹（Hermann von Helmholtz）提出，大脑是一台「预测机器」，我们所看到的、听到的和感受到的，不过是大脑对其感觉输入原因的最佳猜测。可以这样想：大脑被锁在坚硬的头骨里。它所接收的只是模棱两可且有噪声的感觉信号，这些信号与世界中的物体只是间接相关。因此，感知必须是一个推断过程，其中不确定的感觉信号与关于世界样子的先前期望或「信念」相结合，形成大脑对这些感觉信号原因的最优假设——咖啡杯、电脑和云彩。我们所看到的，就是大脑对「外面有什么」的「最佳猜测」。

In the 19th century, the German polymath Hermann von Helmholtz proposed that the brain is a _prediction machine_, and that what we see, hear and feel are nothing more than the brain's best guesses about the causes of its sensory inputs. Think of it like this. The brain is locked inside a bony skull. All it receives are ambiguous and noisy sensory signals that are only indirectly related to objects in the world. Perception must therefore be a process of inference, in which indeterminate sensory signals are combined with prior expectations or 'beliefs' about the way the world is, to form the brain's optimal hypotheses of the causes of these sensory signals – of coffee cups, computers and clouds. What we see is the brain's 'best guess' of what's out there.

在实验室和日常生活中，很容易找到预测性感知的例子。在一个雾蒙蒙的早晨出门，如果我们期望在公交站遇到一位朋友，我们可能会觉得她在那里，直到走近一看才发现是个陌生人。如果我们期望听到某些词语，我们也可以在无意义的噪声流中听到它们（把「天堂之梯」倒放，你可以听到撒旦的诗歌）。甚至感知的最基本元素也受到编码在我们视觉系统中的无意识信念的影响。我们的大脑在进化中形成了光线来自上方这一假设（信念），这影响了我们感知阴影中形状的方式。

It's easy to find examples of predictive perception both in the lab and in everyday life. Walking out on a foggy morning, if we expect to meet a friend at a bus stop, we might perceive her to be there, until closer inspection reveals a stranger. We can also hear words in nonsensical streams of noise, if we are expecting these words (play 'Stairway to Heaven' backwards and you can hear satanic poetry). Even very basic elements of perception are shaped by unconscious beliefs encoded in our visual systems. Our brains have evolved to assume (believe) that light comes from above, which influences the way we perceive shapes in shadow.

人们有意识地看到的，是他们所期望的，而非违反他们期望的东西。

People consciously see what they expect, rather than what violates their expectations.

经典的感知观点认为，大脑以自下而上或「由外而内」的方向处理感觉信息：感觉信号通过感受器（例如视网膜）进入，然后逐步深入大脑，每个阶段都招募越来越复杂和抽象的处理。在这种观点中，感知的「繁重工作」由这些自下而上的连接完成。亥姆霍兹的观点颠倒了这一框架，提出从外部世界流入大脑的信号只传递「预测误差」——即大脑所期望的与所接收的之间的差异。感知内容由沿相反方向（自上而下）流动的感知预测所承载，从大脑深处传向感觉表面。感知涉及在大脑感觉系统的多个处理层级上同时最小化预测误差，通过持续更新大脑的预测。在这种通常被称为「预测编码」或「预测处理」的观点中，感知是一种「受控的幻觉」，其中大脑的假设不断被来自世界和身体的传入感觉信号所约束。正如心理学家克里斯·弗里斯（Chris Frith）在《心智的虚构》（Making Up the Mind, 2007）中精妙地表述的那样：「与现实重合的幻想。」

The classical view of perception is that the brain processes sensory information in a bottom-up or 'outside-in' direction: sensory signals enter through receptors (for example, the retina) and then progress deeper into the brain, with each stage recruiting increasingly sophisticated and abstract processing. In this view, the perceptual 'heavy-lifting' is done by these bottom-up connections. The Helmholtzian view inverts this framework, proposing that signals flowing into the brain from the outside world convey only _prediction errors_ – the differences between what the brain expects and what it receives. Perceptual content is carried by perceptual predictions flowing in the opposite (top-down) direction, from deep inside the brain out towards the sensory surfaces. Perception involves the minimisation of prediction error simultaneously across many levels of processing within the brain's sensory systems, by continuously updating the brain's predictions. In this view, which is often called 'predictive coding' or 'predictive processing', perception is a _controlled hallucination_, in which the brain's hypotheses are continually reined in by sensory signals arriving from the world and the body. 'A fantasy that coincides with reality,' as the psychologist Chris Frith eloquently put it in _Making Up the Mind_ (2007).

有了这种感知理论的武装，我们可以回到意识问题。现在，我们不再问哪些脑区域与有意识（相对于无意识）感知相关，而是可以问：预测性感知的哪些方面与意识相伴？许多实验现在表明，意识更多地依赖于感知「预测」而非「预测误差」。2001年，哈佛医学院的阿尔瓦罗·帕斯卡尔-莱昂内（Alvaro Pascual-Leone）和文森特·沃尔什（Vincent Walsh）要求人们报告漂移点云的运动方向（所谓的「随机点运动图」）。他们使用TMS专门中断视觉皮层的自上而下信号传输，发现这消除了对运动的有意识感知，尽管自下而上的信号保持完好。

Armed with this theory of perception, we can return to consciousness. Now, instead of asking which brain regions correlate with conscious (versus unconscious) perception, we can ask: which aspects of predictive perception go along with consciousness? A number of experiments are now indicating that consciousness depends more on perceptual _predictions_, than on _prediction errors_. In 2001, Alvaro Pascual-Leone and Vincent Walsh at Harvard Medical School asked people to report the perceived direction of movement of clouds of drifting dots (so-called 'random dot kinematograms'). They used TMS to specifically interrupt top-down signalling across the visual cortex, and they found that this abolished conscious perception of the motion, even though bottom-up signals were left intact.

最近，在我的实验室中，我们一直在更详细地探究有意识感知的预测机制。在几项实验中——使用前文提到的双眼竞争方法的变体——我们发现人们有意识地看到的是他们所期望的，而非违反他们期望的。我们还发现，大脑在所谓的「阿尔法节律」中的偏好时间点（或相位）施加其感知预测——这是一种频率约10赫兹的脑电图振荡，在视觉脑区尤为突出。这令人兴奋，因为它让我们一窥大脑可能如何实际实现预测性感知，并且因为它为一个众所周知的脑活动现象——阿尔法节律——提供了新的解释，而这个节律的功能长期以来一直是个谜。

More recently, in my lab, we've been probing the predictive mechanisms of conscious perception in more detail. In several experiments – using variants of the binocular rivalry method mentioned earlier – we've found that people consciously see what they expect, rather than what violates their expectations. We've also discovered that the brain imposes its perceptual predictions at preferred points (or phases) within the so-called 'alpha rhythm', which is an oscillation in the EEG signal at about 10 Hz that is especially prominent over the visual areas of the brain. This is exciting because it gives us a glimpse of how the brain might actually implement something like predictive perception, and because it sheds new light on a well-known phenomenon of brain activity, the alpha rhythm, whose function so far has remained elusive.

预测处理还可以帮助我们理解异常的视觉体验，例如伴随精神病或致幻之旅而来的幻觉。基本思路是，当大脑过少关注传入的感觉信号时，就会产生幻觉，使得感知变得异常地被大脑的先前期望所主导。不同类型的幻觉——从简单的几何线条、图案和纹理体验，到充满物体和人物的丰富幻觉叙事——可以通过大脑在皮层层级的不同层级上过度急于确认其预测来解释。这项研究具有重要的临床前景，因为它触及精神疾病症状背后的机制，就像抗生素针对感染的原因起作用，而止痛药则不然。

Predictive processing can also help us understand unusual forms of visual experience, such as the hallucinations that can accompany psychosis or psychedelic trips. The basic idea is that hallucinations occur when the brain pays too little attention to incoming sensory signals, so that perception becomes unusually dominated by the brain's prior expectations. Different sorts of hallucination – from simple geometric experiences of lines, patterns and textures to rich hallucinatory narratives full of objects and people – can be explained by the brain's over-eagerness to confirm its predictions at different levels in the cortical hierarchy. This research has significant clinical promise since it gets at the mechanisms that underlie the symptoms of psychiatric conditions, in much the same way that antibiotics tackle the causes of infection while painkillers do not.

在我们内在宇宙的众多独特体验中，有一种非常特别。这就是「成为你」的体验。人们很容易将自我体验视为理所当然，因为它们似乎总是在场，而且我们通常在主观存在中感受到一种连续性（当然，从全身麻醉中醒来时除外）。但正如意识不只是一回事，意识自我也最好被理解为大脑生成的一种复杂建构。

Of the many distinctive experiences within our inner universes, one is very special. This is the experience of _being you_. It's tempting to take experiences of selfhood for granted, since they always seem to be present, and we usually feel a sense of continuity in our subjective existence (except, of course, when emerging from general anaesthesia). But just as consciousness is not just one thing, conscious selfhood is also best understood as a complex construction generated by the brain.

有「身体」自我，即作为一个身体和拥有一个特定身体的体验。有「视角」自我，即从特定第一人称视角感知世界的体验。「意志」自我涉及意图和能动性的体验——做这个或那个的冲动，以及成为事物发生原因的感觉。在更高层次上，我们遇到「叙事」自我和「社会」自我。叙事自我是「我」的所在，即作为一个跨越时间的连续而独特的人的体验，建立在丰富的自传体记忆之上。而社会自我是自我体验中通过他人的心灵折射出来的那个面向，由我们独特的社会环境所塑造。

There is the _bodily_ self, which is the experience of being a body and of having a particular body. There is the _perspectival_ self, which is the experience of perceiving the world from a particular first-person point of view. The _volitional_ self involves experiences of intention and of agency – of urges to do this or that, and of being the causes of things that happen. At higher levels, we encounter _narrative_ and _social_ selves. The narrative self is where the 'I' comes in, as the experience of being a continuous and distinctive person over time, built from a rich set of autobiographical memories. And the social self is that aspect of self-experience that is refracted through the perceived minds of others, shaped by our unique social milieu.

在日常生活中，很难区分自我性的这些维度。我们以看似统一的整体在世界中穿行，身体自我体验与过去的记忆、意志和能动性的体验无缝地整合在一起。但内省可能是一个糟糕的向导。许多实验和神经心理学的案例研究讲述了一个不同的故事——大脑在积极地、持续地生成和协调这些多样化的自我体验面向。

In daily life, it can be hard to differentiate these dimensions of selfhood. We move through the world as seemingly unified wholes, our experience of bodily self seamlessly integrated with our memories from the past, and with our experiences of volition and agency. But introspection can be a poor guide. Many experiments and neuropsychological case studies tell a different story, one in which the brain actively and continuously generates and coordinates these diverse aspects of self-experience.

我们「作为」和「拥有」一个身体的体验，是一种非常独特的「受控幻觉」。

Our experiences of being and having a body are 'controlled hallucinations' of a very distinctive kind.

让我们以身体自我为例。在著名的「橡胶手错觉」中，我让你把注意力放在一只假手上，而你的真手被隐藏起来。如果我同时用软画笔轻刷你的真手和假手，你可能会产生一种奇异的感觉——那只假手现在不知怎的成了你身体的一部分。这揭示了我们体验「拥有」自己身体的方式具有惊人的灵活性，也提出了一个问题：大脑如何决定世界的哪些部分是它的身体，哪些不是？

Let's take the example of bodily selfhood. In the famous 'rubber-hand illusion', I ask you to focus your attention on a fake hand while your real hand is kept out of sight. If I then simultaneously stroke your real hand and the fake hand with a soft paintbrush, you may develop the uncanny feeling that the fake hand is now, somehow, part of your body. This reveals a surprising flexibility in how we experience 'owning' our bodies and raises a question: how does the brain decide which parts of the world are its body, and which aren't?

为了回答这个问题，我们可以诉诸与其他感知形式相同的过程。大脑基于其先前信念或期望以及可用的感觉数据，做出其「最佳猜测」。在这种情况下，相关的数据包括身体特有的信号，以及视觉和触觉等经典感官。这些身体感官包括「本体感觉」（proprioception），它传递身体在空间中的构型信息；以及「内感受」（interoception），它涉及一系列传递来自身体内部信息的输入，如血压、胃张力、心跳等。具身自我体验依赖于关于跨内感受和本体感觉通道以及经典感官的感觉信号之身体相关原因的预测。我们「作为」和「拥有」一个身体的体验，是一种非常独特的「受控幻觉」。

To answer this, we can appeal to the same process that underlies other forms of perception. The brain makes its 'best guess', based on its prior beliefs or expectations, and the available sensory data. In this case, the relevant sensory data include signals specific to the body, as well as the classic senses such as vision and touch. These bodily senses include _proprioception,_ which signals the body's configuration in space, and _interoception_, which involves a raft of inputs that convey information from inside the body, such as blood pressure, gastric tension, heartbeat and so on. The experience of embodied selfhood depends on predictions about body-related causes of sensory signals across interoceptive and proprioceptive channels, as well as across the classic senses. Our experiences of being and having a body are 'controlled hallucinations' of a very distinctive kind.

我们实验室的研究正在支持这一观点。在一项实验中，我们使用所谓的增强现实技术开发了一种新版本的橡胶手错觉，旨在研究内感受信号对身体拥有感的影响。参与者通过头戴式显示器观察周围环境，聚焦于他们虚拟现实版本的手，这只手出现在他们面前。这只虚拟手被编程为轻柔地闪红光，要么与他们的心跳同步，要么不同步。我们预测，当虚拟手与心跳同步脉动时，人们会体验到与虚拟手更强的认同感——而这正是我们发现的。其他实验室发现类似的原则也适用于意识自我的其他方面。例如，当传入的感觉数据与行动的预期后果匹配时，我们会体验到对事件的能动性——而能动性体验的崩溃（可能发生在精神分裂症等疾病中）可以追溯到这种预测过程的异常。

Research in our lab is supporting this idea. In one experiment, we used so-called augmented reality to develop a new version of the rubber-hand illusion, designed to examine the effects of interoceptive signals on body ownership. Participants viewed their surroundings through a head-mounted display, focusing on a virtual reality version of their hand, which appeared in front of them. This virtual hand was programmed to flash gently red, either in time or out of time with their heartbeat. We predicted that people would experience a greater sense of identity with the virtual hand when it was pulsing synchronously with their heartbeat, and this is just what we found. Other laboratories are finding that similar principles apply to other aspects of conscious self. For example, we experience agency over events when incoming sensory data match the predicted consequences of actions – and breakdowns in experienced agency, which can happen in conditions such as schizophrenia – can be traced to abnormalities in this predictive process.

这些发现将我们一路带回笛卡尔。与其说「我思故我在」，我们不如说：「我预测（我自己）故我在。」成为你（或我）的特定体验，不过是大脑对自我相关感觉信号原因的最佳猜测。

These findings take us all the way back to Descartes. Instead of 'I think therefore I am' we can say: 'I predict (myself) therefore I am.' The specific experience of being you (or me) is nothing more than the brain's best guess of the causes of self-related sensory signals.

这个故事还有最后一层转折。预测模型不仅适用于推断感觉信号的原因，它们还允许大脑控制或调节这些原因，通过改变感觉数据使之符合既有的预测（这有时被称为「主动推断」）。当涉及到自我，特别是其深层的具身面向时，有效的调节可以说比准确的感知更重要。只要我们的心跳、血压和其他生理量保持在可行的范围内，我们是否缺乏详细的感知表征可能并不重要。这或许与「作为一个身体」的体验的独有特征有关——相比于对世界中的物体或对身体作为物体的体验。

There is a final twist to this story. Predictive models are good not only for figuring out the causes of sensory signals, they also allow the brain to control or regulate these causes, by changing sensory data to conform to existing predictions (this is sometimes called 'active inference'). When it comes to the self, especially its deeply embodied aspects, effective regulation is arguably more important than accurate perception. As long as our heartbeat, blood pressure and other physiological quantities remain within viable bounds, it might not matter if we lack detailed perceptual representations. This might have something to do with the distinctive character of experiences of '_being_ a body', in comparison with experiences of objects in the world – or of the body as an object.

而这也最后一次将我们带回笛卡尔。在将心灵与身体分离时，他认为非人类动物不过是没有任何内在宇宙的「野兽机器」。在他看来，生理调节的基本过程与心灵或意识几乎毫无关系。我逐渐产生了相反的看法。现在我认为，我们意识自我体验的基本面向可能依赖于对我们混乱的生理——我们的动物血肉——的控制导向的预测性感知。我们之所以是有意识的自我，是因为我们也是野兽机器——关心自身存续的自维持肉体之袋。

And this returns us one last time to Descartes. In dissociating mind from body, he argued that non-human animals were nothing more than 'beast machines' without any inner universe. In his view, basic processes of physiological regulation had little or nothing to do with mind or consciousness. I've come to think the opposite. It now seems to me that fundamental aspects of our experiences of conscious selfhood might depend on control-oriented predictive perception of our messy physiology, of our animal blood and guts. _We are conscious selves because we too are beast machines –_ self-sustaining flesh-bags that care about their own persistence_._
