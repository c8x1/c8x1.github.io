技术伦理

驱动 AI 决策的价值观是什么？

Aeon·Aeon·2026年5月31日·11分钟阅读·原文

一个五岁的小男孩正在帮祖母做饼干——从她擀好的面团上切出饼干形状——但他做得相当糟糕。他指挥家里的机器人来接手，尽管机器人从未做过这件事，但它很快就学会了该怎么做，完美地切出了每一块饼干。祖母有些失落，她怀念起儿子小时候做的那些歪歪扭扭、沾满脏手印的饼干，那才是真正的可爱。而她的孙子则继续让机器人做这些事，长大后很可能连像样的手工灵巧度都发展不出来。

A five-year-old boy is helping his grandmother cook by cutting out biscuits from the dough she's made, and he's doing it rather badly. He instructs the family robot to take over and, even though the robot's never done this before, it quickly learns what to do, and cuts out the biscuits perfectly. The grandmother is rather disappointed, remembering fondly the lopsided biscuits, complete with grubby fingerprints, that her son had charmingly baked for her at that age. Her grandson continues to use the robot for such tasks, and will grow up with pretty poor manual dexterity.

男孩的父母回家后，他说：「看，我给你们做了这些饼干。」一位家长说：「啊，真棒，我可以吃一块吗？」另一位则在心里默默想：「不，这不是你自己做的，你这个小骗子。」

When the boy's parents come home, he says: 'Look, I've made these biscuits for you.' One parent says: 'Oh how lovely, may I have one?' The other thinks silently: 'No you didn't make these yourself, you little cheat.'

人工智能（AI）或许有潜力改变我们处理任务的方式，以及我们所珍视的东西。如果我们用 AI 来代替我们思考，那么使用 AI 可能会使我们的思维能力退化。

Artificial intelligence (AI) might have the potential to change how we approach tasks, and what we value. If we are using AI to do our thinking for us, employing AI might atrophy our thinking skills.

我们目前拥有的 AI 还只是弱人工智能——它只能执行特定的、有限的任务。而且，即使 AI 在某些任务上的表现与人类相当甚至更出色，它也不一定是以与人类相同的方式取得这些结果的。AI 非常擅长的一件事是以极快的速度筛选海量数据。通过机器学习，一个经过数千张图像训练的 AI 可以获得识别猫的照片的能力（鉴于互联网上猫咪图片的主导地位，这是一项重要成就）。但人类做这件事的方式截然不同。一个幼儿往往只需要看一个例子就能认出猫。

The AI we have at the moment is narrow AI – it can perform only selected, specific tasks. And even when an AI can perform as well as, or better than, humans at certain tasks, it does not necessarily achieve these results in the same way that humans do. One thing that AI is very good at is sifting through masses of data at great speed. Using machine learning, an AI that's been trained with thousands of images can develop the capacity to recognise a photograph of a cat (an important achievement, given the predominance of pictures of cats on the internet). But humans do this very differently. A small child can often recognise a cat after just one example.

由于 AI 的「思维」方式可能与人类不同，再加上人们普遍容易被其魅力所裹挟，它的使用很可能会改变我们处理任务和做出决策的方式。围绕 AI 的那种诱人光环，实际上正是它的危险之一。业内人士几乎对每一篇关于 AI 的报道都在夸大其能力感到绝望，即使是那些关于 AI 平淡用途的文章，配图也是杀人机器人。

Because AI might 'think' differently to how humans think, and because of the general tendency to get swept up in its allure, its use could well change how we approach tasks and make decisions. The seductive allure that tends to surround AI in fact represents one of its dangers. Those working in the field despair that almost every article about AI hypes its powers, and even those about banal uses of AI are illustrated with killer robots.

技术对价值观的塑造影响是公认的。在最近一次关于 AI 伦理的圆桌讨论中，我所在的小组把大部分时间花在讨论洗衣机这个经典案例上——洗衣机并不仅仅是「接管」了洗衣工作，它对人们关于清洁和家务的态度，以及服装制造业，都产生了深远的影响。由于 AI 被设计出来的目的不仅仅是处理洗衣这样的具体事务，还要参与我们在无数不确定任务中的思考和决策过程，我们需要认真考虑它可能如何改变我们自身的思想和行为。

The impact of technology on shaping our values is well-established. At a recent roundtable discussion on the ethics of AI, the group I was in spent most of our time discussing the well-known example of the washing machine, which did not simply 'take over' the laundry, but which has had a major impact on attitudes to cleanliness and housework, and on the manufacture of clothing. Because AI is designed to contribute not merely to the laundry, but to how we think and make decisions over an indeterminate number of tasks, we need to consider seriously how it might change our own thought and behaviour.

需要记住的是，AI 可以采取多种形式，并以多种不同方式应用，因此上述一切并不是要论证使用 AI 是「好」还是「坏」。在某些情况下，AI 可能会促使我们改进方法。但在其他情况下，它可能会削弱或退化我们处理重要问题的方式。它甚至可能扭曲我们对价值观的思考。

It's important to remember that AI can take many forms, and be applied in many different ways, so none of this is to argue that using AI will be 'good' or 'bad'. In some cases, AI might nudge us to improve our approach. But in others, it could reduce or atrophy our approach to important issues. It might even skew how we think about values.

我们可以非常迅速地习惯技术。变化盲视和对技术的快速适应意味着我们可能并不完全意识到这种文化和价值观的转变。例如，随着通信方式以及数据共享和处理方式的巨大技术变革，人们对隐私的态度发生了显著变化。推动 AI 进步的因素之一恰恰是如今可获取的海量数据，其中很大一部分是关于我们的——在我们的日常生活中被收集。许多人对控制我们数据的组织极其警惕，但同时又继续发布大量非常私人的信息，而这些信息在几年前还会被视为私密。研究表明，人们对数据隐私的关注在不同情境之间并不一致。这并不是说技术「单独」造成了这一切，因为总有其他社会变化在同时起作用。

We can get used to technology very swiftly. Change-blindness and fast adaptation to technology can mean we're not fully aware of such cultural and value shifts. For example, attitudes to privacy have changed considerably along with the vast technological shifts in how we communicate and how data is shared and processed. One of the very things driving progress in AI is indeed the vast amounts of data now available, much of it about us, collected as we go about our daily lives. Many people are extremely wary of the organisations that have control of our data, while nonetheless continuing to post large amounts of very personal information that even a few years ago would have been considered private. Research shows that people's concerns about data privacy are inconsistent from one situation to the next. This is not to say that technology 'alone' has done this, since there are always other social changes operating at the same time.

也许我们对某些技术的影响特别盲目，因为它在很大程度上塑造了我们看待世界的方式。AI 的挑战在于它可能以我们并不完全意识到的方式运作。它帮助塑造了我们彼此交流的方式、我们思考的方式、我们发现世界的方式。这并非全新现象：书写技术、印刷术和电话已经改变了我们感知世界和与世界互动的方式，甚至改变了我们的大脑。但 AI 可能更加强大。嵌入在我们获取大量信息的技术中的算法，可能正在塑造我们接收什么信息、如何接收信息，甚至如何对其做出反应。而 AI 可能在塑造我们的行为——这并非其使用的意外后果，而是有意为之。技术——往往在 AI 的辅助下——正在利用人类心理学来塑造我们的行为。手机和社交媒体平台的设计借鉴了心理学研究——关于如何让用户对其使用产生成瘾性反应的研究。

And perhaps we are especially blind to the effects of some technology because it does so much to shape how we see the world. The challenge of AI is that it might operate in ways we aren't fully aware of. It helps to mould how we communicate with each other, how we think, how we find the world. This is not completely new: writing technology, the printing press and the telephone have already altered how we perceive and interact with our world, and even changed our brains. But AI might be even more powerful. Algorithms embedded into the technology through which we access so much information could be shaping what information we receive, how we receive it, even how we react to it. And AI might be shaping our behaviour, not as an unintended consequence of its use, but by design. Technology, often aided by AI, is exploiting human psychology to shape how we behave. Phones and social media platforms are designed by drawing upon psychological research about how to produce addictive responses to their use.

那么，让我们探讨几个使用或可能使用 AI 的例子，重点关注机器和人类如何使用和分析数据。

So let's explore a few examples of the use or potential use of AI, focusing on how machines and humans use and analyse data.

首先，我们要明确，使用 AI 代替人类做决策可能有很大的优势。AI 执行的快速信息共享和稳健的数据分析是非常有益的。例如，牛津移动机器人组的信息工程师保罗·纽曼（Paul Newman）指出，从人类驾驶的车辆事故中学习是一个缓慢而复杂的过程。其他人类无法从每一个具体案例中直接学习，即使是当事人本身也可能学到的很少甚至什么也没学到。但是，每当一辆自动驾驶汽车发生事故时，所有数据都可以立即在所有其他自动驾驶车辆之间共享，用于降低未来事故发生的概率。

First, let's be clear that there can be great advantages in using AI over human decision making. The fast sharing and robust data-analysis that AI performs can be extremely advantageous. For example, the information engineer Paul Newman of the Oxford Mobile Robotics Group points out that learning from accidents in vehicles driven by humans is a slow and complex process. Other humans can't learn directly from each individual case, and even the human involved might learn little or nothing. But whenever an autonomous car has an accident, all the data can immediately be shared among all other autonomous vehicles, and used to reduce the chances of a future accident.

AI 的这一特性——像蜂群思维一样共享信息，以及快速、严格地分析数据的能力——可能构成我们解决问题方式的真正改进。汇聚数据共享是 AI 极其擅长的事情。快速分析数据是另一个强项。这就是用数千张猫的图片训练 AI 的原理。事实上，正是对海量数据池的获取能力，加上以极快速度分析这些数据的能力，正在推动当前 AI 的蓬勃发展。

This aspect of AI – the ability to share information like a hive mind and to analyse data rapidly and rigorously – might then constitute a real improvement in how we solve problems. Sharing pooled data is something AI is extremely good at. Analysing data quickly is another. That's how training AI on thousands of pictures of cats works. In fact, it's access to vast pools of data, together with the ability to analyse this data at speed, that's helping to drive the current boom in AI.

虽然自动驾驶车辆也会犯错，但这个例子展示了 AI 能够克服的人类缺陷。人类在获取和分析做出良好决策并据此行动所需的数据方面，存在各种各样的失败方式。一辆自动驾驶车辆永远不会因为羞耻而不愿承认错误，永远不会因为虚荣而拒绝佩戴驾驶眼镜，永远不会在疲劳时坚持驾驶，永远不会拒绝参加高级驾驶课程。克服偏见、偏颇和非理性是改善人类决策的一种方式——尤其是在涉及价值观的问题上。其中一些偏见和非理性涉及对相关信息的拒绝或未能处理。因此，这种利用 AI 来汇聚数据的模型，似乎是我们应用于决策的一种优势。

Although autonomous vehicles can also make errors, this example demonstrates the human faults that AI can overcome. There are all sorts of ways in which humans fail to take in or analyse the data needed to make good decisions and to act on them. An autonomous vehicle will never be ashamed to admit fault, never be too vain to wear driving glasses, never insist on driving when tired, never refuse to go on an advanced driver course. Overcoming bias, partiality and irrationality is one way of improving human decision making – especially where issues of value are concerned. Some of these biases and irrationalities involve the rejection of, or failure to process, relevant information. So this model of using AI to pool data seems to be an advantage we can apply to decision making.

但这样的结论可能过于草率。并非我们所有的问题都能通过纯粹的数据驱动方法来解决。避免汽车事故是好事，这一点相当清楚。这是一个安全问题，我们主要是在应用技术修复方案，而且成功与否很容易衡量。车辆要么撞了要么没撞，死亡和受伤人数可以确定。未遂事故也不难统计。但对于不那么纯粹技术性的问题，数据驱动的「蜂群思维」方法是否总是好的，就不那么清楚了。

But such a conclusion might be hasty. Not all our problems can be solved by a purely data-led approach. It is pretty clear that avoiding car accidents is good. It's a safety issue where what we're doing is mostly applying technological fixes, and it's pretty easy to measure success. The vehicle either crashes or it doesn't, and deaths and injuries can be determined. It's also pretty easy to measure near-misses. But for problems that are less purely technical, it's not so clear that a data-driven, 'hive mind' approach is always good.

以医学为例——这是 AI 最有前景的应用领域之一。医学既是一门科学，也是一门艺术：它将科学和技术与价值观的追求结合在一起——健康的价值、良好医患关系的价值、以患者为中心的护理的价值、患者自主权的价值，等等。在医学中，我们不仅仅是在寻找一个技术修复方案。

Take medicine, for example, one of the most promising areas of AI. Medicine is both a science, and an art: it combines science and technology with the pursuit of values: the value of health, the value of good patient relations, the value of person-centred care, the value of patient autonomy, and others. In medicine, we are not just looking for a technological fix.

AI 在诊断中的应用前景非常广阔，例如在辅助解读医学图像方面，通过筛选海量数据来实现。证据表明，AI 能够检测到人眼注意不到的图像间的细微差异。但它也可能犯人类绝不会犯的低级错误。因此，目前将 AI 与人类技能结合似乎是改善诊断的最佳选择。到目前为止，这是非常好的消息。

The use of AI in diagnosis is very promising, for example, in assisting with the interpretation of medical images by trawling through vast amounts of data. The evidence seems to be that AI can detect minute differences between images that the human eye doesn't notice. But it can also make blatant errors that a human would never make. So, currently, combining AI with human skills seems the best option for improving diagnosis. So far, this is excellent news.

但是，2018 年《新英格兰医学杂志》上由加州斯坦福大学医学院的丹顿·夏尔（Danton Char）、尼甘·沙阿（Nigam Shah）和大卫·马格努斯（David Magnus）发表的一篇文章，对使用 AI 进行诊断和治疗决策提出了严肃的质疑。从医学作为一门科学的角度来想。如果 AI 成为「集体医学思维的存储库」，我们在以某种方式使用它之前就必须极其谨慎，因为那种方式可能会导致专业思维的趋同，从而可能排除独立思考和个人的临床经验。目前，人们承认在诊断和治疗方面可能存在不同的医学观点。如果我们能完全确信 AI 只是在提高准确性，那么医学思维更大的趋同性就是好事。但存在一种危险，即 AI 可能过早地切断某些选择，或将我们引向特定的治疗路径。此外，作者警告说，这种机器学习甚至可能被用来将治疗推向达成指标或为既得利益者创造利润，而非为患者提供最佳方案。数据可能主导医学，而非反过来。

But a piece in The New England Journal of Medicine in 2018 by Danton Char, Nigam Shah and David Magnus of the Stanford University School of Medicine in California raises serious questions about using AI in diagnosis and treatment decisions. Think about medicine as a science. If AI forms the 'repository for the collective medical mind', we'd have to be extremely careful before using it in a way that moves towards uniformity of professional thinking, which might foreclose independent thought and individual clinical experience. At the moment, it's recognised that there might be different bodies of medical opinion about diagnosis and treatment. If we could be utterly confident that AI was only improving accuracy, then greater uniformity of medical thinking would be good. But there's a danger that AI might prematurely shut off options or lead us down particular treatment routes. Moreover, the authors warn that such machine learning might even be used to nudge treatment towards hitting targets or achieving profits for vested interests, rather than what's best for patients. The data might drive the medicine, rather than the other way around.

从医学作为一门艺术的角度来想。它涉及将患者视为过着各自生活的真实个体。虽然 AI 可能帮助我们更好地实现健康目标，但综合考虑所有因素，成功率较低的治疗方案对某些患者可能是更好的选择。纯粹数据驱动的方法无法告诉我们这一点。而且我们需要警惕不被技术的力量冲昏头脑。因为我们已经知道，自由和知情同意在实践中极难实现，而医疗体制会影响患者的同意。但在技术的额外权威和全面的专业共识的加持下，将医疗行业现有的权力与 AI 的附加权力结合起来的危险在于——「电脑说该吃药」可能会成为现实。

Think about medicine as an art. It involves relating to patients as real individuals living their own lives. Although AI might help us better achieve the goal of health, treatments with a lower chance of success might be the better option for some patients, all things considered. A data-driven approach alone cannot tell us this. And we need to be cautious we're not carried away by the power of technology. For we already know that free and informed consent is extremely hard to achieve in practice, and that the medical establishment influences patients' consent. But with the added gravitas of technology, and of blanket professional agreement, the danger is that wedding the existing power of the medical profession to the added power of AI, 'Computer says take the drugs' might become a reality.

医患关系是医学的核心，也是我们理解医学伦理的核心。但 AI 的使用可能微妙地，甚至从根本上改变这一点。究竟如何实现使用 AI 改善患者护理这一道德上值得称赞的目标，需要仔细考量。

The relationship between physician and patient is at the heart of medicine, and of our understanding of medical ethics. But the use of AI could subtly, even radically, alter this. Precisely how we implement the morally laudable aim of using AI to improve patient care needs careful consideration.

AI 操纵和处理海量数据的能力可能会推动我们过度重视或唯一重视数据驱动的方法来识别和解决问题。这可能导致思维的趋同，即使在有理由追求思想和方法多样性的情况下也是如此。它还可能使其他因素黯然失色，并在此过程中不仅扭曲我们的思维，还扭曲我们的价值观。

AI's ability to manipulate and process vast amounts of data might push us into giving undue or sole prominence to data-driven approaches to identifying and solving problems. This might lead to uniformity of thinking, even in cases where there are reasons to aspire to variety of thought and approach. It might also eclipse other factors and, in doing so, distort not just our thinking, but our values.

一个决策是如何做出的，由谁做出的；一个行动是如何执行的，由谁执行的——在许多情况下这些都是关键问题。在涉及价值观的问题上尤其如此。那位怀疑男孩是否真的自己做饼干的家长是有道理的。也许如果他先设计并制造了那个机器人，他的说法会更有说服力。这些因素的重要性因具体情况而异，用机器智能替代或补充人类智能的潜在意义也是如此。

How a decision is made, and by whom; how an action is performed, and by whom – these are critical issues in many circumstances. It's especially the case where values are concerned. The parent who was skeptical that the boy had really made the cookies himself has a point. Perhaps if he'd first designed and built the robot, his claim would have had more validity. The significance of these factors will vary from case to case, as will the potential significance of replacing or supplementing human intelligence with machine intelligence.

以陪审团制度为例。我们都知道陪审团有缺陷：他们有时会得出错误的结论。算法已经在帮助美国法院就量刑和假释做出某些决定，利用再犯率等数据，这引发了相当大的争议。人们担心这可能有助于固化对某些群体的既有偏见。但想象一下，我们已经达到了这样的阶段：将所有可用证据输入计算机后得出的裁决比陪审团达成的更准确。在这种情况下，计算机能够以极快的速度汇聚和分析所有数据，并且（在这个假想的例子中）准确高效。对比一下现实中陪审团的运作方式——不同的人可能对案件做了不同的笔记，回忆起不同的内容，即使经过数小时的审议，仍然对证据持有不同看法。AI 收集和分析数据的能力可能大大弥补这些不足。

Take the use of juries. We all know that juries are flawed: they sometimes get the wrong answer. Algorithms are already helping US courts come to some decisions regarding sentencing and parole, drawing on data such as information about recidivism rates, to considerable controversy. There are fears that this can help entrench existing biases against certain groups. But imagine that we have reached the point at which feeding all the available evidence into a computer has led to more accurate verdicts than those reached by juries. In such a case, the computer would be able to pool and analyse all data with speed and (in this imagined example) accuracy and efficiency. Compare how actual juries work, where individuals might have made differing notes about the case, recall different things and, even after hours of deliberations, still have different views of the evidence. The power of AI to gather and analyse data might go a long way to address these shortcomings.

但这个例子清楚地表明，我们关心的不仅仅是把事情做对。即使使用机器能得到更准确的答案，我们仍然可能有理由重视人类担任陪审团所带来的独特贡献。想一想法律改革和个人权利是如何经过斗争争取而来的，以及「由同等地位的同类人组成的陪审团」这一概念中所体现的普通人的价值。也许我们想把这一切交给 AI——但也许不想。

But this example readily demonstrates that we care about more than simply getting things right. Even if, by using a machine, we get a more accurate answer, there can still be some reason to value the distinctive contribution of having humans serving on juries. Consider the history of how legal reforms and individual rights have been fought for, and the value of the common person that's enshrined in the notion of being tried by 'a jury of one's peers'. Perhaps we want to hand that over to an AI – but perhaps not.

人类可能表现出的偏见、容易被情绪左右的倾向，当然是在做出裁决时的潜在弱点。但它也是法律变革的推动力。存在「陪审团否决」的案例——在那些令人头疼的、关于不公正的人类情感的驱使下，陪审团拒绝定罪，即使被告在法律条文上明显有罪。无论一台机器在评估证据方面多么出色，我们距离开发出具有敏锐正义感、关注弱者、并且有道德勇气反抗法律体系机器的机器还差得远。

The bias that humans can display, the tendency to be swayed by emotion, is of course a potential weakness in reaching a verdict. But it has also been the impetus for changes in the law. There are instances of 'jury nullification' where, swayed by those pesky human feelings of injustice, juries have simply failed to convict, even though the defendant is clearly guilty in terms of a strict application of the law. No matter how good at assessing evidence a machine might be, we're a long way off developing machines with a finely tuned sense of justice, an eye for the underdog, and the moral backbone to defy the apparatus of the legal system.

但更广泛的观点仍然是：陪审团作为独立判断来源的角色，是对抗最有权势者的既得利益的制衡。正如德夫林勋爵（Lord Devlin）2004 年在上议院所说：「陪审团审判不仅是正义的工具，也不仅是宪法的一个齿轮：它是显示自由尚存的明灯。」还需要注意的是：AI 在自动驾驶车辆避免事故方面的优势——汇聚信息、融合洞察——在法律的语境下恰恰破坏了陪审团独立性的基本道德原则。这种独立性是对抗始终存在的强大既得利益集团可能性的制衡，也为我们提供了一个理由来保留陪审团审判制度，同时关注的不仅仅是法庭上呈现的信息的处理。

But the more general idea remains that juries perform the role of an independent source of judgment as a counter to the vested interests of the most powerful. As Lord Devlin said in the House of Lords in 2004: '[T]rial by jury is more than an instrument of justice and more than one wheel of the constitution: it is the lamp that shows that freedom lives.' And note this: the very feature of AI that is a strength in avoiding accidents for autonomous vehicles – the pooling of information, the melding of insights – in the context of the law directly undermines the basic moral principle of independence of juries. This independence is a counter to the ever-present possibility of powerful vested interests and gives a reason to keep trial by jury while being concerned with more than the mere processing of information presented in court.

批评者可能会说，我们需要这种独立性仅仅是因为人类如此不可靠：不能仅仅把司法交给法律行业，但一个准确的 AI 就能解决这个问题，而且也许随着时间的推移，我们会习惯这个想法，把我们的司法系统交给机器。

A critic might say that we need this independence only because humans are so unreliable: the legal profession alone can't be left in charge of justice, but an accurate AI would solve the problem and maybe, with the passage of time, we'd get used to the idea, and hand over our justice system to the machines.

但认为我们能够永远逃脱权力失衡和既得利益——这正是拥有陪审团制度的原因——的想法完全是乌托邦式的幻想。解决司法误判问题有其他替代方案，而不是把司法交给机器，比如一个快速、便捷的申诉系统。也许在未来，AI 可以辅助法官和陪审团做出决定——但这与设想 AI 可能取代人类进行法律决策完全不同。即便如此，我们也需要仔细考虑 AI 的影响，以及它是否正在推动我们走向一种更加技术官僚化的方式。法律作为一个人类政治和社会系统，是经过许多斗争才发展起来的。但在法律中使用 AI 可能会随着时间推移帮助改变这一点。我们需要在充分意识到对正义和民主诸多影响的情况下仔细考虑这个问题。

But it's utterly utopian to think that we'll ever escape the power imbalances and vested interests that are the reason for having juries. There are alternatives to the miscarriage of justice problem than handing over justice to the machines, such as a fast, accessible appeals system. Perhaps, in the future, AI might assist judges and juries to come to decisions – but this is rather different to envisaging that AI might replace humans in legal decision making. Even here, we'd need to think carefully about the impact of AI, and whether it was nudging us towards a more technocratic approach. The law developed as a human political and social system through much struggle. But the use of AI within the law could, over time, help to change this. We need to consider this carefully, in full awareness of the many implications for justice and democracy.

使用 AI 的一个巨大吸引力仅仅在于它分析数据的惊人速度。效率是一种美德，但这种美德取决于它被用于什么目的。它也绝不是唯一的美德。如果一台机器能快速高效地完成某件事，我们可能受到的诱惑超出了实际合理的程度。而且它完成任务的速度可能会让我们忽视它在达成目的过程中存在的问题。我们最终可能对这种高效生成的结果赋予过高的价值。

One great attraction of using AI is simply the sheer speed at which it can analyse data. Efficiency is a virtue, but this virtue depends upon the ends to which it is being used. It's also by no means the only virtue. If a machine can accomplish something quickly and efficiently, we might be more tempted to use it than is really merited. And the speed with which it accomplishes tasks might make us overlook problems in how it achieves its ends. We might then end up placing too great a value on such efficiently generated results.

反诽谤联盟（Anti-Defamation League，简称 ADL）与加州大学伯克利分校的 D-Lab 合作，正在开发一种使用机器学习的在线仇恨指数（Online Hate Index，简称 OHI），试图检测网上的仇恨言论。这是一个有趣的例子：它是一种「以技术对付技术」的方案——网上所谓「仇恨言论」的泛滥（表面上看）是计算机技术的产物。然而，向对手恶言相向绝非新鲜事。远在万维网在蒂姆·伯纳斯-李（Tim Berners-Lee）眼中还只是一丝微光之前，17 世纪的法国哲学家笛卡尔（René Descartes）就将竞争对手数学家费马（Pierre de Fermat）的作品称为「狗屎」。侮辱的悠久血统值得注意，因为围绕 AI 的炒作往往鼓励我们将问题视为全新的，或认为其具有独特的危险性。我们可能被引向过度依赖 AI 所拥有的那类狭窄能力范围的一种方式，恰恰是以下两个假设的结合：「技术原因 = 技术解决方案」，以及当今技术尤其充满了新颖的道德危险这一观念。

The Anti-Defamation League (ADL) in conjunction with D-Lab at the University of California, Berkeley, is developing an Online Hate Index (OHI) using machine learning in an attempt to detect hate speech online. This is an interesting example: it's a 'tech on tech' solution – the alleged proliferation of 'hate speech' online is (seemingly) a product of computerised technology. Yet hurling abuse at opponents is hardly new. Long before the World Wide Web was even a twinkle in the eye of Tim Berners-Lee, the 17th-century French philosopher René Descartes described the work of rival mathematician Pierre de Fermat as 'shit'. The long pedigree of insults is worth noting, given the way that hype around AI encourages us to think of issues as new, or uniquely dangerous. One way in which we can be steered in the direction of over-reliance on the narrow range of capacities that AI has is precisely by the combined assumptions that 'tech cause = tech solution', together with the idea that today's tech is especially full of novel moral perils.

「仇恨言论」这一概念本身就有争议。一些人认为，试图消除语言的某些用法——无论是通过法律还是通过管理在线平台的机构——对于实现消除歧视等目标是必要的。另一些人则担心这对言论自由构成威胁，代表了一种审查形式。人们担忧的是，对一个人来说是仇恨言论的内容，对另一个人来说可能只是玩笑，而且在脱离语境的情况下极难将某事物归类为「仇恨言论」。此外，鉴于网上发布的海量内容，任何对「仇恨言论」的监管都将是零散的，而且人们担心某些群体或个人可能被不成比例地针对。

The concept of 'hate speech' is itself controversial. Some consider that attempting to eliminate certain uses of language, whether by law or by those managing online platforms, is necessary to achieve goals such as the elimination of discrimination. Others worry that this is a danger to free speech, and represents a form of censorship. There are concerns that what counts as hate speech to one person is merely banter to another, and that it's extremely hard to classify something as 'hate speech' out of context. Additionally, there are concerns that, with the vast amount of material posted online, any policing of 'hate speech' will be patchy, and there are fears that some groups or individuals might be disproportionately targeted.

如果我们能自动化检测网上的仇恨言论，可能有助于分类和提高一致性。OHI 可以通过比人类更快地处理数据、正确执行政策且不带偏见来解决这些问题。（当然，这取决于它是如何被编程的。如果它被编程为有偏见的，那它将以极高的效率持有偏见。）

If we could automate the detection of hate speech online, it might help with classification and consistency. The OHI could address these issues by processing data faster than a human, and applying the policy correctly, without bias. (Of course, this depends on how it's programmed. If it's programmed to be biased, it will be biased with great efficiency.)

所以问题就在这里。在为 AI 能够以令人类望尘莫及的速度和一致性检测、分类和消除「仇恨言论」的想法所鼓舞，加上担心网络世界正在把我们中太多人变成不负责任的、充满仇恨的网络喷子的情况下，我们可能会迫不及待地使用这项技术，以至于仇恨言论的其他问题在某种程度上被忽视了。这可能有助于推动一场大致可以概括为「仇恨言论对抗言论自由」的辩论朝某一特定方向发展。换言之，它可能有助于塑造我们的价值观。它可能轻易地改变人们在网上交流的方式——因为害怕仇恨言论机器人会将其从相关平台上驱逐。其中某些方面可能是好的，某些则不那么好。

So here is the problem. Enthused with the idea that an AI can detect, categorise and eliminate 'hate speech' with a speed and consistency that leaves humans standing, coupled with fears that the online world is turning so many of us into irresponsible hate-filled trolls, we could use this technology with such alacrity that the other problems of hate speech get somewhat overlooked. This then could help to drive the debate that could be roughly summarised as 'hate speech versus free speech' in one particular direction. In other words, it might help to mould our values. It might easily help to change the ways in which people communicate online, for fear that the hate-speech bot might oust them from the platform in question. Some aspects of this might be good, some not so good.

AI 的优点包括其分享数据以达成普遍看法的独特能力；它帮助排除人类偏见的能力；以及它运作的速度和效率。在所有这些方面，它都能超越人类的能力。但这些优点都必须与我们的其他价值观加以衡量。如果不这样做，我们可能会被 AI 的力量所迷惑，任由它在决定我们如何看待一些最重要的价值观和活动方面占据主导地位。

The virtues of AI include its particular ability to share data to reach a universal view of things; its capacity to help exclude human bias; the speed and efficiency with which it operates. It can transcend human capacity in all these things. But these virtues must all be measured up against our other values. Without doing so, we might be entranced by the power of AI into allowing it to take the lead in determining how we think about some of our most important values and activities.
