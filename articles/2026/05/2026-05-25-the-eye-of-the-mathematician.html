数学家之眼：美、真理与机器无法看见的东西

从欧拉恒等式到厄尔多什的天书——数学之美是真实的吗？

Daily Curated Editorial·每日精选·2026年5月25日·9分钟阅读·原文

有一种奇异的体验，大多数人终其一生都不会拥有，然而它却是人类心智所能经历的最深刻的体验之一。它毫无征兆地降临——一声猝不及防的惊叹，一阵骤然加快的脉搏，一种宇宙刚刚将它的某个秘密低语进你耳中的感觉。数学家们对此再熟悉不过。那是一个证明展露自身之时——它不仅是正确的，而且是美的。等式 e^(iπ) + 1 = 0 落在纸面上，如同一行完美的诗句。欧几里得关于素数无穷的论证徐徐展开，带着百川归海般的必然。我们内心深处的某种东西，在我们尚未来得及阐明原因之前，就已辨认出我们正置身于优雅之中。而那种优雅，正如数学家哈代所言，并非装饰。它就是事物本身。

There is a peculiar experience that most people will never have, and yet it is among the most profound a human mind can undergo. It arrives without warning -- a sudden gasp, a quickening pulse, the feeling that the universe has just whispered one of its secrets into your ear. Mathematicians know this experience well. It is the moment when a proof reveals itself not merely as correct, but as beautiful. The equation e to the power of i times pi, plus one, equals zero, lands on the page like a line of perfect poetry. Euclid's argument for the infinity of primes unfolds with the inevitability of a river reaching the sea. Something in us recognises, before we have had time to articulate why, that we are in the presence of elegance. And that elegance, the mathematician G.H. Hardy argued, is not ornamental. It is the thing itself.

哈代在他1940年的《一个数学家的辩白》中，提出了数学哲学中最具挑衅性的论断之一："美是第一道检验：丑陋的数学在这个世界上没有永久的容身之地。"他并非信口而言。对哈代而言，审美判断是区分有意义的数学与琐碎之作的首要标准。一个仅仅"管用"的证明——一个在枯燥的穷举分析或暴力验证中艰难跋涉的证明——在一种深层意义上是有缺陷的，无论其形式上多么正确。哈代珍视的是简洁、必然与惊奇。美的证明恰如其分地做到了所需的一切，绝不多做一事。

Hardy, in his 1940 "A Mathematician's Apology," made one of the most provocative claims in the philosophy of mathematics: "Beauty is the first test: there is no permanent place in the world for ugly mathematics." He was not speaking loosely. For Hardy, aesthetic judgment was the primary criterion by which meaningful mathematics could be distinguished from the trivial. A proof that merely worked, that slogged through tedious case analysis or brute-force verification, was deficient in a deep sense, regardless of its formal validity. What Hardy valued was economy, inevitability, surprise. The beautiful proof does exactly what is needed and not one thing more.

哲学家罗素以其标志性的雄辩，捕捉到了数学经验的这一维度："数学，如果正确地审视，不仅拥有真理，而且拥有至高的美——一种冷峻而严峻的美，如同雕塑之美，不诉诸我们本性中任何软弱的部分，没有绘画或音乐那般华丽的装饰，然而崇高纯粹，能够达到一种唯有最伟大的艺术才能展现的庄严完美。"罗素将数学比作雕塑，这一类比意味深长。雕塑剔除材料以呈现蕴藏其中的形式。一个美的证明所做的正与之类似：它剥离无关之物和纯粹的运算，直到所余皆为纯粹的结构。

The philosopher Bertrand Russell captured this dimension of mathematical experience with characteristic eloquence: "Mathematics, rightly viewed, possesses not only truth, but supreme beauty -- a beauty cold and austere, like that of sculpture, without appeal to any part of our weaker nature, without the gorgeous trappings of painting or music, yet sublimely pure, and capable of a stern perfection such as only the greatest art can show." Russell's comparison to sculpture is revealing. Sculpture removes material to reveal the form within. A beautiful proof does something analogous: it strips away the irrelevant and the merely computational, until what remains is pure structure.

试想欧拉恒等式：e^(iπ) + 1 = 0。仅此一行，它便关联了数学中最基本的五个常数——e、i、π、1 和 0——这些常数来自截然不同的探究领域。几何、代数、分析与数论全部汇聚于一个如此简洁的关系之中，简洁到几乎让人以为是某位设计者有意安放于此。理查德·费曼称其为"数学中最非凡的公式"。其非凡之处不仅在于这个等式是真的，更在于它是出人意料的。我们对这些常数各自的了解，没有任何一点能让我们为它们的汇合做好准备。

Consider Euler's identity: e to the i pi plus one equals zero. In a single line, it relates the five most fundamental constants in mathematics -- e, i, pi, 1, and 0 -- constants that arise from entirely different domains of inquiry. Geometry, algebra, analysis, and number theory all converge in a relation so concise it seems almost to have been placed there by a designing intelligence. Richard Feynman called it "the most remarkable formula in mathematics." What is remarkable is not merely that the equation is true, but that it is unexpected. Nothing in our separate encounters with these constants prepares us for their convergence.

再以欧几里得关于素数无穷的证明为例。假设——为推导矛盾——素数只有有限个。将它们全部列出，相乘，再加一。这个新数要么本身是素数，要么被一个不在列表中的素数整除，因为用列表中任何一个素数去除它都会余一。无论哪种情况，列表都不完整。矛盾。这个论证用不了一段话的篇幅，不需要任何高等数学，却已基本保持原样超过两千年。每一步都不可或缺，每一步都发人深省。

Or take Euclid's proof that there are infinitely many primes. Suppose, for contradiction, that there are only finitely many. List them all, multiply them together, and add one. This new number is either prime itself or divisible by a prime not on the list, since dividing by any listed prime leaves a remainder of one. Either way, the list was incomplete. Contradiction. The argument takes less than a paragraph, requires no advanced mathematics, and has remained essentially unchanged for over two thousand years. Every step is necessary, and every step illuminates.

然而，当我们称一个证明为美的时候，我们究竟在回应什么？这个问题打开了数学哲学中最深的裂痕之一。一边站着柏拉图主义者，他们相信数学对象独立于人类心灵而存在，栖身于一个永恒真理的抽象领域。对柏拉图主义者而言，数学之美是客观的——不是心理投射，而是发现。当我们觉得欧拉恒等式很美时，我们是在回应数学现实的真实特征，正如我们可能会对一片雪花的对称性作出回应。哈代本人倾向于柏拉图主义："我相信数学现实存在于我们之外，"他写道，"而我们的职责是发现或观察它。"

But what exactly are we responding to when we call a proof beautiful? This opens one of the deepest fault lines in the philosophy of mathematics. On one side stand the Platonists, who believe mathematical objects exist independently of human minds, in an abstract realm of timeless truths. For the Platonist, mathematical beauty is objective -- not a psychological projection but a discovery. When we find Euler's identity beautiful, we are responding to a genuine feature of mathematical reality, the way we might respond to the symmetry of a snowflake. Hardy himself leaned Platonist: "I believe that mathematical reality lies outside us," he wrote, "and that our function is to discover or observe it."

另一边则站着那些将数学之美视为人类认知产物的人。在这种观点看来，优雅衡量的是一个证明与大脑处理架构的契合程度。我们觉得简短的证明是美的，因为我们的工作记忆是有限的。我们觉得出人意料的联系是美的，因为我们的认知系统奖赏模式识别。哲学家伊姆雷·拉卡托斯论证说，数学彻头彻尾是一项人类活动，由猜想、反驳和渐进改善所驱动——这个过程更像经验科学那种杂乱的探索，而非对柏拉图形式的纯粹沉思。大多数一线数学家在这两种立场之间维系着一种富有成效的模糊性：他们把美当作客观之物来对待，同时又承认自己通达美的途径经过了人类心理的过滤。

On the other side stand those who see mathematical beauty as a product of human cognition. On this view, elegance is a measure of how well a proof aligns with the processing architecture of the brain. We find short proofs beautiful because our working memory is limited. We find unexpected connections beautiful because our cognitive systems reward pattern recognition. The philosopher Imre Lakatos argued that mathematics is a human activity through and through, driven by conjecture, refutation, and gradual improvement -- a process more like the messiness of empirical science than the pristine contemplation of Platonic forms. Most working mathematicians operate in a productive ambiguity between these positions, treating beauty as if it were objective while acknowledging that their access to it is filtered through human psychology.

人工智能以一种不可忽视的力量闯入了这场古老的辩论。Lean 和 Coq 等系统能够以机器精度验证复杂的证明。DeepMind 的 AlphaGeometry 已解决奥林匹克级别的问题。大语言模型能够生成导向真正结果的证明概要。这些进展所提出的问题，不是机器能否做数学——它们显然能——而是机器生成的证明能否是美的。由计算机定理证明器生成的证明是一个形式对象：一系列符号操作，每一步都经过机械验证。机器不会停下来赞叹一个巧妙的代换。它不会感受到一种意外联系所带来的震颤。然而，这个证明在形式上可能与人类称之为美的那个证明完全相同。如果美是客观的，那么机器的证明就是美的，无论机器是否知道这一点。如果美是一种心理反应，那么机器的证明就根本不可能美，因为美只存在于感知者的体验之中。

Into this ancient debate, artificial intelligence has arrived with force. Systems like Lean and Coq can verify complex proofs with machine precision. DeepMind's AlphaGeometry has solved Olympiad-level problems. Large language models can generate proof sketches that lead to genuine results. The question these developments pose is not whether machines can do mathematics -- clearly they can -- but whether a machine-generated proof can be beautiful. A proof generated by a computer theorem prover is a formal object: a sequence of symbolic manipulations, each step mechanically verified. The machine does not pause to admire a clever substitution. It does not feel the thrill of an unexpected connection. And yet the proof may be formally identical to one a human would call beautiful. If beauty is objective, the machine's proof is beautiful regardless of whether the machine knows it. If beauty is a psychological response, then the machine's proof cannot be beautiful at all, because beauty exists only in the experience of a perceiving mind.

正是在这里，匈牙利数学家厄尔多什带着他著名的"天书"概念登场了。厄尔多什是二十世纪最多产的数学家之一，他坚信上帝保存着一本包含所有定理最优雅证明的书。当一个优美的论证被发现时，厄尔多什会宣称它直接来自那本天书。这个带有几分顽皮的隐喻承载着真正的哲学分量：它表达了一种信念，即对于任何为真的数学命题，都存在一个最好的可能证明——最直接、最启迪、最优雅。厄尔多什不在乎他的数学是否有用。他在乎的是它是否为真、是否为美，而对他而言，两者是不可分割的。天书的概念意味着，数学中的美不仅仅是趣味问题，证明是可以排序的，而标准不是实用性，而是审美的完美。

This is where the Hungarian mathematician Paul Erdos enters with his famous notion of The Book. Erdos, one of the most prolific mathematicians of the twentieth century, maintained that God keeps a book containing the most elegant proofs of all theorems. When a beautiful argument was discovered, Erdos would declare that it came straight from The Book. This whimsical metaphor carried genuine philosophical weight: it expressed the conviction that for any true mathematical statement, there exists a best possible proof -- the most direct, the most illuminating, the most elegant. Erdos did not care whether his mathematics was useful. He cared whether it was true and beautiful, and for him the two were inseparable. The notion of The Book implies that beauty in mathematics is not mere taste, that proofs can be ranked, and that the standard is not utility but aesthetic perfection.

美与实用性之间的张力是真实存在的。哈代为自己在数论方面的工作没有任何可预见的应用而自豪。"我从未做过任何'有用'的事，"他写道，语气中带着明显的满足。当然，这一点大错特错了。数论如今是现代密码学的基石，守护着每一笔在线交易的安全。但哈代的错误颇具启示意义：它揭示了审美动机能够在多大程度上主导数学家的心灵。他因自己的工作之美而珍视它，即使知道它永远不会有实际用途，他也会同样珍视。然而数学不仅仅只有美。费马大定理的证明，由怀尔斯于1994年在历经三个多世纪的努力之后完成，长达一百多页艰深的代数几何。用哈代的标准——简洁、惊奇、必然——来衡量，它或许并不美。它更像一座大教堂：整体令人敬畏，却由无数毫不起眼的石块砌成。数学之美，事实证明，形态万千。

The tension between beauty and utility is real. Hardy was proud that his work on number theory had no conceivable application. "I have never done anything 'useful,'" he wrote, with evident satisfaction. This was, of course, spectacularly wrong. Number theory now underpins modern cryptography and the security of every online transaction. But Hardy's error is instructive: it reveals how thoroughly the aesthetic motive can dominate the mathematical mind. He valued his work for its beauty, and would have done so even knowing it would never prove useful. And yet mathematics is not only beautiful. The proof of Fermat's Last Theorem, completed by Andrew Wiles in 1994 after more than three centuries of effort, runs to over a hundred pages of abstruse algebraic geometry. In Hardy's sense -- concise, surprising, inevitable -- perhaps it is not beautiful. It is more like a cathedral: awe-inspiring in its totality, but built from countless unremarkable stones. Mathematical beauty, it turns out, comes in many forms.

为什么这一切对哲学而言至关重要？因为数学坐落于人类经验的一个独特交汇点上。它既是我们所拥有的最严密的推理形式，同时——正如其从业者近乎一致的证词——也是最具审美性的。如果美可以成为通向真理的向导——如果哈代是对的，丑陋的证明通常是错误的或不完整的——那么美与真之间的关系就不仅是隐喻性的，而是结构性的。一次又一次，数学家觉得丑陋的证明最终被发现包含错误，而优雅的证明往往是可靠的。审美判断发挥着启发式的作用，成为值得探索的数学地形中可靠的指南。物理学家狄拉克坚持认为，"方程中拥有美比让方程符合实验更重要"，而历史在很大程度上证明了他的远见。他本人的方程预言了反物质——一个在当时被认为荒谬绝伦的想法——然而数学之美指向了一个实验尚无法确认的真理。

Why does any of this matter for philosophy? Because mathematics sits at a unique intersection of human experience. It is the most rigorous form of reasoning we possess, and yet it is also, by the near-unanimous testimony of its practitioners, the most aesthetic. If beauty can be a guide to truth -- if Hardy was right that ugly proofs are usually wrong or incomplete -- then the relationship between the beautiful and the true is not merely metaphorical but structural. Time and again, proofs mathematicians find ugly turn out to contain errors, while elegant proofs tend to be sound. The aesthetic judgment functions as a heuristic, a reliable guide to mathematical terrain worth exploring. The physicist Paul Dirac insisted that "it is more important to have beauty in one's equations than to have them fit experiment," and history has largely vindicated him. His own equation predicted antimatter -- an idea considered absurd at the time -- yet the beauty of the mathematics pointed toward a truth that experiments could not yet confirm.

人工智能的兴起迫使我们追问：这种审美能力是否为人类所独有？当前的 AI 系统能够产生正确的证明，却产生不了美的证明。它们的输出往往在技术上是胜任的，在审美上却是贫瘠的：一系列逻辑步骤达成了正确的结论，却缺乏人类推理所特有的结构性洞见。它们管用，但不能启迪。未来的系统是否能够具备这种启迪能力，是一个悬而未决的哲学问题。它取决于优雅能否被算法捕捉，还是说它依赖于某种不可还原为体验的理解形式。

The rise of AI forces us to ask whether this aesthetic faculty is uniquely human. Current AI systems produce correct proofs, but not beautiful ones. Their outputs tend to be technically competent but aesthetically barren: sequences of logical steps that reach the right conclusion without the structural insight that characterises human reasoning. They work, but they do not illuminate. Whether future systems will be capable of such illumination is an open philosophical question. It depends on whether elegance can be captured by an algorithm, or whether it depends on forms of understanding that are irreducibly experiential.

数学家的眼睛看到了算法所未见之物：论证的轮廓，思想的弧线，一个问题豁然开朗、展露其内在逻辑的那一刻。这不是神秘主义，而是一种专业技能，经由多年实践而养成，浸润于传统而淬炼。它抗拒形式化。优雅没有算法。这也许是关于 AI 当前状态的一个偶然事实，也许是关于审美判断的一个必然真理。如果是后者，数学之美将始终是人类心灵的领地——一个提醒：并非所有值得看见的东西都能被编码。

The mathematician's eye sees something that algorithms do not: the shape of an argument, the arc of a thought, the moment a problem opens up and reveals its internal logic. This is not mysticism but a form of expertise, cultivated over years of practice and refined by immersion in a tradition. It resists formalisation. There is no algorithm for elegance. This may be a contingent fact about the current state of AI, or it may be a necessary truth about aesthetic judgment. If the latter, the beauty of mathematics will remain a province of the human mind -- a reminder that not everything worth seeing can be captured in code.

机器迫使我们面对的问题，不是它们能否匹敌我们的计算。它们已经做到了，而且将越来越多地超越我们。问题在于它们能否匹敌我们的目光——它们能否注视着欧拉恒等式，感受到哈代曾感受到的、罗素曾感受到的、厄尔多什想到天书时曾感受到的那种东西。在它们能够做到之前，数学家的眼睛将始终不可替代。而哲学——它一直以来关切的正是美、真与善之间的关系——将在数学那里找到自己最严苛的试炼场。

The question machines force upon us is not whether they can match our calculations. They already can, and will increasingly surpass them. The question is whether they can match our sight -- whether they can look at Euler's identity and feel what Hardy felt, what Russell felt, what Erdos felt when he thought of The Book. Until they can, the eye of the mathematician will remain irreplaceable. And philosophy, which has always concerned itself with the relationship between the beautiful, the true, and the good, will have in mathematics its most demanding test case.
