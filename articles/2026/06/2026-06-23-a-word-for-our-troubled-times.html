一个属于我们纷乱时代的词

Peter Wehner·The Atlantic·2026年6月23日·4分钟阅读·原文

弗兰克·布鲁尼（Frank Bruni）在2024年出版的《怨恨的时代》（The Age of Grievance）一书中指出，美国正患有一种文化疾疴。我们愤怒、委屈、沉溺于愤慨。我们不断寻找可以怪罪的人、可以怨恨的人、可以惩罚的人。公民话语已沦为一系列指控与反指控，每一方都比上一方更加顽固。

IN HIS 2024 book The Age of Grievance, Frank Bruni argues that America is suffering from a cultural sickness. We are angry, aggrieved, and addicted to our outrage. We are constantly looking for someone to blame, someone to resent, someone to punish. Our civic discourse has been reduced to a series of accusations and counteraccusations, each side more entrenched than the last.

大量实证数据支持布鲁尼的评估。创纪录高位的成年人——80%——认为美国人在最重要的价值观上存在分歧；几乎同样多的人表示对国家的走向不满。对机构的信任——从国会到媒体到教会——已持续下降数十年。社会纽带正在以难以修复的方式磨损。

Plenty of empirical data support Bruni's assessment. A record high of adults—80 percent—believes that Americans are divided on the most important values; nearly as many say they are dissatisfied with the direction of the country. Trust in institutions, from Congress to the media to the churches, has been declining for decades. The social fabric is fraying in ways that are hard to repair.

就个人而言，根据前卫生总监维韦克·穆尔蒂（Vivek Murthy）2023年的报告，美国人正遭受「孤独与隔离的流行病」。心理健康问题正在增加，尤其是在年轻人中。绝望之死——因酒精、毒品和自杀——正在上升。正如历史学家威尔弗雷德·麦克莱（Wilfred McClay）所写，我们是「不再在自己的国土上感到安适的人民。」

Individually, Americans are suffering from an "epidemic of loneliness and isolation," according to a 2023 report by former Surgeon General Vivek Murthy. Mental-health problems are increasing, especially among young people. Deaths of despair—from alcohol, drugs, and suicide—are rising. We are, as the historian Wilfred McClay has written, "a people no longer at home in our own land."

有时候，美国似乎就是T. S. 艾略特（T. S. Eliot）笔下的「暮色王国」，身处「这垂死星辰的谷地」。公民事业仿佛已经停滞，一种我们最好的日子已经过去的感受广泛蔓延。

It seems at times as if America is a "twilight kingdom," in the words of T. S. Eliot, in "this valley of dying stars." The civic project feels as if it has stalled, and there is a widespread sense that our best days are behind us.

我们所缺少的，用一个词概括，就是沙龙（shalom）。我认为沙龙是圣经中最美的词之一。在新约中，这个词被译为「平安」，但这是一种显著的缩减。沙龙远比平安更丰富、更广阔。旧约学者科尼利厄斯·普兰廷加（Cornelius Plantinga）将沙龙定义为「普遍的繁荣、完整与喜悦」——一种「自然关系正当且各在其位」的状态。

WHAT WE LACK, in a single word, is shalom. I think shalom is among the most beautiful words in the Bible. In the New Testament, the word is translated as "peace," but that is a significant reduction. Shalom is far richer and more expansive than peace. The Old Testament scholar Cornelius Plantinga defines shalom as "universal flourishing, wholeness, and delight"—a state in which "natural relationships are right and are where they should be."

在希伯来语中，沙龙承载着一簇意涵：完整、福祉、和谐、福利、安宁、安全、成功和友谊。它是人类注定生活其中的关联之网，是构成良善社会与良善生活的正当关系之整幅锦缎。当沙龙在场时，人们在存在的一切维度中繁荣——灵性、身体、经济、情感、关系和道德。

In Hebrew, shalom carries a constellation of meanings: completeness, well-being, harmony, welfare, tranquility, security, success, and friendship. It is the web of connectedness in which human beings are meant to live, the whole tapestry of right relationships that makes for a good society and a good life. When shalom is present, people thrive in all dimensions of their existence—spiritual, physical, economic, emotional, relational, and moral.

沙龙不仅仅是冲突的不在场；它是福祉在场。它不仅仅是战争的停止；它是和平的现实。一个国家可以没有正在进行的战争，却仍然缺乏沙龙，因为沙龙要求的不仅是人们不在打斗，而是人们正在繁荣。这一区分至关重要：沙龙不是否定性条件，而是肯定性条件。

Shalom is not just the absence of conflict; it is the presence of well-being. It is not just the cessation of war; it is the reality of peace. A nation can have no active wars and yet lack shalom, because shalom requires not merely that people not be fighting, but that they be flourishing. This distinction is crucial: shalom is not a negative condition but a positive one.

圣经对沙龙的愿景是全方位的：它从个人延伸到政治，从个体延伸到群体。它涵盖朋友、家人和邻人之间的正当关系，也涵盖国家、民族和阶级之间的正当关系。它呼唤正义与公义，呼唤怜悯与同情。它意味着没有人被遗落，没有人被排斥，没有人被遗忘。

The biblical vision of shalom is all-encompassing: it extends from the personal to the political, from the individual to the communal. It encompasses right relationships between friends, family members, and neighbors, as well as between nations, peoples, and classes. It calls for justice and righteousness, for mercy and compassion. It means that no one is left behind, no one is excluded, no one is forgotten.

沙龙的丧失就是圣经所谓的罪——而罪，在圣经的理解中，不仅是个人的过错，而是福祉结构的碎裂。罪是整全的破碎，是关系的断裂，是将社会维系在一起的信任与归属之纽带的拆解。罪增加时，沙龙减少；沙龙增加时，罪减少。

The loss of shalom is what the Bible calls sin—and sin, in the biblical understanding, is not just personal wrongdoing but the shattering of the structures of well-being. Sin is the fracturing of wholeness, the rupture of relationships, the undoing of the fabric of trust and belonging that holds a society together. When sin increases, shalom decreases; when shalom increases, sin decreases.

在《耶利米书》中，先知对以色列的状况做出了毁灭性的诊断。百姓已经背离上帝，结果是混乱：「他们轻忽医治我民的伤口，说『平安，平安』，其实没有平安。」假先知向国家保证一切安好，但耶利米看到沙龙已经丧失。百姓生活在幻觉之中，相信自己安然无恙，实则脆弱不堪。

IN THE BOOK of Jeremiah, the prophet delivers a devastating diagnosis of Israel's condition. The people have turned away from God, and the result is chaos: "They have treated the wound of my people carelessly, saying, 'Peace, peace,' when there is no peace." The false prophets assure the nation that all is well, but Jeremiah sees that shalom has been lost. The people are living in a state of delusion, believing themselves to be secure when they are actually vulnerable.

同样的幻觉也困扰着当代美国。我们不断告诉自己事情还好，或将还好，或本来还好直到对方搞砸了一切。但沙龙缺席的证据无处不在：公民纽带的磨损，相互猜疑的上升，共同目标的侵蚀，孤独与绝望的蔓延。

The same kind of delusion afflicts contemporary America. We keep telling ourselves that things are fine, or will be fine, or were fine until the other side ruined everything. But the evidence of shalom's absence is everywhere: in the fraying of civic bonds, in the rise of mutual suspicion, in the erosion of common purpose, in the spread of loneliness and despair.

一个缺乏沙龙的社会，是一个信任已经崩塌的社会，是人们不再相信邻人怀有善意的社会，是合作感觉不可能、团结感觉天真可笑的社会。在这样的社会中，人们退入各自的角落——意识形态的、地理的、经济的、文化的——并以猜疑甚至公然的敌意看待彼此。

A society that lacks shalom is a society in which trust has broken down, in which people no longer believe that their neighbors are well-intentioned, in which cooperation feels impossible and solidarity feels naïve. It is a society in which people retreat into their separate corners—ideological, geographic, economic, cultural—and regard one another with distrust or outright hostility.

相比之下，一个拥有沙龙的社会，是人们可以彼此依靠的社会，是相互信任与尊重支撑共同生活的社会，是归属的纽带足够强大、足以经受分歧与冲突的社会。沙龙不要求众口一词；它要求信任、善意和对共同福祉的承诺。

A society that has shalom, by contrast, is one in which people can rely on one another, in which mutual trust and respect undergird the common life, in which the bonds of belonging are strong enough to withstand disagreement and conflict. Shalom does not require unanimity; it requires trust, goodwill, and a shared commitment to the common good.

要恢复沙龙，美国需要重建代代侵蚀的信任与归属的习惯。这需要的不仅是政策变更或制度改革，而是超越它们的道德与灵性的更新。在最深的意义上，它需要悔改——一种从怨愤、怨恨和自以为义这些已成为我们默认生存模式的习性中回转。

To recover shalom, America would need to rebuild the habits of trust and belonging that have been eroding for generations. This would require a kind of moral and spiritual renewal that goes beyond policy changes or institutional reforms. It would require, in the deepest sense, repentance—a turning away from the patterns of grievance, resentment, and self-righteousness that have become our default modes of being.

但在圣经传统中，悔改不仅仅是感到歉疚；它是改变方向。它是从死亡转向生命，从碎裂转向整全，从孤立转向群体。希伯来语中悔改一词——特舒瓦（teshuvah）——字面意思是「回归」。它是回归沙龙，回归福祉与正当关系的道路。

But repentance, in the biblical tradition, is not just about feeling sorry; it is about changing direction. It is about turning from death to life, from fragmentation to wholeness, from isolation to community. The word for repentance in Hebrew—teshuvah—literally means "return." It is a return to shalom, a return to the path of well-being and right relationships.

我最近问神学家罗恩·威廉斯（Rowan Williams），如果他不是信仰之人，他身上哪个不可或缺的部分会缺失。威廉斯，前坎特伯雷大主教，回答说：「我想缺失的会是那种感觉——整个生命可以被托置于信任的语境之中。」没有那个信任的语境，威廉斯说，「你就不断防备身后，不断检查外围，不断确认自己没有被背叛。」

I RECENTLY ASKED the theologian Rowan Williams what part of him that is essential would be missing were he not a person of faith. Williams, the former Archbishop of Canterbury, replied, "I suppose the thing that would be missing is the sense that the whole of life can be held in a context of trust." Without that context of trust, Williams said, "you're constantly guarding your back, you're constantly checking your perimeter, you're constantly making sure you haven't been betrayed."

许多基督教信仰之外的人，以及不少信仰之内的人，会说出了偏差，共同的生活已经丧失，我们过着碎片化、孤立、脱节的生活。沙龙正是我们缺失的东西，无论我们是否以此名称之。它是我们注定拥有却在某种程度上丧失的整全——或更准确地说，是我们主动以怨愤、宿怨和怨怼交换出去的整全。

Many people outside the Christian faith, and not a few within it, would say that something has gone sideways, that a common life has been lost, that we are living fragmented, isolated, disconnected lives. Shalom is what we are missing, whether or not we call it by that name. It is the wholeness that we were meant for but have somehow forfeited—or, more accurately, have deliberately traded away for grievances, grudges, and grievances.

基督教信仰——至少在我视为楷模的那些人身上所体现的——并未磨砺人性更粗粝的棱角，而是柔化它们。它并未加深怨恨，而是疗愈它们。它并未使人更加部落化，而是使之更少部落化；并未使人更加猜疑，而是使之更加信任；并未使人更加怨怼，而是使之更加宽厚。换言之，它提供了一条通向沙龙的道路——这恰恰是我们在这些纷乱时代所需要的，也恰恰是我们最匮乏的。

The Christian faith, at least as embodied in the people to whom I look to as exemplars, does not sharpen rougher edges of humanity but softens them. It does not deepen resentments but heals them. It does not make people more tribal but less tribal, not more suspicious but more trusting, not more aggrieved but more gracious. It offers, in other words, a path toward shalom—which is exactly what we need in these troubled times, and exactly what we are most lacking.
