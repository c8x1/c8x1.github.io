永远到不了终点的跑者

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年6月26日·2分钟阅读

在维迪亚（Verdia）的低地，两种人比邻而居：养山羊的牧人，以及猎食山羊的狼人（Wolves）。世世代代以来，他们一直困在同样的较量中。牧人修起更高的围栏，狼人就学会跃过它；牧人培育出更快的山羊，狼人便长得更精瘦、更敏捷。每一年，双方都把所有家当投入，只为比对方强上那么一点；而每一年，双方之间的平衡都纹丝不动。

In the lowlands of Verdia, two kinds of people lived side by side: the Herders, who kept goats, and the Wolves, who hunted them. For generations they had been locked in the same contest. The Herders built higher fences, and the Wolves learned to leap them. The Herders bred faster goats, and the Wolves grew leaner and swifter. Each year, both sides poured everything they had into becoming just a little better than the other, and each year the balance between them stayed exactly the same.

一位名叫索尔（Sol）的老牧人曾爬上山坡，俯瞰他们双方。他发现一件怪事：尽管修了那么多围栏、做了那么多繁育、跑了那么多路，两百年来双方都没有取得任何实质性的进展。狼人捕到的羊并不比从前多，牧人损失的羊也不比从前少。索尔为此困惑不已，因为照理说，那么多的努力、那么多的精进，总该积累成点什么。他原以为终有一方会脱颖而出，让这场较量画上句号。可它始终没有。

An old Herder named Sol once climbed the hill to watch them both. He noticed something strange: despite all the fences, all the breeding, all the running, neither side had gained any real ground in a hundred years. The Wolves were not catching more goats than before, and the Herders were not losing fewer. Sol puzzled over this, because surely all that effort, all that improvement, ought to add up to something. He expected that one side would eventually pull ahead and the contest would end. It never did.

索尔终于明白了。那些精进，从来不是用来领先的——而是用来保平的。当牧人变得更快，狼人也不得不更快，仅仅是为了还能吃上饭；当狼人长出更利的牙，牧人也不得不修起更坚固的围栏，仅仅是为了保住羊群。一方每迈出一步，另一方就迈出同样的一步，两者相互抵消。在这场较量里想要留在原地，双方都必须拼尽全力地奔跑。

Then Sol understood. The improvements were never spent on getting ahead — they were spent on staying even. When the Herders grew faster, the Wolves had to grow faster too just to keep eating. When the Wolves grew sharper teeth, the Herders had to build tougher fences just to keep their herds. Each gain on one side was met by a matching gain on the other, so the two cancelled out. To stand still in this contest, both sides had to run as hard as they could.

索尔把这番话讲给年轻的牧人听，其中一个抱怨道：「那我们这一切劳作又有什么意义？既然永远领先不了，干吗还要跑？」索尔苦笑着说：「因为一旦你停下，你并不会留在原地——你会被甩在身后。在这里，站着不动是不行的。这场比赛没有终点线，抵达也不可能，但放弃意味着失去一切。」年轻牧人回头望向狼人，他们已经在为下一个季节磨砺自己，于是他默默系紧了鞋带。

Sol told the younger Herders this, and one of them complained: 「Then what is the point of all our work? If we never get ahead, why run at all?」 Sol smiled sadly and said, 「Because the moment you stop running, you do not stay where you are — you fall behind. Standing still is not an option here. The race has no finish line, and arriving is impossible, but quitting means losing everything.」 The young Herder looked back at the Wolves, already sharpening themselves for the next season, and laced up his shoes.

这则故事揭示了「红皇后假说」（Red Queen hypothesis）的本质——这一概念借自爱丽丝的那句感叹：在那片奇异的土地上，人必须拼尽全力奔跑，才能勉强留在原地。在生物学中，它意味着物种并不是朝着某个固定的终点线演化，而是与捕食者、猎物、寄生者和竞争者一起，陷入了无休止的军备竞赛。一方每出现一项适应，另一方就演化出对应的反制，于是种种精进相互抵消，相对适应度大致保持不变。生物体必须不断地演化，仅仅是为了不被甩在身后，却永远无法真正领先。

This story reveals the essence of the Red Queen hypothesis — the idea, borrowed from Alice's remark that in her strange land one must run as fast as one can just to stay in the same place. In biology, it means that species are not evolving toward some fixed finish line; they are locked in endless arms races with predators, prey, parasites, and rivals. Every adaptation on one side provokes a counter-adaptation on the other, so improvements cancel out and relative fitness stays roughly constant. Organisms must keep evolving constantly merely to avoid falling behind, never truly getting ahead.
