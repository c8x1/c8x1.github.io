哲学认知科学

为什么你需要全身——从头顶到脚趾——来思考

Anna Ciaunica·Aeon·2026年6月1日·11分钟阅读·原文

人们很容易想象奥古斯特·罗丹（Auguste Rodin）的《思想者》（The Thinker, 1904）被深刻的哲学问题所折磨：「我是谁？这一切的意义是什么，生命是什么？我为什么在这里，既然我并没有签署过一份同意书要在此时此地活着，那么这一切到底是怎么回事？」

One can easily imagine Auguste Rodin's The Thinker (1904) tortured by deep philosophical questions such as 'Who am I? What is the meaning of all this, what is life? Why am I here, given that I haven't signed a consent form to be alive here and now, so what's this all about, really?'

作为一名年轻的哲学系学生，我也曾被这些深刻的问题所折磨，曾经站在巴黎比龙府邸（Hotel Biron）庭院中罗丹雕像的铸件前沉思。我想我在寻找某种东西，这一切的意义。从那以后，在喝掉了足以淹没一座城市的咖啡之后，我仍然没有找到答案。然而有一天，某件事发生了：一次突破，或者不如说一次顿悟。

I too was tortured by these deep questions as a young student in philosophy, and used to ponder them standing in front of a cast of Rodin's statue in the grounds of the Hôtel Biron in Paris. I guess I was looking for something, the meaning of all this. Since then, and after drinking so much coffee that I could flood a city with it, I still haven't got an answer. And yet, one day, something happened: a breakthrough, or perhaps an epiphany.

几年前，我再次回到《思想者》面前，就像年轻时无数次做过的那样：他仍然在那里，仍然在思考，用手托着头部，仿佛那些深刻而沉重的思想已将他的头颅变成了石头。

A couple of years ago, I went back to The Thinker as I had so many times when younger: he was still there, still thinking, holding his head as if all those deep, heavy thoughts had transformed his skull to stone.

就在我寻找合适角度自拍的时候，一个奇迹发生了：我饿了。部分因为天气炎热，部分因为我早上只喝了一杯黑咖啡，罗丹思想者的头颅开始倾斜、融化，他庞大的躯体重量在我脑海中变得清晰可见。仿佛雕塑正在慢慢液化，变成一种植物般的活物，像沙拉，或者黄瓜？反正是某种新鲜的东西，某种我当时就能一口吃掉的东西。然后一些问题冒了出来：思想者喜欢黄瓜沙拉吗？他在哪里长大的？他更喜欢夏天还是冬天？白葡萄酒还是红葡萄酒？他来自哪里？

While searching for the right angle to take a selfie, a miracle happened: I got hungry. Partly because of the heat, partly because I'd had just one black coffee in the morning, the head of Rodin's Thinker started tilting and melting, and the massive weight of his body became visible to my mind. It was as if the statue was slowly liquefying and transforming into a vegetal living thing, something like a salad, or perhaps a cucumber? Something fresh anyway, something that I could have eaten on the spot. And then some questions popped into my head: did the Thinker like cucumber salad? Where did he grow up? Did he prefer summer or winter? White wine or red? Where was he from?

就在那一刻，我意识到自己一直以来都弄错了。我如此痴迷于他思考的大脑，以至于忽略了他的脚趾——更不用说他身体的其他部分了。

And in that moment I realised I had got it all wrong. I was so obsessed with his thinking brain that I had ignored his toes – not to mention the rest of him.

大脑非常时髦。一个极为普遍的观念是，人类心智和认知位于头部，在一个被称为「大脑」的海绵状灰白色器官中，被颅骨和皮肤包裹。当我注视着思想者的头颅、胃里咕咕叫的时候，我意识到自己完全忽视了他的身体。我痴迷于理解他的心智，并通过他的大脑理解我的心智，却完全忽略了他身体的其他部分。和历史上绝大多数科学家和哲学家一样，我也把认知放进头颅，忘记了身体，把它当作纯粹理性思考的障碍物。

Brains are highly fashionable. A very common belief is that the human mind and cognition lie in the head, within the confines of a spongy greyish organ called 'the brain' encased within skull and skin. While I was looking at that Thinker's head, with my stomach protesting, I realised I had completely dismissed his body. I was obsessed with understanding his mind, and my mind via his brain, but I completely ignored the rest of him. Like the great majority of scientists and philosophers throughout history, I too was putting cognition into the head and forgetting about the body, treating it as something that got in the way of pure cerebral thought.

当哲学家并不是我唯一的梦想。

Being a philosopher hadn't been my only dream.

我是在成为一名失意的音乐家之后决定学习哲学的。我的父母——愿上帝保佑他们——认为演唱歌剧和弹钢琴是给来访客人留下深刻印象的酷炫方式，但不会成为一份事业。现在他们后悔当初没有让我走上音乐之路。当来访者礼貌地询问他们的女儿靠什么谋生，他们回答说「她是个哲学家」时，来访者会露出困惑的表情，好像需要进一步解释：「是的，但她具体在做什么？」我的父母不能说「她在思考」，因为那会让他们显得很傻，而他们是非常骄傲和理性的人。如果我是音乐家的话，那就简单多了。每个人都知道音乐家是做什么的。

I'd decided to study philosophy after becoming a frustrated musician. My parents, God bless them, considered that singing opera and playing piano were cool ways to impress the visitors, but wouldn't lead to a career. Now they regret their decision not to allow me to take a musical path. When visitors politely ask what their daughter is doing for a living and they reply: 'She's a philosopher,' the visitors look puzzled as if needing an extra explanation: 'Yes, but what exactly is she doing?' My parents can't say 'she's thinking' because that would make them look silly, and they are extremely proud and reasonable folks. Now, if I were a musician, well, that would have been way simpler. Everyone gets what musicians do.

毫无疑问，现代社会对思维大脑作为人类认知基础有着深深的迷恋。但为什么？这是我们理解「我们是谁」和「人类认知是什么」的唯一入口吗？我在此提出另一个角度。虽然神经元确实令人着迷，但其复杂的活动只是人类认知故事的一部分。

There is no doubt a modern fascination with the thinking brain as the fundamental basis of human cognition. But why? Is this the only entrance point that allows us to understand who we are and what human cognition is? Here I propose another angle. While neurons are indeed fascinating, their complex activity is only part of the story of human cognition.

毕竟，大脑是身体的一部分，而身体由细胞和许多其他成分构成，其中一些严格来说并不属于我们——不像我们独特的DNA那样。世界各地有许多「大脑与身体」研究所，但没有「肝脏与身体」研究所。为什么？因为我们认为肝脏是身体的一部分。但为什么我们如此不愿意把大脑仅仅视为身体的另一个部分？没有证据表明大脑由一种与身体其他部分不同的「物质」构成。

After all, brains are part of the body, and bodies are made of cells and many other constituents, some of which are not literally ours, like our unique DNA is. There are many 'Brain and Body' research institutes around the world, but no 'Liver and Body' institutes. Why is that? It's because we think the liver is (part) of the body. But why are we so reluctant to consider the brain as just another part of the body? There's no evidence that the brain is made of a different kind of 'physical stuff' from the rest of the body.

我提议将焦点从神经处理转向细胞处理，以突出细胞在构成人体等生物自组织系统中的根本作用。

I propose a shift in focus from neural to cellular processing to highlight the fundamental role of cells in constituting biological self-organising systems such as the human body.

从科学和哲学的角度理解我们是谁的旅程，传统上始于一个思考者——是的，通常是一个男性，一位孤独的男性天才——在象牙塔中独自解开宇宙之谜。然而，我们作为有生命的存在，这段旅程开始得远比这更早——当一组细胞开始在子宫深处与另一群细胞协商能量资源的时候。

The journey of understanding who we are, scientifically and philosophically, starts traditionally with a thinking guy – yes, it's usually a guy, a solitary male genius – sorting out the mystery of the Universe from within his ivory tower. Yet, the journey of who we are as living beings starts way before, when a couple of cells start negotiating energetic resources with some other bunch of cells in the depths of a womb.

作为一名哲学家/失意的音乐家/认知神经科学家，我现在在世界各地做学术报告，而我最喜欢做的事情之一就是说下面这句话，同时仔细观察听众的反应：「你们都曾在另一个人的身体里生长过一段时间。」大多数时候，人们的眼睛会睁大，仿佛某个巨大的秘密刚刚被揭示给他们。有些人点头，有些人显得困惑或不以为然。但这些聪明的学者当然知道婴儿不是从天上掉下来的，也不是从卷心菜里蹦出来的？作为成年人，我们都知道自己曾经是婴儿，都曾在另一个人的身体里发育成长。为什么这个生物学和科学事实对我们的知识界来说如此难以承认？为什么我们如此痴迷于心智、纯粹的自我意识，却忘记了身体，尤其是忘记了那个承载和喂养我们自身身体的另一个身体？想想看：早在成为这些可爱的、怀揣希望与梦想和失败的成年人，喝着咖啡、缴着税、在网上读着精深的书籍和文章之前，我们每个人都曾一度是一个单细胞。

As a philosopher/frustrated musician/cognitive neuroscientist, I now give academic talks all over the world, and one of my favourite things to do is to say the following line while carefully scanning the audience for their reactions: 'You have all spent time growing inside another person's body.' Most of the time, people's eyes widen as if some big mystery has just been revealed to them. Some nod their heads, others seem perplexed or in denial. But surely these smart academics know that babies don't just drop from the sky or pop out from cabbages? As adults, we all know that we have been babies ourselves, and that we have all developed inside another person's body. Why is this biological and scientific fact so difficult to recognise for our intelligentsia? Why are we so obsessed with minds, with pure self-conscious minds, while forgetting about bodies, and specifically about the other body that carried and fed our own one? Just think about it: long before being these lovely adults with hopes and dreams and failures, drinking coffee, paying taxes, and reading fancy books and articles online, each of us spent some time as a single cell.

更令人震撼的是：所有人类都是在另一个人体之内，从细胞逐渐发育成人体。怀孕是一个涉及所有人的普遍性人类事实，不仅仅是少数人的事。人体所有细胞都源于一个单细胞——受精卵（zygote），它们紧密互联、相互影响、同步运作以实现共同目标——维持稳态（homeostasis）并持续适应周围环境的变化。这意味着我们来到这个世界时并非某位智者抛下的一块孤石，而是作为深深依赖并与另一个活体相连的生物存在。然后，我们需要他人照顾相当长一段时间，才能找到一块石头坐下来思考生命意义或量子物理这样的深刻问题。

Even more mind-blowing: all human beings gradually develop from cells to a human body within another human body. Pregnancy is a universal human fact that concerns everyone, not just a few of us. All cells of the human organism originate from a single cell, the zygote, are closely interconnected, mutually influence one another, and operate in synchrony to achieve common goals – maintenance of homeostasis and constant adaptation to changes of the surrounding environment. This means that we arrive into this world not on a solitary rock dropped by some mastermind, but as living biological creatures deeply dependent upon and connected with another living body. And then we need others to take care of us for quite some time before we can find a rock to sit on and think about deep things like the meaning of life or quantum physics.

要理解心智，我们首先需要理解我们如何成为心智。我们需要从构成我们卑微脚趾的细胞开始，然后再深入大脑的奥秘。为什么？因为我们的细胞在没有大脑、在我们拥有大脑之前，就已经解决了这场被称为生命的伟大而神秘的冒险中最大、最紧迫的问题：如何活下去。

To understand minds, we need to grasp first how we become minds. We need to start with the cells that compose our humble toes before zooming into the mystery of the brain. Why? Because our cells solve the biggest and most urgent problem of this great and mysterious adventure called life without a brain, and before we had a brain: how to stay alive.

人类是有生命的存在。有生命的存在是自组织生物系统，其身体持续执行着一项卑微却至关重要的基本任务——追踪与自身相关的信息处理，以确保生存。简言之，这意味着你的身体即使在您沉睡或麻醉状态下也在为你工作：你的心脏持续为你跳动，而不是为别人。这也意味着所有身体过程都以自我为核心，原因很简单——它们遵循着支配所有生命系统的普遍驱动力：不要死！自我保存是根本。但正因为我们的身体是一个受自我保存基本法则支配的生命系统，这意味着我们所有的经验必然是具身的自我经验。在感知和体验世界时，我们「夹带」了自身根本性的自我生存目标。这是我们与猫、蠕虫和病毒共同拥有的特征。这是否也是我们与人工系统共同拥有的，则是另一个话题。

Humans are living creatures. Living creatures are self-organising biological systems whose bodies constantly perform the humble yet essential, basic task of keeping track of self-related information-processing to secure survival. In short, this means that your body works for you even when you are asleep or under anaesthesia: your heart keeps beating for you, not for someone else. This also means that all bodily processes have the self at their very core, simply because they obey the universal drive governing all living systems which is: don't die! Self-preservation is fundamental. But because our body is a living system governed by the basic law of self-preservation, this means that all our experiences are necessarily embodied self-experiences. In perceiving and experiencing the world, we 'smuggle in' our own fundamental self-survival goals. This is something we share with cats and worms and viruses. Whether this is also something we share with artificial systems is another story.

因此，身体经验必然与生物自我调节（或稳态）和自我保存相联系。例如，企鹅可以在极低的温度下生存，而人类不能。每种生物都有一套必须维持的最优状态以保持存活。如果你太冷、太热，需要上厕所，或者饥饿到幻想沙拉，那么你就无法正常思考并解决复杂的抽象问题。要让那个思想者能够在石头上沉思生命的意义，他首先必须成功自我调节那些卑微的内在身体状态。简言之，身体就是生命——它是人类存在的必要条件。如果一个人活着，他就是通过身体体验世界的，即使在沉睡中，即使在麻醉状态下。而这个非常简单的观点，对于重新定义我们如何理解认知至关重要。

Bodily experiences are thus necessarily connected to biological self-regulation (or homeostasis) and self-preservation. For example, a penguin can survive in very cold temperatures, while humans can't. Every organism has a set of optimal states it has to maintain in order to stay alive. If you are too cold, or too hot, or you need to go to the loo, or you are hungry and dream of salads, then you can't think properly and solve complex abstract problems. For that Thinker to be able to contemplate the meaning of life on a rock, first he has to successfully self-regulate his humble internal bodily states. In short, the body is life – it is the sine qua non of human existence. If one is alive, one is experiencing through one's body, even when asleep, even under anaesthesia. And this very simple idea is crucial for redefining how we understand cognition.

让我们暂时忘记那个静止地、孤立地站在基座上的思想者，回到那个正在生长或发育中的思想者。她是怎样来到这里的？这个思想者由什么构成？她饿吗？不高兴吗？所有这些问题对于「什么是认知」这个崇高问题来说可能显得愚蠢或不相关，但我在此提出，实际上恰恰相反。

Let's forget for a moment the Thinker, static and isolated on his pedestal, and go back to the growing or developing thinker. How did she get there in the first place? What is this thinker made of? Is she hungry or upset? All these questions may seem silly or irrelevant for the noble question 'What is cognition?' but here I suggest that, actually, it's quite the opposite.

要理解神经元如何运作，以及我们如何从神经元到达心智，我们首先需要回到起点，理解我们如何从细胞到达自我。

To understand how the neurons work and how we get from neurons to minds, we first need to get back to square one, to understand how we get from cells to selves.

来自神经生物学和生物化学的越来越多的证据表明，「感知」「记忆」和「学习」等认知范畴可以非隐喻地应用于细菌等简单生物的行为。先前关于「基础认知」（basal cognition）的研究质疑了只有大脑（即神经元细胞的集合）才具有「认知」或「学习」能力的流行观点。相反，非神经细胞和简单生物也可以被视为主动的、原始的「认知者」。

A growing body of evidence from neurobiology and biochemistry suggests that cognitive categories such as 'sensing', 'memory' and 'learning' can be applied non-metaphorically to the behaviour of simple organisms such as bacteria. Previous work on 'basal cognition' questioned the prevailing idea that only brains (ie, collectives of neuronal cells) have the ability to 'cognise' or 'learn'. Rather, non-neural cells and simple organisms may also be perceived as active, primitive 'cognisers'.

数以千计的细胞必须协同合作，才能产生一个「胚胎」——在解剖学形态空间中导航时实现特定目标。但如果将胚泡（blastocyst）切割成碎片，每个片段都能自组织成自己的胚胎，产生同卵双胞胎、三胞胎等等（可能是连体，也可能不是）。因此，每个细胞都是另一个细胞的「外部」邻居，而这个细胞集合体必须动态地决定胚胎在哪里结束，外部世界从哪里开始。

Many thousands of cells must cooperate toward the production of one 'embryo' – achieving specific goals in their navigation of anatomical morphospace. But if the blastocyst is cut into pieces, each fragment self-organises its own embryo, resulting in monozygotic twins, triplets and so forth (which may or may not be conjoined). Thus, each cell is some other cell's 'external' neighbour, and the collective must dynamically decide where the embryo ends and the outside world begins.

此外，像人体这样动态而复杂的自组织系统必须能够同时打好两手扑克牌，才能生存并可能繁殖。首先，它必须成功地将感官状态维持在一定的生理可行范围之内。其次，它必须灵活地改变这些状态以适应多变的世界。

In addition, a dynamic and complex self-organising system such as the human body needs to be able to play a double poker game, so to speak, to survive and potentially reproduce. First, it must successfully maintain sensory states within certain physiologically viable bounds. Second, it must flexibly change these states to adapt to a volatile world.

现在，假设你是人体这个自组织生物系统中的一个细胞，像所有明智的生命体一样，你痴迷于生存。你还没有大脑，没有神经元可以使用，但你仍然需要想办法活下去。你该怎么做？在你还没有大脑的时候，你如何追踪自我？这是大自然经过数十亿年的试错才不得不解决的一个重大难题。而她做到了！怎么做到的？

Now, suppose you are a cell within a self-organising biological system called the human body and you are obsessed with survival, like all sensible living creatures are. You don't have a brain yet, no neurons at your disposal, but you still need to survive somehow. What do you do? How do you keep track of you when you don't have a brain yet? It's a major puzzle that Nature had to solve via billions of years of trials and errors. And she did! How?

要回答这个问题，我们需要回答以下问题：在生物体层面，哪个基本系统告诉你的细胞哪个是你自己的细胞，哪个不是？世界充满了信号和干扰性的噪声。你必须拥有某种过滤器，让你能专注于对你至关重要的东西，并忽略或抵抗对你不利的东西。这就是免疫系统的工作。对于像人体这样的适应性生物自组织系统，免疫细胞在神经元之前发育，以照顾和追踪自我。

To answer this, we need to answer the following question: which is the basic system at the organismic level that tells your cells which one is your cell, and which one is not? The world is full of signals, and disturbing noise. You have to have some sort of filter that allows you to focus on what is vital for you and to dismiss or fight back against what is not good for you. That's the job of the immune system. For adaptive biological self-organising systems such as the human body, immune cells develop before neurons, in order to take care and keep track of one's self.

我在此提出的新理论认为，所有身体细胞及其复杂的互联对认知都是根本性的，而不仅仅是神经元。在我们的细胞中，免疫系统扮演着非常特殊的角色，与神经系统协同工作，帮助我们构建「自我」。大脑通常被视为演奏你心智协奏曲的管弦乐队指挥。我不赞同。大自然发明了一位更加低调的大师，也许不如大脑及其闪耀的连接那么引人注目，但更为古老且非常高效！

The new theory I propose here considers all bodily cells and their complex interconnections as being fundamental for cognition, not just neurons. Among our cells, the immune system plays a very special role, working in tandem with the neural system to help us build the 'self'. The brain is usually considered the conductor of the orchestra playing the concertos of your mind. I disagree. Nature invented a maestro way more discrete, less showy and dazzling perhaps than the brain and its sparkling connections, but older and very efficient!

代谢自我调节和免疫系统是人体生物体自组织的根本组成部分。它包含一个细胞网络，能够区分自我、非我、缺失的自我和异常的自我，包括错位的细胞以及异常的细胞内和细胞外分子。此外，免疫系统还调节神经系统、行为、代谢和产热，并参与战斗或逃跑反应（fight-or-flight response）。

The metabolic self-regulation and the immune system are fundamental components of organismic self-organisation in humans. It comprises a cellular network capable of distinguishing between self, non-self, missing self, and aberrant self, including misplaced cells and aberrant intracellular and extracellular molecules. In addition, the immune system also regulates the nervous system, behaviour, metabolism and thermogenesis, and takes part in the fight-or-flight response.

神经免疫协同系统充当所有与自我生存相关的传入信息的关键「事实核查员」。从身体的角度来看，不存在感知休息时间或「停电」（除非你已经死了）。身体需要始终追踪「自我」信息。

The neuroimmune tandem acts as key 'fact-checker' for all incoming information relevant for self-survival. From the body's perspective, there is no such thing as perceptual time off or 'blackout' (except when one is dead). The body needs to keep track of one's 'self' information all the time.

作为根本上的身体系统，大脑需要仔细编排并将其神经处理与复杂的其他类型细胞处理网络（特别是免疫系统）对齐，以确保生物体的生存并使其能够继续与世界进行有效互动。重要的是，这一观点已经存在于弗朗西斯科·瓦雷拉（Francisco Varela）及其同事的先驱性思想中，他们花了相当多的时间研究与认知系统相关的免疫系统。

Being fundamentally a bodily system, the brain needs to carefully orchestrate and align its neural processing with a complex network of other types of cellular processing, particularly the immune system, to ensure the organism's survival and to allow it to continue to have viable interactions with the world. Importantly, this idea was already present in the pioneering thinking of Francisco Varela and colleagues who spent quite some time working on the immune system in relation to cognitive systems.

早在神经元开始发育之前，你的免疫系统就需要足够聪明，能分辨哪个细胞是「自我」，哪个不是。如果由于某种原因你的免疫细胞被欺骗，让一种病毒进入，那么我们那位思想者的生命可能就此结束，之后也就不可能在石头上沉思生命的意义了。生命远远在此之前就发生了——在身体的、细胞的层面，没有我们明确的意识参与。可以说，生命在我们未经许可的情况下降临到我们身上。我们中没有谁选择了活着。它就这样发生了。它也发生在我的猫西蒙（Simon）身上！

Well before neurons get off the ground, your immune system needs to be clever enough to tell which cell is 'self' and which one is not. If, for some reason, your immune cells get tricked and let a virus in, say, then that may be the end of our Thinker, and no contemplation of the meaning of life on a rock will be possible afterwards. Life happens way before, at the bodily, cellular level, without our explicit awareness. And life happens to us without our permission, so to speak. None of us chose to be alive. It simply happens to us. It happened to my cat, Simon too!

当我们开始系统性地审视和内省人类心智及其奥秘，将其作为一项科学和哲学事业时，我们不可避免地已经是成年人，因此我们是从成年人的视角来追问世界的。然而，这样做时，我们可能在盲目地使用一副成人偏见的透镜，它阻止我们捕捉心智的根本方面。这个观察乍看之下似乎微不足道，但实际上并非如此。

When we begin examining and introspecting the human mind and its mysteries systematically, as a scientific and philosophical endeavour, inevitably we are adults and hence we question the world from our adult perspective. In doing so, however, we may blindly use an adult-biased lens, which prevents us from capturing fundamental aspects of our minds. This observation seems trivial at first sight, yet it is not.

人类不是像雅典娜（Athena）从宙斯（Zeus）头颅中蹦出来那样出现的，也不是像思想者从罗丹手中诞生那样。相反，我们是被孕育、出生、发育、衰老，最终死亡的。在现实生活中，只有在极少数场合，一个人才能像罗丹的人物那样孤独而孤立地坐在石头上思考。在现实生活中，人需要移动、互动、改变、跌倒、爬起来再次跌倒。大多数时候，我们需要在行动和与世界及他人互动时临场应变。此外，我们很少独处，我们从来不只是抽象的心智，我们是深深具身化的存在，被饥饿驱使、渴望连接。

Humans do not pop out like Athena from Zeus' head, or like the Thinker from Rodin's hands. Rather, we are gestated, born, developing, decaying, and eventually dying. In real life, only at very rare occasions can one afford to stop and think like Rodin's character, solitary and isolated on a rock. In real life, one needs to move, to interact, to change, to fall, to get up and fall again. Most of the time, we need to think on the fly while acting and interacting with the world and others. Also, we are rarely alone, and we are never just abstract minds, we are deeply embodied creatures, driven by hunger and longing to connect.

那么，这篇文章是关于吃沙拉和与母亲建立连接比思考更重要的吗？在某种程度上，是的。你可以不思考而存在，但你不能不进食或不与他人连接而存在（至少不能长久存在）。

Now, is this piece about how eating salads and connecting with your mother is more important than thinking? In a way, yes. You can exist without thinking, but you can't exist (for very long) without eating or connecting with others.

直白地说：人可以不思考而有体验，但不能不体验而思考。经验通过身体浮现在存在的表面，而不是通过心智，也不是通过某种坐在我们头脑中的小矮人，试图去「理解」一个他看不到的世界，因为世界被隐藏在头盖骨的黑匣子里。我们不是通过位于头部的某个孤独的内部透镜来感知世界。我们通过身体的每一个细胞来感知世界。

To put it bluntly: one can experience without thinking, but one cannot think without experiencing. Experiences come to the surface of being through the body, and not through the minds, or some sort of homunculus sitting in our heads, trying to 'make sense' of a world he doesn't see, because the world is hidden in the black box of the scalp. We don't perceive the world through some sort of inner solitary lens situated in our heads. We perceive the world through every single cell of our body.

而且，由于人体首先是在另一个人体内发育的，在我们生命的起点，我们是通过另一个身体来感知世界的。

And because human bodies first develop within another human body, at the beginning of our life, we perceive the world literally through another body.

在怀孕期间，（至少）两个免疫系统需要协商资源和信息的交换，以维持嵌套系统的可行自我调节。怀孕期间两个自组织系统之间的关系和互动，可能是理解人类生物自组织本质的关键。

In pregnancy, (at least) two immune systems need to negotiate the exchange of resources and information in order to maintain viable self-regulation of nested systems. The relationship, and the interactions between the two self-organising systems during pregnancy may play a pivotal role in understanding the nature of biological self-organisation per se in humans.

虽然传统上认为胎盘和胎儿是被动的免疫器官——主要依赖母体免疫系统——但最近的研究揭示了更为复杂的图景。正如生理学研究者吉尔·莫尔（Gil Mor）和英格丽德·卡德纳斯（Ingrid Cardenas）所言，胎盘和胎儿代表「一个额外的免疫器官，影响母体对微生物感染的整体反应」。胎盘中启动的反应类型可能决定母体的免疫反应，从而影响妊娠结局。胎盘是一个活跃的免疫器官，对外来病原体高度敏感。例如，研究表明，胎盘作为胎儿与母体之间物质传输的调节者，而非屏障。胎儿和胎盘都具有活跃的免疫系统，直接影响母体对环境的反应方式。对我们的讨论来说同样重要的是，胎盘免疫系统创造了一个对怀孕友好的保护环境，同时仍然完全运作，能够保护母体和胎儿免受感染。

While it was traditionally assumed that the placenta and the fetus are non-active immunological organs – largely depending on the maternal immune system – recent work suggests a more complex picture. The placenta and the fetus represent an 'additional immunological organ which affects the global response of the mother to microbial infections,' as the physiology researchers Gil Mor and Ingrid Cardenas put it. The type of response initiated in the placenta may determine the immunological response of the mother, impacting the pregnancy outcome. The placenta represents an active immunological organ, highly responsive to foreign pathogens. For example, it has been shown that the placenta functions as a regulator of, rather than a barrier to, the trafficking between the fetus and the mother. Both the fetus and the placenta present an active immune system that has a direct effect on the way the mother responds to the environment. Importantly for our discussion here, the placental-immune system creates a pregnancy-friendly protective environment while still being fully operational and able to defend the mother and fetus against infections.

生命始终是一项集体决策和集体努力。如果真是这样，那么为什么我们要聚焦于孤独的大脑和下棋等高级认知能力来理解认知？我在此提出的激进主张是：一个仅有神经元的生物无法在野外发育、行动和存活。归根结底，这才是思考的意义所在：成功应对生命那混乱而不可预测的丛林，而非国际象棋那种干净而可预测的空间。

Life is always a collective decision and a collective endeavour. If this is so, then why are we focusing on solitary brains and high-level cognitive abilities such as chess-playing to understand cognition? The radical claim here is that a creature with neurons only cannot develop, act and survive in the wild. And, ultimately, this is what thinking is for: successfully dealing with the messy and unpredictable jungle of life rather than with the clean and predictable space of chess-playing.

也许，所有那些关于思考和认知神经模型的现代痴迷，都是为了将我们纯粹心智的天花板抬高，保护我们免受身体中那些不纯粹的、混乱的、不断死亡和新生的细胞的影响。

Perhaps all those modern obsessions about thinking and neural models of cognition were built to raise high the ceiling of our pure minds protecting us from the impure, messy and constantly dying and birthing cells of our bodies.

但如果我们确实是用整个身体的每一个细胞在体验和认知世界，而不仅仅是那个作为指挥的大脑呢？我们所有卑微的身体细胞都参与了我们的经验和认知过程的塑造，而不仅仅是大脑中那些「高贵」的神经元。

But what if we are experiencing and cognising the world with every single cell in our entire body, not just the maestro brain? All our humble bodily cells participate in the making of our experiences and cognitive processes, and not just the 'noble' neurons in the brain.

这真的意味着我们需要整个身体来思考吗？当然，我可以切掉脚趾，仍然可以思考，不是吗？那么说认知不在大脑中、我需要整个身体，到底是什么意思？然而，真正重要的问题是：在你拥有大脑之前，你的身体是「愚笨」的吗？如果是，你是如何在没有神经元的情况下存活下来的？在最初让大脑得以正常生长的过程中，是谁完成了为生存而进行的智慧型信息处理重任？

Does this really mean that we need our whole body to think? Surely I can cut off my toe, say, and still think? So what exactly is meant by saying that cognition is not in the brain and that I need the whole body? However, the really important question is: was your body 'dumb' before you had a brain? If so, how did you manage to survive without neurons? Who did the smart heavy lifting of information-processing for survival, to allow brains to grow properly in the first place?

有趣的是，那座著名的罗丹雕塑原本代表的不是一位哲学家，而是一位诗人——但丁（Dante），《神曲》（The Divine Comedy）的作者。艺术家描绘了这位诗人坐在地狱之门和其他世界的门前，凝思两者之间的空间——边界、生死之间的通道。也许罗丹想向我们展示的是：这一切的意义并非隐藏在人的头脑之中，而在于我们与世界、与他人之间的那个空间。而我在此要说的是，这个迷人的「之间」——在我们的西方社会中还没有一个对应的词语——早在子宫中就开始了，伴随着胎盘，这座神秘而混沌的桥梁，通过另一个活着的身体将生命引向生命。

Interestingly, that famous Rodin sculpture was supposed to represent not a philosopher, but a poet – Dante, the author of The Divine Comedy. The artist represented the poet sitting at the door of the Inferno and other worlds, contemplating the space in between, the border, the passage between life and death. Perhaps what Rodin was trying to show us was that the meaning of all this lies not hidden inside one's head, but in what lies in between us, the world, and others. And here I say that this fascinating 'in between', for which we don't have a word in our Western societies yet, starts already in the womb, with the placenta, the mysterious messy bridge that brings life to life through another's living body.
