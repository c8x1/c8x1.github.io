哲学/伦理学

德州将柏拉图送回他的洞穴

Adam Kirsch·The Atlantic·2026年6月23日·4分钟阅读·原文

托马斯·杰斐逊（Thomas Jefferson）厌恶柏拉图（Plato）。1814年，他写信给约翰·亚当斯（John Adams），说自己读了《理想国》后毫无触动。杰斐逊抱怨说，柏拉图的「梦幻与寓言」不过是「异想天开的幻象」，其道德观「不可理喻」。杰斐逊总结道：「我认为，苏格拉底（Socrates）的不幸在于有了柏拉图这样一个弟子。」

Thomas Jefferson loathed Plato. In 1814, he wrote to John Adams that he had been reading the Republic and came away unimpressed. Plato's "dreams and fables," Jefferson complained, were "whimsical visions" and his morality was "unintelligible." "It was, I think, Socrates' misfortune," Jefferson concluded, "to have had Plato for his disciple."

即便如此，杰斐逊也会对上周德克萨斯A&M大学（Texas A&M University）发生的事深感震惊。春季学期开始前几天，哲学系教授马丁·彼得森（Martin Peterson）——他已出版过多部关于伦理学、技术哲学和心灵哲学的著作——收到了系主任的邮件。系主任通知他，由于大学董事会（Board of Regents）通过的新政策，他不能再开设一门他已教授多年的课程：「性与爱的哲学」（Philosophy of Sex and Love）。

Even so, Jefferson would have been appalled by what happened last week at Texas A&M University. Days before the spring semester began, Martin Peterson—a professor in the philosophy department who has published books on ethics, technology, and the philosophy of mind—received an email from his department chair. The chair informed him that, due to a new policy adopted by the university's Board of Regents, he could no longer teach a course he had offered for years: "Philosophy of Sex and Love."

据《高等教育内讯》（Inside Higher Ed）报道，彼得森在回复系主任的邮件中警告说：「你们正在让德克萨斯A&M因成为英语世界中最哲学倒退的大学而闻名。」系主任回复道：「我毫不怀疑你说得对，但我必须遵守董事会的政策。」

In his email response to his department chair, Inside Higher Ed reported, Peterson warned, "You are making Texas A&M famous for being the most philosophically regressive university in the English-speaking world." The chair replied, "I have no doubt you are right, but I must comply with the Regents' policy."

泰勒·奥斯汀·哈珀（Tyler Austin Harper）：人文学科播下了自我毁灭的种子

Tyler Austin Harper: The humanities have sown the seeds of their own destruction

然而，尽管这种审查既荒谬又阴险，讽刺的是，在所有哲学家中，恰恰是柏拉图被送回了他的洞穴。毕竟，柏拉图是西方传统中思想自由的最初敌人。在《理想国》中，他主张哲学家应当统治社会，但他同时也相信大多数人无力理解哲学，反而会被它腐蚀。

But although this kind of censorship may be absurd and sinister, it is ironically fitting that Plato, of all philosophers, should be the one who is now being sent back to his cave. After all, Plato was the original enemy of intellectual freedom in the Western tradition. In the Republic, he argued that philosophers should rule society, but he also believed that most people were incapable of understanding philosophy and would be corrupted by it.

这一悖论从一开始就困扰着博雅教育的理念。柏拉图相信哲学是最高的知识形式，但他也相信普通人接触哲学是危险的。在著名的洞穴比喻中，他将大众描绘成被锁链束缚在洞穴中的囚徒，观看墙上的影子并将其误认为现实。只有哲学家能爬出洞穴，看到知识的真正太阳——但当他回来告诉囚徒他所见之时，他们会试图杀死他。

This is the paradox that has haunted the idea of liberal education from the beginning. Plato believed that philosophy was the highest form of knowledge, but he also believed that it was dangerous for ordinary people to encounter it. In the famous allegory of the cave, he described the masses as prisoners chained in a cave, watching shadows on a wall and mistaking them for reality. Only the philosopher could climb out of the cave and see the true sun of knowledge—but when he returned to tell the prisoners what he had seen, they would try to kill him.

就这样，柏拉图预见了始终被加诸哲学之上的指控：它腐蚀青年，教会他们质疑权威和公认的观点。苏格拉底因这一罪名被处死，柏拉图毕生致力于表明哲学——若被正确理解——不是破坏性的，而是建设性的。然而他自己对这个问题的解决方案却是威权主义的：他设想的国家由哲人王统治，由他们决定大众可以读什么、听什么、想什么。

In this way, Plato anticipated the charge that is always brought against philosophy: that it corrupts the young by teaching them to question authority and received opinions. Socrates was executed for this crime, and Plato spent his life trying to show that philosophy, properly understood, was not destructive but constructive. Yet his own solution to the problem was authoritarian: the ideal state he imagined was ruled by philosopher-kings, who would decide what the masses could read, hear, and think.

德克萨斯A&M董事会的政策明确以柏拉图所倡导的审查制度为样板，这绝非偶然。该政策规定，课程不得在「社会、政治或文化议题」领域「倡导」任何「有争议」的立场。在实践中，这意味着任何严肃审视争议问题的课程——而这正是每一门哲学课程定义上所做的事——都成了嫌疑对象。

It is no accident that the Texas A&M Regents' policy is explicitly modeled on the kind of censorship Plato recommended. The policy states that courses must not "advocate" for any position that is "controversial" in the realm of "social, political, or cultural issues." In practice, this means that any course that seriously examines a contested question—which is what every philosophy course does, by definition—is suspect.

「性与爱的哲学」成为靶子，使柏拉图的回声更加响亮。在《理想国》中，柏拉图主张国家的守卫者必须在爱与欲望的问题上接受精心教育，因为这些力量最容易使他们偏离职责。性和爱始终位居审查者想要控制的议题之首，因为正是在这些领域，人们最为激情澎湃，也最容易被撼动既有的确信。

The fact that "Philosophy of Sex and Love" was targeted makes the Platonic echo even louder. In the Republic, Plato argues that the guardians of the state must be carefully educated in matters of love and desire, because these are the forces most likely to lead them astray from their duty. Sex and love are always at the top of the list of subjects that censors want to control, because they are where people feel the most passionate and the most vulnerable to having their certainties shaken.

在古希腊世界，人们同样担心哲学对柔软心灵的颠覆效应。阿里斯托芬（Aristophanes）的喜剧《云》（The Clouds）将苏格拉底描绘成一个危险的诡辩家，教年轻人质疑传统道德、用论证摆脱一切义务。该剧写于苏格拉底受审前24年，它帮助营造了导致苏格拉底被判罪和处死的氛围。

In the ancient Greek world, too, people worried about philosophy's subversive effect on tender minds. Aristophanes' comedy The Clouds portrays Socrates as a dangerous sophist who teaches young men to question traditional morality and argue their way out of any obligation. The play was written 24 years before Socrates' trial, and it helped create the atmosphere that led to his conviction and execution.

两千五百年后，德克萨斯A&M大学的董事会正在重演阿里斯托芬剧中角色的同一角色——他以为可以通过嘲讽和压制异见者来保护社会免受颠覆性思想的侵害。然而，正如对苏格拉底的攻击未能摧毁雅典的哲学，对「性与爱的哲学」的攻击也不会摧毁德克萨斯A&M的哲学。审查的冲动始终基于对某些思想过于强大、不宜自由讨论的恐惧，而正是这种恐惧，哲学的存在就是为了驱散它。

Two and a half millennia later, the Regents of Texas A&M University are reprising the same role as Aristophanes' character, who thought he could protect society from subversive ideas by lampooning and silencing their proponents. But just as the attack on Socrates did not destroy philosophy in Athens, the attack on "Philosophy of Sex and Love" will not destroy philosophy at Texas A&M. The instinct to censor is always based on the fear that certain ideas are too powerful to be freely discussed, and this is the same fear that philosophy exists to dispel.

事实是，哲学不是一套能够腐蚀青年的危险教义，而是一种能够解放他们的追问方法。苏格拉底教导他的学生不是思考什么，而是如何思考，正是这一点使他的范例对任何自称珍视自由的社会具有永恒的相关性。

For the truth is that philosophy is not a set of dangerous doctrines that can corrupt the young, but a method of questioning that can liberate them. Socrates taught his students not what to think but how to think, and this is what makes his example permanently relevant to any society that claims to value freedom.

德克萨斯A&M的董事会或许会回应说，他们并不反对哲学本身，只是反对对哲学的「倡导」而非中立描述。但这种区分在实践中站不住脚，因为哲学的全部要旨就是倡导——不是倡导某个特定结论，而是倡导理性探究的过程本身。

The Texas A&M Regents might reply that they are not against philosophy per se, only against its being "advocated" rather than neutrally described. But this is a distinction that does not hold up in practice, because the whole point of philosophy is to advocate—not for a particular conclusion, but for the process of reasoned inquiry itself.

阿齐兹·胡克（Aziz Huq）：美国大学的下一个时代

Aziz Huq: The next era of the American university

柏拉图写作《理想国》时，并非仅仅在描述一个理想国家；他也在为自己的哲学家权威提供辩护。声称只有哲学家能看到真理，自然意味着只有哲学家才应被允许教授真理。这就是为什么柏拉图的理想国家也是一个威权国家，在其中大众被困在洞穴里，而哲人王从上方统治。

When Plato wrote the Republic, he was not merely describing an ideal state; he was also justifying his own authority as a philosopher. The claim that only philosophers can see the truth naturally implies that only philosophers should be allowed to teach it. This is why Plato's ideal state is also an authoritarian one, in which the masses are kept in the cave while the philosopher-kings rule from above.

德克萨斯A&M的董事会极不可能在起草政策之前读过柏拉图。如果他们读过，就会发现柏拉图对审查的论证同时也是对哲学家绝对权力的论证——这不是董事会想要授予其教职人员的那种权力。审查的悖论在于，它总是最终赋予它试图压制的人更大的力量，因为它确认了他们的思想太过危险，不宜自由流通。

It's highly unlikely that the Texas A&M regents read Plato before drafting their policy. If they had, they would have discovered that his argument for censorship is also an argument for the absolute power of philosophers—not the kind of power that a board of regents wants to grant to its faculty. The paradox of censorship is that it always ends up empowering the very people it is trying to suppress, because it confirms that their ideas are too dangerous to be allowed to circulate freely.

相信每个学生都具备这种思维能力并值得体验它，是美国大学最崇高的理想之一。德克萨斯A&M的董事会现在已经表明，他们并不认同这一信念。

The belief that every student is capable of this kind of thinking, and deserves to experience it, was one of the noblest ideals of the American university. The Texas A&M Regents have now shown that they do not share this belief.
