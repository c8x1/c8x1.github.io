为什么智识谦逊并不总是一种美德

麦克林托克的故事与德性认识论的困境

匿名·Aeon·2026年6月6日·16分钟阅读·原文

假设你想成为一个更好的人。（我们很多人都想。）你会怎么做呢？你也许会努力变得更加慷慨，承诺将更多的收入捐给慈善机构。又或者你会努力变得更加耐心，练习倾听你的伴侣，而不是对他们发脾气。这些常识性的建议其实呼应了一种古老的伦理传统。慷慨与耐心是美德——即品格上的卓越，践行它们能使我们蓬勃发展。德性伦理学家告诉我们，过上好生活就是要培养并践行这样的品格卓越。

Suppose you want to be a better person. (Lots of us do.) How might you go about it? You might try to become more generous and commit to donating more of your income to charity. Or you might try to become more patient, and practise listening to your partner, instead of snapping at them. These commonsense prescriptions invoke an ancient ethical tradition. Generosity and patience are virtues – excellences of character, whose exercise makes us flourish. To live well, says the virtue ethicist, is to cultivate and exercise just such excellences of character.

然而，好好生活的一部分是好好思考。我们的灵魂既有实践的部分，也有理智的部分；除非我们在理智上也蓬勃发展，否则就不可能过上充分繁盛的生活。那么，是否存在着专门的智识美德——即智识品格上的卓越，践行它们能使我们成为优秀的思考者？亚里士多德——其著作至今仍是当代德性理论家的试金石——当然这么认为。他在《尼各马可伦理学》中写道，灵魂的理智部分致力于追求真理；因此他认为，智识美德正是使灵魂能够履行这一功能的那些品性。德性伦理学家敦促我们变得慷慨和耐心、节制和勇敢，而德性认识论学家则敦促我们变得深思熟虑和公正，勤勉和开放。

Part of living well, though, is thinking well. Our souls have an intellectual, as well as a practical, part; we cannot live fully flourishing lives unless we flourish intellectually. Are there, then, specifically intellectual virtues – excellences of intellectual character, whose exercise makes us good thinkers? Aristotle certainly thought so. The intellectual part of the soul, he wrote in his Nicomachean Ethics, strives to attain truth. Where the virtue ethicist bids us to be generous and patient, temperate and brave, the virtue epistemologist bids us to be thoughtful and fair, to be diligent and open-minded.

德性认识论学家最为雄心勃勃的主张不仅仅是：这些品格本身具有价值，或者践行这些美德倾向于产生知识；他们进一步主张，我们对知识本身的理解，根本上是依附于我们对这些美德的理解的。如果我知道DNA具有双螺旋结构，那是因为我相信，在类似于我的处境下，一个具有智识美德的行为者也会对DNA持有同样的信念。

At their most ambitious, the virtue epistemologist argues that our grasp of what knowledge is, in the first place, parasitic on our understanding of such virtues. If I know that DNA has a double helix shape, that's because I believe what an intellectually virtuous agent would believe about DNA, under circumstances similar to mine.

像所有事物一样，美德也有流行的起落。其中一种据称的智识美德近来变得尤为时髦。哲学家、心理学家和记者都纷纷敦促我们要更加智识谦逊。不同的思想家对智识谦逊有不同的界定，但有一些反复出现的主题。智识谦逊的人对自己的可错性有敏锐的感知。他们容忍不确定性。他们认识到证据的片面性和模糊性，也认识到自己评估证据的能力的局限。

Like everything else, virtues go in and out of style. One purported intellectual virtue in particular has recently become intensely fashionable. Philosophers, psychologists and journalists all urge us to be more intellectually humble. Different thinkers characterise intellectual humility differently, but there are some recurring themes. The intellectually humble have a keen sense of their own fallibility. They tolerate uncertainty. They recognise the partiality and ambiguity of their evidence, along with the limits of their ability to assess it.

从1800年到21世纪初，智识谦逊鲜少被讨论，但在2010年代初，该特质被提及的次数开始呈指数级增长。因此，对智识谦逊的热情似乎与互联网和社交媒体时代信息管理相关的一系列特定的认识论焦虑密切相关。事实上，智识谦逊常被认为恰好能够抵御社交媒体所滋生的那些病理现象。

Intellectual humility was rarely discussed between 1800 and the early 2000s, but in the early 2010s, the number of mentions began to grow exponentially. Enthusiasm for intellectual humility looks to be bound up with epistemological anxieties related to information management in the age of the internet and social media. Indeed, intellectual humility is often said to guard against precisely those pathologies that social media can incubate.

然而，支撑这些溢美之词的实证研究往往值得商榷。许多研究通过自我报告来评估实验参与者的智识谦逊水平。令人担忧的是，恰恰是那些缺乏谦逊的人才最可能给自己打高分。毕竟，真正谦逊的人不会到处宣扬自己有多谦逊。说自己非常谦逊是一个令人啼笑皆非的自我消解式的吹嘘。

But the empirical work that underwrites these glowing assessments is often questionable. Many studies assess intellectual humility via self-reports. The worry is that it is precisely those who are lacking in humility who are likely to give themselves high scores. Humble people, after all, don't go around talking about how humble they are.

这种人类心理模型强调的是我们的急躁和傲慢。但我们也受制于其他缺陷——懦弱和自我欺骗。而当面对这些其他缺陷时，智识谦逊往往不再起护栏的作用，而更像是一块挡箭牌。西蒙娜·德·波伏瓦世纪中叶的杰作《名士风流》（1954）戏剧化地呈现了这种动态。

This model of the human psyche emphasises our hastiness and hubris. But we are subject to other flaws, too – to cravenness, and self-deception. And when it comes to these other flaws, intellectual humility is prone to function less as a guardrail, and more as an alibi. Simone de Beauvoir's midcentury masterpiece The Mandarins (1954) dramatises this dynamic.

大约在书的中段，一位神秘的陌生人从俄罗斯到来。他向亨利和他的朋友罗伯特出示了文件，表明他们政治希望的北极星——俄国社会主义——依赖于一个残酷的强制劳改营体系。

About halfway through the book, a mysterious stranger arrives from Russia. He presents Henri and his friend Robert with documents showing that 'Russian socialism' – the lodestar of their political hopes – relies on a brutal system of forced labour camps.

亨利和罗伯特对证据的反应截然不同。经过最初的抵触之后，亨利开始相信劳改营是真实存在的。他意识到证据来源太过多元，使他无法令人信服地加以质疑。亨利痛苦地意识到，他不能再将希望寄托于俄国社会主义。在俄国也一样，他心想，一些人也在把另一些人活活累死。

Henri and Robert respond quite differently. After some initial resistance, Henri comes to believe that the labour camps are real. He realises that the evidence comes from too many different sources for him to credibly doubt it. 'In Russia, too,' he thinks, 'men were working other men to death.'

罗伯特的反应则更为犹疑。他选择了怀疑。安娜沉默了。她记录道：我没有坚持。毕竟我有什么权利抗议呢？我太无知了。在波伏瓦的三个角色中，亨利显然是最值得钦佩的。罗伯特和安娜比亨利更忠实地遵循智识谦逊的要求。然而，亨利比他们任何一人都更值得赞扬。

Robert responds with more diffidence. He chooses to doubt. Anne falls silent. Of Beauvoir's trio of characters, Henri is clearly the most admirable. Robert and Anne cleave closer than Henri to the dictates of intellectual humility. And yet, Henri deserves more praise than either.

然而，我们是否能够在智识谦逊与智识懦弱之间做出原则性的区分，并不那么清楚。想象一下另一种可能，其中那些文件全是伪造的。在这样的背景下，我们之前倾向于评估为罗伯特的懦弱，看起来更像是真正的谦逊。教训在于，我们很难将我们对智识品格的判断与该品格在特定情境中行使的结果分离开来。

It's not so clear that we can draw a principled distinction between intellectual humility and intellectual cowardice. Imagine a version where the documents were all forgeries. Against such a backdrop, what we were before inclined to assess as Robert's cowardice looks more like genuine humility. The lesson is that it's hard to isolate our judgments of intellectual character from the results of that character's exercise within a given context.

以遗传学家芭芭拉·麦克林托克为例。1920年代，她还在康奈尔大学求学时就对遗传学产生了浓厚的兴趣，此后数十年间一直在探索玉米的染色体结构。当许多遗传学家因为果蝇繁殖周期快而选择研究果蝇时，麦克林托克却坚持研究更为传统的玉米，花时间真正去了解每一批新植株。

Consider the geneticist Barbara McClintock. She became fascinated by genetics while still a student at Cornell University in the 1920s. At a time when many geneticists studied the fruit fly Drosophila, McClintock stuck with the more traditional maize, taking her time to really get to know each new batch of plants.

我认识田里的每一棵植物。我对它们了如指掌，麦克林托克告诉她的传记作家。她感觉自己与所观察的染色体融为一体。当你注视这些东西时，她回忆道，它们成为你的一部分。而你忘记了自己。

'I know every plant in the field. I know them intimately,' McClintock told her biographer. She felt herself merge with the chromosomes she examined. 'As you look at these things,' she reflected, 'they become part of you. And you forget yourself.'

1950年代初，麦克林托克开始报告令同行们不安的研究结果。她发现基因可以被打开或关闭。一个生物体如何表达某个基因，并非由该基因自身刚性地决定，而是取决于该基因与其他遗传单元如何相互作用。而且，这些控制元件能够在染色体的不同位置之间跳跃。

In the early 1950s, McClintock began reporting on results that disturbed her peers. She realised that genes could be turned 'on' or 'off'. How an organism will express a given gene is not rigidly determined by that gene on its own, but by how that gene interacts with other genetic units. Further, these controlling elements are able to 'jump' between different spots on the chromosomal string.

麦克林托克的同行们对她的工作感到困惑不解。当她首次提出自己的观点时，迎面而来的是死一般的沉寂。然而麦克林托克无视了那些批评者。我就是知道我是对的，她后来坚称。在那次灾难性的报告三十多年之后，她获得了诺贝尔奖。那是1983年，她是第一位独自获得生理学或医学奖的女性。

McClintock's peers were baffled by her work. When she first presented her ideas, she was greeted with a 'dead silence'. Yet McClintock ignored her detractors. 'I just knew I was right,' she later insisted. More than 30 years after her disastrous presentation, she won a Nobel Prize. It was 1983, and she was the first woman to win the prize for physiology and medicine unshared.

按照任何合理的标准，麦克林托克都度过了一个智识上蓬勃发展的生命。但她并不智识谦逊。她为自己对植物的直觉性把握感到自豪。如果麦克林托克更加关注自己潜在的智识局限，她是否还能发展出一套完全属于自己的遗传学研究和思维方式——这就不好说了。

By any plausible metric, McClintock lived an intellectually flourishing life. But she was not intellectually humble. She was proud of her intuitive grasp of her plants. Had McClintock been more attentive to her potential intellectual limitations, it's not clear that she could have developed a way of doing and thinking about genetics that was so wholly her own.

因此，麦克林托克的例子印证了弗里德里希·尼采心理学的一个关键格言。在《善恶的彼岸》中，尼采指出，即使是我们最高贵的冲动，也与我们更阴暗的驱力彻底交织在一起。麦克林托克对玉米植株的热爱与她的自负，她的创造力与她尖刻的顽固——所有这些都构成了一个不可分割的整体。

McClintock serves to illustrate a key dictum of Friedrich Nietzsche's psychology. In Beyond Good and Evil, Nietzsche argued that even our noblest impulses are thoroughly mingled with our darker drives. McClintock's love of her corn plants and her egotism, her creativity and her caustic obstinacy – all these form a cohesive whole.

划定智识美德开始变得像是规定一个画家如果想画好画应该使用哪些颜色。任何颜色的颜料，在合适的手中，都能用来创作一幅美丽的画作。同样，几乎任何品格特质，在足够适宜的环境下，都能成为知识的助力。

Delineating intellectual virtues starts to seem like prescribing which colours an artist should use if they want to paint well. Any colour of paint can, in the right hands, be used to create a beautiful painting. Similarly, almost any character trait can, under sufficiently congenial circumstances, serve as a handmaiden to knowledge.

因此，智识谦逊不是一种美德，因为根本不存在所谓的智识美德。存在的是有时有利于知识生产的品格，和有时不利于知识生产的品格。但关于哪些品格属于哪一类，并没有普遍的规则。对智识美德的追寻，是对规则手册或配方的追寻：一种保证我们能站在真理正确一方的方法。但当涉及到智识的美好生活时，并不存在这样的规则手册或配方。认识论的焦虑与哲学本身一样古老。它值得一个比道德主义的要谦逊训诫更好的回应。

Intellectual humility, then, isn't a virtue, because there are no intellectual virtues. There are traits that are sometimes conducive to knowledge, and traits that are sometimes not. But there are no general rules about which traits are which. The search for intellectual virtues is the search for a rulebook: a way to guarantee that we will find ourselves on the right side of truth. But when it comes to the intellectual good life, there are no such rulebooks. Epistemological anxiety is as old as philosophy itself. It deserves a better rejoinder than the moralistic injunction to be humble.
