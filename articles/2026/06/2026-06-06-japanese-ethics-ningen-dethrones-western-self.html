日本「人间」伦理学如何颠覆西方自我观

和辻哲郎对西方主体性的批判与「之间性」伦理

匿名·Aeon·2026年6月6日·17分钟阅读·原文

和辻哲郎（1889-1960）以其对思想史和伦理思想的全面分析而闻名于世。在日本思想史上，他被视为存在主义研究的先驱——他在1910年代出版了关于弗里德里希·尼采的早期研究著作，并撰写了日语世界中第一部关于索伦·克尔凯郭尔的专著。

Watsuji Tetsurō (1889-1960) is best known today for his comprehensive analysis of intellectual history and ethical thought. In Japanese intellectual history, he is regarded as a pioneer in existentialism studies, publishing an early secondary resource on Friedrich Nietzsche and the first book on Søren Kierkegaard in Japanese in the 1910s.

他对日本伦理学的探索同样以其与西方哲学家和思想的批判性对话而著称，其中包括对黑格尔辩证法以及海德格尔现象学和诠释学的深入批判。

His investigation into Japanese ethics is also known for its critical engagement with Western philosophers and thought, including G W F Hegel's dialectic and Martin Heidegger's phenomenology and hermeneutics.

1934年，和辻通过《作为人间之学的伦理学》奠定了日本伦理学的方法论基础，并在1935年出版的《风土》中提出了日本环境伦理学的最早构想。随后他出版了自己的代表作《伦理学》，该书最初是1937年至1949年间撰写的一系列论文。

In 1934, Watsuji laid out the methodological foundation of Japanese ethics with his Ethics as the Study of the Human [ningen], and gave the earliest formulation of Japanese environmental ethics in Fūdo (1935). He then published his magnum opus, Rinrigaku, written between 1937 and 1949.

在《伦理学》中，和辻主张伦理学就是研究我们作为人意味着什么的学问。我们如何思考人类存在的本性，决定了我们理解伦理价值的方式。因此，他批判了西方哲学对现代主体的构想，认为西方对主体性的阐释既是有问题的，也与东亚和日本哲学数千年来思考何为人间（ningen，人間）的方式格格不入。

In Rinrigaku, Watsuji argues that ethics is the study of what it means for us to be human. How we think about the nature of human existence dictates the ways in which we understand our ethical values. Hence, he criticises Western philosophical conceptions of the modern subject, arguing that the Western rendering of subjectivity is both problematic and foreign to the ways in which what it means to be human (ningen, 人間) has been thought about for millennia in East Asian and Japanese philosophy.

首先，和辻指出，西方主体的构想既是个人主义的，又是自我指涉的，尽管大多数伦理体系都试图将其描绘为普遍适用的。以笛卡尔通过其我思论证所推导出的自我为例。对一位日本读者来说，这不过是一个有闲的法国人沉思自己心灵运作方式的故事。然而，这个西方哲学思维模式却不知何故成为了描述全人类心灵结构的规范性模型。

First, Watsuji shows that the conception of the Western subject is both individualistic and self-referential, although most ethical systems have tried to paint it as being universal. Take the example of the Cartesian ego. To a Japanese reader, this is a story about a Frenchman who could afford the time to meditate on how his mind works. But then this Western philosophical model somehow became the prescriptive model to describe the structure of the mind for all human beings.

现代西方哲学的基本方法论如出一辙：一位来自特定文化和历史背景的哲学家反思自己如何构想心灵的结构，然后宣布这种自我指涉的抽象是适用于一切时空中有情众生的普遍模型。对和辻而言，这存在深刻的问题。

The basic methodology of modern, Western philosophy is the same: a philosopher from a specific cultural and historical background reflects upon how they conceive of the structure of their mind, and declares that their 'self-referential abstraction' is the universal model that theoretically applies to every sentient being across all space and time. For Watsuji, this is deeply problematic.

使得这一构想如此成问题的是，现代主体制造了作为伦理价值源泉的人类主体与作为无意义的在那里的自然之间的冲突。自然被构想为一个他律的他者——对人类自主性的威胁，一个需要被征服的非理性外在实体。

What makes this so problematic is that the modern subject creates conflict between human subject as the source of ethical values, and the objective world or nature as meaningless 'thereness'. Nature is conceived of as a heteronomous other – a threat to human autonomy, an irrational outside entity that needs to be conquered.

至此，西方哲学所描述的那个理性的现代主体，看起来更像是一个不合理的暴君，自认为是最高形式的意识存在；甚至像一个任性的孩子，声称自己是世界可理解性的唯一决定者，而实际上不过是与自己的母亲——自然——断绝了联系。

At this point, what Western philosophy describes as an intelligent modern subject looks more like an unreasonable despot who believes themselves to be the highest form of conscious existence, or even a delinquent child who claims to be the sole determiner of world intelligibility, yet in truth is just severed from its mother, Nature.

日本对人类存在（人間存在）的理解与西方的人性观截然不同。使我们成为人间（ningen）的，既不是本体论结构，也不是理性或精神，而是构成我们人间性（人間性）的之间性的具体实践。而这种之间性的实践总是已经伴随着对空的实践性自觉。

The Japanese conception of human being (ningen sonzai, 人間存在) is radically different. What makes us human (ningen) is not ontological structure, nor reason, nor spirit, but rather the 'concrete practice of betweenness' that constitute our humanity (ningen-sei, 人間性). And this practice of betweenness always already comes with the practical self-awareness of emptiness.

要从日本哲学的角度构想人类存在的本质，我们必须进行双重解构-重构的运动。首先，我们必须解构从欧洲哲学史继承而来的固定的自我概念。其次，我们必须在多元参照中重新定位对人类存在的恰当理解——将其视为在世界之中的一种动态生命。

To conceive of the nature of human existence in Japanese philosophy, we must make a double de-/re-constructive movement. First, we must deconstruct the fixed notion of the self. Second, we must triangulate the proper understanding of human existence as a dynamic life in the midst of the world.

这种固定的、本质化的主体性构想是虚妄的，因为它倾向于将每个人视为一种自我维持的存在，从其社会的、历史的和气候/自然的群体中抽离出去。

This fixed, essential conception of subjectivity is delusional because it suffers from a propensity to view each human as a 'self-sustaining being', abstracted or removed from their social, historical and climatic/natural groups.

世界始终处于流变之中，毫不妥协地保持着动态。然而西方哲学家却屈服于一种诱惑，坚持一种固定的自我概念，以便构建某种普遍的伦理理论。我们承受着殖民思维的问题——这种思维将欧洲人思考何为人类的方式凌驾于所有其他替代性构想之上。

The world is always in flux and uncompromisingly dynamic. But Western philosophers succumb to the temptation to hold on to a fixed notion of the self, so that we can somehow construct a universal theory of ethics. We endure the problem of colonial thinking that privileges the European way of thinking about what it is to be human above all other alternative conceptions.

与此不同，和辻对人间存在的理解采用了源自大乘佛教空之概念的动态个体性与多声部开放全体的辩证结构。一切事物最终都必须归于无，而正因为这种归于无，一切事物才获得了成为其所是的空间。

Instead, Watsuji's understanding of ningen adopts the dialectical structure of dynamic individuality and polyphonic open totality derived from the Mahayana Buddhist notion of emptiness. Everything must ultimately turn to nothing and, thanks to this 'turning into nothing', everything has a space to be what it is.

不同于西方伦理学追求一种将自我视为具有固定本质的永恒概念，日本伦理学致力于觉知自我——将其作为一种暂时的、有限的表达，即作为一个自我空化的人间意味着什么。

Unlike the Western ethics that strives for the perennial conception of self as a being with a fixed essence, Japanese ethics strives to become aware of the self as a temporary and finite expression of what it means to be a self-emptying human.

人间（ningen）一词由两个汉字组成：nin即hito（人）和gen即aida（間）。正如我们可以从汉字人的形状所想象的那样，它表示两个人互相支撑以构成一个人或人格之感。在日本伦理学中，hito即人，既是单数的，也是复数的。

The term ningen consists of two characters, nin or hito (人) and gen or aida (間). As we can imagine from the shape of the Chinese character 人, it indicates two persons supporting each other. In Japanese ethics hito or person is both singular and plural.

第二个字間（aida）由門和日组成。第一个字符象征佛教的寺门，通常是敞开的。第二个字符日象征太阳。和辻指出，将这个象征之间性的字加到人字之上并非偶然。它表明了一个历史洞见：人类个体不能脱离社会-自然整体而存在。

The second character, aida (間), consists of 門 and 日. The first character symbolises a Buddhist temple gate, which is usually open. The second character symbolises the sun. Watsuji shows that adding this character symbolising betweenness to the character of person was not accidental. It indicates the historical insight that human individuals cannot exist apart from the social-natural whole.

人间的之间性是纯粹的转化。自我否定的原理是在存在与非存在之域间的运动，作为一个开放的、富有慈悲之心的有情众生共同体，彼此允许对方以自己的方式表达自身。

The betweenness of ningen is pure transformation. The principle of self-negation is the movement between the zone of being and of non-being, as an open community of compassionate beings who allow each other to express themselves in their own terms.

早在1930年代，和辻就在为去殖民伦理学奠基——他的方式是将伦理学的思想谱系与世界哲学的各个子领域建立关联，而不是寻求普遍伦理。日本伦理学从一个假设出发：每个个体总是在与其社会的、历史的和自然的环境的相互关系中，构成自己对于何为人间的理解。

As early as the 1930s, Watsuji was laying the foundation for decolonial ethics by practising the intellectual genealogy of ethics in relation to each subfield of world philosophies, rather than seeking a universal ethic. Japanese ethics starts with an assumption that each individual constitutes their sense of what it means to be human always already in their interrelation with their social, historical and natural surroundings.
