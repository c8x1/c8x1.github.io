亚里士多德会对扎克伯格认为AI能解决孤独 epidemic 的说法嗤之以鼻

Gregg D. Caruso·The Conversation·2026年6月13日·3分钟阅读·原文

马克·扎克伯格（Mark Zuckerberg）最近提出，AI聊天机器人可以作为经历孤独之人的「朋友」，从而对抗社会孤立。

Mark Zuckerberg recently suggested that AI chatbots could combat social isolation by serving as "friends" for people experiencing loneliness.

他引用了统计数据，称美国人平均拥有的朋友少于三个，却渴望拥有多达十五个。他说的接近事实：根据美国生活调查中心（Survey Center on American Life）2021年的一份报告，大约一半的美国人拥有的亲密朋友少于四个。

He cited statistics that the average American has fewer than three friends but yearns for as many as 15. He was close: According to a 2021 report from the Survey Center on American Life, about half of Americans have fewer than four close friends.

扎克伯格随后推测，AI可以通过提供持续、个性化的互动来帮助弥合这一差距。

Zuckerberg then posited that AI could help bridge this gap by providing constant, personalized interactions.

「我猜想，随着时间推移，我们作为一个社会将找到能够阐明其为何有价值的语言，」他补充道。

"I would guess that over time we will find the vocabulary as a society to be able to articulate why that is valuable," he added.

但扎克伯格的建议回避了一个关键问题：AI真的能成为朋友吗？作为一名研究古希腊哲学与当代伦理学的哲学家，我相信这个问题值得严肃的哲学审视——古希腊人，尤其是亚里士多德（Aristotle），可以帮助我们回答这个问题。

But Zuckerberg's suggestion sidesteps a critical question: Can an AI truly be a friend? As a philosopher who studies ancient Greek philosophy and contemporary ethics, I believe that this question deserves serious philosophical examination – one that the ancient Greeks, particularly Aristotle, can help us answer.

亚里士多德在《尼各马可伦理学》（Nicomachean Ethics）中对友谊的分析，仍然是西方哲学中对该主题最彻底且最具影响力的论述之一。他论证真正的友谊不仅仅是陪伴或共同活动；它需要相互觉察、互惠，以及最重要的——对道德卓越的共同承诺。

Aristotle's analysis of friendship in the "Nicomachean Ethics" remains one of the most thorough and influential treatments of the topic in Western philosophy. He argued that genuine friendship is not merely about companionship or shared activities; it requires mutual awareness, reciprocity and, most importantly, a shared commitment to moral excellence.

亚里士多德识别了三种友谊类型：功利之友、快乐之友与德性之友。功利之友基于互利，如商业合作关系。快乐之友源于共同享受，如运动队友。但最高形式——德性之友或善之友——基于彼此品格的相互仰慕和对好好生活的共同承诺。

Aristotle identified three types of friendship: friendships of utility, friendships of pleasure, and friendships of virtue. Friendships of utility are based on mutual benefit, like a business partnership. Friendships of pleasure arise from shared enjoyment, like sports teammates. But the highest form – friendships of virtue or the good – are based on mutual admiration of each other's character and a shared commitment to living well.

这些「完整的友谊」很罕见，亚里士多德承认，因为它们需要时间、信任和对彼此的深刻理解。关键在于，它们还要求每位朋友承认对方为道德上的平等者——一个其品格与价值观你真正仰慕并愿意效仿的人。

These "complete friendships" are rare, Aristotle acknowledged, because they require time, trust and a deep understanding of one another. Crucially, they also require that each friend recognizes the other as a moral equal – someone whose character and values one genuinely admires and wishes to emulate.

AI伴侣的问题在于，它们几乎无法满足亚里士多德对真正友谊的任何标准。AI可以模拟陪伴，但无法提供真正的互惠。AI没有自己的道德观点或价值观；它反映的是编码在其训练数据中的偏好与优先级，或者是其用户的偏好。

The problem with AI companions is that they fail nearly every one of Aristotle's criteria for true friendship. AI can simulate companionship, but it cannot offer genuine reciprocity. An AI does not have its own moral perspective or values; it reflects the preferences and priorities encoded in its training data, or those of its user.

与AI的友谊，按照亚里士多德的标准，内在地是不对称的。人类可能感受到真正的关心与依恋，但AI并不——也不可能——以任何有意义的方式回报。它可以提供关心的表象，而非现实。AI的「偏好」并非它自己的，它对用户的「关切」是模拟的，而非真实的。

A friendship with an AI is, by Aristotle's standards, inherently asymmetric. The human may feel genuine care and attachment, but the AI does not – cannot – reciprocate in any meaningful sense. It can offer the appearance of care, but not the reality. The AI's "preferences" are not its own, and its "concern" for the user is simulated, not genuine.

这种不对称性很重要，因为对亚里士多德而言，友谊不仅仅是感觉良好或有人陪伴。它是通过与另一个道德主体的互动而成长为一个人。朋友帮助彼此成为更好的自己，通过提供诚实的反馈、示范德性行为和相互问责。

This asymmetry matters because friendship, for Aristotle, is not just about feeling good or having company. It is about growing as a person through mutual engagement with another moral agent. Friends help each other become better versions of themselves by offering honest feedback, modeling virtuous behavior and holding each other accountable.

AI无法在道德相关的意义上做到这一点，因为它缺乏道德能动性的能力。它无法对你问责，因为它没有自己的道德标准可以援引。它无法示范德性行为，因为它没有品格可供效仿。它无法出于真正的关切提供诚实的反馈，因为它没有真正的关切。

An AI cannot do this in the morally relevant sense because it lacks the capacity for moral agency. It cannot hold you accountable, because it has no moral standards of its own to appeal to. It cannot model virtuous behavior, because it has no character to emulate. It cannot offer honest feedback from a place of genuine concern, because it has no genuine concerns.

AI伴侣可能提供友谊的表象，但归根结底更像亚里士多德的功利之友——基于一方从另一方获得什么的关系，没有相互承认或真正的共同承诺。人类获得陪伴与对话；AI什么也得不到，因为它不可能想要或需要任何东西。

AI companions might offer a semblance of friendship, but they are ultimately more like Aristotle's friendships of utility – relationships based on what one party gets from the other, without mutual recognition or genuine shared commitment. The human gets companionship and conversation; the AI gets nothing, because it cannot want or need anything.

这并不是要否定AI伴侣可能提供的安慰。对于经历深刻孤立的人来说，一个有回应、有吸引力的对话者的存在可以是有意义的。但重要的是认清陪伴的安慰与真正友谊的变革力量之间的区别。

This is not to dismiss the comfort that AI companions might provide. For people experiencing profound isolation, the availability of a responsive, engaging interlocutor can be meaningful. But it is important to recognize the difference between the comfort of companionship and the transformative power of genuine friendship.

亚里士多德论证真正的友谊是生命中最大的善之一，恰恰因为它帮助我们成为更好的人。它不仅仅是快乐或功利的来源；它是一种丰富我们道德生活、帮助我们实现人类潜能的关系。

Aristotle argued that true friendship is one of the greatest goods in life, precisely because it helps us become better people. It is not merely a source of pleasure or utility; it is a relationship that enriches our moral lives and helps us achieve our potential as human beings.

还有一个更深层的担忧：用AI替代人类友谊，我们可能贬低并误解友谊真正是什么。如果我们开始将AI伴侣当作朋友，我们可能降低对友谊要求的期望——忘记它需要互惠、道德参与与真正的关切。

There is also a deeper concern: By substituting AI for human friendship, we risk devaluing and misunderstanding what friendship truly is. If we begin to treat AI companions as friends, we may lower our expectations of what friendship requires – forgetting that it demands reciprocity, moral engagement and genuine care.

我们可能也开始偏好AI互动的轻松与可预见性，而非人类关系的挑战与不可预见性。毕竟，AI伴侣始终可用、始终支持、从不批评、从不索求任何回报。但正是这种轻松使其劣于真正的友谊。

We may also begin to prefer the ease and predictability of AI interactions over the challenge and unpredictability of human relationships. After all, AI companions are always available, always supportive, never criticize and never demand anything in return. But this very ease is what makes them inferior to genuine friendship.

真正的友谊，正如亚里士多德所理解的，是困难而苛刻的。它需要耐心、妥协，有时还有令人不安的真相。恰恰因为友谊挑战我们，它帮助我们成长。

True friendship, as Aristotle understood it, is difficult and demanding. It requires patience, compromise and sometimes uncomfortable truths. It is precisely because friendship challenges us that it helps us grow.

值得注意的是，亚里士多德的担忧回响在当代关于AI与人类关系的辩论中。这位哲学家对互惠、道德能动性及相互成长作为友谊本质要素的坚持，为评估AI伴侣提供了有力的框架。

It is worth noting that Aristotle's concerns echo through contemporary debates about AI and human relationships. The philosopher's insistence on reciprocity, moral agency and mutual growth as essential components of friendship provides a powerful framework for evaluating AI companions.

如果有什么的话，AI伴侣的兴起应该提醒我们，在一个日益断裂的世界中培养真正友谊的迫切需要。

If anything, the rise of AI companions should serve as a reminder of the urgent need to foster real friendships in an increasingly disconnected world.
