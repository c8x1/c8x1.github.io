有些人如何能够逆道德潮流而行？

Anneli Jefferson·Aeon·2026年6月9日·12分钟阅读·原文

欧内斯特·海明威（Ernest Hemingway）曾宣称：「所有现代美国文学都源自马克·吐温（Mark Twain）的那本叫作《哈克贝利·费恩历险记》（Huckleberry Finn）的书……这是我们所拥有的最好的书。」这部1884年出版的小说之所以如此出色，其中一个原因便是其同名主人公哈克（Huck）。他在战前的南方长大，却明知故犯地违背了当时的道德主流，决心保护姨妈家的逃亡奴隶吉姆（Jim）。故事的高潮恰与哈克的良知危机重合：

Ernest Hemingway once declared that 'All modern American literature comes from one book by Mark Twain called Huckleberry Finn … it's the best book we've had.' One of the features that makes the 1884 novel so good is its eponymous protagonist, Huck. Raised in the antebellum South, he goes, knowingly and willingly, against the moral grain when he commits to protecting his aunt's runaway slave, Jim. The story's climax coincides with Huck's crisis of conscience:

我得在两件事之间[保护吉姆和告发他]做出永远的决定，我明白这一点。我琢磨了一分钟，屏住呼吸，然后对自己说：「好吧，那我就下地狱吧」……

I'd got to decide, forever, betwixt two things [protecting Jim and turning him in], and I knowed it. I studied a minute, sort of holding my breath, and then says to myself: 'All right, then, I'll go to hell' …

在相信自己正在犯罪这件事上，哈克显然是他那个时代和地域的产物。但他终究做了正确的事，这又表明他显然不止于此。然而，何为逆流而上？哈克又是如何做到的？

In believing he's committing a sin, Huck is clearly a product of his time and place. In doing the right thing anyway, he is clearly more than that. But what is it to go against the grain and how is Huck able to do it?

哲学家们用「道德能动性」（moral agency）这一概念来描述我们做出正确或错误决定的能力。我们是能动者，因为我们有能力负责任地选择可以被评判为道德上善或恶的行为。动物、婴儿以及某些患有特定精神障碍的人似乎不是道德能动者，因为他们对自己的行为不承担同等意义上的责任。但这引出了激烈争论的问题：道德能动性从何而来？我们如何能够做出道德决定？这些问题的答案居于道德本质的核心。

Philosophers use the term 'moral agency' to describe our ability to make right or wrong decisions. We are agents in the sense that we are capable of responsibly choosing actions that can be morally good or morally bad. Animals and babies and people suffering from certain mental problems don't seem to be moral agents because they're not responsible for their actions in the same way. But this leads to hotly contested questions: where does moral agency come from? How are we able to make moral decisions? The answer to these questions lies at the heart of the nature of morality.

初步来看，我们可能认为道德在本质上要么主要是个体的，要么主要是社会的。前者与所谓康德式（Kantian）的道德观相一致，这种观点强调规则和原则高于美德和实践，强调理性和义务高于欲望和品格。其最终关注的是个体意志的自主性。另一种观点则无法归诸单一作者，它强调我们的道德概念和价值的共同体根源，以及道德共同体在塑造这些概念和价值、塑造我们对它们的回应方面所扮演的角色。从这个角度看，道德能动性与道德的本质是无法分离的。

On a first pass, we might think of morality as being either primarily individual or primarily social in nature. The first aligns with what is referred to as the Kantian conception of morality, which emphasises rules and principles over virtues and practices; rationality and duty over desire and character. Its ultimate concern is the autonomy of the individual will. An alternative view, which cannot be linked to a single author, emphasises the communal roots of our moral concepts and values, and the role of the moral community in shaping them and our responsiveness to them. Moral agency cannot be prized apart, in this light, from the nature of morality.

尽管存在这种二分，人们广泛认同道德与社会性是相互关联的。一个合理的道德能动性理论需要找到一种方式来解释这种关联。按照我所偏好的「生态学」路径（ecological approach），社会生态学是根本性的：我们的道德能力在很大程度上（即使不是完全）是它的产物。生态学路径的支持者面临的挑战在于解释像哈克这样的个体如何能够做出与这一生态相冲突或在其中不存在的行为。这一挑战值得应对，因为另一种选择——在道德能动性的叙事中优先考虑个体——无法解释道德的社会性质，进而也无法解释道德能动性的社会性质。

Despite this dichotomy, there is wide agreement that morality and sociality are interlinked. A plausible view of moral agency will find a way to account for this linkage. On my favoured 'ecological' approach, the social ecology is fundamental: our moral capacities are largely, if not entirely, products of it. The challenge for ecological proponents is to explain how individuals like Huck can do things that conflict with or are absent from this ecology. This challenge is worth taking on because the alternative – prioritising the individual in the story of moral agency – fails to account for the social nature of morality and, by extension, moral agency.

没有道德受众的关键性支架（赞扬与责备），我们无法成为良好的道德能动者。

We cannot be good moral agents without the critical scaffolding (praise and blame) of a moral audience.

根据哲学曼努埃尔·巴尔加斯（Manuel Vargas）——生态学路径的支持者——的观点，不同形式的文化教化会导致不同的道德能力。在一个重视种族平等的社会中长大的人，比在一个种族主义社会中长大的人更有可能反对种族主义。巴尔加斯在《塑造更好的存在》（Building Better Beings, 2013）一书中论证道，个体层面的和结构性的压迫会影响被压迫者和压迫者分辨是非的能力，并削弱他们对不当行为的责任感。切希尔·卡尔霍恩（Cheshire Calhoun）同样认为，压迫者有时可能不具备可责备性。这是因为压迫性的不当行为发生在社会实践本身的层面上，而社会对这些实践的接纳阻碍了压迫者对不当行为的意识。

According to the philosopher Manuel Vargas, who is a proponent of the ecological approach, different forms of acculturation result in differing moral capacities. Someone who has grown up in a society that values racial equality is much more likely to be anti-racist than someone brought up in a racist society. Individual and structural oppression, Vargas argues in Building Better Beings (2013), can affect the ability of both oppressed individuals and their oppressors to discern right from wrong, and undermine their responsibility for wrongdoing. Cheshire Calhoun similarly suggests that oppressors might sometimes lack blameworthiness. This is because oppressive wrongdoing happens at the level of the social practices themselves, and social acceptance of these practices impedes an oppressor's awareness of wrongdoing.

巴尔加斯特别关注责备和赞扬实践，因为它们在塑造我们对道德考量的回应能力方面发挥着作用。虽然他的兴趣主要在于为这些实践辩护，但其他生态学路径的支持者（维多利亚·麦吉尔（Victoria McGeer）、菲利普·佩蒂特（Philip Pettit）和安妮莉·杰斐逊（Anneli Jefferson））则根据这一角色来定义道德能动性。他们认为，道德能动者只需具备对道德受众的责备和赞扬的敏感度。人们有动力去满足他们认为属于这一受众（他们的道德共同体成员）的道德期望。由于这些期望通过赞扬和责备来传达，通过恰当地敏感于责备和赞扬，我们有望获得对相关道德考量的理解。负责任的个体在决定最佳行动方案时，会关心潜在受众可能如何评价他们。

Vargas is especially interested in blame and praise practices because of their role in shaping our responsiveness to moral considerations. While his interest is primarily in vindicating these practices, other ecological proponents (Victoria McGeer, Philip Pettit, and Anneli Jefferson) define moral agency in terms of this role. Moral agents, they say, need only possess a sensitivity to the blame and praise of their moral audience. People are motivated to fulfil the moral expectations of those they take to be a part of this audience (their fellow moral community members). Since these expectations are communicated via praise and blame, in being appropriately sensitive to blame and praise, we stand to gain an appreciation of the relevant moral considerations. Responsible individuals care about how a potential audience might evaluate them when deciding the best course of action.

按照这种「支架」版本的生态学观点，没有道德受众的关键性支架（赞扬与责备），我们无法成为良好的道德能动者。支架论的支持者举了一位名叫简（Jane）的警察的例子：在多年的执法过程中，她逐渐丧失了对适当使用武力的判断力。通过想象受众会如何评判她处理激烈逮捕事件的方式，或通过调适于她的实际受众（以及他们的手机摄像头），简警官能够批判性地审视自己。这促使她反思适当使用武力背后的理由，她对那些理由的理解得以重新确立。

According to this 'scaffolding' version of the ecological view, we cannot be good moral agents without the critical scaffolding (praise and blame) of a moral audience. Scaffolding proponents give the example of a police officer, Jane, who, over the course of many years loses her sense of appropriate use of force. By imagining how an audience would react to her handling of a heated arrest, or attuning to her actual audience (and their phone cameras), Officer Jane is able to view herself critically. This prompts her to reflect on the reasons behind appropriate use of force, and her appreciation of these reasons is renewed.

支架论的支持者认为，这个例子清楚地表明，我们道德行动的能力取决于我们的环境。他们说，这种依赖性首先归因于道德的高度人际性、复杂性和动态性，其次归因于我们道德能力的脆弱性。只要至少有一些道德考量是人际性的——不管道德是否严格来说就是T·M·斯坎伦（T M Scanlon）所论证的那种「我们彼此亏欠的东西」——我们自然而然地依赖社会互动来获得完整的道德知识。赞扬和责备的规劝力量为我们对这些考量的回应能力提供了支架。

This example, scaffolding proponents suggest, makes clear that our ability to act morally depends on our environment. This dependence, they say, owes first to the highly interpersonal, complex and dynamic nature of morality, and, second, to the fragility of our moral capacities. Provided that at least some moral considerations are interpersonal, whether or not morality is strictly a matter of what we owe to each other, as T M Scanlon has argued, we naturally depend on social interactions for full moral knowledge. The exhortative power of praise and blame scaffold our responsiveness to these considerations.

规范性图景的复杂性也可以通过不断变化的认知和物质环境来解释。当新技术出现时，无论它们是否带来新的道德义务，我们都可能需要协商履行现有义务的方式。随着手机日益普及，诸如不在开车时发短信或与朋友共餐时不看手机等适当的使用标准，必须经过协商。麦吉尔认为，这种协商通过与批判性道德受众的交流来完成。

The complexity of the normative landscape can also be explained by ever-changing epistemic and material circumstances. When new technologies emerge, whether or not they necessitate new moral obligations, we may have to negotiate the means of fulfilling existing ones. As mobile phones became increasingly prevalent, appropriate standards of use, such as not texting while driving or when dining with a friend, had to be negotiated. This negotiation happens, McGeer suggests, through exchanges with a critical moral audience.

至于我们道德能力的脆弱性，主流观点——由当代哲学家如约翰·多里斯（John Doris）表达，其渊源至少可追溯至柏拉图（Plato）《理想国》（Republic）中的「古格斯之环」（ring of Gyges）——认为我们很容易受到外部压力和心理因素的影响。实证研究表明，当我们遇到一点好运或知道有人在观察时，我们更有可能做正确的事。在古格斯之环的故事中，柏拉图暗示，即使是一个普通人，如果获得了隐身和免于惩罚的能力，也会做出恶行。正是基于这幅图景，支架论的支持者论证了我们对批判性道德受众的支架的持续依赖。他们认为，即使是有德之人也需要这样的支架来保持正轨。

As to the fragility of our moral capacities, the predominant view, expressed by contemporary philosophers like John Doris and with roots going at least as far back as Plato's 'ring of Gyges' in the Republic, is that we are easily affected by external pressures and psychological factors. Empirical studies show that we are more likely to do the right thing when we've had a bit of good luck or when we know we're being observed. With the ring of Gyges, Plato suggests that even an ordinary person would act viciously if granted the power of invisibility and impunity. It is with this picture in mind that scaffolding proponents argue for our perpetual dependence on the scaffolding of a critical moral audience. Even the virtuous, they say, require such scaffolding to stay on the right track.

把符合受众期望的正确行为解释为这种支架的产物是容易的。把违背这些期望的错误行为解释为支架不足（或对其敏感度不足）的结果也是容易的。然而，这并不适用于我所说的逆流而上的情形——在这些情形中，个体出于真正的道德考量而行动，违背了其受众错误的道德期望。既然我们的道德能动性确实是我们的生态的产物，我们如何解释看起来像是根据超越这一生态的道德价值来行动的情形呢？

It would be easy to explain right action that conforms to an audience's expectations as a product of this scaffolding. It would be easy to explain wrong action that goes against these expectations as the result of insufficient scaffolding (or insufficient sensitivity to it). However, this is not the case for the going-against-the-grain cases I have in mind, where individuals act for genuine moral considerations, against their audience's false moral expectations. Insofar as our moral agency truly is a product of our ecology, how do we explain something that appears to involve acting on moral values from beyond that ecology?

哈克对其支持奴隶制的道德受众的期望是敏感的。需要解释的是他对这一期望的拒斥。

Huck is sensitive to the expectation of his pro-slavery moral audience. What needs explaining is his repudiation of this expectation.

乍看之下，支架论的支持者有三种可能的回应。第一种是否认逆流而上这种事情真的存在；它只是看起来如此。哈克确实违背了某个道德受众的主流，但那并非他真正的道德受众。他真正的道德受众——他实际上对其期望敏感的那个受众——由废奴主义者组成，或者也许只是吉姆一个人。我们可以想象，在他们共同经历的艰难困苦中，吉姆的道德反馈对哈克变得比他原来道德受众的谴责更为重要。他逐渐感到自己主要对吉姆负责。在这种描述下，仍然可以把哈克的正确行为归因于相关的生态能力——他对受众的敏感度。因此我们可以说，他是顺着他真正道德受众的主流而行，而非逆流而上。

There are, at first blush, three responses available to scaffolding proponents. The first is to deny that there is such a thing as going-against-the-grain; it only appears that there is. Huck goes against the grain of a moral audience, but not his true moral audience. His true moral audience, the audience to whose expectations he is in fact sensitive, is composed of abolitionists or, perhaps, just Jim. We can imagine that, during their trials and tribulations together, Jim's moral feedback begins to matter more to Huck than the condemnation of his original moral audience. He grows to feel answerable primarily to Jim. Under this description, it is still possible to attribute Huck's right action to the relevant ecological capacity, his sensitivity to audience. Accordingly, we could say that he goes with the grain of his true moral audience, not against it.

这种回应的问题在于，它将要求我们接受以下两个互斥的主张：我们完全依赖道德受众，同时我们又具备选择那个受众的能力。这种能力将需要一种程度的受众独立性，而这是第一个主张所排除的。哈克对其支持奴隶制的道德受众是敏感的——对他们的谴责及其隐含的期望（告发吉姆）是敏感的。哈克的良知危机反映了这种敏感度。需要解释的是他对这一期望的拒斥。

The problem with this response is that it would require accepting the following two mutually exclusive claims: we wholly depend on a moral audience and we have the ability to choose that audience. Such an ability would require a degree of audience-independence precluded by the first claim. Huck is sensitive to his pro-slavery moral audience – to their condemnation and its implicit expectation (to turn Jim in). Huck's crisis of conscience reflects that sensitivity. What needs explaining is his repudiation of this expectation.

第二种回应（由朱尔斯·霍尔罗伊德（Jules Holroyd）提出）认为，逆流而上者回应的是一个理想道德受众的反馈，而非他们实际的、有缺陷的道德受众的反馈。理想受众会认同正确的价值观，并据此影响行为。哈克的理想受众自然是废奴主义的。按照这种回应策略，他帮助吉姆的决定是因为被想象中来自理想受众成员的赞扬所打动——也许是一位慈祥的祖父般的人物对这件善举的赞许。

The second response (proposed by Jules Holroyd), is that going-against-the-grainers are responsive to the feedback of an ideal moral audience rather than their actual, flawed moral audience. An ideal audience is going to endorse the right values and influence behaviour accordingly. Huck's ideal audience would, naturally, have been abolitionist. According to this response strategy, his decision to help Jim is the result of having been moved by the praise he imagined being bestowed upon him (for this good deed) by one of its members, a grandfatherly figure, perhaps.

对这种回应可以提出与第一种回应类似的反对意见。正如安妮莉·杰斐逊和卡特里娜·西弗德（Katrina Sifferd）所指出的，挑出一个理想道德受众并认识到其期望才是正确的那种能力，需要一种生态独立性，而支架论观点恰恰排除了这种独立性。既然我们对道德期望的认知是通过这一受众获得的，那么它就局限于该受众的期望。同样不清楚的是，想象的、理想化的反馈是否会对我们产生任何影响。假设一个人能够唤起想象中的怨恨，这种怨恨是否能劝退另一个被强烈情绪所裹挟的人？

A similar objection as to the first response can be raised against this one. As Anneli Jefferson and Katrina Sifferd point out, the ability to pick out an ideal moral audience and to recognise its expectations as the right ones requires a degree of ecology-independence precluded by the scaffolding view. Insofar as our knowledge of moral expectations is acquired via this audience, it is limited to its expectations. It also isn't clear that imaginary, idealised feedback would hold any sway over us. Would one person's imaginary resentment, assuming it could be conjured up, dissuade another person in the grip of intense emotion?

第三种、也是最有希望的回应是调和性的。它承认支架论观点在道德能动性与生态之间的关系上确实有某种失误，同时试图以某种形式保留这一关系。要么我们并不完全依赖我们的生态，要么——如果我们确实如此——我们依赖的是它的多个特征，而不仅仅是道德受众。对于前者，我们应当弱化道德能动性与生态之间的联系。某些道德能力（至少对某些个体而言）天然就足够强健。可以说，逆流而上者的天生道德指南针只是比他们的同侪更强大。

The third and most promising response is conciliatory. It concedes that the scaffolding view gets something wrong about the relationship between moral agency and ecology, even while seeking to preserve it in some form. Either we don't wholly depend on our ecology or, if we do, we depend on multiple features of it, not just the moral audience. For the first, we should weaken the link between moral agency and ecology. Some moral capacities (for at least some individuals) are sufficiently robust by nature. The innate moral compass of going-against-the-grainers, we could say, is simply stronger than that of their fellows.

对于后者——它维持道德能动性与生态之间的强联系——我们应当将道德生态学的视野扩展到道德受众之外。逆流而上者受到其生态中支持他们对真正道德考量做出回应的那些特征的培育。在哈克的情形中，这样的一个特征可能是与吉姆合作的机会。在其他情形中，相关特征可能是某些榜样的存在，或照护者在理解和预期自己的行为对他人后果方面的引导，或关于道德问题的对话等等。

For the second, which maintains a strong link between moral agency and ecology, we should expand our view of moral ecology beyond the moral audience. Going-against-the-grainers have been cultivated by features in their ecology that support their responsiveness to genuine moral considerations. In Huck's case, one such feature might be the opportunity to collaborate with Jim. In other cases, the relevant features might be the presence of certain role models, or caregiver guidance on understanding and anticipating the consequences of one's actions on others, or conversations about moral matters, etc.

正是这一答案的一个版本——逆流而上者是一个复杂且足够幸运的道德生态的产物——是我认为最有希望的。他们从道德受众那里学到了东西，但由于额外的社会支架，他们能够超越它。按照这种观点，道德能动性同时依赖于道德生态又独立于道德生态。理解这一表面矛盾的方法是，将我们的依赖视为很大程度上是一种发展状态，而我们的独立性则是最终目标。

It is a version of this answer – that going-against-the-grainers are products of a complex and sufficiently fortunate moral ecology – that I find most promising. They have learned from their moral audience but, thanks to additional social scaffolding, they are able to transcend it. According to this view, moral agency is simultaneously dependent on and independent of the moral ecology. The way to make sense of this prima facie contradiction is to view our dependence as largely a developmental state and our independence as the ultimate aim.

逆流而上者已经获得了某种程度的道德自主——一种对相关道德考量的自主的、直接的回应能力。对受众的敏感度对于回应全方位的道德考量至关重要，但它并不充分。第一个原因是，某些真正的道德考量为实际道德受众的期望所缺失或与之相冲突。第二个原因是，回应受众反馈——为了避免责备或获得赞扬而行动——并不等同于（也不必然导致）被道德考量所感召。

Going-against-the-grainers have attained some degree of moral autonomy – an autonomous, direct responsiveness to the relevant moral considerations. Sensitivity to audience is crucial for responsiveness to the full range of moral considerations, but it is not sufficient. The first reason is that some genuine moral considerations are absent from or in conflict with the expectations of an actual moral audience. The second reason is that responding to audience feedback – acting to avoid blame or garner praise – does not equate (or necessarily lead) to being moved by the moral consideration.

道德自主（如同道德价值）意味着因为某些考量是真正的道德考量而为之行动（例如因为那些使之正确的特征而行动），而不是因为这样做值得赞扬或会招致责备。一个废奴主义者只有当她出于对被奴役者固有尊严的关切而行动——而非仅仅因为废奴主义令人钦佩——才算是在道德自主地行动。因此，道德自主的人对其道德受众的期望是敏感的，但由于其道德生态中的其他特征，他们获得了对道德考量本身更强的敏感度。

Moral autonomy (like moral worth) is a matter of acting for moral considerations because they are genuine moral considerations (such as acting for the features that make them right), not because doing so is praise- or blameworthy. An abolitionist acts morally autonomously only when she acts out of a concern for the inherent dignity of the enslaved people she is trying to emancipate rather than because abolitionism is admirable. The morally autonomous are thus sensitive to the expectations of their moral audience, but, thanks to other features of their moral ecology, they have acquired a stronger sensitivity to the moral considerations themselves.

责备和赞扬可能削弱内在的道德动力和道德自主。

Blame and praise can undermine intrinsic moral motivation and moral autonomy.

那么，这些其他生态特征究竟是什么？在哈克的情形中，与吉姆合作的机会似乎是关键性的，它使哈克能够把吉姆看作一个人类存在，看作一个目的本身，具有内在的价值和尊严。正如诺米·阿帕利（Nomy Arpaly）所提示的那样，哈克直接回应了这些考量，尽管他并不理解它们就是相关的、真正的道德考量。此外，还有哈克独特的生活方式和成长环境的问题。他得到的教养前后不一致，住在镇子外面。他经常逃学、不去教堂，总体上过着一种相当无忧无虑的、在他所在社区眼中不太文明的生活。因此，哈克避免了其他道德共同体成员所接受的文化教化和思想灌输。这种自由度反过来使得他天生的道德指南针——在有机会锻炼它的条件下——得以正常发展。

Just what are these other ecological features? In Huck's case, the opportunity to collaborate with Jim appears to have been crucial, enabling Huck to see Jim as a human being, as an end in himself, with intrinsic worth and dignity. As Nomy Arpaly has suggested, Huck responds directly to these considerations despite not understanding that they are the relevant, genuine moral considerations. There is also the matter of Huck's unique lifestyle and upbringing. He was inconsistently parented and lived outside of town. He often skipped school and church, and generally lived in a rather carefree and, in the eyes of his community, uncivilised way. Huck therefore avoided the acculturation and indoctrination to which other members of his moral community were subjected. This degree of freedom, in turn, allowed his innate moral compass – in conjunction with opportunities to exercise it – to develop properly.

当代社会科学表明，我们拥有一种天生的道德指南针或「前道德倾向」（premoral disposition）。道德始于内在，而非外在。我们天然具有同情心和合作精神。早在三岁时，儿童就能区分社会惯例与真正的道德考量。然而，这种前道德倾向仍然需要被纳入一个价值框架之中。但这一过程应当支持和强化我们的自然倾向，而非强加于或限制它们。一种更早的道德发展观——最初由让·皮亚杰（Jean Piaget）于1932年提出——则持相反的看法。我们作为空空的容器来到这个世界，道德考量必须由照护者和教育者注入，然后加以内化。通过内化过程，这些考量成为我们自己的，而一旦完全内化它们，我们就变得道德自主了。

Current social science suggests that we have an innate moral compass or 'premoral disposition'. Morality starts from within, not from without. We are naturally sympathetic and cooperative. As young as aged three, children are able to distinguish social conventions from genuine moral considerations. This premoral disposition, however, must still be enlisted into a framework of values. But this process should, presumably, support and strengthen our natural tendencies rather than impose or constrict them. An earlier view of moral development, originally proposed by Jean Piaget in 1932, suggests the opposite. We enter the world as empty vessels into which moral considerations must be injected by our caregivers and educators, and then internalised. Through the internalisation process, these considerations become our own and, upon fully internalising them, we become morally autonomous.

根据心理学家理查德·瑞安（Richard Ryan）和爱德华·德西（Edward Deci）的当代研究，责备和赞扬可能削弱内在的道德动力和道德自主。他们认为，也许正确类型的赞扬（多于责备）能够支持自主性，但要说清哪些赞扬是正确的以及为什么，远非简单。想想反复赞扬一个正在学钢琴的孩子所产生的效果。她可能会变得依赖正面反馈这种外在奖励，在没有支持性受众的情况下就失去演奏的兴趣。赞扬一个孩子的善行（比如分享玩具）可能会产生类似的结果。此外，我们还可能由此外化了孩子的道德权威感，削弱了她的道德自主。赞扬——尤其是责备——可能将注意力从真正重要的事情上转移开，即那些受我们行为影响的人。

According to contemporary research by the psychologists Richard Ryan and Edward Deci, blame and praise can undermine intrinsic moral motivation and moral autonomy. It might be, they say, that the right kinds of praise (more than blame) can support autonomy, but saying which ones and why is far from straightforward. Consider the effects of repeatedly praising a budding child pianist. She might become dependent on the extrinsic rewards of positive feedback and lose interest in performing in the absence of a supportive audience. Praising a child's virtuous behaviour (eg, sharing toys) could have similar results. Moreover, we thereby risk externalising the child's sense of moral authority and undermining her moral autonomy. Praise, but especially blame, can draw attention away from what really matters, namely those who are affected by our actions.

从这个角度看，我们目前对儿童进行责备和赞扬的做法似乎更符合皮亚杰的道德发展模型——按照该模型，我们在主动地试图灌输道德，而非培育天性中已有的东西。一种替代方式是帮助儿童预期和理解他们的行为如何影响他人，引导他们关注那些受影响的人，而不是我们和我们的评判。我们在使用赞扬和责备时应当格外谨慎，以避免其削弱效果。

In this light, our current practices of blaming and praising children appear to conform better to Piaget's model of moral development, according to which we are actively trying to impose morality rather than foster what is there by nature. One way to do this is to help children to anticipate and understand the ways their actions affect others, drawing their attention to their victims and beneficiaries rather than to us and our judgments. We should be especially cautious with praise and blame to avoid their undermining effects.

大多数关注责备和赞扬的哲学家关心的是它们何时是公平或恰当的，而非它们的效果。因此他们往往忽视了「承担责任」与「追究责任」（赞扬和责备）之间的关系。支架论观点在这一趋势中是值得注意的例外。支架论的支持者论证道，在被追究责任的过程中，我们变得有责任感。这不仅适用于儿童。由于支架论的支持者认为道德能动性本质上是脆弱的和人际性的，规范性图景是复杂且动态的，他们将道德能动性对被追究责任的依赖视为持续的。

Most philosophers interested in blame and praise are concerned with what makes them fair or fitting rather than with their effects. They therefore tend to overlook the relationship between being responsible and holding responsible (praising and blaming). Scaffolding views are notable exceptions to this tendency. In being held responsible, scaffolding proponents argue, we become responsible. This is not just the case for children. Because scaffolding proponents view moral agency as inherently fragile and interpersonal, and the normative landscape as complex and dynamic, they regard the dependence of moral agency on being held responsible as perpetual.

支架论观点的问题在于，它忽视了赞扬和责备与自主性之间的紧张关系。我们可能仅仅为了避免责备或追求赞扬而满足道德受众的期望，这呈现的是一种肤浅且缺乏吸引力的道德能动性图景。或者，通过对一个腐败受众的赞扬和责备的敏感，我们可能拥抱了错误的道德价值。通过以这种敏感度来定义道德能动性，支架论观点忽略了真正的道德行动，从而也忽略了逆流而上的能力。

The problem with the scaffolding view is that it ignores the troubled relationship between praise and blame and autonomy. We might fulfil our moral audience's expectations strictly by way of avoiding blame or seeking praise, which makes for a shallow and unappealing picture of moral agency. Or, by way of a sensitivity to the praise and blame of a corrupt audience, we might come to embrace false moral values. By defining moral agency in terms of this sensitivity, the scaffolding view overlooks genuine moral action and thus the ability to go against the grain.

支架论观点的正确之处在于，对责备和赞扬的敏感度是道德自主所必需的；我们无法独自成为良好的道德能动者。然而，我们应当拒绝如下观念：责备和赞扬对于道德自主是充分的（且无问题的），以及我们持续不断地依赖它们（或更广泛的外部因素）。通过将初生的道德指南针视为天生的——但也需要多层面的社会支架来实现充分发展，在获得道德自主之时——我们可以在生态学观点的框架内解释逆流而上的情形。

What the scaffolding view gets right is that a sensitivity to blame and praise is necessary for moral autonomy; we cannot become good moral agents on our own. However, we should reject the idea that blame and praise are sufficient (and unproblematic) for moral autonomy and that we depend perpetually on them (or on external factors more generally). By viewing the fledgling moral compass as innate – but also as in need of multifaceted social scaffolding for full realisation, at which point moral autonomy is acquired – we can explain going-against-the-grain cases from within an ecological view.

这种路径至少面临三种可能的反对意见。第一种涉及运气。认为道德自主在某种程度上是幸运环境的结果，可能会让一些人感到不安。自然，某些个体比其他个体对批判性反馈更敏感。某些受众比其他受众更具批判性。某些个体比其他个体获得更多的照护者指导。并非每个人都有机会与道德共同体之外的人合作。有些人似乎在较少帮助的情况下就成为道德自主的人。简言之，成为道德自主的人似乎并不真正取决于我们自己。

There are at least three possible objections to this approach. The first concerns luck. It may be disconcerting to some to think of moral autonomy as in any way the result of lucky circumstances. Naturally, some individuals are more sensitive to critical feedback than others. Some audiences are more critical than others. Some individuals get more caregiver guidance than others. Not everyone gets an opportunity to collaborate with people from outside their moral community. Some appear to become morally autonomous with less help than others. In short, it would seem that becoming morally autonomous isn't really up to us.

现代人看似违背了一套价值的潮流，但实际上是在顺应另一套价值的潮流。

Modern individuals will appear to go against the grain of one set of values but, in reality, go with the grain of another.

然而，这幅图景应该更多地激发我们而非令人沮丧，它促使我们探究道德自主的条件，鼓励我们致力于提供这些条件。逆流而上的案例是这一事业的良好起点，关于道德动力和自主性的实证研究也是如此。重要的是，如果所提出的观点是正确的，那么促进道德自主不会是增加赞扬和责备实践的问题，也不会是更广泛地注入更多道德权威的问题。它将涉及以前面描述的以及有待发现的方式来支持我们天生的道德倾向。

This picture should inspire more than dishearten, however, prompting enquiry into the conditions of moral autonomy and encouraging efforts aimed at furnishing them. Going-against-the-grain cases are a helpful starting point for that project, as are empirical studies on moral motivation and autonomy. Importantly, if the proposed view is right, promoting moral autonomy is not going to be a matter of increasing our blame and praise practices or of inserting more moral authority more generally. It will involve supporting our innate moral tendencies in the ways described earlier and in ways yet to be identified.

第二个担忧如下。人们可能认为，支架论的支持者确实为先天的、初生的道德指南针留出了空间，而且正是它区分了那些随波逐流的人和那些出于真正的道德考量而逆流而上的人。然而，如前所述，逆流而上的情形中涉及的道德指南针必须比对受众反馈的敏感度更强。在支架论观点中，没有空间容纳这一点，因为道德能动性的界定性特征恰恰是对受众的敏感度。

A second concern is the following. Scaffolding proponents, one might think, do leave room for an innate, fledgling moral compass, and it is the difference-maker between those who just go with the flow and those who act for genuine moral considerations against the grain. However, as discussed, the moral compass involved in going-against-the-grain cases must be stronger than sensitivity to audience feedback. There isn't room, on the scaffolding view, for this to be the case since the defining feature of moral agency is sensitivity to audience.

第三个、也是最后的担忧是，现代的逆流而上案例可以轻易地被消解。与哈克·费恩的时代不同，现代道德受众在价值上是异质的。这在西方自由社会中是成立的，可能在许多其他社会中也是如此，尤其归功于我们的数字互联性。不断接触到大量截然不同的观点和价值，现代人不可避免地会看似违背某个社会群体或某套价值的潮流，而实际上他们是在顺应另一套潮流。

The third and final worry is that modern-day going-against-the-grain cases can be explained away easily. Unlike in Huck Finn's era, the modern moral audience is value heterogeneous. This is true of Western, liberal societies, though likely many others too, especially thanks to our digital interconnectedness. Continually exposed to a vast array of contrasting views and values, modern individuals will inevitably appear to go against the grain of one segment of society or one set of values while, in reality, they're going with the grain of another.

有两种方式来解释价值异质性——要么以多个相邻的道德共同体来解释，要么以内部价值多元的道德共同体来解释。按照前者，所谓的逆流而上者根据他们新近优先的价值从一个共同体转向另一个共同体，从而顺应其当前共同体的潮流。另一种可能是，他们同时属于多个道德共同体，因此虽然让某个共同体的期望落空，却满足了另一个共同体的期望。因此，他们始终是在顺应其某个共同体的潮流。在一个内部价值多元的道德共同体中，所谓的逆流而上者只是对拥有真正道德考量的共同体成员比那些持有错误期望的成员更有回应。

There are two ways to account for value heterogeneity – either in terms of multiple, contiguous moral communities or in terms of internally value-diverse moral communities. Under the first, putative going-against-the-grainers shift their membership from one community to the next, according to their newly prioritised values, and so go with the grain of their current community. Alternatively, it could be that they belong to multiple moral communities simultaneously and so, while disappointing one community's expectations, they fulfil another's. They are thus always going with the grain of one of their communities. Putative going-against-the-grainers within an internally value-diverse moral community are simply more responsive to fellow community members with genuine moral considerations than those with false expectations.

我不确信道德共同体内部存在真正的价值异质性，也不认为人们真的可以选择自己的道德共同体，理由我在论文《超越反应性支架》（Rising above Reactive Scaffolding, 2025）中有所探讨。但支架论观点的问题比这更深。做正确之事的个体只是碰巧做对了。这是因为他们回应的是受众反馈，而非直接回应真正的道德考量。更重要的是，我们可以指出某些案例，个体所回应的道德考量是其受众从未涉及过的，甚至在一个高度多样化的受众中也不存在的。要解释这类案例，我们必须诉诸一种独立的能力。然而，出于生态学支持者所阐明的原因，我们必须承认这种能力并非凭空产生。

I'm not convinced that there is true value heterogeneity within moral communities, or that one can really choose their moral community, for reasons I explore in my paper 'Rising above Reactive Scaffolding' (2025). The problem for the scaffolding view runs deeper than this, though. Individuals who do the right thing do so only coincidentally. This is because they're responding to audience feedback rather than to genuine moral considerations directly. What is more, we can point to cases where individuals respond to moral considerations that their audience has never addressed, or which are not present even within a highly diverse audience. To account for these kinds of cases, we must appeal to an independent capacity. For reasons that ecological proponents make clear, however, we must acknowledge that this capacity does not arise on its own.

吐温的《哈克贝利·费恩历险记》的天才之处在于看似不相容之物的统一——传统与新奇、口语与雄辩、地方与普世、落后与开明。人物哈克的天才同样在于看似不相容之物的共存。哈克既是他那个时代和地域的产物，又超越了它。他是道德共同体的一部分，又违背了它。他既落后又开明。他的道德成就——克服了他自己的、由受众引入的扭曲价值——在我看来，并不因其道德生态在其中发挥了关键作用而有所减损。这反而是其魅力的一部分。在逆道德潮流而行的能力——道德自主——的核心处，可以发现一种类似的并置——个体与社会之间、天生与生态之间、独立与依赖之间——我希望这种并置能够得到理解与欣赏。

The genius of Twain's Adventures of Huckleberry Finn is to be found in the union of seemingly incompatible things – the traditional and the novel, the colloquial and the eloquent, the local and the universal, the backwards and the enlightened. The genius of the character, Huck, likewise lies in the coexistence of seemingly incompatible things. Huck is both a product of his time and place, and ahead of it. He is part of a moral community, and goes against it. He is both backwards and enlightened. His moral achievement – overcoming his own, audience-imported, distorted values – is not diminished, in my view, by the fact that his moral ecology played a vital role in it. It's rather part of the appeal. At the heart of the going-against-the-moral-grain capacity, moral autonomy, a similar juxtaposition – between the individual and the social, the innate and the ecological, independence and dependence – can be found and, I hope, appreciated.
