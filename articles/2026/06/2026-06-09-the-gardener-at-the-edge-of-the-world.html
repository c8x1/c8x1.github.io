世界尽头的园丁

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年6月9日·2分钟阅读

从前有一个村庄坐落在巨大悬崖的边缘，悬崖之下无人知晓究竟有什么——只知道那深不可测。一位名叫玛伦（Maren）的老妇人在那里经营花园已有五十年，每天清晨她都会浇灌她的玫瑰，仿佛脚下的土地是坚硬的花岗岩。一个名叫霍尔特（Holt）的旅行商人每季经过此地，总觉得好笑。「你难道不知道，」有一年春天他靠在她的围栏上说，「整个村庄随时都可能滑入深渊吗？从来没有人见过底部。据你所知，那下面可能什么都没有。」

There was once a village perched at the very edge of a great cliff, and beyond the cliff no one knew what lay below — only that it was impossibly deep. An old woman named Maren had tended a garden there for fifty years, and every morning she watered her roses as though the earth beneath her feet were solid granite. A traveling merchant named Holt, who passed through each season, found this amusing. "You do know," he said one spring, leaning on her fence, "that the whole village could slide into the abyss at any moment? No one has ever seen the bottom. For all you know, there is nothing down there at all."

玛伦擦去手上的泥土，平静地看着他。「如果下面真的有什么呢？」霍尔特大笑。「下面能有什么？天堂？审判？那是村里长辈们说的，但他们从未证明过。我说什么都没有——只有虚空和黑暗。所以我随心所欲地活着。我喝酒，做买卖，继续赶路。何必在不稳固的土地上浪费哪怕一天去建造什么呢？」

Maren wiped the soil from her hands and looked at him calmly. "And what if there is something down there?" Holt laughed. "What could possibly be down there? A paradise? A judgment? That's what the village elders say, but they've never proven it. I say there's nothing — just empty air and darkness. So I live as I please. I drink, I trade, I move on. Why waste a single day building something on unstable ground?"

「让我问你一件事，」玛伦放下铲子说。「假设你是对的，悬崖之外什么都没有。你随心所欲地活着，有一天地面塌陷，你坠入虚无。你对了又能得到什么？什么也得不到。你只是消亡了。但现在假设我是对的——下面确实有某种东西，某种会审视我们如何活着的东西。那又如何？」

"Let me ask you something," Maren said, setting down her trowel. "Suppose you are right, and there is nothing beyond the cliff. You live as you please, and one day the ground gives way, and you fall into nothing. What have you gained by being right? Nothing. You simply cease. But now suppose I am right — and there is something down there, something that takes account of how we lived. Then what?"

霍尔特挪了挪身子。「嗯，那我恐怕就有麻烦了。」玛伦缓缓点头。「如果我错了，什么都没有——那我也什么都没有失去。我生活在花丛之中。我对邻居友善。我在每一天细小的劳作中找到了意义。即使脚下的大地最终是空洞的，我的一生也不算虚度。」她拿起水壶。「但你呢，我的朋友——如果你错了，你就失去了一切。」

Holt shifted his weight. "Well, then I suppose I'd be in trouble." Maren nodded slowly. "And if I am wrong, and there is nothing — then I have lost nothing either. I have lived among flowers. I have been kind to my neighbors. I have found meaning in each day's small work. My life was not wasted even if the ground beneath it turns out to be hollow." She picked up her watering can. "But you, my friend — if you are wrong, you have lost everything."

霍尔特沉默了很久。他看着她跪在泥土中，用饱经风霜的拇指将一颗种子按入土地，轻轻覆盖，仿佛这是世界上最重要的事情。「但你怎么能选择去相信呢？」他轻声问。「你知道的并不比我多。」玛伦微笑道：「我不是选择相信。我是选择活得好像那很重要——因为如果它确实重要，我就准备好了；如果它不重要，我仍然活得很好。问题从来不在于确定无疑，而在于你承受得起失去什么。」

Holt said nothing for a long while. He watched her kneel in the dirt and press a seed into the earth with her weathered thumb, covering it gently, as though it were the most important act in the world. "But how can you choose to believe?" he asked quietly. "You don't know any more than I do." Maren smiled. "I don't choose to believe. I choose to live as though it matters — because if it does matter, I am ready, and if it doesn't, I have still lived well. The question was never about certainty. It was always about what you can afford to lose."

这个故事揭示了帕斯卡赌注（Pascal's Wager）的本质——当理性无法判定一个问题时，我们应当权衡每种选择的后果。如果上帝存在而你选择相信，你将获得一切；如果上帝存在而你不信，你将失去一切。如果上帝不存在，信者的损失微乎其微——不过是一种节制而虔诚的生活——而不信者也并未获得任何持久的东西。帕斯卡（Pascal）认为，一个理性的人因此应当像上帝存在那样去生活，因为潜在的回报是无限的，而代价是有限的。这不是关于上帝存在的证明，而是关于在确定无疑不可能时该如何行动的实践论证——将信仰框定为一场赌局，其中一方的安全性压倒性地高于另一方。

This story reveals the essence of Pascal's wager — the idea that when reason cannot settle a question, we should weigh the consequences of each choice. If God exists and you believe, you gain everything; if God exists and you do not believe, you lose everything. If God does not exist, the believer has lost little — a life of restraint and devotion — while the nonbeliever has gained nothing permanent. Pascal argued that a rational person should therefore live as though God exists, because the potential reward is infinite while the cost is finite. It is not a proof of God's existence but a practical argument about how to act when certainty is impossible, framing belief as a bet where one side is overwhelmingly safer than the other.
