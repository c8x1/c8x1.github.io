人类视觉：我们实际看到与没看到的，揭示了意识的许多奥秘

Henry Taylor·The Conversation·2026年6月22日·3分钟阅读·原文

你现在能看到什么？这看起来可能是个愚蠢的问题，但进入你意识中的并不是视觉的全部故事。大脑中大量的视觉处理活动远在我们的意识觉察之下进行。

What can you see right now? This might seem like a silly question, but what enters your consciousness is not the whole story when it comes to vision. A great deal of visual processing in the brain goes on well below our conscious awareness.

一些研究探索了视觉的无意识深层机制。一个证据来源是一种被称为盲视（blindsight）的神经学症状，它由大脑中处理视觉信息的区域受损引起。患有盲视的人报告说他们完全看不见，或者在他们视野的某一部分看不见。

Some studies have probed the unconscious depths of vision. One source of evidence comes from the neurological condition known as blindsight, which is caused by damage to areas of the brain involved in processing visual information. People with blindsight report that they are unable to see, either entirely or in a portion of their visual field.

例如，在2004年发表的一项针对盲视患者的实验中，一条黑杆被显示在该患者视野中看不见的区域。患者被要求「猜测」这条黑杆是垂直的还是水平的。

For example, in an experiment published in 2004 on someone with blindsight, a black bar was displayed in the portion of the visual field to which the person was blind. The person was asked to "guess" whether the bar was vertical or horizontal.

尽管患者否认对黑杆有任何意识觉察，但其回答的正确率远高于随机水平。该患者甚至表现出能够对黑杆加以注意的证据——当一支箭（放置在其视野正常区域）指向黑杆时，其反应速度更快。

Despite denying any conscious awareness of the bar, the participant could answer correctly at a level well above chance. The participant even showed evidence of being able to pay attention to the bar – they were faster to respond when an arrow (placed in a healthy area of their visual field) correctly pointed towards the bar.

最流行的解释（尽管不是唯一的）是，盲视患者能够看到这些物体，但不是有意识地看到。他们看到了那里存在的东西，但这一切都在意识层面之下进行，超出了他们的觉察范围。

The most popular interpretation (though not the only one) is that people with blindsight can see these objects, but not see them consciously. They see what is there, but it all goes on unconsciously, below their awareness.

非注意性盲视（inattentional blindness）现象似乎表明，你可以看到信息，但该信息并未进入你的意识。任何人都可以体验到非注意性盲视。这种现象早已为人所知，但我们最容易理解它的方式是看一个著名的实验。

The phenomenon of inattentional blindness seems to show you can see without the information crossing into your consciousness. Anyone can experience inattentional blindness. The phenomenon has been known about for a long time, but we can most easily get a handle on it by looking at a well-known experiment.

在这个实验中，参与者观看一段人们打篮球的视频，并被要求统计穿白衬衫的球员之间传球的次数。如果你以前从未做过这个实验，我强烈建议你现在停下来去看一下那段视频。

In this experiment, participants are shown a video of people playing basketball, and told to count the number of passes between the players wearing a white shirt. If you've never done this before, I urge to you stop reading now and watch the video.

在很多情况下，人们忙于计数传球次数，完全错过了一只大猩猩从场景中间走过、拍打胸膛然后离开的画面。大猩猩就在那里，处于你视野的正中央。来自大猩猩的光线进入你的眼睛，并在你的大脑中被处理，但你不知为何没有注意到它。

In many cases, people are so busy counting the passes that they completely miss a large gorilla walking across the middle of the scene and beating its chest, then walking off. The gorilla's right there, in the centre of your visual field. Light from the gorilla enters your eyes, and is processed in your brain, but somehow you don't notice it.

大猩猩还有更多东西可以教我们。在2013年报告的另一项实验中，放射科医生被给予一系列肺部扫描图。他们被告知要在每张扫描图上寻找结节（表现为小的浅色圆圈）。在其中一张扫描图上，一张大猩猩跳舞的大图被叠加在肺部图像之上。这只大猩猩的大小是平均结节的48倍。大多数放射科医生——他们是查看这些扫描图的专家——都没有注意到这只大猩猩。

The gorilla has more to teach us. In another experiment reported in 2013, radiologists were given a series of lung scans. They were told to look for nodules (which show up as small light coloured circles) on each scan. In one of the scans, a large picture of a dancing gorilla was superimposed on top of the lung. The gorilla was 48 times the size of the average nodule. A majority of the radiologists – who are expert at looking at these scans – failed to notice the gorilla.

对这些实验的解释存在争议。一些科学家认为，在这些情况下，你有意识地看到了大猩猩，但立刻就忘记了它（尽管肺部中一只跳舞的大猩猩似乎不是你会忘记的那种东西）。另一些人则认为，你在无意识层面上看到了大猩猩，但它没有跨过进入意识的阈值。

The interpretation of these experiments is controversial. Some scientists suggest that in these kinds of cases, you consciously see the gorilla, but immediately forget it (although a dancing gorilla in someone's lung doesn't seem like the kind of thing you'd forget). Others argue that you see the gorilla unconsciously, but it doesn't cross the threshold into consciousness.

让我们假设，在盲视和非注意性盲视的情况下，信息被看到了，但没有一路到达意识层面。那么问题就变成了：是什么使某些信息成为有意识的，而非那些停留在无意识状态的信息？这是意识科学的核心问题之一。

Let's assume that in the case of blindsight, and inattentional blindness, the information is seen, but didn't make it all the way to consciousness. Then, the question is: what makes some information conscious, rather than the information that stays unconscious? This is one of the central questions for the science of consciousness.

关于哪种意识理论最佳，目前尚无共识，但在我看来，最有力的候选者是全局神经元工作空间理论（global neuronal workspace theory）。

There's no agreement on which is the best theory of consciousness, but in my opinion, the strongest contender is the global neuronal workspace theory.

根据这一理论，意识与大脑中一个作为「工作空间」所在地的特定区域密切相关。工作空间是一个容量有限的系统，因此它在任何时刻都无法容纳大量信息。工作空间的任务是接收无意识信息并将其广播到整个大脑。

According to this theory, consciousness is all to do with a particular area of the brain which is the seat of the "workspace". The workspace is a system with a small capacity, so it can't hold a lot of information at any one time. The job of the workspace is to take unconscious information and broadcast it across the whole brain.

工作空间的作用就像大脑的扩音器，而意识就是被广播出去的信息。工作空间接收无意识信息并将其放大，使得大脑中许多不同的系统能够获知这些信息并在各自的处理过程中加以利用。这是该理论的核心。

The job of the workspace is to act like the brain's loudspeaker, and consciousness is the information that gets broadcast. The workspace takes unconscious information and boosts it so that many of the different systems in the brain hear about it and can use that information in their own processes. This is the core of the theory.

全局神经元工作空间理论最引人注目的一个推论是，有多少信息能到达意识层面。既然工作空间的容量相当有限，由此可以推断，我们任何时候能够意识到的内容只有很少一点点。我们可能以为面前有一个丰富的视觉世界，但根据这一理论，我们永远只能意识到其中极小的一部分。

One of the most striking implications of the global neuronal workspace theory is how little information makes it to consciousness. Since the workspace has quite a small capacity, it follows that we can only ever be conscious of a little at a time. We might think there's a rich visual world in front of us, but according to this theory, we are only ever conscious of a tiny fraction of it.

一些哲学家和科学家基于这些理由对该理论提出了异议。他们认为意识「溢出」了工作空间：我们意识到的信息量超过了工作空间在任何时刻所能「容纳」的量。即便这些争论仍在继续，我认为全局神经元工作空间理论仍然为我们提供了关于有意识视觉与无意识视觉之间关系的最佳解释。

Some philosophers and scientists have objected to the theory on these grounds. They suggest that consciousness "overflows" the workspace: we are conscious of more information than can "fit" into the workspace at any one time. Even with these debates still ongoing, I think the global neuronal workspace theory gives us the best explanation we have of the relationship between conscious and unconscious vision.

在我们的大脑中，意识只是一座巨大冰山露出水面的一角。但全局神经元工作空间理论也许能让我们窥见，是什么使得这一角如此特殊。

In our brains, consciousness is only the tip of a very large iceberg. But the global neuronal workspace theory might give us insight into what makes that tip so special.
