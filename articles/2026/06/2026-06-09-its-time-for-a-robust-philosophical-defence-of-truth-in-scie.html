科学哲学

是时候为科学中的真理进行有力的哲学辩护了

Michela Massimini·Aeon·2026年6月9日·17分钟阅读·原文

想一想在科学中有多少场景关乎真理。我们关心二氧化碳排放量的增加是否会导致气候变化，以及变化的速度有多快。我们关心吸烟是否会增加患肺癌的风险。我们关心不良饮食是否会增加儿童肥胖的风险，或者经济增长预测是否正确。科学中的真理并非不切实际的空谈。它影响着气候科学、医学、公共卫生、经济以及许多其他世俗事务。

Think of the number of scenarios in which truth matters in science. We care to know whether increased CO2 emission levels cause climate change, and how fast. We care to know whether smoking tobacco increases the risk of lung cancer. We care to know whether poor diet exposes children to the risk of developing obesity, or whether forecasts of economic growth are correct. Truth in science is not esoteric dilly-dallying. It shapes climate science, medicine, public health, the economy and many other worldly endeavours.

真理对科学至关重要，这早已不是新闻。长期以来，人们一直向科学寻求关于世界的真理。科学革命不正是伽利略（Galileo）的科学真理——通过望远镜观测艰难赢得的真理——对数百年日心说教条的胜利吗？托勒密（Ptolemy）天文学以其本轮和均轮体系，既精巧又错误。它充其量只能「拯救现象」——解释行星在天空中看起来如何运动。直到哥白尼（Copernicus）的解释被发现，它才揭示了关于行星运动的真理。再看 18 世纪末的化学革命。毕竟，我们不再相信燃素——格奥尔格·恩斯特·施塔尔（Georg Ernst Stahl）、约瑟夫·普里斯特利（Joseph Priestley）和其他当时的自然哲学家所相信的那种虚构的、不可称量的流体，认为它在燃烧和煅烧现象中发挥作用。安托万·拉瓦锡（Antoine Lavoisier）关于氧气的科学真理战胜了关于燃素的错误信念。

That truth matters to science is hardly news. For a long time, people have looked to science for truths about the world. The Scientific Revolution was nothing if not the triumph of Galileo's scientific truth – hard-won through his telescopic observations – over centuries of dogma about the geocentric system. With its system of epicycles and deferents, Ptolemaic astronomy was at once sophisticated and false. It served to, at best, 'save the appearances' about how planets seemed to move in the sky. It did not tell the truth about planetary motion until the discovery of the Copernican explanation. Or consider the Chemical Revolution at the end of the 18th century. We no longer, after all, believe in phlogiston – the fictional imponderable fluid that Georg Ernst Stahl, Joseph Priestley and other natural philosophers at the time believed to be at work in combustion and calcination phenomena. Antoine Lavoisier's scientific truth about oxygen prevailed over false beliefs about phlogiston.

这些科学革命的主要推动者往往倡导这样一种观念：科学是一种必然导致真理战胜过去错误的探究。伽利略成功捍卫日心说两个世纪之后，关于科学真理进程的这种观念仍然激励着哲学家。奥古斯特·孔德（Auguste Comte）在其《实证哲学教程》（Cours de philosophie positive，1830-42）中，将人类知识的演进划分为三个主要阶段：「神学的，即虚构的；形而上学的，即抽象的；以及科学的，即实证的。」在「实证的」这一第三也是最后的阶段中，「对事实的解释不过是建立单个现象与某些一般事实之间的联系，而后者的数量随着科学的进步不断减少。」

The main actors of these scientific revolutions often fostered this way of thinking about science as an enquiry leading to the inevitable triumph of truth over past errors. Two centuries after Galileo's successful defence of the heliocentric system, this idea of the course of scientific truth continued to inspire philosophers. In his Cours de philosophie positive (1830-42), Auguste Comte saw the evolution of human knowledge in three main stages: 'the Theological, or fictitious; the Metaphysical, or abstract; and the Scientific, or positive'. In the 'positive', the third and last stage, 'an explanation of facts is simply the establishment of a connection between single phenomena and some general facts, the number of which continually diminishes with the progress of science'.

孔德的实证主义很快脱离了其政治和社会背景（毕竟，孔德最初是为社会理论家亨利·德·圣西门（Henri de Saint-Simon）工作，而实证主义的灵感来自工业革命）。到了 19 世纪末（以及它在 20 世纪初的再次兴起——此处没有篇幅讨论），「实证主义」一词在许多人耳中已经成为对科学技术力量的不懈乐观及其稳步迈向真理的代名词。

Comte's positivism was soon decontextualised from its political and social context (after all, Comte started his career working for the social theorist Henri de Saint-Simon, and positivism was inspired by the Industrial Revolution). By the end of the 19th century (and in its early 20th-century reappearance, which I do not have the space to discuss here), the word 'positivism' had become – to the ears of many – synonymous with an unfailing optimism in the power of science and technology, and their steady progress toward truth.

在一些科学界，这种孔德式的关于科学如何演进和进步的观念仍然是主流。但在过去半个世纪里，科学哲学家已经反对将科学描绘为不断向真理前进的行军。这并非科学实际运作的方式，也不是它在历史中前行的方式。它无视了科学革命实际上发生的那些精彩而微妙的历史细节。它无法容纳一些最伟大的科学头脑如何执着于某些错误信念。它有意忽略了科学知识在历史上往往通过众多声音、分歧和争论而取得进步。「辉格式」（Whiggish）科学叙事的简单信仰天真地假定进步是以某种累积方式获取「更多真信念」来标志的。

In some scientific quarters, this Comtean notion of how science evolves and progresses remains common currency. But philosophers of science, over the past half-century, have turned against the representation of science as a ceaseless forward march toward truth. It is just not how science works, how it moves through history. It flies in the face of the wonderful and subtle historical nuances of how scientific revolutions have in fact occurred. It does not accommodate how some of the greatest scientific minds held dearly to some false beliefs. It wilfully ignores the many voices, disagreements and controversies through which scientific knowledge has often advanced and progressed over time. Simple faith in the 'Whiggish' narrative of science naively presumes that progress is marked by some cumulative acquisition of 'more true beliefs'.

然而，许多针对这种天真的科学观的批评（在其自身权利上是正当的）犯了一个类似的错误。他们描绘了一幅清除了对真理任何承诺的科学图景。他们将真理视为科学中一个不便的、可有可无的特征。尽管真理的理想和追求确实容易导致狭隘的教条主义，但试图将其清除仍是一个错误。实证主义哲学的谬误在于认为科学以某种阶段形式出现，或遵循特定路径或历史周期。科学哲学中的反真理趋势往往最终重复了同样的错误。超越旧有的（准实证主义的）科学真理观与近几十年来与之对立的反真理趋势之间的僵化二分法，这很重要。

However, many (and legitimate in their own right) criticisms against this naive view of science have committed a similar mistake. They have offered a portrait of science purged of any commitment to truth. They see truth as an inconvenient and disposable feature of science. Fraught as the ideal and pursuit of truth is with tendencies to petty doctrinairism, it is nonetheless a mistake to try to purge it. The fallacy of positivist philosophy was to think of science as coming in stages of some sort, or following a particular path, or historical cycles. The anti-truth trend in the philosophy of science has often ended up repeating this same misstep. It is important to move beyond the sterile dichotomy between the old (quasi-positivist) view of truth in science and the rival anti-truth trend of recent decades.

让我们从一些关于科学中真理的真正的哲学问题开始。以下是三个问题：1）科学以真理为目标吗？2）科学告诉我们真理了吗？3）我们应该期望科学告诉我们真理吗？

Let us start with some genuine philosophical questions about truth in science. Here are three: 1) Does science aim at truth? 2) Does science tell us the truth? 3) Should we expect science to tell us the truth?

在这些问题的每一个中，「科学」都是我们感兴趣追问的任何科学学科的一般性代称。第一个问题可能让人觉得多余，但事实上，它引发了过去 40 年来最热烈的争论之一。巴斯·范·弗拉森（Bas van Fraassen）以其开创性著作《科学的形象》（The Scientific Image，1980）发起了关于科学是否以真理为目标的争论。科学是否旨在向我们讲述关于自然的真实故事？还是仅仅旨在「拯救可观察现象」（即提供一个能解释我们所观察到的事物的说明，而不期望它是关于自然的真实说明）？

In each of these questions, 'science' is a generic placeholder for whichever scientific discipline we are interested in questioning. Question one might strike us as otiose but, in fact, it triggered one of the liveliest debates of the past 40 years. Bas van Fraassen launched this debate as to whether science aims at truth with his pioneering book The Scientific Image (1980). Does science aim to tell us a true story about nature? Or does it aim only at saving the observable phenomena (namely, providing an account that makes sense of what we can observe, without expecting it to be the true account about nature)?

对第一个问题的肯定回答定义了被称为「科学实在论」的哲学立场。科学实在论者主张，最好的科学理论以真理为最终目标。但范·弗拉森指出（追随法国哲学家皮埃尔·迪昂（Pierre Duhem）的观点），在科学中真理往往并非必需，「拯救现象」（或外观）就足够了。例如，托勒密天文学可以解释行星在数月间在天空中做环形运动的表象。这被称为「逆行运动」，托勒密的理论通过引入一系列几何设计（本轮和均轮），假设行星围绕它们运动来加以解释。事实上，并不存在这种圆中之圆，逆行运动只是一种由所有行星以不同速度绕太阳公转所造成的错觉。正如哥白尼天文学几个世纪后所认识到的，托勒密天文学是错误的，尽管它在「拯救可观察现象」方面取得了长达数世纪的成功。

A positive answer to the first question defines the philosophical stance known as 'scientific realism'. Scientific realists maintain that the best scientific theories aim at truth as their final goal. But Van Fraassen pointed out (following the French philosopher Pierre Duhem), that often in science truth is not needed, and 'saving the phenomena' (or appearances) suffices. For example, Ptolemy's astronomy could account for the appearance of planets moving in loops in the sky over months. This was called 'retrograde motion', and Ptolemy's theory accommodated it by introducing a series of geometrical contrivances (epicycles and deferents) around which planets were supposed to move. In fact, there are no such circles of circles, and retrograde motion is just an illusion caused by the relative speeds of planets all orbiting the Sun. As Copernican astronomy realised several centuries later, Ptolemaic astronomy was false, despite its century-long success at 'saving the observable phenomena'.

如今有一些哲学家接受这样的观点：科学不需要是真的也能是好的。他们认为，要求真理是有风险的，因为它迫使人相信一些事物（无论是本轮、燃素、以太还是其他什么），而这些事物在未来可能被证明是错误的。在他们看来，「经验上适当的」理论——即「拯救可观察现象」的理论——对科学来说已经足够好了。例如，人们可以不把高能物理学中的标准模型视为旨在揭示世界是否真的被划分为夸克、轻子和力的载体；这些实体是否真的具有标准模型所描述的属性；等等。一种更审慎的方法追求的是经验适当性。只要标准模型拯救了所有可观察现象（当然不是关于不可见粒子的现象，而是我们肉眼可见的宏观可观察情境），该模型就是经验上适当的。为什么还要要求更多？

There are philosophers today who embrace the view that science does not need to be true in order to be good. They argue that asking for truth is risky because it commits one to believing in things (be it epicycles, phlogiston, ether or something else) that might prove false in the future. In their view, 'empirically adequate' theories, theories that 'save the observable phenomena', are good enough for science. For example, one might take the Standard Model in high-energy physics not as aiming at the truth about whether the world is really carved up into quarks, leptons and force carriers; whether these entities really have the properties that the Standard Model says they have; and so on. A more prudent approach aims for empirical adequacy. Insofar as the Standard Model saves all the observable phenomena (which are not about invisible particles, of course, but about macroscopic observable situations that we can see with our naked eye), the model is empirically adequate. Why ask for more?

当涉及第二个问题——科学告诉我们真理了吗？——各色科学实在论者和反实在论者对此一直争论不休。撇开科学的目的不谈，让我们集中讨论它的历史记录。科学是否告诉了我们真理？审视科学史，它是否构成了一部令人信服的数世纪累积真理的故事？哲学家、历史学家、社会学家和科学研究学者都对这一问题的简单肯定回答提出了质疑。他们尤其质疑了问题中隐含的事实真理观念。在这场争论中，哲学家将自然界中一组确定的事实视为使科学主张成真或成假的依据，而科学进步在于揭示更多这样的事实，直到它们最终全部被发现。

When it comes to the second question – does science tell us the truth? – scientific realists and anti-realists of various stripes have debated it. Leaving aside the aim of science, let us concentrate on its track record instead. Has science told us the truth? Looking at the history of science, does it amount to a persuasive story of truth accumulated over the centuries? Philosophers, historians, sociologists and science-studies scholars have all challenged a simple affirmative answer to this question. They have in particular challenged the notion of factual truth implicit in the question. In this debate, philosophers take a well-defined set of facts about nature as responsible for making scientific claims true or false, and scientific progress consists in unveiling more of these facts until they are all finally revealed.

这一观点的一个清晰例证见于《逻辑哲学论》（Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus，1922），路德维希·维特根斯坦（Ludwig Wittgenstein）在开篇即宣告：「世界是事实的总和」；「事实的总和既决定了什么是如此，也决定了什么不是如此。」维特根斯坦给出了一个看似简单的关于语言如何映射到现实的图景，更重要的是，映射到他所谓的「原子事实」。数十年来（并非直接以维特根斯坦为靶子或对话者参与这场关于科学的具体争论），科学哲学中的反实在论趋势一直质疑原子事实的可能性及其使科学主张成真或成假的能力。许多人认为这些看似简单的问题面临着一些令人生畏的挑战。

A crisp illustration of the view can be found in the Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus (1922), where Ludwig Wittgenstein right at the outset announced: 'The world is the totality of facts'; and 'the totality of facts determines both what is the case, and also all that is not the case'. Wittgenstein gave a deceptively simple view of how language maps on to reality and, more importantly, on to what he called 'atomic facts'. For decades (without taking Wittgenstein as a direct target nor as an interlocutor in this specific debate on science), anti-realist trends in philosophy of science have called into question both the possibility of atomic facts and their ability to make scientific claims true or false. Many think these apparently simple matters face some formidable challenges.

这场长达数十年、多路并进的、对科学中真理的祛魅趋势始于否定这样一个观念：存在着关于自然的事实使我们的科学主张成真或成假。事实建构主义只是这一多路祛魅趋势的一个方面。尽管这听起来可能匪夷所思，其捍卫者声称不存在一个单一的、客观的世界面貌；相反，存在许多不同的、「同等真实的世界描述，而它们的真理性是其忠实性的唯一标准」，正如哲学家纳尔逊·古德曼（Nelson Goodman）所言。例如，他声称我们确实在制造事实，但不像面包师做面包或雕塑家做雕像那样。在古德曼看来，每当我们构建他所谓的一种世界「版本」（通过艺术、音乐、诗歌或科学作品）时，我们就在制造事实。例如，卡米尔·圣桑（Camille Saint-Saëns）通过创作他的 D 小调第一小提琴奏鸣曲创造了一个世界版本，这反过来启发了马塞尔·普鲁斯特（Marcel Proust）在其小说《追忆似水年华》（À la Recherche du Temps Perdu，1913-27）第一卷中想象出「万特伊奏鸣曲」。读过普鲁斯特的人，在听到圣桑那令人着迷的小提琴奏鸣曲时，都不禁会想起在维迪兰沙龙中展开的奥黛特（Odette）和斯万（Swann）的爱情故事。

This decades-long, multi-pronged, disenchantment-with-truth trend in philosophy of science starts by rejecting the idea that there are facts about nature that make our scientific claims true or false. Fact-constructivism is only one aspect of this multi-pronged disenchantment-with-truth trend. Outlandish as this might sound, its defenders claim that there is not a single, objective way that the world is; there are rather many different and 'equally true descriptions of the world, and their truth is the only standard of their faithfulness', in the words of the philosopher Nelson Goodman. For example, he claimed that we do make facts, but not like, say, a baker makes bread, or a sculptor makes a statue. In Goodman's view, we make facts any time we construct what he called a 'version' of the world (via works of art, of music, of poetry, or of science). For example, Camille Saint-Saëns created a version of the world by composing his violin sonata no 1 in D minor, which in turn inspired Marcel Proust to imagine the Vinteuil sonata in the first volume of his novel À la Recherche du Temps Perdu (1913-27). Anyone who has read Proust cannot help thinking about the love story of Odette and Swann unfolding in the Verdurin salon when they hear Saint-Saëns's mesmerising violin sonata.

一些人认为科学不告诉我们真理，但确实提供了重要的非事实性理解。

Some argue that science does not tell the truth but does provide important non-factive understanding.

另一种构建世界版本的方式是根据特定形状对物体进行聚类并为之命名。我们一直在这样做，例如对星星和星座。正如哲学家希拉里·普特南（Hilary Putnam）所表述的：「如今，天上有一个北斗七星，而我们可以说是通过构建那个版本在天上「放置」了一个北斗七星。」古德曼的世界制造观对科学中的真理有严重的含义。他写道：「真理远非一位庄严严厉的主人，而是一个温顺听话的仆人。自以为一心追寻真理的科学家是在自欺……他既是在颁布也是在发现他所阐述的定律，既是在设计也是在辨识他所描绘的模式。」古德曼的观点在某些相关方面与法国哲学家布鲁诺·拉图尔（Bruno Latour）相似。在《潘多拉的希望》（Pandora's Hope，1999）中，拉图尔论证说，关于热带稀树草原是否在推进（以及亚马逊雨林是否在退缩）的事实，是关于土壤取样的实验室实践的产物，而不是关于自然本身的独立事实。

Another way of constructing a version of the world is by clustering objects according to a particular shape and giving them a name. We do this all the time, for example, with stars and constellations. As the philosopher Hilary Putnam expresses it: 'Nowadays, there is a Big Dipper up there in the sky, and we, so to speak, "put" a Big Dipper up there in the sky by constructing that version.' Goodman's world-making view has severe implications for truth in science. 'Truth,' he wrote, 'far from being a solemn and severe master, is a docile and obedient servant. The scientist who supposes that he is single-mindedly dedicated to the search for truth deceives himself … He as much decrees as discovers the laws he sets forth, as much designs as discerns the patterns he delineates.' Goodman's view is similar in some relevant respects to the French philosopher Bruno Latour's. In Pandora's Hope (1999), Latour argues that facts about whether or not the savannah is advancing (and the Amazon forest retreating) are the outcome of laboratory practices about sampling soil, rather than independent facts about nature itself.

事实建构主义对许多哲学家来说听起来过于激进，对大多数科学家来说更是难以接受。因此，这里有另一种反对事实真理的路径，在科学哲学家中广为人知。在过去 40 年中，他们发表了大量关于科学中模型的研究。他们认为，科学模型中抽象和理想化的作用是选择并扭曲相关目标系统的某些方面。例如，布朗运动的台球模型将分子的运动理想化为完美的球形台球。此外，该模型将分子从其实际环境中抽象出来或移除，而实际环境正是分子碰撞发生的地方。

Fact-constructivism sounds too radical to many philosophers, and alienating to most scientists. So here is another approach against factual truth, well-known among philosophers of science. Over the past 40 years, they have produced an extraordinary amount of work on models in science. The role of abstractions and idealisations in scientific models, they maintain, is to select and to distort aspects of the relevant target system. The billiard-ball model of Brownian motion, for example, represents the motion of molecules by idealising them as perfectly spherical billiard balls. Moreover, the model abstracts, or removes, molecules from their actual environment, which is of course where collisions among molecules take place.

研究科学中的建模实践使一些人主张，科学不告诉我们真理，但确实提供了重要的非事实性理解。以波义耳（Boyle）气体定律为例，该定律描述了恒温下理想气体中压力 p 与体积 v 之间的关系。波义耳定律充其量只是在高度理想化和人为设定的条件下才是_ceteris paribus_（即，在其他条件不变的情况下）为真的。根本不存在具有完美球形分子的理想气体来展示使波义耳定律成真的「原子事实」（在一种准维特根斯坦式的意义上）。尽管不关于任何真实事物为真，布朗运动的台球模型和波义耳理想气体定律仍然提供了关于真实气体行为的重要的、非事实性的理解。因为它们使科学家能够理解任何气体中体积减小与压力增大之间的关系，即使自然界中不存在关于完美球形分子对应于此类理想化的原子事实。

Studying modelling practices in science has led some to argue that science does not tell the truth but it does provide important non-factive understanding. Consider, for instance, Boyle's gas law, which captures the relation between pressure p and volume v in an ideal gas at constant temperature. At best, Boyle's law is true ceteris paribus (ie, all else being equal) in highly idealised and contrived circumstances. There simply is no ideal gas with perfectly spherical molecules displaying 'atomic facts' (in a quasi-Wittgensteinian sense) that make Boyle's law true. Despite being true of nothing real, the billiard-ball model of Brownian motion and Boyle's ideal gas law do nonetheless provide important non-factual understanding of the behaviour of real gases. For they allow scientists to understand the relation between decreasing volume and increasing pressure in any gas, even if there are no atomic facts in nature about perfectly spherical molecules corresponding to such idealisations.

反教条主义和反一元论的科学路径也质疑了真理的价值以及真理的事实性。从 20 世纪 60 年代起，科学研究学者开始将「真理」一词视为令人不快的狭隘教条主义的代名词，以及在越南战争、后现代主义以及后来被称为「科学大战」的余波中文化内斗争的象征。许多人将物理学家托马斯·库恩（Thomas Kuhn）视为一种新历史主义趋势的先驱，这种趋势拆解了他们所认为的关于科学以真理为目标或追踪真理的天真观念。库恩自视为「一个热爱事实和追寻真理的人」。然而在其经典著作《科学革命的结构》（The Structure of Scientific Revolutions，1962）的结语中，他发出了一个有先见之明的、近乎不祥的警告：

Anti-dogmatic and anti-monist approaches to science have also questioned the value, as well as the facticity, of truth. From the 1960s, science-studies scholars began to see the word 'truth' as evoking unpalatable petty doctrinairism and intracultural battles in the wake of the Vietnamese war, postmodernism and, later on, what became known as the 'science wars'. Many saw the physicist Thomas Kuhn as the forefather of a new historicist trend that dismantled what they perceived as the naive view that science aims at or tracks truth. Kuhn saw himself as 'a fact lover and a truth seeker'. Yet in the final remarks to his classic The Structure of Scientific Revolutions (1962), he made a prescient, almost ominous, warning:

想象存在着某种关于自然的完整的、客观的、真实的描述，而科学成就的适当衡量标准是它在多大程度上使我们接近这一终极目标，这种想象真的有帮助吗？……这一发展过程的各个阶段以阐述和专门化的增加为标志。而整个过程可能就像我们现在认为的生物进化那样，在没有一个既定目标、一个永久固定的科学真理的情况下发生了——科学知识发展的每个阶段都是这一真理的更好范例。

Does it really help to imagine that there is some one full, objective, true account of nature and that the proper measure of scientific achievement is the extent to which it brings us closer to the ultimate goal? … Successive stages in that developmental process are marked by an increase in articulation and specialisation. And the entire process might have occurred, as we now suppose biological evolution did, without benefit of a set goal, a permanent fixed scientific truth, of which each stage in the development of scientific knowledge is a better exemplar.

对库恩而言，真理不是跨越科学革命的科学总目标。科学革命（例如从托勒密天文学到哥白尼天文学）也不追踪真理。它们所做的，充其量是增加我们解决困扰前一个范式的异常现象的能力（正如我们最终发现逆行运动只是一种错觉，而不需要本轮和均轮来解释）。

For Kuhn, truth is not an overarching aim of science across scientific revolutions. Nor do scientific revolutions (eg, from Ptolemaic to Copernican astronomy) track truth either. What they do, at best, is to increase our ability to solve anomalies that beset the previous paradigm (as when we eventually discovered that retrograde motion was only an illusion, and not something that needed epicycles and deferents to be explained).

我们今天在讨论中仍能看到库恩警告的精神。真理本身不足以解决甚至引导关于科学中的专业知识、信任、共识和分歧的争论。科学哲学家因马库拉达·德·梅洛-马丁（Inmaculada de Melo-Martín）和克丽丝滕·英特曼（Kristen Intemann）在她们的著作《对抗怀疑》（The Fight Against Doubt，2018）中很好地描述了这个问题。当涉及科学在政策制定中的作用时，关键在于「与所有相关方就涉及的价值观进行讨论，而不是关于特定科学主张的真理」。政策制定涉及政治和价值观，而「关于价值观的分歧不能也不应仅由科学家来决定」，也不能仅由科学证据来决定。英特曼和德·梅洛-马丁进一步主张，即使真理是科学的目标，学科或子领域共同体中的共识也不总能「作为真理的代理指标，而且它是否为采纳公共政策所必需仍然存疑」。

We see the spirit of Kuhn's warning in discussions today. Truth itself is not enough to settle or even guide debates about expertise, trust, consensus and dissent in science. The philosophers of science Inmaculada de Melo-Martín and Kristen Intemann have described the matter well in their book The Fight Against Doubt (2018). When it comes to the role of science in policymaking, the key is 'engaging in discussions with all relevant parties about the values at stake, rather than the truth of particular scientific claims'. Policymaking involves politics and values, and 'disagreement about values cannot, and should not, be decided by scientists alone' or by just scientific evidence. Intemann and de Melo-Martín have further maintained that, even if truth were the goal of science, consensus in a disciplinary or subfield community cannot always be 'a proxy for truth, and whether it is necessary for adopting public policy is still questionable'.

总而言之，科学史、科学实践和科学政策都为我们对科学中真理的祛魅提供了理由。这就是为什么在我们这个时代，自称实在论者的科学哲学家往往在「实在论」一词前加上某种限定性形容词（无论是「结构的」、「选择性的」、「实用的」、「视角的」还是「局部的」）。最终的结果——真理被拉下神坛——使关于真理本质的哲学讨论留给了逻辑学家、语言哲学家和形而上学家，而不是科学哲学家。

To summarise, the history of science, the practice of science, and science policy all provide reasons for disenchantment with truth in science. That is why, in our time, philosophers of science who call themselves realists tend to add some qualifying adjective to the word 'realism' (be it 'structural', 'selective', 'pragmatic', 'perspectival' or 'local'). The net result – the dethroning of truth – has left philosophical discussions about its nature to logicians, philosophers of language and metaphysicians, as opposed to philosophers of science.

第三个问题是，我们是否应该期望科学告诉我们真理，还是真理（或者至少事实真理的概念）最好留给逻辑学家和形而上学家？

The third question is whether we should expect science to tell us the truth, or is truth (or at least the notion of factual truth) not best left to logicians and metaphysicians?

虽然对事实真理的批判性分析确实最好留给逻辑学家和形而上学家，但科学哲学家不应放弃他们谈论科学中真理的责任。准维特根斯坦式的原子事实作为科学主张之真理制造者的神话已被证明甚至无法触及科学中极其复杂实践的表面。但这并不是放弃真理的好理由（或借口）。也不是得出科学不应被期望告诉我们真理这一结论的理由。科学哲学家有谈论科学中真理的社会责任。关于真理的问题不能被搁置不谈、被委托他人，或更糟的是，以不满和疑虑来回应。因为认真对待科学中真理这一话题——尽管它可能棘手而困难——必须是任何在民主、宽容和多元社会中认真对待科学的出发点。

While critical analyses of factual truth are indeed best left to logicians and metaphysicians, philosophers of science should not abdicate their responsibility to talk about truth in science. The quasi-Wittgensteinian myth of atomic facts as the truth-makers of scientific claims has proved inadequate to even scratch the surface of very complex practices in science. But that is not a good reason (or pretext) for forgoing truth altogether. Nor is it a reason for concluding that science should not be expected to tell us the truth. Philosophers of science have a social responsibility to talk about truth in science. Questions about truth cannot be left unspoken, delegated, or, worse, met with discontent and misgivings. For a serious engagement with the topic of truth in science – thorny and difficult as it might be – must be the starting point of any serious engagement with science in democratic, tolerant and pluralistic societies.

但是谁的真理？根据谁的标准？此时，有些人可能会被詹姆斯式的实用主义真理论所吸引。美国实用主义传统上提供了一种另类的思考真理的方式，一些科学哲学家认为这种方式更适合捕捉科学实践的复杂细微之处和权力结构。约翰·杜威（John Dewey）、威廉·詹姆斯（William James）和查尔斯·桑德斯·皮尔士（Charles Sanders Peirce）等实用主义者之间在真理问题上存在重大而重要的差异，不应将这一多样而细致的哲学观点谱系混同或漫画化。用詹姆斯的话说：「「真的」……只是我们思维方式中的权宜之计，正如「对的」只是我们行为方式中的权宜之计。」撇开修辞上的修饰，对詹姆斯来说，为真（近似地说）就是成功地起作用。一个科学模型——按照一种宽泛的詹姆斯式观点——如果它成功地促进和便利了各种活动（无论是否与认识相关），就是真的。例如，如果布朗运动的台球模型帮助科学家预测气体分子的行为，那么该模型就是（实用主义意义上）真的。完美球形分子这一假定的错误并不重要。詹姆斯的真理概念是否更适合科学，更有助于民主社会中的多元主义？

But whose truth? By whose lights? Some might be tempted at this point by a Jamesian pragmatist theory of truth. American pragmatism has traditionally provided an alternative way of thinking about truth, which some philosophers of science see as more congenial to capturing the complex nuances and the power structure of scientific practice. There are significant and important differences among pragmatists such as John Dewey, William James and Charles Sanders Peirce when it comes to truth, and one should avoid assimilating or caricaturing this diverse and nuanced range of philosophical views. In James's words: '"The true" … is only the expedient in the way of our thinking, just as "the right" is only the expedient in the way of our behaving.' Stripped of its rhetorical flourishes, for James to be true is (to a good approximation) to work successfully. A scientific model is true – on a loosely Jamesian view – if it successfully facilitates and enables activities (be they epistemic or not). If the billiard-ball model of Brownian motion helps scientists to predict the behaviour of gas molecules, for example, the model is (pragmatically) true. The falseness of the presumption of perfectly spherical molecules does not matter. Is the Jamesian conception of truth more congenial to science, and more conducive to pluralism in democratic societies?

在我看来，受詹姆斯启发的真理概念的风险在于，它过于柔韧，无法抵挡时间的浪潮和在科学中无休止运作的社会力量的压力。受詹姆斯启发的真理观放弃了对科学告诉我们真理的期望，代之以一种未加充分界定的科学实践的「成功」。但如何区分成功确实追踪真理的情况与成功并未追踪真理的情况？更重要的是，当涉及气候变化、儿童接种疫苗的益处或经济预测等问题时，我们似乎需要比柔韧的詹姆斯式真理概念更多的东西，以做出不屈从于强大的游说团体和政治议程压力的科学决策（以「可能有效的东西」的名义）。但有人可能会反驳说，如果不选择詹姆斯式的真理概念（如果我们真的在乎真理的话），真理和多元主义如何能够并行不悖？

The risk with a James-inspired conception of truth, as I see it, is that it is too malleable to resist the tides of time and the stresses of social forces endlessly at work in science. A James-inspired view of truth abdicates the expectation that science tells us the truth in the name of a non-better-qualified kind of success of a scientific practice. But how to tell apart cases where success does indeed track truth from cases where it does not? More to the point, when it comes to matters such as climate change, the benefit of vaccinating children, or economic forecasts, we seem to need more than a malleable Jamesian conception of truth for the sake of scientifically informed decisions that do not bow to pressure from powerful lobbies and political agendas (in the name of what 'might work'). But, someone might reply, how can truth and pluralism go hand in hand if not by opting for a Jamesian conception of truth (if we really care about truth at all)?

还有另一种思考真理和多元主义如何并行不悖的方式，而无需将真理问题简化为计算对某个时刻共享某一科学视角的个人或共同体什么是实用的。首先，有必要理解「科学视角」这一关键术语及其与科学多元主义的关系。哲学家罗纳德·吉尔（Ronald Giere）于 2006 年最初使用时，「科学视角」类似于库恩的学科基质：一组科学模型（包括收集数据的相关实验仪器）。更广泛地说，科学视角是任何历史时期真实科学共同体的学科实践。它包括他们所生产的知识，以及他们所使用的或指导其工作的理论、技术和实验资源。

There is another way of thinking about how truth and pluralism might go hand in hand, without reducing matters of truth to calculations of what is pragmatically good to individuals or communities sharing a scientific perspective at some point in time. First, it is necessary to understand the key term 'scientific perspective' and how it impinges on scientific pluralism. In its original use by the philosopher Ronald Giere in 2006, 'scientific perspective' is akin to Kuhn's disciplinary matrix: a set of scientific models (including the relevant experimental instruments to gather data). In broader terms, scientific perspective is the disciplinary practice of a real scientific community at any given historical time. It includes the knowledge they produce, and the theoretical, technological and experimental resources they use, or that guide their work.

真理是科学知识中固有的规范性承诺。

Truth is a normative commitment inherent in scientific knowledge.

因此，在物理学中，可以说有牛顿（Newton）视角与爱因斯坦（Einstein）视角；在化学中，有普里斯特利视角与拉瓦锡视角；在经济学中，有凯恩斯主义（Keynesian）视角与货币主义视角；在生物学中，有经典遗传学视角与分子遗传学视角，等等。关于这一繁荣多样的科学视角阵列，有何可说？有一点是确定的：科学通过产生多元视角而运作。随着时间的推移，这推动了科学进步——孔德错误地将其理论化为一种「实证科学」的一元论图景。但科学视角的多元性并不能解决真理问题。相反，它引发了令人担忧的想法：真理对科学视角而言要么是多余的；或者更糟，被相对化到各个视角中。人们可能再次追问：谁的真理？根据谁的标准？

Thus, in physics, one can speak of the Newtonian perspective vs the Einsteinian perspective; in chemistry, the Priestley vs Lavoisier perspective; in economics, the Keynesian vs the monetarist perspective; or the classical-genetics vs molecular-genetics perspective in biology, and so forth. What is to be said about the flourishing array of scientific perspectives? One thing is certain: science works through producing a plurality of perspectives. Over time, it leads to scientific progress, a process Comte mistakenly theorised into a monist picture of 'positive science'. But a plurality of scientific perspectives does not solve the problem of truth. On the contrary, it invites worrisome thoughts that truth is either redundant to scientific perspectives; or, worse, relativised to perspectives. One might ask again: whose truth? By whose lights?

为科学中的真理进行辩护的时候到了。它始于一种把事情弄对的承诺，这是实在论纲领的核心，尽管科学史中不断涌现的库恩式挑战、关于建模的考量以及当代科学实践中的价值观都在质疑这一点。在简单的意义上，把事情弄对意味着事物确实如相关科学理论所说的那样。如果气候科学所说的关于二氧化碳排放（及其对气候变化的影响）对应于自然界中事物的实际状况，那它就是真的。为了强大的经济利益、社会政治后果或仅仅是不同的经济原则，人们可以试图贬低、减轻、补偿、忽视或完全无视事物的实际状况。但这样做就是放弃了科学中实在论承诺的规范性。我们已经看到，科学世界太过复杂和混乱，无法被任何准维特根斯坦式的原子事实图景所代表。孔德实证科学的天真形象也无法公正地对待它。但承认复杂性和历史细微差别并不能成为放弃真理的理由（或正当理由），更不能得出科学从事某种虚假之事的结论。作为科学哲学家，在此类问题上澄清事实是我们社会责任的一部分。

The time for a defence of truth in science has come. It begins with a commitment to get things right, which is at the heart of the realist programme, despite mounting Kuhnian challenges from the history of science, considerations about modelling, and values in contemporary scientific practice. In the simple-minded sense, getting things right means that things are as the relevant scientific theory says that they are. Climate science is true if what it says about CO2 emissions (and their effects on climate change) corresponds to the way that things are in nature. For the sake of powerful economic interests, sociopolitical consequences or simply different economic principles, one can try to discount, mitigate, compensate for, disregard or ignore altogether the way that things are. But doing so is to forgo the normative nature of the realist commitment in science. The scientific world, we have seen, is too complex and messy to be represented by any quasi-Wittgensteinian picture of atomic facts. Nor can the naive image of Comte's positive science render justice to it. But acknowledging complexity and historical nuances gives no reason (or justification) for forgoing truth altogether; much less for concluding that science trades in falsehoods of some kind. It is part of our social responsibility as philosophers of science to set the record straight on such matters.

我们应该期望科学告诉我们真理，因为按照实在论的标准，这正是科学应当做的。真理——被理解为把事情弄对——不是科学的目的，因为它不是科学（或更准确地说，科学家）应该追求的东西（假设一个人有实在论倾向）。相反，它是按照实在论标准科学应当做的事。因此，将一个科学理论或模型判断为真的，就是将其判断为一个「值得我们赞同」的理论或模型。真理最终不是一种追求；一个令人向往（但也许无法实现）的目标；一个在一线科学家脑海中的虚构；或者更糟，科学研究中一个难以承受且可有可无的负担。真理是科学知识中固有的规范性承诺。

We should expect science to tell us the truth because, by realist lights, this is what science ought to do. Truth – understood as getting things right – is not the aim of science, because it is not what science (or, better, scientists) should aspire to (assuming one has realist leanings). Instead, it is what science ought to do by realist lights. Thus, to judge a scientific theory or model as true is to judge it as one that 'commands our assent'. Truth, ultimately, is not an aspiration; a desirable (but maybe unachievable) goal; a figment in the mind of the working scientist; or, worse, an insupportable and dispensable burden in scientific research. Truth is a normative commitment inherent in scientific knowledge.

建构经验主义者、工具主义者、詹姆斯式实用主义者、相对主义者和建构主义者不分享同样的承诺。他们不与实在论者共享一个恰当的「正确性」概念。举个例子，将对把事情弄对的规范性承诺与哲学家理查德·罗蒂（Richard Rorty）的观点做一比较。在罗蒂手中，普特南的真理作为「理想化的有保证的可断言性」被简化为对「我们理想中的我们……我们这些受过教育的、老练的、宽容的、温和的自由派，那些总是愿意倾听另一面、思考所有含义的人」来说可接受的东西。把事情弄对不是关于处于最佳状态的「我们」——「受过教育的、老练的、宽容的、温和的自由派」——的规范。它是科学知识中固有的规范。声称在科学中（或关于某个科学主题或领域）知道某事，就是声称对关于该主题或领域的相关信念的真理性的主张。但批评者可能反驳说，我们的知识——它是视角性的，深植于历史和文化上明确的科学实践之中——如何能够追踪世界实际的样子？我们怎么能被期望真诚地相信我们在科学中所相信的东西，尽管我们置身于多元的科学视角之中？换一种方式说，我们——作为历史和文化上情境化的认知主体——如何能够可靠地随着时间推移建立科学知识，尽管过去（以及当前）的科学视角可能存在错误和失误？

Constructive empiricists, instrumentalists, Jamesian pragmatists, relativists and constructivists do not share the same commitment. They do not share with the realist a suitable notion of 'rightness'. As an example, compare the normative commitment to get things right with the view of the philosopher Richard Rorty, in whose hands Putnam's truth as 'idealised warranted assertibility' reduces to what is acceptable to 'us as we should like to be … us educated, sophisticated, tolerant, wet liberals, the people who are always willing to hear the other side, to think out all the implications'. Getting things right is not a norm about us at our best, 'educated, sophisticated, tolerant, wet liberals'. It is a norm inherent in scientific knowledge. To claim to know something in science (or about a scientific topic or domain) is to claim for the truth of the relevant beliefs about that topic or domain. But, a critic might reply, how can our knowledge – which is perspectival, entrenched in historically and culturally well-defined scientific practices – track the way the world is? How can we be expected to truthfully believe the things we believe in science, despite being situated in a plurality of scientific perspectives? To put it in a different way, how can we – historically and culturally situated epistemic agents – reliably build scientific knowledge over time, despite the possible errors and false steps of past (and current) scientific perspectives?

将真理视为科学知识概念中固有的规范性承诺会带来一些好处。它克服了原子事实与非事实性的、非朝向真理的推论之间的虚假二分法。它使实在论与视角主义兼容。支持历史和文化上情境化的科学视角的科学共同体（无论是跨科学史还是在当代科学中，跨不同领域或不同科学纲领）共享（而且确实应该共享）一种把事情弄对的规范性承诺。这是通过我们视为「科学知识」的门槛的最低要求。

Thinking of truth as a normative commitment inherent in the very notion of scientific knowledge brings some benefits. It overcomes a false dichotomy between atomic facts and non-factive, non-truth-conducive inferences. And it makes realism compatible with perspectivism. Scientific communities that endorse historically and culturally situated scientific perspectives (either across the history of science or in contemporary science, across different fields or different scientific programmes) share (and indeed ought to) a normative commitment to get things right. That is a minimum requirement to pass the bar of what we count as 'scientific knowledge'.

自 16 世纪以来，科学方法一直是一场重要争论的核心。人们希望一种方法（如果能找到的话）能够带来应当被共享、传播和认同的结论。但我们了解到，不存在单一的科学方法，不存在某种在任何地方、任何时间、跨学科有效的神奇配方。然而，科学视角的多元性并非通向某种令人担忧的视角性唯我论的捷径，这得益于科学视角应当（而且通常确实）共享的规范性实在论承诺。

Since the 16th century, the method of science has been at the centre of an important debate. The hope was that a method (if any could be identified) could lead to conclusions that ought to be shared, communicated, agreed upon. But we learned that there is no single scientific method, some magic recipe valid always and everywhere, across disciplines. A plurality of scientific perspectives is not however a royal road to some worrisome kind of perspectival solipsism, thanks to the normative realist commitment that scientific perspectives ought to (and typically do) share.

支持不同科学视角的科学家（我在此强调「科学」这一形容词，以排除可能利用「视角」一词歧义的可能反驳）并不建构视角性的事实。这样做可能构成科学欺诈。相反，他们应当共享对同一证据法庭的承诺。他们可能采用不同的实验策略、不同的模型、不同的理论。他们可能对某个领域什么才是真正重要的持有不同的价值观。但证据法庭是他们最终都应当回应的。首先把证据弄对——通过准确的测量、合理的非特设性程序以及稳健的推理策略——定义了任何值得被称为「科学」的研究纲领。把事情弄对的实在论承诺必须从把证据弄对开始。没有任何值得被称为「科学」的视角能在伪造证据、篡改或更改数据或丢弃证据之后存活下来。

Scientists endorsing different scientific perspectives (and I stress the adjective scientific here to undercut a possible rejoinder that might trade on the ambiguity of the word 'perspective') do not construct perspectival facts. To do so would be to possibly commit scientific fraud. Rather, they ought to share a commitment to the same tribunal of evidence. They might adopt different experimental strategies, different models, different theories. They might endorse different values about what really matters in a given field. But it is the tribunal of evidence that they all ultimately ought to respond to. Getting the evidence right, in the first instance – via accurate measurements, sound non-ad-hoc procedures, and robust inferential strategies – defines any research programme that is worth being called 'scientific'. The realist commitment to get things right must begin with getting the evidence right. No perspective worthy of being called 'scientific' survives fudging the evidence, massaging or altering the data or discarding evidence.

支持不同科学视角的科学家并不炮制用以评估其知识主张及其随时间推移的韧性的科学标准。这样做会带来偏颇或特设操作的风险。显然，「科学标准」的概念是复杂的，必须谨慎行事。尽管如此，总的来说，科学家应当共享对共同科学标准的承诺。他们可能对什么算作一个「更简单的」理论存在分歧（例如，引入新实体与修改自然定律）；他们可能在涉及不同尺度的建模时，对一致性与解释力之间的权衡给予不同的重视。但他们应当同意，任何可行的科学理论都必须能够大致按照这些（以及许多其他可能的）科学标准来解释现有证据。

Scientists endorsing different scientific perspectives do not concoct the scientific standards by which their knowledge claims, and their resilience over time, are assessed. To do so would run the risk of partiality, or ad-hoc manoeuvring. Obviously, the notion of 'scientific standard' is complex and one has to tread carefully. Nonetheless, by and large (without any presumption of producing an exhaustive list), scientists ought to share a commitment to common scientific standards. They might disagree about what counts as a 'simpler' theory (eg, introducing new entities vs modifying laws of nature); they might place a different emphasis on the role of consistency vs explanatory power when it comes to modelling at different scales. But they ought to agree that any viable scientific theory must be able to make sense of the available evidence broadly along the lines of those (among many other possible) scientific standards.

真理不是可以随旧科学范式一起抛弃的商品。

Truth is not a commodity disposable with the old scientific paradigm.

科学家应当共享跨视角评估的规则。我们的知识是情境化的和视角性的，这一事实并不使科学真理被相对化到各个视角中。往往，科学视角本身就提供了跨视角评估的规则。这些规则可以很简单，比如将今天爱丁堡（Edinburgh）的 10 摄氏度温度换算为华氏 50 度的等效值。它们也可以很复杂，比如在统计力学中检索流体的粘度，其中流体被视为大量离散分子的统计系综。在其他时候，科学家可能并不一定拥有一本用于将知识主张从一个科学视角「翻译」到另一个科学视角的「规则手册」。在那些情况下，我们需要类似于库恩所谓的「双语能力」（而非翻译）。

Scientists ought to share rules for cross-perspectival assessment. That our knowledge is situated and perspectival does not make scientific truths relativised to perspectives. Often enough, scientific perspectives themselves provide the rules for cross-perspectival assessment. Those rules can be as simple as translating the 10 degree Celsius temperature in Edinburgh today into the 50 degree equivalent on the Fahrenheit scale. Or they can be as complex as retrieving the viscosity of a fluid in statistical mechanics, where fluids are treated as statistical ensembles of a large number of discrete molecules. At other times, scientists might not necessarily possess a rule-book, so to speak, for 'translating' knowledge claims from one scientific perspective into another. In those situations, we need something like what Kuhn called 'bilingualism' (rather than translation).

即使是在真正的科学革命中，当旧的分类体系崩塌、全新的、前所未见的分类体系取而代之时，真理也不是一种可以随旧科学范式一起抛弃的商品。库恩的批评者——他们强调危机时期和科学革命中的连续性，并关注科学概念跨世纪的微妙变迁——在这里有道理。即，正是我们把事情弄对的承诺——跨越视角（实际上跨越重大的概念变革）——使得（革命前后的）共同体能够相互理解并在科学知识上不断增长——无论他们的科学主张可能以多么不同的、视角性的地方语言来表达。

Even in genuine scientific revolutions, when old taxonomies fall and new, previously undreamt ones, take their place, truth is not a commodity disposable with the old scientific paradigm. Critics of Kuhn – who emphasised continuities during periods of crises and scientific revolutions, and paid attention to the subtle nuances that scientific concepts undergo across centuries – have a point here. Namely, that it is our commitment to get things right across perspectives (indeed across major conceptual upheavals) that allows communities (before and after a revolution) to understand one another and to grow in their scientific knowledge – no matter how different and perspectival the local idioms in which their scientific claims might be couched.

伽利略试图逃避宗教裁判所，他提出了一种新的「重力」（gravitas）概念，最终在数世纪以来对亚里士多德（Aristotle）运动理论的批评背景下推进了哥白尼主义的新词汇——这些批评由让·布里丹（Jean Buridan）和尼克尔·奥雷斯姆（Nicole Oresme）在 14 世纪的巴黎提出，在他们之前则是由中世纪阿拉伯世界的亚里士多德注释者（所谓的巴格达学派，以及阿布·巴拉卡特（Abu'l-Barakāt）对加速运动几乎具有先见之明的研究）所完成。拉瓦锡是法国大革命的悲剧性牺牲品，他知道如何运用旧药剂师的重量概念来推进他对燃素理论的批判，并引入了氧气的全新概念以及化学的新角色。中国人关于如何使用硝石制造火药的知识通过丝绸之路传入欧洲，使得罗伯特·波义耳（Robert Boyle）和艾萨克·牛顿（Isaac Newton）极具影响力的关于爆炸中释放「真正恒久的空气」的推测性实验主义成为可能，并为 18 世纪英国和荷兰自然哲学中对火和电的整个研究纲领奠定了基础（除了在烟火艺术中的应用）。正如桑德拉·哈丁（Sandra Harding）在《客观性与多样性》（Objectivity and Diversity，2015）中所论证的，密克罗尼西亚的航海系统知识至今仍有许多值得我们学习的地方。

Galileo, trying to escape the Inquisition, arrived at a new concept of gravitas that eventually advanced the new lexicon of Copernicanism against the backdrop of century-long criticisms of Aristotle's theory of motion, elaborated by Jean Buridan and Nicole Oresme in 14th-century Paris, and before them by the Medieval Arabic commentators of Aristotle (the so-called Baghdad school, and Abu'l-Barakāt's almost prescient study of accelerated motion). Lavoisier, a tragic victim of the French Revolution, knew how to use the old apothecary's concept of weight to advance his critique of phlogiston theory and to usher in the new concept of oxygen and a new role for chemistry. Chinese knowledge about how to use saltpeter for making gunpowder entered Europe through the Silk Road and made possible Robert Boyle and Isaac Newton's highly influential speculative experimentalism about 'true permanent air' being released in explosions, and an entire research programme for the study of fire and electricity in the British and Dutch natural philosophy of the 18th century (in addition to its use in pyrotechnic arts). And as Sandra Harding has argued in Objectivity and Diversity (2015), Micronesian knowledge about navigation systems has a lot to teach us today.

毫无疑问：科学知识是我们在多元文化的视角性科学史上把事情弄对的结果。科学知识不是我们西方文化视角（及其特定学科的科学视角）的特权，而是历史上和文化上情境化的多元科学视角的产物，这些视角数千年来凭借各自可用的工具、资源和概念，可靠地生产着知识。

Let there be no doubt: scientific knowledge is the product of our getting it right across our perspectival multicultural scientific history. Scientific knowledge is not a prerogative of our Western cultural perspective (and its discipline-specific scientific perspectives) but the outcome of a plurality of historically and culturally situated scientific perspectives that, over millennia, have reliably produced knowledge with the tools, resources and concepts respectively available to each and every one of them.

认知主体的共同体学会了如何在时间中、在历史上和文化上情境化的科学视角之间把事情弄对。这不是因为存在着作为原子命题之真理制造者的原子事实，而是因为我们的科学知识的视角性特征类似于维特根斯坦所说的那根线——「我们把纤维拧到纤维上。线的强度不在于某根纤维贯穿其整个长度，而在于许多纤维的重叠。」科学真理是多元科学视角的坚韧而稳健的产物，这些视角随着时间的推移，以它们（默会的、隐含的且往往是生存适应性的）规范性承诺——为我们人类可靠地生产科学知识——相互交织。这就是为什么真理非但不是对科学多元主义的难以忍受的障碍，反而是其在宽容、开放和民主的社会中的最佳保障——这些社会真正致力于以科学知识所呈现的众多面貌来推动其发展。

Communities of epistemic agents learn how to get things right across time, across historically and culturally situated scientific perspectives. Not because there are atomic facts as truth-makers of atomic propositions, but because the perspectival nature of our scientific knowledge resembles what Wittgenstein called the thread that 'we twist fibre on fibre. And the strength of the thread resides not in the fact that some one fibre runs through its whole length, but in the overlapping of many fibres.' Scientific truths are the resilient and robust outcome of a plurality of scientific perspectives that, over time, have meshed with one another in their (tacit, implicit and often survival-adaptive) normative commitment to reliably produce scientific knowledge for us as humankind. That is why, far from being an insufferable hindrance to scientific pluralism, truth is in fact its best safeguard in tolerant, open and democratic societies that are genuinely committed to the advancement of scientific knowledge in the very many faces it comes with.
