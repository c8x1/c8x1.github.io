人工智能提出深刻的道德问题——全人类都应参与回答

Renée Sieber & Emmanuelle Vaast·The Conversation (McGill University)·2026年6月18日·3.5分钟阅读·原文

你会在边境被标记吗？你的房贷申请会获批吗？战争时期，武器系统会瞄准哪个社区？这些都是涉及伤害与公平的道德选择——过去由人来做出。

Will you be flagged at the border? Will your mortgage application be approved? During wartime, whose neighbourhood would a weapon system target? These are moral choices — about harm and fairness — and they used to be made by people.

如今，这样的道德选择由人工智能（AI）及其开发公司做出。不是政府，不是公众，而是企业。

Now moral choices like these are made by artificial intelligence (AI) and by the companies developing it. Not government, not the public, but corporations.

克里斯·奥拉（Chris Olah），AI公司Anthropic的联合创始人、自称无神论者，最近在梵蒂冈坐在教皇利奥十四（Pope Leo XIV）身旁，坦言他所在行业不可信任其自行治理。他说：「有人可能认为AI事务最好交给像我这样的计算机科学家来处理，他们错了。」

Chris Olah, co-founder of the AI company Anthropic and a self-described atheist, recently sat beside Pope Leo XIV at the Vatican and said his own industry cannot be trusted to govern itself. “Some might believe that matters of AI are best handled by computer scientists like myself,” he said. “They are mistaken.”

奥拉呼应了教皇的第一份通谕《宏光人道》（Magnifica Humanitas：论人工智能时代守护人之为人），该通谕警告AI必须服务于人类，而非集中权力。

Olah was echoing the Pope’s first encyclical, Magnifica Humanitas: On Safeguarding the Human Person in the Time of Artificial Intelligence, which warns that AI must serve humanity rather than concentrate power.

显然，AI需要一个独立的监管机构，拥有否决的权力——就像当局可以拒绝一款新药或阻止一座核反应堆一样。普通人，不仅仅是技术专家，需要设定该监管机构执行的道德标准。

It is clear that AI needs an independent regulator with the power to say no in the same way that authorities can refuse a new drug or block a nuclear reactor. Ordinary people, not just technical experts, need to set the moral standards this regulator enforces.

开发者并非必然疏忽或冷漠。我们的研究表明，许多AI开发者深切反思其工作的道德层面，同时承认那些让这些关切变得边缘化的压力。

Developers are not necessarily careless or cynical. Our research shows many AI developers deeply question the moral aspects of their work while acknowledging the pressures that can make these concerns more peripheral.

在一个以快速行动为使命的公司内部坚守道德底线并不容易。

It is hard to hold a moral line inside a company built to move fast.

作为数字技术研究者，我们给负责管理这种张力的人员起了一个名字：「伦理负责人」（ethics owners）。这些人既要回应外界批评，又完全留在引发批评的公司内部。

As digital technology researchers, we have a name for the staff hired to manage this tension: “ethics owners.” These are the people tasked with responding to outside criticism while remaining wholly inside the company that provokes it.

技术伦理学者还指出，企业AI伦理可能硬化为一种「美德经济」（economy of virtue）——一个伦理技能、知识与审批的市场，企业必须采购这些资源以瓦解批评者并抵御监管。

Scholars of technology ethics also argue that corporate AI ethics can harden into an “economy of virtue” — a marketplace of ethical skills, knowledge and approvals that companies must procure to disarm critics and fend off regulation.

《宏光人道》的发布和奥拉的发言时机恰当。世界各国政府正在制定AI规则。加拿大刚推出联邦「AI惠及所有人」战略，却几乎没有关注安全或伦理，更没有讨论谁来决定更棘手的道德问题。

The launch of Magnifica Humanitas and Olah’s speech were timely. Governments around the world are writing the rules about AI right now. Canada just launched a federal AI for All strategy with little focus on safety or ethics, let alone discussion of who will decide the harder moral questions.

纠正必须来自外部。公众必须拥有发言权。

The corrective must come from the outside. The public must have a voice.

相关实验已经存在。Anthropic邀请约一千名美国人参与为其AI聊天机器人Claude的一个版本撰写规则，该项目称为「集体宪章AI」（Collective Constitutional AI）。

Experiments already exist. Anthropic invited about 1,000 Americans to help write the rules for a version of its AI chatbot Claude in a project called Collective Constitutional AI.

公众参与研究者研究了公民议会（citizens' assemblies）——由随机选出的公民组成，研究某一议题并达成共同建议——这种方式已被用于处理诸如堕胎立法和无家可归等高度争议性的问题。它们为公众参与AI治理带来了希望。

Public engagement researchers have studied citizens’ assemblies — groups of randomly selected people who study an issue and reach shared recommendations — as a way of tackling questions as charged as abortion law and homelessness. They offer promise for public participation in the governance of AI.

然而这些实践很少能真正影响决策。研究表明，公众通常被晚阶段引入、被问及狭窄的问题、对结果没有权力。公司或政府机构控制着议程、数据和决策。

Yet these exercises rarely shift real decisions. Studies show the public is typically brought in late, asked narrow questions and given no power over the outcome. A corporation or government agency controls the agenda, the data and the decision.

当参与只是咨询性质时，它可以滑向我们所说的「参与洗白」（participation-washing）——有公众发声的外观，却无公众权力的实质。可以被推翻的输入不是治理，只是让AI治理看起来比实际更具民主性的咨询。

When participation is advisory, it can slide into what we call “participation-washing” — the appearance of public voice without the substance of public power. Input that can be overruled is not governance. It is consultation that makes AI governance look more democratic than it is.

我们如何处理既能治愈也能杀人的其他技术？我们不会让制药公司独自决定一款药物是否安全上市。独立监管机构权衡证据并可以否决。我们不会让核设施自行建造反应堆并认证其安全。

How do we handle other technologies that can heal or kill? We do not let a pharmaceutical company alone decide that a drug is safe to sell. An independent regulator weighs the evidence and can say no. We do not let a nuclear facility build a reactor and certify its own safety.

几十年前，我们认定某些选择太过重大，不能留给从中获利的人。这些选择中，技术问题之下隐藏着道德问题。多安全才算足够安全？我们作为一个社会接受多少伤害，谁被迫承受——穷人、老年人、少数群体？

Decades ago, we decided some choices are too consequential to leave to those who profit from them. These choices have moral questions hiding inside the technical ones. How safe is safe enough? How much harm do we as a society accept and who is made to bear it — the poor, the elderly, a minority?

民主道德治理将这种选择交到公众手中。在AI的语境下，人民应设定监管机构适用的标准，应决定底线划在哪里。

Democratic moral governance moves this choice into public hands. In the case of AI, people should set the standards a regulator applies and should determine where the lines are drawn.

一个显而易见的反对意见是，AI不同于药物和核能。AI创新的速度快于任何药物试验；没有一个单一产品可以审批；它不受国界约束。而且很难在抽象层面为AI颁发许可，因为它涵盖从精准营销到机器人导航和面部识别等众多技术。

An obvious objection is that AI differs from pharmaceuticals and nuclear power. AI innovation is moving faster than any drug trial; there is no single product to approve; it respects no borders. And it is difficult to license AI in the abstract because it covers so many technologies, from targeted marketing to robot navigation and facial recognition.

然而作为一个社会，我们可以做出具体决定：是否发布一个强大的新模型，是否在警务、医院或法庭中部署它。监管机构可以滚动审查各类问题。此外，因为AI无视国界，共享的国际监督是合理的。2025年在联合国创建的「AI治理全球对话」（Global Dialogue on AI Governance）值得认真对待。

As a society we can, however, make discrete decisions: whether to release a powerful new model or to deploy one in policing, hospitals or courts. A regulator can review issues on a rolling basis. Also, because AI ignores borders, shared international oversight makes sense. The Global Dialogue on AI Governance, created at the United Nations in 2025, is worth taking seriously.

社区同样重要：城市正在本地部署各类AI，正在摸索治理问题。社区成员在关于AI如何使用的决策中——无论是修补路坑还是建造住房——都应发挥作用。

Communities matter as well: cities are deploying various kinds of AI at a local scale and are navigating governance issues. Community members have a role in decisions about how AI is used, whether to fix potholes or build housing.

加拿大展示了已有进展和仍有差距。联邦《自动化决策指令》（Directive on Automated Decision-Making）要求联邦政府的自动化系统透明且负责。其《算法影响评估》（Algorithmic Impact Assessment）是政府内部使用的自主决策系统的强制性风险评估工具。

Canada illustrates how far this has come and how far it still has to go. The federal Directive on Automated Decision-Making requires the federal government’s automated systems to be transparent and accountable. Its Algorithmic Impact Assessment is a mandatory risk assessment tool for autonomous decision systems used within government.

这些工具依赖联邦机构和部门的自报。它们尽力治理加拿大政府对AI的使用。然而，迄今为止的选择仍然是在向技术专家而非公众征询何为可接受、何应被阻止。

These tools rely on self-reporting from federal agencies and departments. They do their best to govern how Canadian governments use AI. Still, choices have so far involved asking technical experts instead of the public what counts as acceptable and what should be stopped.

这是民主道德治理需要弥合的缺口。

This is a gap for democratic moral governance to close.

加拿大的《自动化决策指令》和《算法影响评估》被视为黄金标准。然而，诸如加拿大《数字宪章》（Digital Charter）、2025年10月的30天在线公众咨询以及最近的联邦AI战略「AI惠及所有人」等举措表明，加拿大不应被奉为AI公众参与的典范。

Canada’s Directive on Automated Decision-Making and Algorithmic Impact Assessment are considered to be the gold standard. However, initiatives like Canada’s Digital Charter, the 30-day online public consultation in October 2025 and the recent federal AI strategy, AI for “All,” have demonstrated that Canada should not be heralded as a model for public participation around AI.

AI的民主道德治理是可能的，但前提是公众的道德判断须伴随政治权力。低于此即为表演。

Democratic moral governance of AI is possible, but only if the public’s moral judgment is accompanied by political power. Anything less is performative.

教皇和克里斯·奥拉一致认为，公司不能决定AI应该对我们做什么。尚未完成的民主工作，在于找到让人民就道德问题做出决定并拥有执行这些决定的权威的途径。

The Pope and Chris Olah agree companies cannot decide what AI should do to us. The unfinished, democratic work involves finding ways for people to decide on moral issues and to have the authority to enforce those decisions.
