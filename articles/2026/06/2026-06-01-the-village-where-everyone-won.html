人人皆赢的村庄

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年6月1日·2分钟阅读

在两座繁华贸易城市之间的一个狭窄山口，坐落着一个名叫卡萨（Karsa）的村庄。每天清晨，两位互相竞争的茶商——老布伦（Bren）和年轻的西娅（Thea）——都会推着木车前往当天的集市。每个人都必须在两条路中选择一条：高脊路（high ridge road）很快，但非常窄，如果两人都走这条路，就要卡上好几个小时才能勉强错车；山谷路（valley road）蜿蜒曲折，比较慢，但足够宽敞，多少辆马车都能并排行走。在太阳升起、车子已经上路之前，谁都不知道对方选了哪条路。

In a narrow mountain pass between two great trading cities, there sat a village called Karsa. Every morning, two rival tea merchants — old Bren and young Thea — would push their wooden carts to the day's market. Each had to choose one of two roads: the high ridge road, which was fast but so narrow that if both took it they would be stuck for hours edging past one another, or the winding valley road, which was slow but wide enough for any number of carts. Neither knew the other's choice until the sun rose and the carts were already rolling.

一开始，布伦和西娅试图互相猜测对方的选择。布伦会提前一个小时起床，偷看西娅的车，但西娅看穿了这个把戏，开始在一条路旁放一辆假车做诱饵，自己却走另一条路。他们花在互相算计上的精力比卖茶还多。有些日子，两人都走上了高脊路，陷入固执的对峙，车轮互相摩擦，而城里的顾客早已从别的商人那里买到了茶。还有些日子，两人都走了山谷路，到得太晚，完全错过了早市的高峰。每个人都怪对方，谁也不肯改变。

At first, Bren and Thea tried to outguess each other. Bren would wake an hour earlier to peek at Thea's cart, but Thea caught on and began leaving a decoy cart by one road while taking the other. They spent more energy on trickery than on selling tea. Some days both ended up on the high ridge, locked in a stubborn standoff, wheels of their carts scraping while customers in the city bought from other merchants. Other days both took the valley road and arrived late, missing the morning rush entirely. Each blamed the other, and neither changed.

一天傍晚，一位名叫哈林（Harin）的旅行教师路过卡萨，听到两位商人各自抱怨。他用树枝在泥土上画了四个方格。如果两人都走高脊路，两人都吃亏。如果两人都走山谷路，两人都小有损失。如果一人走高脊路、另一人走山谷路，走高脊路的人先到，获利丰厚，而走山谷路的人也能及时赶到，过上不错的日子。哈林抬起头说：「不要再想着打败对方了。换一种方式思考：无论对方做什么选择，你是否有某个选择会让你后悔？」

One evening a traveling schoolteacher named Harin stopped in Karsa and listened to the two merchants complain. He drew four squares in the dirt with a stick. If both took the ridge, they both lost. If both took the valley, they both lost a little. If one took the ridge and the other the valley, the one on the ridge arrived first and profited greatly, while the valley traveler still arrived in time and made a decent living. Harin looked up and said, "Stop trying to beat each other. Instead ask: given whatever the other person does, is there a choice you would regret?"

两位商人盯着地上的图案。布伦想了想：如果西娅走山谷路，我应该走高脊路，获得最大利润。如果西娅走高脊路，我就应该走山谷路——至少还能挣到一些。西娅也做了同样的推算。哈林点了点头。「你们看到了吗？你们每个人都有一个无论对方怎么选都不会后悔的最佳答案，而且这个最佳答案是一样的。如果你们都只是选择那条不会让自己后悔的路——每个人都只从自己的利益出发，不需要合作，不需要信任，不需要任何对话——你们就再也不会撞到一起了。」

The merchants stared at the dirt. Bren reasoned: if Thea takes the valley, I should take the ridge and profit. If Thea takes the ridge, I should take the valley — at least I still earn something. Thea reasoned the same way. Harin nodded. "You see? Each of you has a best answer no matter what the other does, and that best answer is the same regardless. If you both simply choose the road that never makes you regret — each acting only in your own interest, with no cooperation, no trust, no conversation — you will never collide again."

从那天起，每天清晨两位商人都不看任何人，不与任何人商量，只是径直驶向那条在任何情况下都对自己最有利的路。有时布伦走高脊路，西娅走山谷路；有时反过来。这并不重要。当他们看到对方的车驶向另一条路时，谁都不会希望自己做了不同的选择。卡萨村因此繁荣了起来，而那位旅行教师继续踏上了旅途，只留下尘土中画着的四个方格。

From that day forward, each morning the two merchants looked at no one, consulted no one, and simply drove to the road that served them best under any circumstance. Sometimes Bren took the ridge and Thea the valley; sometimes the reverse. It did not matter. Neither ever wished they had chosen differently once they saw the other's cart rolling the opposite way. The village of Karsa became prosperous, and the traveling teacher walked on, leaving behind nothing but four squares drawn in the dust.

这个故事揭示了纳什均衡（Nash equilibrium）的本质——一种每个参与者的策略都是对其他人策略的最佳回应的状态，因此没有人有动力改变自己正在做的事情。它不需要信任、沟通或利他主义；它纯粹源于每个人追求自身利益，前提是他们正确理解了其他人可选的策略。其中最深刻的洞见在于：当每个人都这样推理时，单纯的利己之心就能产生一个没有人后悔自己选择的稳定结果。纳什均衡是博弈论（game theory）的基础概念，因为它预测了理性的行动者在命运相互交织却无法协调时会如何行事。

This story reveals the essence of Nash equilibrium — a situation where each participant's strategy is the best possible response to everyone else's strategy, so nobody has an incentive to change what they are doing. It does not require trust, communication, or altruism; it arises purely from each person pursuing their own interest, provided they correctly understand the choices available to others. The remarkable insight is that self-interest alone, when everyone reasons this way, can produce a stable outcome where no one regrets their choice. Nash equilibrium is foundational in game theory because it predicts how rational actors will behave when their fates are intertwined but they cannot coordinate.
