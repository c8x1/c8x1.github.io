两座花园的看守人

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年6月28日·3分钟阅读

在一个宁静的村庄里，住着一个名叫 Mara（玛拉）的男子，被委派为两座相邻花园的看守人，两园之间只隔着一扇小门。东园寒冷潮湿，泥土上凝着厚重的露水；西园温暖干燥，被阳光晒得金黄。村民们深知两园的法则：温热从不自行逆流而上。把一颗滚烫的苹果放进冷园，它会渐渐变凉；但把一颗冰冷的苹果放进暖园，它只能从周围窃取热量才慢慢变温。两座花园若任其自然，便会缓缓趋向同一个不冷不热的温度——暖的渐凉，冷的渐温，直到彼此再无值得称道的分别。

In a quiet village there lived a man named Mara, who was appointed caretaker of two adjoining gardens separated by a single gate. The east garden was cold and damp, its soil heavy with dew, while the west garden was warm and dry, baked golden by the sun. The villagers understood the rule of the two gardens well: warmth never flowed uphill of its own accord. A hot apple placed in the cold garden would cool, but a cold apple placed in the warm garden would only grow warm by stealing heat from its surroundings. The two gardens, left to themselves, were drifting slowly toward the same lukewarm temperature, the warm one cooling and the cold one warming, until neither held any distinction worth speaking of.

然而 Mara 是个耐性十足、目光敏锐的人。他搬了张凳子坐在门前，开始观察那些在两园之间随风流和孩童的脚步翻滚跳跃的苹果。他注意到有些苹果飞得又快又猛，嗡嗡地满含能量；而另一些则懒洋洋地挪动，几乎不带声息。他还进一步发现：那些飞快炽热、满身火气的苹果，闯进冷园的几率，和那些迟缓冰凉的苹果闯进暖园的几率一样大。要知道，那扇门两边都能自由开合，对任何过客都不加盘问。

Mara, however, was a patient and watchful sort. He took his stool beside the gate and began to observe the apples as they tumbled and bounced between the two gardens, carried by the wind and the feet of children. He noticed that some apples flew fast and fierce, humming with energy, while others crept along lazily, barely stirring. And he noticed something further: the fast, hot apples, the ones full of fire, were just as likely to wander into the cold garden as the slow, cold ones were to wander into the warm garden. The gate, you see, swung freely both ways and asked no questions of whoever passed.

于是 Mara 想出一个小巧的手艺。他下定决心：只为那些飞快炽热、奔向暖园的苹果开门，也只为那些迟缓冰凉、奔向冷园的苹果开门。当一颗火热的苹果朝冷园扑来，他便当面把门紧紧关上。当一颗慵懒的苹果朝暖园飘来，他也关上门，任它停留原地。就这样，时辰一寸寸过去，暖园愈发温暖，将所有最迅捷、最饱满的苹果都聚拢到自己怀里；冷园则愈发寒冷，收拢了所有昏沉羸弱的苹果。原本正滑向乏味同一的两座花园，此刻狠狠地彼此拉开，一座炽烈，一座霜寒。

So Mara devised a small craft. He resolved to open the gate only for the fast, hot apples heading toward the warm garden, and only for the slow, cold apples heading toward the cold garden. When a fiery apple bounded toward the cold garden, he shut the gate firmly in its face. When a sluggish apple drifted toward the warm garden, he closed the door and let it be. In this way, hour by hour, the warm garden grew warmer still, gathering unto itself all the swiftest and most energetic apples, while the cold garden grew colder still, collecting all the drowsy and faint ones. The two gardens, which had been sliding toward a dull sameness, now pulled apart with a vengeance, one blazing and one frosty.

村民们大为惊诧，因为这件事违逆了世间一切寻常的流向。Mara 接手了两座正趋于均同的花园，却让其一热一寒，而他没有点燃任何炉火，也没有汲取一桶冰水。他们从热园引出蒸汽，推动一架小小的磨坊；又从冷园取来寒意，保存他们的果实。功业便从两园的温差中汩汩流出，而 Mara 除了一张凳子和一份耐性，别无所求。一位睿智的长者皱起眉头，说这种事不可能永远无偿地持续下去，那一份守望本身必定有所耗费，纵然 Mara 的双手从未动弹，他的门也只在润滑的铰链上轻转。

Now the villagers were astounded, for here was a thing contrary to all the ordinary drift of the world. Mara had taken two gardens that were slowly evening out, and made one hot and one cold, all without lighting a fire or drawing a single bucket of ice. From the hot garden they drew steam to turn a little mill; from the cold garden they drew chill to preserve their fruit. Work flowed out of the difference between the two, and Mara asked nothing in return but his stool and his patience. A wise elder frowned and said that such a thing could not be sustained forever for free, that the watchfulness itself must cost something, though Mara's hands never stirred and his gate swung on oiled hinges.

长者是对的，只是那耗费隐而不显。要知道哪颗苹果快、哪颗慢，Mara 就必须在每一颗靠近时加以度量，而度量从来不是无偿之举。他放行过的每一颗苹果、拦阻过的每一颗苹果，都得在某处留有记录——或记在脑中，或刻在门柱上——而那本账簿无休止地膨胀。只要 Mara 无所遗忘，两园便维持着它们辉煌的分裂；可一旦他的记忆填满，非得抹去旧记才能辨冷热，整套安排便会崩塌，退回它被哄劝而出之前那不冷不热的均同。这手艺唯有在看守人持续记账时才管用，而账簿要永远记下去，便不可能不付代价。

The elder was right, though the cost was hidden. To know which apple was fast and which was slow, Mara had to measure each one as it approached, and measurement is never a free act. The record of every apple he had admitted and every apple he had turned away had to be kept somewhere, in his memory or in marks upon the gatepost, and that ledger grew without end. So long as Mara forgot nothing, the gardens held their splendid divide; but the moment his memory filled and he could no longer distinguish hot from cold without erasing old notes, the whole arrangement would collapse back into the lukewarm sameness from which it had been coaxed. The craft worked only so long as the caretaker kept his books, and the books could not be kept forever without cost.

这则故事揭示了 Maxwell's demon（麦克斯韦妖）的本质——一个关于微小而聪慧的守门人的思想实验：它将快速（炽热）的粒子与缓慢（冰冷）的粒子分拣开来，只让迅捷者进入一室，只让迟缓者进入另一室。借由这种有选择的分拣，这「妖」似乎能让热的一侧更热、冷的一侧更冷，而看不见它做任何功，仿佛逆转了热的自然流向，违背了热力学第二定律。然而正如长者所疑，这「妖」必须对它分拣的每一颗粒子加以度量、记忆并记录信息，而抹除这些信息本身就是一个不可逆的、产生热量的过程。Landauer 原理（兰道尔原理）指出，销毁这些累积的记忆有着最低限度的热力学代价，因此这「妖」赢不了：它为清空账簿所必须付出的热量，抵消了分拣气体所得的收益，第二定律遂巍然不破。

This story reveals the essence of Maxwell's demon — a thought experiment about a tiny intelligent gatekeeper who sorts fast (hot) particles from slow (cold) ones, letting only the swift into one chamber and only the sluggish into another. By this selective sorting, the demon appears to make a hot side hotter and a cold side colder without doing any visible work, seemingly reversing the natural flow of heat and violating the second law of thermodynamics. The catch, as the elder suspected, is that the demon must measure, remember, and record information about every particle it sorts, and the erasure of that information is itself an irreversible, heat-producing process. Landauer's principle shows that destroying this accumulated memory has a minimum thermodynamic cost, so the demon cannot win: the heat it would have to pay to clear its books cancels the gain from sorting the gases, and the second law stands unbroken.
