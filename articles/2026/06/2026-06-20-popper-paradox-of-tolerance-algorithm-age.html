当宽容成为自毁的武器：波普尔悖论在算法时代的警示

每日精选编辑部·每日精选·2026年6月20日·7分钟阅读

2024年，一个AI聊天机器人被部署在某社交平台上，用于自动识别和删除仇恨言论。短短数周内，它开始大量标记少数族裔用户用母语表达文化认同的帖子为"仇恨内容"，同时却对用英文书写的、更为隐蔽的种族主义论调视而不见。这一事件不是孤例——它揭示了一个深刻的哲学困境：当我们试图用规则维护宽容时，规则本身可能成为不宽容的工具。这正是卡尔·波普尔（Karl Popper）在八十年前预见的问题。

In 2024, an AI chatbot was deployed on a social media platform to automatically identify and remove hate speech. Within weeks, it began flagging posts by minority users expressing cultural identity in their native languages as "hateful content," while letting more sophisticated racist rhetoric written in English slip through undetected. This incident was not an anomaly—it revealed a deep philosophical dilemma: when we deploy rules to protect tolerance, the rules themselves may become instruments of intolerance. This is precisely the problem Karl Popper foresaw eighty years ago.

1945年，在《开放社会及其敌人》（The Open Society and Its Enemies）中，波普尔提出了著名的"宽容悖论"（Paradox of Tolerance）：无限制的宽容必然导致宽容的消亡。如果我们对不宽容者也给予无限的宽容，如果我们不愿捍卫一个宽容社会免受不宽容者的攻击，那么宽容者将被摧毁，宽容本身也将随之消失。波普尔因此主张：以宽容的名义，我们有权利不对不宽容者保持宽容。

In 1945, in The Open Society and Its Enemies, Popper formulated the famous Paradox of Tolerance: unlimited tolerance must lead to the disappearance of tolerance. If we extend unlimited tolerance even to those who are intolerant, if we are not prepared to defend a tolerant society against the onslaught of the intolerant, then the tolerant will be destroyed, and tolerance with them. Popper thus argued: in the name of tolerance, we should claim the right not to tolerate the intolerant.

然而，波普尔的论述比流行的截取版本更为审慎。他紧接着补充了一个关键限定——"我并非主张我们应当总是压制不宽容哲学的表达；只要我们能通过理性论证反驳它们、通过公共舆论遏制它们，压制就无疑是不明智的。"换言之，波普尔并非在呼吁普遍审查，而是在界定一条边界：只有当不宽容的运动已构成切实威胁、无法通过理性对话加以制衡时，社会的自我防卫才是正当的。这一限定使他的悖论成为一种有条件的、而非绝对的原则。

Yet Popper's argument was more nuanced than the popular truncated version suggests. He immediately added a crucial qualification: "I do not imply that we should always suppress the utterance of intolerant philosophies; for as long as we can counter them by rational argument and keep them in check by public opinion, suppression would certainly be unwise." In other words, Popper was not calling for blanket censorship. He was drawing a boundary: suppression is justified only when intolerant movements become genuinely dangerous and cannot be countered through rational dialogue. This qualification makes his paradox a conditional, not an absolute, principle.

在社交媒体时代，波普尔悖论以前所未有的规模显现。数字平台的架构天然地放大了极端声音——算法优先推送引发情绪反应的内容，而仇恨与偏见恰恰是最能触发情绪的燃料。研究表明，一条带有攻击性的帖文，其传播速度和范围远超一条温和理性的评论。这意味着，在无干预的状态下，数字公共空间会自动向不宽容倾斜。平台面临的抉择正是波普尔悖论的核心：是容忍一切言论，让公共空间被仇恨淹没；还是主动限制某些言论，承担审查者的角色？

In the era of social media, Popper's paradox manifests at unprecedented scale. Digital platforms are architecturally biased toward amplifying extreme voices—algorithms prioritize content that triggers emotional reactions, and hatred and prejudice are the most reliable emotional fuel. Research shows that an aggressive post spreads faster and farther than a measured, rational comment. This means that without intervention, digital public spaces automatically tilt toward intolerance. The choice platforms face is the very heart of Popper's paradox: tolerate all speech and let the public sphere be overwhelmed by hatred, or actively restrict certain speech and assume the role of censor.

人工智能的内容治理使这一困境更为复杂。AI系统无法在哲学意义上区分"表达不宽容"与"讨论不宽容"——批评种族主义的帖子可能因其包含"种族主义"一词而被标记，而真正的种族主义言论若使用委婉话术则可能逃过检测。这制造了悖论的递归版本：用以捍卫宽容的AI工具，本身可能执行着不宽容的判断。欧盟《数字服务法》（Digital Services Act）在2024年要求平台公开AI审核决策的逻辑，正是试图在技术层面回应这一哲学张力——让"谁在界定不宽容"这一问题变得透明可审查。

AI content moderation deepens the dilemma. AI systems cannot philosophically distinguish between "expressing intolerance" and "discussing intolerance"—a post criticizing racism may be flagged because it contains the word "racism," while genuine racist rhetoric using euphemistic language may evade detection. This creates a recursive version of the paradox: the AI tools deployed to safeguard tolerance may themselves execute intolerant judgments. The EU's Digital Services Act, enforced in 2024, requires platforms to disclose the logic behind AI moderation decisions—an attempt to render the question of "who defines intolerance" transparent and auditable at the technical level.

在民主话语的层面，波普尔悖论指向一个更深的矛盾：开放社会（Open Society）是否必须对那些旨在摧毁开放社会的力量保持开放？当威权主义运动利用言论自由和民主程序来获取权力，然后一朝得势便压制言论自由与民主程序时，波普尔的警告便不再是抽象的哲学推演，而是历史中反复上演的现实。法西斯主义的崛起、魏玛共和国的覆灭，都是对这一悖论的惨痛注脚。

At the level of democratic discourse, Popper's paradox points to a deeper contradiction: must an Open Society remain open to forces that aim to destroy it? When authoritarian movements exploit free speech and democratic procedures to gain power, then suppress free speech and democratic procedures once they hold it, Popper's warning ceases to be abstract philosophical reasoning and becomes a pattern repeated throughout history. The rise of fascism and the collapse of the Weimar Republic are tragic footnotes to this paradox.

哲学上的关键区分在于：宽容不应被视为一种无条件的绝对价值（absolute value），而应是一种有原则的实践性承诺（principled commitment）。自由言论（free speech）同样如此——它不是一张通行无碍的通行证，而是民主对话的条件。当某些言论系统地剥夺他人参与对话的能力时，它们不是在行使自由言论，而是在摧毁自由言论的基础。波普尔悖论的核心洞见正是：宽容与自由不是相互独立的价值观，而是相互依存的——没有边界保护的宽容会吞噬自身，没有宽容精神的边界会沦为暴政。

The crucial philosophical distinction is this: tolerance should not be understood as an unconditional absolute value, but as a principled commitment. The same applies to free speech—it is not an unlimited pass, but a condition for democratic dialogue. When certain forms of speech systematically deprive others of the ability to participate in that dialogue, they are not exercising free speech; they are destroying its foundation. Popper's paradox captures the core insight that tolerance and freedom are not independent values but interdependent ones—tolerance without boundary protection consumes itself, while boundaries without a spirit of tolerance become tyranny.

在实践中，社会如何导航这一张力？首先，限定宽容的边界应当由公开的民主程序而非隐蔽的算法决定——什么构成不可容忍的威胁，应由公民辩论而非技术黑箱裁断。其次，对不宽容的限制应遵循比例原则（proportionality），优先采用论证反驳与公共舆论制衡，而非直接压制——正如波普尔本人所强调的。第三，任何边界划定都应保持可修正性（revisability），承认判定标准可能出错，并为误判留出纠正空间。

In practice, how should societies navigate this tension? First, the boundaries of tolerance should be set through open democratic processes, not hidden algorithms—what constitutes an intolerable threat should be debated by citizens, not adjudicated by technical black boxes. Second, restrictions on intolerance should follow the principle of proportionality, prioritizing counterargument and public opinion over direct suppression—exactly as Popper himself emphasized. Third, any boundary-drawing must maintain revisability, acknowledging that criteria of judgment can be wrong and leaving room for correcting errors.

生成式AI（Generative AI）的崛起加剧了紧迫性。当不宽容内容可以被机器批量生产——仇恨言论、虚假信息、骚扰攻击——无限制的宽容便不再只是哲学上的自相矛盾，而是技术上的现实灾难。一个能够以工业规模制造不宽容的时代的确需要一种有边界的宽容，但这边界必须由人类的判断而非算法的惯性来划定。

The rise of Generative AI intensifies the urgency. When intolerant content can be manufactured at industrial scale—hate speech, disinformation, coordinated harassment—unlimited tolerance is no longer merely a philosophical contradiction but a practical catastrophe. An era capable of mass-producing intolerance does require a bounded tolerance, but those boundaries must be drawn by human judgment, not by algorithmic inertia.

波普尔在1945年写下这段话时，他的眼前是法西斯主义的废墟与极权主义的阴影。八十年后，我们面对的是算法驱动的信息生态与AI治理的新命题。悖论的形态变了，但其内核未变：宽容不是被动的不干预，而是对宽容本身的积极守护。一个真正宽容的社会，不是对一切都无动于衷的社会，而是知道何时必须说"不"——并且知道，那个"不"本身，正是宽容得以存续的条件。

When Popper wrote these words in 1945, his horizon was the ruins of fascism and the shadow of totalitarianism. Eighty years later, we face algorithm-driven information ecosystems and the new challenges of AI governance. The form of the paradox has changed, but its kernel has not: tolerance is not passive non-interference, but active guardianship of tolerance itself. A genuinely tolerant society is not one that is indifferent to everything, but one that knows when it must say "no"—and knows that this "no" is itself the condition for tolerance's survival.
