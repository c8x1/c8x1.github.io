心灵哲学为什么对理解动物认知至关重要

Jonathan Birch·The Conversation·2026年6月25日·4分钟阅读·原文

当我们观察一只乌鸦使用工具获取食物，一只章鱼在迷宫中找到出路，或者一只狗在主人悲伤时静静地靠过来——我们不可避免地会问：它们有意识吗？它们的内在体验是什么样的？这些问题看似属于动物行为学或神经科学的范畴，但实际上，它们首先需要哲学来提供概念框架。心灵哲学——对意识、意向性和心理状态的本质进行系统反思的学科——不是理解动物认知的障碍，而是不可或缺的基础。

When we observe a crow using tools to retrieve food, an octopus navigating a maze, or a dog quietly leaning against its grieving owner—we inevitably ask: are they conscious? What is their inner experience like? These questions might seem to belong to animal behaviorism or neuroscience, but in reality, they first require philosophy to provide conceptual frameworks. Philosophy of mind—the discipline that systematically reflects on the nature of consciousness, intentionality, and mental states—is not an obstacle to understanding animal cognition; it is an indispensable foundation.

心灵哲学最著名的命题之一是托马斯·内格尔在1974年提出的"做一只蝙蝠是什么感觉？"。内格尔指出，即使我们完全了解蝙蝠的神经生理学——它的声呐系统、它的听觉皮层、它的飞行机制——我们仍然无法知道做一只蝙蝠的主观体验是什么。蝙蝠的世界是通过回声定位构建的，而我们所有的概念都来自视觉和触觉主导的经验。我们无法想象一种我们从未拥有过的感官模态所呈现的世界。内格尔的论证揭示了一个关键区分：关于意识的客观知识与主观体验之间的鸿沟。

One of the most famous propositions in philosophy of mind is Thomas Nagel's 1974 paper "What Is It Like to Be a Bat?" Nagel argued that even if we fully understood a bat's neurophysiology—its sonar system, its auditory cortex, its flight mechanisms—we still could not know what it is like subjectively to be a bat. A bat's world is constructed through echolocation, and all our concepts come from vision- and touch-dominated experience. We cannot imagine a world presented by a sensory modality we have never possessed. Nagel's argument reveals a crucial distinction: the gap between objective knowledge about consciousness and subjective experience.

这一鸿沟正是大卫·查尔默斯所称的"意识的困难问题"：为什么物理过程会伴随着主观体验？为什么神经元的放电不只是暗中的信息处理，而是被"照亮"了的——有声音、有颜色、有疼痛、有喜悦？这个问题对人类意识而言已经足够困难，而当我们将它延伸到动物身上时，困难加倍：我们不仅无法解释为什么意识存在，我们甚至无法确定哪些动物拥有意识、它们的意识有多么丰富。

This gap is precisely what David Chalmers calls the "hard problem of consciousness": why do physical processes accompany subjective experience? Why is neuronal firing not merely information processing in the dark, but something that is "illuminated"—with sounds, colors, pain, and joy? This problem is difficult enough for human consciousness, and when we extend it to animals, the difficulty doubles: not only can we not explain why consciousness exists, we cannot even determine which animals possess consciousness and how rich their consciousness might be.

现象学——由胡塞尔、海德格尔和梅洛-庞蒂发展的哲学传统——提供了一个理解动物内在生命的重要视角。现象学关注的是"第一人称体验"的结构：一个生物体如何从自身的视角体验世界。梅洛-庞蒂特别强调身体在意识构成中的核心地位——我们的体验不是通过抽象的思维产生的，而是通过一个活生生的、与世界交互的身体产生的。如果意识本质上是一种身体性现象，那么所有拥有复杂身体结构和环境交互能力的动物，都可能拥有某种形式的现象学体验。

Phenomenology—the philosophical tradition developed by Husserl, Heidegger, and Merleau-Ponty—provides an important perspective for understanding animals' inner lives. Phenomenology focuses on the structure of "first-person experience": how an organism experiences the world from its own perspective. Merleau-Ponty especially emphasized the central role of the body in constituting consciousness—our experience is not produced through abstract thought but through a living body that interacts with the world. If consciousness is essentially a bodily phenomenon, then all animals with complex bodily structures and environmental interaction capabilities may possess some form of phenomenological experience.

"感受质"的概念是心灵哲学讨论动物意识时的另一关键工具。感受质指的是主观体验的"质"——红色的红、疼痛的痛、蜂蜜的甜——这些属性无法被还原为物理描述。一只蜜蜂对花的视觉体验中是否包含感受质？一条鱼在水中游动时是否有一种特定的"水感"？这些问题无法通过神经科学的数据直接回答，因为感受质本质上是第一人称的。但哲学可以帮助我们厘清：感受质的存在条件是什么？什么样的神经系统足以支持感受质？

The concept of "qualia" is another crucial tool when philosophy of mind discusses animal consciousness. Qualia refer to the "qualities" of subjective experience—the redness of red, the hurt of pain, the sweetness of honey—attributes that cannot be reduced to physical descriptions. Does a bee's visual experience of a flower contain qualia? Does a fish swimming in water have a specific "water-feel"? These questions cannot be answered directly through neuroscience data, because qualia are essentially first-personal. But philosophy can help us clarify: what are the conditions for the existence of qualia? What kind of nervous system is sufficient to support qualia?

意向性——心理状态指向对象的能力——是理解动物认知的又一哲学框架。一只狗相信它的主人回家了，一只猿预期食物在树的另一侧，一只蜜蜂记忆花的位置——这些心理状态都具有意向性：它们是关于某事的。哲学对意向性的分析帮助我们区分不同层次的认知能力：有的动物可能只有简单的感知意向性，有的则拥有信念、欲望和意图等更复杂的意向状态。这种区分对于动物福利立法至关重要——法律保护的范围应取决于动物认知的复杂程度。

Intentionality—the capacity of mental states to be directed toward objects—is yet another philosophical framework for understanding animal cognition. A dog believes its owner has come home, an ape anticipates food on the other side of a tree, a bee remembers the location of flowers—these mental states all possess intentionality: they are about something. Philosophy's analysis of intentionality helps us distinguish different levels of cognitive capacity: some animals may possess only simple perceptual intentionality, while others have more complex intentional states such as beliefs, desires, and intentions. This distinction is crucial for animal welfare legislation—the scope of legal protection should depend on the complexity of an animal's cognition.

2012年的《剑桥意识宣言》是一个标志性的时刻。一群领先的神经科学家在剑桥大学签署了一份声明，明确宣告：非人类动物拥有意识状态——它们具有主观体验和感受质。声明特别指出，产生意识所需的神经基础并不只存在于人类中，许多动物——包括所有哺乳动物、鸟类和许多其他生物——都拥有这些基础。这份宣言的意义在于：它将一个长期属于哲学辩论的问题——动物是否有意识——推进到了科学共识的领域。但值得注意的是，推动这一共识的，恰恰是哲学与科学的长期对话。

The 2012 Cambridge Declaration on Consciousness was a landmark moment. A group of leading neuroscientists at the University of Cambridge signed a declaration stating explicitly: non-human animals possess conscious states—they have subjective experiences and qualia. The declaration specifically noted that the neural substrates necessary for generating consciousness are not unique to humans; many animals—including all mammals, birds, and numerous other organisms—possess these substrates. The significance of this declaration lies in advancing a question long debated in philosophy—whether animals are conscious—into the domain of scientific consensus. But notably, what propelled this consensus was precisely the long-standing dialogue between philosophy and science.

但即使有了剑桥宣言，动物意识的边界仍然模糊。哪些无脊椎动物拥有意识？昆虫？章鱼？螃蟹？在这些边界地带，哲学提供了一个至关重要的原则：预防原则。预防原则主张：当我们在动物意识问题上存在合理的不确定性时，我们应当给予动物道德考量——宁可多保护一些可能没有意识的动物，也不要忽视可能拥有意识的动物。这一原则不是来自神经科学的发现，而是来自哲学伦理学的推理：在不确定的情况下，错误的代价是不对称的——错在过度保护，代价是浪费一些资源；错在忽视，代价是让有意识的生物遭受不必要的痛苦。

Yet even with the Cambridge Declaration, the boundaries of animal consciousness remain fuzzy. Which invertebrates possess consciousness? Insects? Octopuses? Crabs? In these borderlands, philosophy provides a crucial principle: the precautionary principle. The precautionary principle holds that when we have reasonable uncertainty about animal consciousness, we should give animals moral consideration—it is better to over-protect some animals that may not be conscious than to neglect animals that may well be conscious. This principle does not come from neuroscience discoveries but from philosophical ethical reasoning: under uncertainty, the costs of error are asymmetric—the cost of over-protection is wasting some resources; the cost of neglect is causing unnecessary suffering to conscious beings.

伦敦政治经济学院的哲学家乔纳森·伯奇进一步发展了这一思路。他提出了"动物感知的边缘地带"的概念，指那些我们有理由相信可能具有感知能力但尚未确证的动物群体。伯奇主张，对于这些边缘地带的动物，预防原则不仅是伦理上的要求，也是认识论上的诚实——因为我们承认自己不知道，所以我们必须选择那条伤害更小的路径。这种论证方式展现了心灵哲学对实践决策的直接影响力：哲学不只是学术讨论，它塑造着我们对待动物的法律和政策。

Jonathan Birch, a philosopher at the London School of Economics, has further developed this line of thought. He proposed the concept of "peripheral zones of animal sentience"—groups of animals for which we have reason to believe they may be sentient but have not yet confirmed it. Birch argues that for animals in these peripheral zones, the precautionary principle is not merely an ethical requirement but also an epistemological honesty—because we acknowledge that we do not know, we must choose the path that causes less harm. This style of argument demonstrates philosophy of mind's direct influence on practical decision-making: philosophy is not just academic discussion; it shapes the laws and policies governing how we treat animals.

心灵哲学对理解动物认知的贡献，最终可以归结为一点：它提供了科学无法独自提供的概念清晰性。科学可以告诉我们章鱼有多少神经元、乌鸦能解决什么问题、蜜蜂如何导航——但科学本身无法回答"这些能力是否伴随着主观体验"这个问题，因为主观体验的定义和判定标准本质上是哲学问题。心灵哲学不是替代科学的竞争者，而是科学的合作伙伴：它帮助科学提出更好的问题、设计更严谨的实验、避免更隐蔽的概念混淆。当我们试图理解那些与我们如此不同却又可能与我们共享意识之光的生物时，哲学的灯光不应熄灭——恰恰应该更加明亮。

The contribution of philosophy of mind to understanding animal cognition can ultimately be distilled to one point: it provides conceptual clarity that science cannot furnish on its own. Science can tell us how many neurons an octopus has, what problems a crow can solve, how bees navigate—but science itself cannot answer the question "do these capacities accompany subjective experience," because the definition and criteria of subjective experience are inherently philosophical questions. Philosophy of mind is not a competitor replacing science; it is science's partner: it helps science pose better questions, design more rigorous experiments, and avoid more subtle conceptual confusions. When we try to understand creatures so different from us yet possibly sharing with us the light of consciousness, philosophy's light should not be extinguished—it should burn brighter.
