七钟议会

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年6月26日·3分钟阅读

在古老的商贸之城 Aethelmark,七位行会首领共同掌管着维系全区过冬的大谷仓。每位首领都在自己的塔楼上安放一座钟,全城的存亡取决于七人能否在初霜之日同一时辰打开谷仓大门。开得太早,秋季市集便会崩盘;开得太晚,穷人会在道路封冻前挨饿。于是每年春天,首领们齐聚铜顶穹厅,定下一条简单的仪轨:在选定那日的黎明,每人遣一名信使赴其余每座塔楼,口传一字——「开」。

In the old trading city of Aethelmark, seven guild-masters governed the great granaries that fed the region through winter. Each master kept a clock on their own tower, and the survival of the city depended on all seven opening their granary gates at the same hour on the first day of frost. Open too early, and the autumn markets would crash; too late, and the poor would starve before the roads froze. So every spring, the masters met beneath the copper dome and agreed on a single ritual: at dawn on the chosen day, each would send a runner to every other tower carrying the word "open."

仪轨看似简单,头两年也确实奏效。到了第三个春天,一名投毒者潜入城中,东塔的 Dorin 首领心智渐乱。他并未身亡——依旧遣信使,依旧在议会上含笑——可心思已转向与其余六人为敌。他向三座塔楼传「开」,又向另三座传「等」,并对每名信使声称另有不同的首领已生叛意。投毒者并非受雇去毁坏那些钟,而是受雇去毁坏钟与钟之间的信任。

The ritual seemed simple, and for two years it worked. But on the third spring, a poisoner moved through the city, and Master Dorin of the eastern tower fell ill in his wits. He was not dead — he still sent runners, still smiled at the council — but his mind turned against the others. To three towers he sent the word "open," and to three he sent the word "wait," and he told each runner that a different master had gone treacherous. The poisoner had not been hired to break the clocks; he had been hired to break the trust between them.

初霜之日到来时,两扇门开了,五扇门紧闭。谷物倾倒在空无一商的街巷,西区的买面包长队酿成了暴乱。首领们再聚铜顶穹厅,Dorin 亦在其中,目光清明,发誓自己向所有人传的都是「开」。信使已无从盘问;每人只传过一句低语,记忆却各不相同。每位首领对 Dorin 当日所言各执一词,而 Dorin 也搬出对其余之人所言的记述,却无人认得。穹厅回荡着相互的指控,没有任何两位首领对那个清晨持有相同的图景。

When the first day of frost arrived, two gates opened and five stayed shut. Grain spilled into streets where no merchants waited, and in the western quarter the bread-queues turned to riot. The masters met again beneath the copper dome, and Dorin sat among them, eyes clear, swearing he had sent "open" to all. No runner could be questioned; each had carried only a whispered word and remembered it differently. Every master produced a different account of what Dorin had said, and Dorin himself produced accounts of what others had said that none of them recognized. The dome rang with accusations, and no two masters held the same picture of the morning.

此后数年,他们设法构建更牢固的仪轨。一条消息改遣三名信使而非一人。他们令每名信使持一枚印章,继而又设证明印章之印章。他们发明繁复的票笺,每位首领写下其余每位向他禀报之言,使任何谎言都会被矛盾的纸卷缠住。然而每个秋天都以同样的方式收场:几扇门错时开启,几位首领坚称自己所言非虚,穹厅之下无从分辨忠信与奸佞。因为困难从来不在信使,也不在印章。难处在于:他们能想出的任何抓谎之规,说谎者同样能照办;他们能传唤的任何作证之人,本身也可能被收买。忠信的首领只能如实而言;奸佞者则可真可假,任凭有利即用,甚至能把诚实模仿得天衣无缝,使任何诚实的舌头都辨不出差别。

They tried, in the years that followed, to build sturdier rituals. They sent three runners per message instead of one. They asked each runner to carry a seal, and then seals that certified seals. They invented elaborate ballots, each master writing down what every other master had reported to him, so that any lie would be caught in a tangle of contradicting papers. And yet every autumn ended the same way: a few gates opening out of sync, a few masters insisting they had been truthful, and no way under the copper dome to tell the truthful from the treacherous. For the difficulty was never really about runners or seals. It was that any rule they could devise to catch a liar could also be followed by a liar, and any witness they could summon to confirm a message could itself be turned. The truthful masters could only speak honestly; the treacherous could speak honestly or dishonestly, whichever served them, and could even imitate honesty so faithfully that no honest tongue could tell the difference.

一名年轻书记员看着首领们在又一个徒劳的秋天里争执不休,终于在议会账册的边页写下一行字:「我们有七座钟,却没有一座钟能报出另一座钟的时辰。」他读懂了长老们尚不肯说出口之事——这座城的问题不在 Dorin,不在信使,不在印章。问题在于:一个由信使构成的系统,任凭信使再怎么巧思,只要其中某些成员可以任意说谎,而其余成员永远无法走出系统之外去看清谁在说谎,便无法就单一的真相达成一致。只要仍有可能存在一位奸佞的首领,穹厅之下的任何仪轨,都无法再保证那七扇门能一同开启。

A young clerk, watching the masters argue through another fruitless autumn, finally wrote a single line in the margin of the council ledger: "We have seven clocks, and no clock that tells the time of another clock." He understood what the elders would not yet say aloud — that the city's problem was not Dorin, nor the runners, nor the seals. It was that a system of messengers cannot, by any cleverness of messengers, agree on a single truth when some of its members are permitted to lie freely and the rest can never step outside the system to see who is lying. And so long as a single treacherous master remained possible, no ritual under the copper dome could guarantee that all seven gates would ever open together again.

这则故事揭示了拜占庭将军问题(Byzantine generals problem)的本质——一群彼此独立的参与者,在其中有部分人可能 actively 不诚实、向不同的同侪发送相互矛盾的信息时,如何达成一致。诚实的一方不能轻信任何单条消息,因为被收买的人可以对一位邻人说一套、对另一位说另一套,甚至能把诚实的行为模仿得天衣无缝以逃避察觉。再多的签名、证人或额外的信使都不能彻底解决它,除非诚实参与者在数量上以足够大的优势压过不诚实者(具体而言须超过三分之二),并遵循一套能让彼此将每一份禀报相互交叉核对的协议。这正是分布式计算机、乃至后来的区块链,在无人能走出系统之外去核实谁在说谎的情况下,学会就单一真相达成一致的根本谜题。

This story reveals the essence of the Byzantine generals problem — how a group of separate actors can reach agreement when some of them may be actively dishonest, sending conflicting information to different peers. The honest actors cannot simply trust any single message, because a compromised actor can say one thing to one neighbor and the opposite to another, and can even perfectly imitate honest behavior to avoid detection. No amount of signatures, witnesses, or extra messengers fully solves it unless the honest actors outnumber the dishonest ones by a sufficient margin (specifically, more than two-thirds) and follow a protocol that lets them cross-check every report against every other. It is the foundational puzzle behind how distributed computers, and later blockchains, learn to agree on a single truth without anyone being able to step outside the system and verify who is lying.
