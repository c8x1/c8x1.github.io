最短的描述

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年7月18日·2分钟阅读

在画家公会的鉴定厅里，每一幅画都要由一段口述配方重建。坐在指定讲台后的一位师傅聆听描述，逐字遵从，在空墙上重现这幅画；唯有能且仅能生成这幅画的那段描述，才是这幅画的真正尺度。公会固定一台讲台作标准，因为换任何别的讲台，差别只在开头多说或少说几句固定的引子，与描述的是哪幅画无关。

In the appraisal hall of the Painters' Guild, every canvas was to be reconstructed by a single spoken recipe. A master seated at a chosen podium would hear a description and, obeying it word by word, reproduce the picture on a blank wall; the description that yielded that picture and no other was its true measure. The guild kept one podium fixed as the standard, for any other podium differed only by a fixed handful of prefatory words added or removed, regardless of which picture was being described.

公会珍视简短的描述，因为配方短的画藏着不多余的结构：一片纯靛蓝只需一口气便能说尽。但若一幅画遍布紊乱，每一处斑点都落在规则所不及之处，便无法被讲得比画本身更短，只能一处一处地，把整幅画背诵下来。师傅们称这样的画为随机的，不是说它造不出，而是说它不可压缩：最短的配方与物等长。

The guild prized short descriptions, for a painting whose recipe was brief held little hidden structure: a field of pure indigo was told in a single breath. But a canvas seethed with disorder, each fleck placed where no rule reached, could not be told shorter than the picture itself; one had to recite, fleck by fleck, the whole of it. The masters named such a picture random, meaning not that it could not be made, but that it could not be compressed: the shortest recipe was as long as the thing.

年轻的师傅埃利亚瑟问长老，某一幅画能否被证明，它无需比千言更短的配方。长老们沉默，继而答道：你能开出的任何证书，那张写明千言之限的凭据，其本身即是一段描述，其自身的长度便界定了它能担保的范围，至多再差那几句固定的引子。越过某个高度，一幅画之不可压缩这一事实便无法被证明，因为证明之本身必须至少与它所约束的画等长。

Eliasse, a junior master, asked the elders whether a given canvas might be proven to need no recipe shorter than a thousand words. The elders fell silent, then answered: any certificate you can issue, the written warrant declaring the thousand-word limit, is itself a description, and its own length bounds what it can certify, up to that same fixed handful of words. Beyond a certain height, the fact of a picture's incompressibility cannot be proven, because the proof would have to be at least as long as the picture it claims to constrain.

也没有任何一位师傅，无论多么耐烦，能接过一幅画便交还其真正的最短尺度。要确知最短的配方，须得穷试一切配方，包括那些永不讲完的配方；公会的规矩只准开给出上界，一段能用、却冗长的配方，并自上方逼近真相，永不触及。每幅画的真正长度，对每一位师傅、每一架言词之机，都遥不可及。

Nor could any master, however patient, take a canvas and return its true shortest measure. To know the shortest recipe one would have to try every recipe, including those that never finish speaking; the guild's rule was to issue only upper bounds, a recipe that worked however bloated, and to approach the truth from above, never to grasp it. The true length of a picture remained, for every master and every machine of tongues, beyond reach.

然而埃利亚瑟渴求那唯一的真数，提议另造一台更精巧的标准讲台，望一副更聪明的口舌能把每幅画钉死。长老们不准：更精的讲台或能让每段配方省下几句，但所省不过是那同一把固定的引子，且会继承同样不可企及的深处、同样无法越过的担保之限。换一件器具，于实质无所改易。

Yet Eliasse, hungry for the one true number, proposed to rebuild the standard podium itself, hoping a cleverer tongue would pin each canvas exactly. The elders forbade it: a finer podium might shorten every recipe by some words, but only by the same fixed handful, and would inherit the same unreachable depths, the same ceiling on what could be sworn. To change the instrument was to change nothing of substance.

埃利亚瑟遂立于两幅靛蓝幅面相同的画前：一幅是单一的未调和之色，另一幅出自一只随手掷刷、乱抹一气的腕下。观之几近相同，依规则却是两极。他面对自己的抉择：要么为那幅乱画立下千言之限的担保，把自己这张凭据押在一桩永不能完全兑现的断言上；要么缄口，让公会只记下那冗长的上界，那个诚实的、却对画最内形状只字不提的数字。真数与清白的凭据，他不能兼得。

Eliasse stood then before two paintings of identical indigo extent: one a single unmixed field, the other tossed into chaos by a hand that had flung the brush at random. To the eye they were near the same; to the rule they were opposite poles. He faced his choice: swear the thousand-word limit on the chaotic one and so stake his own warrant on a claim he could never fully back, or stay silent and let the guild record only the bloated upper bound, the honest number that says nothing of the picture's inmost shape. He could not have both the true number and a clean conscience.

他选择了缄口，终于明白，使一物成其本身的并非其表面，而是它无法摆脱的最短讲述；而这一单一个数字，虽无任何师傅能读出，却早已作为其最内的形状，坐在这幅画中。

He chose silence, and understood at last that what made a thing itself was not its surface but the shortest telling it could not be rid of, and that this single number, though no master could ever read it, already sat in the picture as its inmost shape.

注解：这里讲的是柯尔莫古洛夫复杂性（Kolmogorov complexity）：在固定的讲述标准上，能生成一个对象的最短描述的长度；这个数与所择器具无关、至多只差一个常数，无法由任何程序算出，且越过某个大小便无法被证明。一物之所以随机，正因其最短描述与它自身等长。

Annotation: This is an account of Kolmogorov complexity（柯尔莫古洛夫复杂性）: the length of the shortest description that produces a given object on a fixed standard of telling, a number that is the same up to a constant regardless of the instrument chosen, that cannot be computed by any procedure, and beyond a certain size can no longer be proven. A thing is random precisely when its shortest description is as long as itself.
