镇边那座山丘

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年7月5日·3分钟阅读

从前有座小镇坐落在谷地里,镇子背后矗立着一座山丘,又高又陡,从未有人翻越过。老人们说那岩石从里到外都是实心的,比十个成年人叠起来还要高,除非用巨大的铁制机械,否则别想凿出一条路来。传说山丘另一边是一片更富饶的谷地,那里的阳光停留得更久,水井也从不干涸,但山丘把两片谷地隔开,就像一堵墙隔开两间屋子,镇上的人也把这当作世界本来的样子接受了下来。

There was once a town nestled in a valley, and behind it rose a hill so steep and so high that no one had ever crossed it. The elders said the stone was solid all the way through, taller than ten men stacked, and that nothing lighter than a great iron engine could ever hope to bore a path. Beyond the hill, the stories went, lay a richer valley where the sun lingered longer and the wells never ran dry, but the hill kept the two valleys apart the way a wall keeps two rooms apart, and the townspeople accepted this as simply the shape of the world.

镇上的孩子里有个叫 Marin(马林)的男孩,他不肯把这座山丘当作边界。他每天早晨走到山脚下,把掌心贴在冰冷的岩石上,静静地听。别的孩子笑他。「你不可能穿过石头走,」他们说,「这是谁也做不到、也从来没人做到过的事。」Marin 只是耸耸肩,因为他注意到了一件别人没留意的事:当他把一颗小石子掷向山壁时,有时候——不是每次,但偶尔——那石子并不像一颗本分的石子那样弹回来,它就那样消失在他以为它会落下的地面上,再也找不着。

Among the children of the town was a boy named Marin, who refused to accept the hill as a boundary. He would walk to its base each morning and press his palms against the cold rock, listening. The other children laughed at him. "You cannot walk through stone," they said. "That is not a thing anyone does, or ever has done." Marin only shrugged, because he had noticed something the others had not: when he threw a small pebble at the face of the hill, sometimes — not always, but sometimes — the pebble did not bounce back the way a sensible pebble should. It simply was not there on the ground where he expected it to fall.

Marin 开始计数。每掷出一百颗石子,总有大约三四颗会消失。不是滚到旁边,不是掉进他够得着的裂缝里——就是消失了,仿佛山丘把它们整个吞了下去。后来某个下午,他绕到山丘东侧坡脚,那里地势渐缓,长成一片灌木丛,他发现了一小堆不属于他的光滑石子,堆在一个它们本不该出现的地方——那堵石墙的另一侧。他在石子旁坐了很久,心跳得有些古怪,因为唯一诚实的解释,正是镇上的人一直说不可能的那一种。

Marin began to keep count. Out of every hundred pebbles he threw, perhaps three or four vanished. Not gone to the side, not rolled into a crack he could reach — gone, as though the hill had swallowed them whole. And then one afternoon, coming around the eastern shoulder of the hill where the slope eased into a thicket, he found a small pile of smooth stones that were not his, in a place they had no business being, on the far side of the wall of rock. He sat down beside them for a long time, his heart beating strangely, because the only honest explanation was the one his town had always said was impossible.

起初他谁也没告诉,只是一直投掷,也一直在另一侧发现那些小小的抵达者。他注意到,凡是石墙较薄的地方——山丘在东侧坡脚附近变窄处——穿过去的石子就越多。而在岩石最厚、位于山丘心脏的位置,几乎没有什么能穿越。他还发现,抵达另一侧的从来不是最大最重的石头,而总是那些最小的,小到你几乎会忘记它还在手心里。山丘并不算是在挑选;更像是小的、快的那些,拥有大的、慢的所没有的机会。

He told no one at first, only kept throwing, and kept finding the small arrivals on the far side. He noticed that the thinner the wall of rock — where the hill narrowed near its eastern shoulder — the more pebbles came through. Where the rock was thickest, at the heart of the hill, almost none made the crossing. And he noticed, too, that it was never the biggest or heaviest stones that arrived on the other side, but always the smallest, the ones whose weight you could almost forget in your hand. The hill did not choose, exactly; it was more that the small and the quick had a chance the large and the slow did not.

当他终于领着老人们绕到另一侧,把那一堆穿过实心石墙却没有经过任何裂缝或门洞的石子指给他们看时,众人沉默地站着。他们一辈子都相信,身处山丘的一侧就意味着与另一侧隔绝,岩石是最终的裁决。但这些石子讲出了另一个故事:墙是真的,却不是绝对的。对一辆马车或一个人而言是坚实、不可逾越的东西,对一颗足够小、被足够耐心地投掷出去的石子而言,仅仅是非常不太可能——而非常不太可能,原来并不等于永不。

When he finally led the elders to the far side and showed them the pile of stones that had crossed a wall of solid rock without any crack or door, they stood in silence. They had spent their lives believing that to be on one side of the hill was to be cut off from the other, that the rock was final. But the pebbles told a different story: the wall was real, and yet it was not absolute. What was solid and impassable to a cart or a man was, to a small enough stone thrown with enough patience, merely very unlikely — and very unlikely, it turned out, was not the same as never.

这个故事揭示了 quantum tunneling(量子隧穿)的本质——一个粒子可以穿过一道按 classical physics(经典物理)的所有规则来看它都没有足够能量跨越的能量势垒。在日常世界里,一颗掷向墙壁的球要么弹回,要么只有足够有力才能击穿,不存在第三种选择。但在量子尺度上,像电子这样的粒子更像弥散开来的波,而不是实心的小玻璃球,它们的存在由一种概率来描述,这种概率在势垒内部会衰减,却并不总是衰减到零。于是有极小的概率,粒子就这样出现在了另一侧,它既没有钻也没有破坏任何东西——就像一颗又小又轻的石子,有极小却真实的机会出现在山丘另一边。势垒越薄、粒子越轻,这种穿越就越可能发生,正因如此,从太阳内核的核聚变到手机里 flash memory(闪存)的擦写机制,背后都有隧穿在起作用。

This story reveals the essence of quantum tunneling — a particle can pass through an energy barrier that, by all the rules of classical physics, it does not have enough energy to cross. In the everyday world, a ball thrown at a wall either bounces back or breaks through only if it is powerful enough; there is no third option. But at the quantum scale, particles such as electrons behave more like spread-out waves than like solid marbles, and their presence is described by a probability that decays inside the barrier yet does not always reach zero. So a tiny fraction of the time, the particle simply turns up on the other side, having never drilled or broken anything — just as a small, light pebble has a small but real chance of arriving beyond the hill. The thinner the barrier and the lighter the particle, the more likely this passage becomes, which is why tunneling powers everything from the nuclear fusion in the Sun to the erase-and-write mechanism of flash memory in a phone.
