千门之夜宴

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年7月2日·2分钟阅读

在大殿（the Great Hall）的创建章程里，建造者们明令：国中千门，无一可凌驾于他者之上。大殿是圆的，开着一千扇门，中央的酒桌是一枚完美的圆盘。无论客人从哪一扇门而入，都会发现那杯酒被安放在与所有人等距之处——这是国中公正的标尺。一个时代如此过去，大殿安然栖息于完美的平衡之中，无扰无改。

In the founding charter of the Great Hall, the builders decreed that no door of the realm should be exalted above another. The Hall was round, with a thousand doors, and the table at its center was a perfect disc. Whichever door a guest entered, they would find the cup of wine placed at an equal distance from all — a measure of the realm's impartiality. For an age this was so, and the Hall rested in a state of perfect balance, untroubled and unchanged.

于是到了大宴之夜。一千位客人同时抵达，各自穿过一扇门，各立于圆缘之上的一席。酒静静安在正中，与每一只手都等距。章程被恪守——然而无人饮酒。因为对每一位客人而言，那只酒杯离他们与离他人恰好一样远，没有人握有先伸手之权，于是众人就那样立着等候，悬于一场永远无法自行了结的礼让之中。

Then came the evening of the great banquet. A thousand guests arrived at once, one through each door, and stood each at their place around the rim. The wine sat at the still center, equidistant from every hand. The charter was honored — yet no one drank. For each guest, the cup being exactly as far from them as from any other, no one held the privilege of reaching first, and so all stood waiting, suspended in a courtesy that could never resolve itself.

在临近午夜的喘息之间，东门旁的一个孩子打了个喷嚏——一桩微末之事，一丝穿堂风里的一粒尘。她身旁的客人受惊，向内倾了半步。酒杯未被触及，并未移动；但此刻它离那一对人，较之旁人已近了一发。而那一发便已足够。一只手伸出，一只杯被举起，一扇门便独占了头一口饮。

In the breath before midnight, a child at the eastern door sneezed — a nothing, a mote of dust in a draft. The guest beside her, startled, leaned a half-step inward. The cup, untouched, did not move; but now it was a hair closer to that pair than to the rest. And that hair was enough. A hand reached, a cup was lifted, and a single door claimed the first draught.

这一连锁之变即刻而不可逆。东杯方才饮尽，两侧的客人便发觉自己此刻离余酒最近，于是也伸手。在一下心跳之间，圆缘那完美的对称坍缩为一个单一的饮宴方向，如焰苗沿烛芯蔓延般席卷整圈。当寂静重归，一千位客人都已饮过，而每一个人都已转向——无论多么微弱——东方。大殿已做出了选择。

The cascade was immediate and irreversible. No sooner had the eastern cup been drained than the guests to either side found themselves now the nearest to the remaining wine, and they too reached. Within a heartbeat the perfect symmetry of the rim collapsed into a single direction of feasting, sweeping around the circle like a flame catching along a wick. When silence returned, a thousand guests had drunk, and every one of them had turned, however slightly, toward the east. The Hall had chosen.

宴后，长者们聚拢，细审章程。他们发觉无一字被改、无一门被废、桌之尺度无一被更。大殿仍是圆的，千门依然如故。那公正之律，一字不差，如旧而立。然而众人如今言及东门，皆仿佛它是首席之门；言及那孩子之喷嚏，皆仿佛它是命运。一个于纸上仍完美平衡的国度，却已然沉淀进了自身的某一面——再无法以寻常手段，将它哄回那它所从中跌落的、未决之境。

After the banquet the elders gathered and examined the charter. They found no line amended, no door unmade, no measure of the table altered. The Hall was round still, and the thousand doors remained. The law of impartiality stood exactly as written. And yet everyone now spoke of the eastern door as though it were the chief door, and the child's sneeze as though it were destiny. A realm that remained, on paper, perfectly balanced, had nonetheless settled into one particular face of itself — and could not, by any ordinary means, be coaxed back into the indecision from which it had fallen.

这则故事揭示了自发对称破缺（spontaneous symmetry breaking）的本质——一个系统的规则可以完美对称，把每一种可能性都等量齐观，然而系统却无法栖息于斯。在大宴之上，律法对每一扇门都同等礼敬，但那平衡态本身并不稳定：最轻微的一推——一个孩子的喷嚏——便足以将它倾覆；一旦倾覆，正是同一套对称的律法驱动了一场雪崩，从众多方向中拣定其一。对称从未从规则中丢失，它只是隐没在了系统恰好沉淀其中的那个具体选择里。自发对称破缺，确切而言正是此意：底层之律法依然不偏不倚，但系统实际占据的状态却单独挑出了一个结果，事后看去那结果仿若必然——尽管原则上，任何一扇门都可能成为被选中的那一扇。

This story reveals the essence of spontaneous symmetry breaking — the rules of a system can be perfectly symmetric, treating every possibility as equal, yet the system cannot rest there. At the banquet, the laws honored every door equally, but the equilibrium itself was unstable: the tiniest nudge — a child's sneeze — was enough to tip it, and once tipped, the same symmetric laws drove an avalanche that picked out one direction out of many. The symmetry was never lost from the rules; it was only hidden in the particular choice the system happened to settle into. Spontaneous symmetry breaking is precisely this: the underlying laws remain unbiased, but the state the system actually occupies singles out one outcome, and after the fact that outcome looks inevitable, even though any door could, in principle, have been the one.
