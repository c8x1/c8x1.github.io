长戟村的最后一只杯子

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年7月17日·3分钟阅读

长戟村曾住着七户人家，每户的架上都摆着一只彩绘茶杯——七只杯子，七种花纹。樵夫家的杯上有黑纹，铁匠家的有绿环，织布家的有红点。山口的路一断，杯子再也运不进运不出，村子也缩成了寥寥几户。教书先生把孩子们招到跟前说：每年春天，各家年纪最长的孩子把自己家的杯取下，蒙眼行旅人面前一摆，行旅人朝架上一指，指到哪只，哪只便被洗净、用一遭、再放回去；其余的再积一年灰。他指到哪只，下一个孩子就学着把哪只认作自家之物。

In Halberd Village there were once seven families, and each family kept a painted teacup on the shelf — seven cups, seven patterns. The woodcutter's cup bore a black stripe; the smith's a green ring; the weaver's a red dot. When the road through the pass closed, no cup could be carried in or out again, and the village shrank to a handful of houses. The schoolmaster sat the children down and said: every spring the oldest child of each house takes down its cup, holds it to the blindfolded Traveller, and the Traveller points at one cup on the shelf. That cup alone is washed, used, and put back; the others gather dust another year. The cup he points to is the cup the next child will learn to call its own.

行旅人全凭手气指。他不懂手艺，也不偏爱绿胜过红。教书先生把这话讲得清清楚楚，孩子们仍要争。铁匠家的男孩说：我的绿环最齐整，他必会指它。织布家的女孩说：我的红点最醒目，最勾人眼。教书先生摇头：他蒙着眼，看不见你的环、你的点。他指到的杯不是最好的杯，也不是最坏的杯，只是他手停住时指尖底下那只。你家杯子能不能再撑一年，与它的花纹无关，与它的好坏无关。

The Traveller points by chance. He has no eye for craft, no love of green over red. The schoolmaster said this plainly, and still the children argued. The smith's boy said: my green ring is the fairest, he must point to it. The weaver's girl said: my red dot is the boldest, it draws the eye. The schoolmaster shook his head: he is blindfolded, he cannot see your rings or your dots. The cup he picks is not the best cup and not the worst — it is the cup beneath his finger when his hand stops. Whether your cup survives another year has nothing to do with its pattern or its worth.

头一年春天，行旅人指到了樵夫家的黑纹，和磨坊主家的素杯。第二年春天他又指，再下一年还指——有时连着两年指同一只，有时指到一只多年无人动过的杯。孩子们望着架子，生出一种说不清名字的怕。一年年过去，有些杯子上的灰越积越厚，底下的花纹越褪越淡，本该认它们为自家的孩子，长大后认了别的杯当家。没有一只杯被丢出去，没有一个人做过选择。可一年又一年，架上再被人取下的杯子越来越少。

The first spring the Traveller pointed at the woodcutter's black stripe, and the miller's plain cup. The second spring he pointed again, and again — sometimes the same cup twice running, sometimes a cup no one had touched in years. The children watched the shelf and felt a dread that had no name. Each year the dust on some cups thickened, and the pattern beneath grew faint, and the children who might have called those cups their own grew up calling some other cup home. No cup was thrown out. No one chose. And yet, year by year, fewer cups ever left the shelf at all.

到了第七个春天，架上还在被人取动的只剩两只：樵夫家的黑纹，和铁匠家的绿环。其余的都压在厚得孩子们已说不清原有什么花纹的灰底下。教书先生问铁匠家那已经长大的男孩：若行旅人的手停在黑纹上，绿环就会积灰，积到再没有孩子记得它。若停在绿环上，黑纹也是一样下场。无论哪种，都会有一只杯年年被人取用，另一只再也无人动。男孩说：那我便去求行旅人偏向我们——我家杯子更精，我爹的炉子更响。教书先生说：你可以求。隔着那块布，他听不见你。

By the seventh spring only two cups still ever moved: the woodcutter's black stripe and the smith's green ring. The rest sat under dust so thick the children could no longer say what pattern they had borne. The schoolmaster asked the smith's boy, now grown: if the Traveller's hand stopped on the black stripe, the green ring would gather dust until no child remembered it. If it stopped on the green ring, the black stripe would go the same way. Either way, one cup would be used every year, and the other never again. The boy said: then let me ask the Traveller to favor us — my cup is finer, my father worked the better forge. The schoolmaster said: you may ask. He cannot hear you through the cloth.

第八个春天，行旅人指到了黑纹。铁匠家的男孩站了很久没动，才开口说：绿环是我父亲的炉子，山口独一份，比这村里所有纹饰都值钱。教书先生说：我知道。男孩说：它没了，只因为一只蒙眼的手停偏了一寸。教书先生说：是的。不是因为黑纹更精，也不是因为绿环有瑕。是因为村子缩得太小，一根手指的一次指点，就足以定下哪只杯活、哪只杯失。若山口的路不断，若七户还是七百户，再怎么指，也清不空一面架子。

The eighth spring the Traveller pointed at the black stripe. The smith's boy stood very still, then said: the green ring was my father's forge, the only one in the pass, and worth more than every stripe in this village. The schoolmaster said: I know. The boy said: and it is gone because a blindfolded hand stopped one inch to the left. The schoolmaster said: yes. Not because the stripe was finer. Not because the ring was flawed. Because the village had grown so small that a single pointing of a single finger could settle which cup lived and which was lost. Had the road stayed open, had the seven houses stayed seven hundred, no one pointing could have cleared a shelf.

第九个春天，行旅人又指，仍指黑纹。架上再没有别的杯可指。指与不指已无分别；架子成了一只杯，而这只杯将是此后每个孩子的杯。教书先生把六种花纹的灰收进一只匣子，搁在黑纹旁边，对孩子们说：这便是小村子做的事。它不挑最好的、丢最坏的。它让一只盲眼的手去挑，村子越小，那手把一切定得越快——直到只剩一种花纹，那手再无可决。

The ninth spring the Traveller pointed, again, at the black stripe. There was no other cup left to point at. The pointing no longer changed anything; the shelf had become a single cup, and the single cup would be the cup of every child after. The schoolmaster gathered the dust of six patterns into a box and set it beside the black stripe, and said to the children: this is what a small village does. It does not choose the best and throw out the worst. It lets a blind hand choose, and the smaller the village, the faster the hand settles everything — until only one pattern remains, and the hand has nothing left to decide.

注解：这里讲的是遗传漂变（genetic drift）——在小种群里，随机抽样的误差使某种性状的频率在代际间无方向地起伏，与该性状优劣无关，直到某一种性状被固定、其余丢失，整体的多样性随之降低。

Note: This describes genetic drift — in a small population, random sampling error makes a trait's frequency drift up and down between generations, with no regard for whether the trait is better or worse, until one trait becomes fixed and the others are lost, lowering the diversity of the whole.
