更远处，另有疆域

Steve Finbow·3:AM Magazine·2026年7月6日·4分钟阅读·原文

那低沉的轰鸣或许是一场正在聚拢的暴风雨，或许是行李箱在水泥地上有节奏的拖曳——也许是某位俄罗斯航空（Aeroflot）女乘务员的箱子，她刚在希思罗机场（Heathrow）航站楼的洗手间里与人仓促交欢，此刻面颊绯红、喘息未定。无论如何，那声音侵入了他的梦境。他赤裸地躺在黑暗中，浑身滚烫，手指沿着那条从胸骨蜿蜒至耻骨的剖腹探查切口缓缓描摹，那道疤如同一串异星的红宝石，他思忖着正在缝线之下孕育的虫豸般的神经症。

The low rumble might have been a gathering storm, or the rhythmic drag of luggage over concrete – perhaps the suitcase of an Aeroflot stewardess, flushed and breathless from a post-flight fuck in the terminal toilets at Heathrow. Regardless, the sound breached his dreams. He lay naked and feverish in the dark, tracing the laparotomy scar that meandered from his sternum to his pubis like a string of alien rubies, contemplating the insectoid neuroses gestating within his sutures.

那道疤及其合成单丝缝线，成了一座不可能的都市的蓝图，其瘢痕隆起如锯齿状的尖塔，挑衅着人类的几何学。在他的指尖下，皮肤以一种冰冷的金属自律搏动，暗示着一座由某种恶意的智识——或一个脑积水的孩童——所设计的迷宫之城，用以收容正在他粘连松解的腹腔里殖民的种种焦虑。他把这次手术感知为一种结构性的入侵——在他躯干核心的组织间隙里，植入了一座盲目而色情的城堡。他想象缝线正在收紧——如张力索，将这寄生式的建筑深锚于他的脏器，把他的躯架准备成一个颤抖的宿主，以承载一具他已无法认作自己的未来解剖学。

The scar and its synthetic monofilament sutures served as the blueprint for an impossible metropolis, its keloid ridges rising into serrated spires that defied human geometry. Under his fingertips, the skin pulsed with a cold, metallic autonomy, suggesting a labyrinthine city designed by a malign intelligence – or a hydrocephalic child – to house the anxieties colonising his adhesiolytic abdomen. He sensed the surgery as a structural intrusion – the implantation of a sightless, erotic citadel within the interstitial tissue of his core. He imagined the sutures tightening – tension cables, anchoring this parasitic architecture deep into his viscera, preparing his frame to serve as the shuddering host for a future anatomy that he could no longer recognise as his own.

房间之外的走廊，空气里嗡响着增压机舱的气息，一种由护士们维持的幻影大气；她们像被切除双翼的天使一般飘然而过，一波一波地到来，朱红色的制服笔挺，紧窄的白衬衫，颈巾如小旗，圆筒小帽扣死在漆亮的发上，是上世纪中叶女乘务员的浆硬仿像——全是冷峻的剪影——在病房间穿行，仿佛那是波音747（Boeing 747）长途航班窄而逼仄的过道。她们挥舞着医疗器械——血压计、血氧探头、耳温枪——仿佛那是天体导航的信标。他以一种惊恐而肉欲的虔敬注视她们，确信病床与机舱厨房之间那层薄膜已然消融；而从他身侧蜿蜒而出、灌满不明体液的塑料管，是光缆般的脐带，将他崭新的异星生理接入一个新生的全球中继，确保他体内的溃散正被传译为那提喻式平流层静默的遥测数据。

Beyond the room, the corridor air hummed with the scent of a pressurised airline cabin, a phantom atmosphere maintained by nurses who glided past like resected angels; they arrived in waves, their vermilion-red uniforms crisp, tight white blouses, the pennants of their neck scarves, their pillbox hats cocked and locked to their lacquered hair, starch-stiffened facsimiles of the mid-century stewardess – all severe silhouettes – navigating the ward as if it were the narrow, claustrophobic aisle of a Boeing 747 long-haul flight. They brandished their medical equipment – the sphygmomanometers, the oxygen saturation probes, the tympanic thermometer – as if they were celestial navigation beacons. He watched them with a terrified, carnal reverence, convinced the thin veil between his hospital bed and the airline cabin’s galley had dissolved; and the plastic tubing filled with unidentifiable bodily fluid snaking from his side were fibre-optic umbilical cords connecting his new alien physiology to a neoteric global relay, ensuring his internal decay was being transmitted into the silent telemetry of the synecdochic stratosphere.

后来，当他从古老梦境的退行过程中爬出，他猛然以一种幽闭的顿悟意识到：他康复的剧场是一只摇篮，而他是某个垂死时代最后的、被弃的亚当，他的疤是一道生物气闸，下一代人类必须经由这孔口被强行显形，是他器官髓质之中一种新的过度增生。他感到自身孤绝的重量；他颤抖的肌肉组织是一颗行星表面，被指定为某个物种的创生之地，那物种由中子星、白矮星的恒星残骸工程化而来；他的脐是一枚太初黑洞，从微观脉动到百万太阳质量。他腹部的疼痛从一种临床的疾患，转为节律性的、准备性的宫缩，肩负着以不锈钢、钴、钛、消毒空气、以及他那被重建的血肉令人战栗的柔软几何，向空寂宇宙播撒子嗣的任务。

Later, when he had crawled from the catagenic processes of ancient dreams, he realised with a jolt of claustrophobic revelation that the theatre of his recovery was a cradle, and he was the final, derelict Adam of a dying era, and that his scar was a biological airlock, the aperture through which the next iteration of humanity must be forcibly manifested, a new form of hyperplasia in the medullary of his organs. He felt the weight of his isolation; his shivering musculature a planetary surface designated for the genesis of a species engineered from the stellar remnants of neutron stars, white dwarves; his umbilicus, a primordial black hole pulsing from microscopic to a million solar masses. The pain in his abdomen shifted from a clinical affliction to the rhythmic, preparatory contractions of a gestation chamber, tasked with seeding the empty cosmos with children born of stainless steel, cobalt, titanium, antiseptic air, and the harrowing soft geometry of his reconstructed flesh.

午夜过后的某个时刻，只剩监护仪的嘀嘀声，与时钟尖锐的滴答，那位白大褂女外科医生俯身床前，一把带俄语口音的锐利语言手术刀，投射出这女人严酷的宇宙论。她把他的呓语、他翱翔的异象，统统斥为认知碎屑，深度镇静与脓毒症侵入性免疫失调留下的化学残渣。她的目光以冰冷的、诊断式的冷漠钉在他腹上那道锯齿状的线，把复杂的地形简化为单纯的生物学错误。她把他那些深刻的、末世论的启示，等同于在毒性血流中窒息的神经元的随机误放。她无菌的观察，把整个发烧的隐秘建筑——恒星的导管与天使的机组——统统视作一种内部畸变，一台垂死机器试图解读自身崩坏的感觉静电时所发出的疼痛性失语法。

At some time after midnight, with just the beeps from the monitors, and the acute ticking of a clock, the white-coated female surgeon leaned over the bed, a sharp, Russian-inflected linguistic scalpel projected the woman’s brutal cosmology. She dismissed his ranting, his soaring visions, as cognitive debris, the chemical residue of deep sedation and the invasive immune dysregulation of sepsis. Her gaze fixed on the jagged line of his abdomen with a cold, diagnostic indifference, reducing the complex topography to simple biological error. She equated his profound, apocalyptic revelations with the random misfiring of neurons suffocating in a toxic bloodstream. Her sterile observations regarded the entire cryptic architecture of the fever – the stellar conduits and the angelic flight crews – solely as an internal distortion, an agrammatism of pain generated by a dying machine attempting to interpret the sensory static of its own breakdown.

意识退潮之际，重症监护室的白色四壁消融，他向药物诱导昏迷那压倒性的引力交出了自己偏执的思绪。那条河状的疤断裂成一种几何的传染，掠过他胀大的躯干，绽放为一张生物荧光的网，一个转移性的麻风万花筒。每一个缝线点都点燃，硬化成一根根搏动的、水晶般的神经元，连向一张无形的新心血管网格。他衰弱的脉搏变成一串节律性的数据脉冲，把他内部的风景经由医院的医疗信息系统向外广播，送入万维网的全球基础设施。他正在缓缓变形成一座行星级发射机；那张网络以他溃散的生肉般的、狂乱的语言脉动，把他的伤害感受器信号尖叫着送过地壳，去与上空静默待命的波音747、隐身飞机、以及国网（Guowang）与千帆（Qianfan）卫星星座的航迹对齐。

As consciousness receded, the white walls of the intensive care unit dissolved, and he surrendered his monomaniacal thoughts to the crushing gravity of a pharmacologically induced coma. The riverine scar fractured into a geometric contagion that raced across his bloated torso, blooming into a bioluminescent web, a metastatic leprotic kaleidoscope. Every suture point ignited, hardening into a throbbing, crystalline neuron linked to an invisible new cardiovascular grid. His failing pulse became a rhythmic burst of data, broadcasting his internal landscape outward through the hospital’s medical information system into the global infrastructure of the World Wide Web. He was slowly transforming into a planetary transmitter; the network pulsed with the raw, frantic language of his dissolution, sending his nociceptor signals screaming through the earth’s crust to align with the flight paths of the silent, waiting Boeing 747s, stealth aircraft, and the Guowang and Qianfan satellite constellations above.

当他终于浮出水面，发现的宣称萎缩成一种可悲的讽喻，他所感应到的浩瀚扩张，完全被费尔瑟姆（Feltham）卧室那四壁坟茔所拘束。翻涌的神经之风，与他受感染的血液可闻的奔涌，在抵达与离港喷气引擎那多重低频的嗡鸣里被回声、被放大——那些飞往邻近希思罗燃烧的沥青静脉的航班，令人迷醉地下降。天光中的辉光，一滴琥珀色的泪珠，封住了谢珀顿（Shepperton）、费尔瑟姆、希灵登（Hillingdon）、哈蒙兹沃思（Harmondsworth）之间的天地，封住了希尔顿（Hilton）、阿里尔（Ariel）、精益求精（Excelsior）、天际（Skyline）诸酒店——发光树脂里的气泡。

When he finally surfaced, the claim of discovery withered into a pathetic irony, the vast expansion he sensed constrained entirely by the four-walled tomb of his Feltham bedroom. Swirling neural winds and the audible rush of his infected blood were echoed and amplified in the multiple low-frequency drone of arriving and departing jet engines, the exhilarant descent of airliners bound for the burning asphalt veins of nearby Heathrow. The glow in the sky, an amber teardrop enclosing the land and sky between Shepperton, Feltham, Hillingdon, and Harmondsworth, the Hilton, Ariel, Excelsior, and Skyline hotels, air bubbles in the luminescent resin.

卧室是一只残存的囊胚，是他碎裂心灵的龛位，漆成木兰色的石膏墙被他那荒凉、网状、流转的地平线遮没，像一艘执行前往MOMO-z14-1星系任务的孤人飞船的内部。床的上方，伊夫·坦吉（Yves Tanguy）的《手与手套》那梦境般钙化的平原，渗入罗伯托·马塔（Roberto Matta）的《夜的入侵》那生物形态的、混沌的幽灵电路，它们幻影般的深处映照、又嘲弄着他崭新的内部地理。他向上伸手，感到新生的毛细血管在指尖只触到马克斯·恩斯特（Max Ernst）的《沉默之眼》那装框的、锯齿状的恐怖时绷紧、破裂，那画的静止仿佛粘附在他病榻凝滞的空气上。

The bedroom served as a residual blastosphere, and a shrine to his splintering psyche, the magnolia-painted plaster walls obscured by the desolate, reticulate shifting horizons of his obsession, like the interior of a solitary manned craft on a mission to the MOMO‑z14‑1 galaxy. Above the bed, the dreamlike, calcified plains of Yves Tanguy’s The Hand and the Glove bled into the biomorphic, chaotic spectral circuitry of Roberto Matta’s Invasion of the Night, their phantom depths mirroring and yet mocking his new internal geography. He reached up, felt the newly generated capillaries strain and burst as his fingers brushed only the framed, jagged terrors of Max Ernst’s The Eye of Silence, its stillness seeming to adhere to the stagnant air of his sickbed.

在起落架的轰鸣之下，他听见尼龙长袜尖锐而有节奏的摩擦——一种承载着他色情往昔低语的声响，一千个女乘务员在时间、空间与欲望那无限循环的过道里踱步的合成窸窣。那声响把房间系往天空中的瑞利散射（Rayleigh scattering），一种肤色聚合物的触觉记忆，粉色的云与红色的精灵，回响着被切除双翼天使们的股部运动与生殖分泌。

Beneath the roar of the landing gear, he heard the sharp, rhythmic friction of nylon stockings – a sound freighted with the whisper of his erotic past, the synthetic rustle of a thousand stewardesses pacing the infinite, recirculated aisles of time, space, and desire. That sound tethered the room to the Rayleigh scattering in the sky, a tactile memory of flesh-coloured polymer, the pink clouds and red sprites echoing the movement of the thighs and the genital secretions of the resected angels.

他尽可能一动不动地躺着，那疼痛——让他想起故障闪烁的航班信息显示系统——脉动、闪烁，却拒绝结晶为一个目的地。他用手指抚过腹部，疤痕结成一种瘢痕盲文，令人想起理查德·阿维顿（Richard Avedon）那张著名的照片：安迪·沃霍尔（Andy Warhol）被子弹贯穿的躯干的创伤。他仍是一个囚徒，困在一座悬置解剖学的市郊剧场里，唯一的照明是十字架状阴影的间歇图案，与过往飞机频闪般的强光，描着那些从未逃出期待与离港之悖论力量的轨迹。

He lay as still as possible, the pain – reminding him of glitching flight information display systems – pulsed and flickered but refused to crystallise into a destination. He ran his fingers over his abdomen, the scars forming a cicatricial braille reminiscent of the famous Richard Avedon photograph of the trauma of Andy Warhol’s bullet-traversed torso. He remained a prisoner in a suburban theatre of suspended anatomy, where the only illumination was the intermittent pattern of cruciform shadows and the strobe-like glare of passing aircraft, tracing trajectories that never escaped the aporistic forces of expectation and departure.
