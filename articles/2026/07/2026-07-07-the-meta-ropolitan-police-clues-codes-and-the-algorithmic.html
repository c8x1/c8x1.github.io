元大都市警察——线索、密码与算法信息圈

Steve Finbow·3:AM Magazine·2026年7月7日·7分钟阅读·原文

清晨是一片灰色的单调，那关于死去的电视机和毫无他选地照耀着的太阳的写作亦然。在伦敦，人们会发现建筑物与天气的颜色精确地吻合，而若外面有一片湿漉漉的沥青，那么某处便总有某个不知何故潜伏着的、已退役的警察局。在伦敦，人们所见的是新奇之无物紧邻熟悉之无物。一座幽灵满布、未来赛博公民之投影萦绕的诡异之城。

The morning is a grey monotony, all that writing about dead televisions and the sun shining having no alternative. What one finds in London is that the buildings precisely match the colour of the weather, and if there is wet asphalt outside, then there is a decommissioned police station lurking somewhere for some reason. In London, one sees the nothing new adjacent to the nothing familiar. An uncanny city full of ghosts and the projections of its future cyber citizens.

雷克斯·金（Rex King）警长不动，至少他的身体保持静止，坐在一张钢制办公桌前——这批量生产的家具，是警察程式小说的一个平板包装式的隐喻。而对这位侦探而言，那是沉重铜马蹄铁的「上手」（readiness-to-hand）实在，是更为平凡的手套、车票、碎裂手机、弯曲钥匙、啤酒罐、断裂项链、摔碎的手表、停车罚单的图腾式亲属——那些通常散落于犯罪小说中的「现成在手」（presence-at-hand）线索。对雷克斯·金警长而言，他通常解析的线索更多是语言性质的：「Relax、free、each、foreseen、horseshoe、breakthrough、Red Sea、Rosyth、globe trotter、rook、titans、town planning、resemble、obsolescence、utterly、reheat、clip、three-foot、hack-saw、taxi rank、bacon、arboreal。」因为这不是你寻常的惊悚小说，这是你卓尔不群的实验性犯罪小说。

Detective Sergeant Rex King does not move, at least his body remains still, sitting before a steel desk – the mass-produced furniture, a flat-packed metaphor for the police procedural. And, for this detective, it is the “readiness-to-hand” reality of a heavy brass horseshoe, totemic relative of the more mundane glove, bus ticket, cracked phone, bent key, beer can, broken necklace, smashed watch, parking ticket – the “presence-at-hand” clues that normally litter a crime novel. For Sergeant Rex King, the clues he usually parses are more of the linguistic kind: “Relax, free, each, foreseen, horseshoe, breakthrough, Red Sea, Rosyth, globe trotter, rook, titans, town planning, resemble, obsolescence, utterly, reheat, clip, three-foot, hack-saw, taxi rank, bacon, arboreal.” For this is not your average thriller, this is your exceptional experimental crime novel.

托尼·怀特（Tony White）要为《森林中的喷泉》（The Fountain in the Forest）撰写续篇，将先锋派与「谁干的」谜案相融合，并把小说建立在伦敦一桩谋杀案的调查之上，无异于买下一块柔软粉红、易碎的橡皮——恰如罗伯-格里耶（Robbe-Grillet）《橡皮》（The Erasers）中的瓦拉斯（Wallas）——擦去此前犯罪小说中已存在的大部分内容。那些线索——如同橡皮的品牌——被抹除，而穿行于勘测过的街道间、搜寻一种特定质地、一条特定线索的漫步，只把人带回原点，而那原点同时也始终是出发点。这座大都市的地理学是一片想象的地形学，街道便是脑回与脑沟，金的轨迹——以及怀特的叙事——之所以如此，只因其中固有的概观性与系统性结构。

For Tony White to write a sequel to The Fountain in the Forest, to merge the avant-garde with the whodunnit, and to base his novel on the investigation of a murder in London, is to purchase a soft pink friable eraser – much like Wallas in Robbe-Grillet’s The Erasers – and to rub out much of what has gone before in crime fiction. The clues – like the brand of the eraser – are effaced and the walks through the chartered streets, searching for a specific texture, a specific clue, merely returns one to the point of origin but one that is also and always the point of departure. The geography of the metropolis is a topography of the imagination, the streets are the gyri and sulci, and King’s trajectory – and White’s narrative – are only so because of the synoptic and systemic structures inherent within them.

揭露情节会——如同亨利·詹姆斯（Henry James）对复述梦境的执拗——失去一位读者。然而，詹姆斯可能从未说过这话，我方才所写的这句话便是一条假线索——一条熏青鱼（false clue）。不过，作为主线剧情，内政部坚持认为人工智能应当取代人类侦探，认为算法能绕过污垢、侦破罪行，即便此过程毫无兴奋可言——但鉴于AI不断产生幻觉，且建立于阿西莫夫式（Asimovian）取悦其主人的谄媚规则之上，那么所有罪犯或将沦为切萨雷·龙勃罗索（Cesare Lombroso）颅相学画像的聚合体，以及由阿尔伯特·霍夫曼（Albert Hofmann）构想、再经皮克斯（Pixar）重新想象出来的幻影卡通角色。官僚体制渴望（以那种冷水浴自慰式意义上的）抹除人类、擦除变量。但它未曾料到的是，人性——大体上——是一道残留在最临床化的形式主义之下的深色污痕。

To reveal the plot would – like Henry James’s insistence on the retelling of dreams – lose a reader. But then, James may never have said that, and the sentence I have just written is a false clue – a read herring. However, as a main storyline, the Home Office insists that artificial intelligence should replace the human detective, that an algorithm can bypass the grime and solve the crime, even if there is no excitement involved in doing so – but seeing as AI continually hallucinates and is built on the sycophantic Asimovian rule of pleasing its masters, then all criminals may become aggregations of Cesare Lombroso’s phrenological profiling and phantasmal cartoon characters created by Albert Hofmann reimagined by Pixar. The bureaucracy desires (in that cold-shower-masturbatory sense) to erase the human, to rub out the variable. But what it does not reckon for is that humanity is – mostly – a dark smudge that remains beneath the most clinical formalism.

金的马蹄铁——小说的书页——拥有属于另一个年代的重量，两件物什都把我们带回一个过去：彼时共同体尚有意义，社会尚且存在，人们仍对他人心存关切。但怀特并未于此过甚其辞；CND（核裁军）的符号与游行、2011年英国骚乱、各色历史学会，与警官们及人口贩子们一样，都是叙事那张纵横填字格中不可或缺的方格。正如D·H·劳伦斯（D. H. Lawrence）所言：「小说本身为我们立下这些法则，而我们终其时间都在规避它们。小说中的人必须是『敏捷的』。而这在一堆未知含义之中意味着一件事：他必须与小说中所有其他事物——雪、臭虫、阳光、阳具、火车、丝礼帽、猫、悲伤、人、食物、白喉、倒挂金钟、星辰、观念、上帝、牙膏、闪电和卫生纸——保持敏捷的关联。他必须与这一切事物处于敏捷的关系之中。他的所言所行必须与它们全体相关。」

King’s horseshoe – the pages of the novel – have a weight that belongs to another decade, both objects return us to a past in which community matters, society exists, and people still give a fuck about other people. But White does not overdo this; the CND symbols and demos, the 2011 UK riots, the historical societies, are integral to the crossword grid squares of the narrative as much as the police officers and the human traffickers. As D. H. Lawrence noted: “The novel itself lays down these laws for us, and we spend our time evading them. The man in the novel must be ‘quick’. And this means one thing, among a host of unknown meaning: it means he must have a quick relatedness to all the other things in the novel: snow, bed-bugs, sunshine, the phallus, trains, silk-hats, cats, sorrow, people, food, diphtheria, fuchsias, stars, ideas, God, tooth-paste, lightning, and toilet-paper. He must be in quick relation to all these things. What he says and does must be relative to them all.”

那声响——是什么？是小说在以太中的震颤？还是国家犯罪局（National Crime Agency）先进AI终端的嗡鸣与呼噜？一台人造的机器，其唯一的「存在理由」（raison d'être）（抑或「存在的电阻」resistor d'être？）便是处理数据行，以揭示肉眼所遗漏者。于是怀特创造了一座平行的伦敦，一处关于双身与背叛的场域，其中人与算法（谁是共生体？）令人联想到柴纳·米耶维（China Miéville）的《城市与城市》（The City and the City）：贝赛尔（Besźel）与阿尔库玛（Al Qoma）的公民占据着相似而又互为两个不同且截然分明的地理空间，他们的存在（他们的「此性」haecceity）唯有在他们严格地「视而不见」那「另一方」时才得以成立。这套计算机程序被训练去无视异常，把现实切分为齐整、合法的范畴——未来是康德式（Kantian）的。但金无法视而不见：「当你在调查纯粹的邪恶，那些本不该发生、你却看着它们一遍又一遍发生的罪行，一股未被看见却无孔不入、在统计学上——可悲地——完全可预测的性暴力潮水，就在社会表面之下不断舔蚀，总有什么东西要崩裂，而有时那崩裂的东西就是你。」然而有太多未被看见之物——未被看见的猎物、未被看见的蝉、未被看见的事件、未被看见的一张张白色A4纸、未被看见的乌鸫、未被看见的水、未被看见的手机——仿佛怀特正在把世界物化、造物，以此作为对数字之「去物化」的替代。

That noise – what is it? Is it the novel’s tremulations on the ether? Or is it the National Crime Agency’s advanced AI terminal humming and whirring? A machine of human construct that’s only raison d’être (resistor d’être?) is to process data lines in order to reveal what the human eye misses. And so White creates a parallel London, a site of doubles and duplicity in which the human and the algorithm (who is the symbiont?) are reminiscent of China Miéville’s The City and the City in which the citizens of Besźel and Al Qoma occupy similar geographical spaces that are two different and distinct loci, their existence (their haecceity) is only so if they rigorously unsee the “other”. The computer program is trained to unsee the anomalies, to partition reality into neat, legal categories – the future is Kantian. But King cannot unsee: “When you were investigating pure evil, crimes that really shouldn’t happen but you were watching them happen over and over again, an unseen yet pervasive and statistically – sadly – wholly predictable tide of sexual violence lapping away just beneath society’s surface, something had to give, and sometimes that something was you.” But there are many unseen things – unseen prey, unseen cicadas, unseen events, unseen sheets of white A4 paper, unseen blackbirds, unseen water, unseen phones – as if White is reifying, thing-making the world as an alternative to the de-thing-ification of the digital.

那些活页档案夹是真实的，它们的物质性摆在金面前，宛如从过去渗出的某种血迹；而那过去，是社会之死的空间与场所——1984至1985年的矿工大罢工（Miners' Strike）——一桩权力结构欲使之长埋地下的悬案。正如贝赛尔与阿尔库玛这对孪生城市，历史与建筑相互重叠；它们占据着与人类尸体完全相同的物理空间——既缺席又始终在场——两个截然不同的年代呼吸着同一空气，却以不同方式代谢氧气：「雷克斯知道，一位前内政大臣曾承认扣下了三十份与矿工罢工相关的档案，即便与此同时他们正拒绝『奥格里夫调查』（Orgreave Inquiry）的呼声。而那公开的三十份档案，想必只是冰山一角，那三十份是他们得以承认其存在而不致出事的。」

The lever-arch files are real, their materiality sits before King as a kind of bleed from the past; and that past is the space and place of the death of society, the 1984–1985 Miners’ Strike – a cold case that the power structure wants to keep buried. And like the twin cities of Besźel and Al Qoma, the history and the architecture overlap; they occupy the exact same physical space as the human corpse – at once absent and always present – two distinct eras breathing the same air but metabolising the oxygen in different ways: “Rex knew that a former Home Secretary had admitted to holding back thirty files relating to the Miners’ Strike, even as they were rejecting calls for an ‘Orgreave Inquiry’. And those stated thirty files had presumably been just the tip of the iceberg, the thirty that they could get away with admitting to the existence of.”

大卫·皮斯（David Peace）也在《GB84》中绘制过这片领地——一切都在污垢里、在罢工里、在黑暗里。此处有一种马克思主义的进步/倒退：没有任何事件是分离的，它们是同一事件，是不义横跨四十年的重复，是一段关于制度性腐败与权力滥用的咒语；一段关于共同体与家庭之毁坏的不可磨灭的记忆，无法被生成程式报告的计算机程序所打断或安抚。历史并非事实的聚合，它是一种块茎式的、质性的情绪、感受与活的联结的多样性。过去是宴席上的幽灵，未来则取决于我们分析过去的方式所产生的反刍效应。但正如怀特所知、正如D·H·劳伦斯所坚持：「艺术之事，在于揭示人与其周遭宇宙在活的瞬间之中的关系。」

David Peace has also mapped this territory in GB84 – all of it in the dirt, in the strike, in the dark. There is a Marxist progression/retrogression here in which no events are separate, they are the same event, injustice repeating across forty years, an incantation of institutional corruption, of the abuses of power; an indelible memory of the destruction of community and family that cannot be broken or appeased by a computer program producing a procedural report. History is not an amalgamation of facts, it is a rhizomatic and qualitative multiplicity of emotions, feelings, and living connections. The past is the phantom at the feast, the future is the regurgitative effects of how we analyse the past. But as White knows and as D. H. Lawrence insisted: “The business of art is to reveal the relation between man and his circumambient universe, at the living moment.”

尽管其自觉的实验性——抑或正因如此——怀特的叙事学是真空密封的。是的，情节推进且展开，但缰绳收得很紧，有一种对压力的理解。劳伦斯总警司（DCI Lawrence）失踪了。一名失踪者——一个「misper」。人们会想起佩雷克（Perec）《消失》（A Void）中的安东·沃尔（Anton Vowl）——一个如此彻底的虚空，以至于扭曲了语言形式，减去一个关键字符，迫使叙述围绕一个恒定的缺口蜷曲——看哪，一声向乌力波（Oulipo）未被看见的眨眼。少了DCI劳伦斯，部门开始停滞，时间放缓，某个二元功能正在出故障。

Despite its knowing experimentation – or because of it – White’s narratology is vacuum sealed. Yes, the plot advances and expands, but there is a tight rein, an understanding of pressure. Detective Chief Inspector Lawrence vanishes. A missing person – a misper. One thinks of Anton Vowl in Perec’s A Void – a void so total it twists linguistic form, subtracting a vital glyph and forcing narration to curl around a constant gap – behold an unseen wink to Oulipo. Without DCI Lawrence, the department begins to stall, time slows down, one of the binary functions is glitching.

怀特及其主角熟悉伦敦的街道——而且，如同在《达洛维夫人》（Mrs Dalloway）中一样，伦敦是这部小说的主要角色之一。苏荷区（Soho）——它从帮派主宰、红灯区、声名狼藉的场所与意大利熟食店向数字娱乐中心的精神变形——如同金的记忆一样，仍保留着若干真实的飞地：「而现在他们在这里，用些额外的小犒赏把一周年庆典再凑合着延长几天。昨天安妮从苏荷区糕点铺梅森·贝尔托（Maison Bertaux）取回了一只蛋糕，那是雷克斯在一个寒冷、漆黑的冬日午后领她去的当地珍宝。而今晚他们要吃外卖。」过去萦绕着这本书，那是克里斯·佩蒂（Chris Petit）笔下罗宾逊（Robinson）的伦敦，或是迈克尔·摩考克（Michael Moorcock）《母亲伦敦》（Mother London）那层层叠叠的神话废墟，又或是伊恩·辛克莱（Iain Sinclair）《白教堂，猩红痕迹》（White Chappell, Scarlet Tracings）中移时的回声。《宴席上的幽灵》（Phantom at the Feast）——如同伦敦——建立在它自身那些幽灵之上：《森林中的喷泉》。每一条巷弄、每一个句子，都是一座关于金融欺诈、人口贩卖、色情业，以及前作中情节线测绘的档案。城中每一块砖、页上每一个词，都保留着过去的振动，人们仍能听见冲突乐队（The Clash）1985年的街头卖唱巡演——一道褴褛的、原声的幽灵，拖过浇在朋克尸体之上的混凝土。

White and his protagonist know the streets of London – and, like in Mrs Dalloway, London is one of the main characters in this novel. Soho – its psycho-metamorphosis from gang-ruled, red-light, seedy establishment, and Italian delis to the centre of digital entertainment – like King’s memories, still holds enclaves of the authentic: “And now here they were, eking out their first-anniversary celebration for a few more days with some extra treats. Yesterday Annie had picked up a cake from Soho patisserie Maison Bertaux, a local treasure to which Rex had introduced her one cold, dark winter’s afternoon. And tonight they were having a takeaway.” The past haunts the book, the London of Chris Petit’s Robinson or the multi-layered, mythic ruins of Michael Moorcock’s Mother London, or the time-shifting echoes of Iain Sinclair’s White Chappell, Scarlet Tracings. Phantom at the Feast – like London – is built on top of its own ghosts – The Fountain in the Forest. Every alleyway, every sentence, is an archive of financial fraud, human trafficking, pornography, and the mappings of storyline in the previous novel. Every brick in the city, every word on the page, retains the vibration of the past, one can still hear The Clash’s 1985 busking tour – a ragged, acoustic ghost trailing through the concrete poured over the corpse of punk.

金记得他当卧底警察（spycop）的那些时日，一个不同的名字，一个不同的目的。他潜入激进团体，成为影子中的影子，一如德里克·雷蒙德（Derek Raymond）《他睁着眼睛死去》（He Died with His Eyes Open）中那位无名的侦探。和那位侦探一样，金在夜里吸收遇难者的身份，聆听死者的声音，直到自己在暗窗中的倒影变得陌生。猎手与国家集体创伤之间的边界薄如纸，轻易便能刺穿——「工厂」（the Factory）即是国家——雷蒙德的无名侦探与金有着近似的信条：「大多数人闭着眼睛生活，但我打算睁着眼睛死去。我们都本能地想让死亡对自己不那么艰难。」但雷蒙德的侦探持有一种齐奥朗式（Cioranesque）的态度：「每一天你都在与死亡的疯狂赛跑中积累知识，而那场赛跑死亡必胜。你想在拥有的时间内查明一切；可到头来你却纳闷当初何必费心，这一切终将丢失。」而怀特笔下的金则有一种听天由命的生机：「他究竟什么时候才该有空去归档这些东西？把它们统统扔进垃圾桶倒是省事。万一这里头真有什么要紧东西，结果某项关键任务因此搁置未办，自然会有人大惊小叫。这你大可放心。」

King remembers his time as a spycop, a different name, a different purpose. He infiltrated the radical groups, became a shadow among shadows like the unnamed detective in Derek Raymond’s He Died with His Eyes Open. Like that detective, King spent his nights absorbing the identities of the casualties, listening to the voices of the dead until his own reflection in the dark window became unfamiliar. The boundary between the hunter and the collective trauma of the state is thin, easily punctured – the Factory is the state – Raymond’s unnamed detective has a similar ethos to that of King: “Most people live with their eyes shut, but I mean to die with mine open. We all instinctively try to make death less difficult for ourselves.” But whereas Raymond’s detective has a Cioranesque attitude: “Every day you amass knowledge in a frantic race against death that death must win. You want to find out everything in the time you have; yet in the end you wonder why you bothered, it’ll all be lost,” White’s King has a resigned vitality: “When was he ever supposed to have the time to file this stuff? It was tempting to simply chuck the lot in the bin. In the unlikely event that there was anything really important in here, and some vital task that had gone unactioned as a result, someone would soon make a fuss. You could guarantee it.”

金的柏格森式（Bergsonian）「生命冲动」（élan vital）——他那种绝望的敏捷——意味着，与其踹门而入、用米尔沃尔砖（Millwall bricking）砸向嫌疑人，他宁愿等待，被一种特定的迟疑、一种深沉的缄默攫住，这令人想起让-菲利普·图森（Jean-Philippe Toussaint）的反侦探小说《缄默》（Reticence），其中神秘类型的传统动能被一种深沉的、病态的惰性所短路。在图森与怀特手中，程式性的机制——线索、观察、监视的迫近威胁——被剥夺了其动力学功能，转化为一种纯粹的、令人痛苦的关于延宕的现象学。

King’s Bergsonian élan vital – his desperate quickness – means that, rather than kicking down doors and Millwall bricking suspects, he waits, seized by a particular hesitation, a profound reticence reminiscent of Jean-Philippe Toussaint’s anti-detective novel Reticence, in which the traditional momentum of the mystery genre is short-circuited by a profound, pathological inertia. In Toussaint’s and White’s hands, the procedural mechanics – clues, observations, the looming threat of surveillance – are stripped of their kinetic function and converted into a pure, agonising phenomenology of delay.

典型的侦探小说依靠一连串主动演绎来撬开叙事；然而此处，调查被重新构想为一场缓慢的、熵增的冻结，但尚未冷到AI的低温配方无法被撤销、解冻并重新验尸的地步。米歇尔·福柯（Michel Foucault）指出，圆形监狱（Panopticon）的最终胜利在于使权力自动化与去个体化，一种被其主体内化的永久凝视——这同样是对小说与小说写作的一种敏锐描述。金既是这台装置的工具，也是其囚徒，从国家的代理人滑向被自身过度观察、记忆与悲悯所驯服的囚徒。如一切伟大的犯罪小说那样，真相不是一种启示，而是选项的逐渐钙化与事实性的主权。

The typical detective novel relies on a cascade of active deductions to force open a narrative; here, however, investigation is re-envisioned as a slow, entropic freeze but not to the point where AI’s cryogenic formulations cannot be undone, thawed, and re-autopsied. Michel Foucault notes that the Panopticon’s ultimate triumph is to make power automated and disindividualised, a permanent gaze internalised by its subjects – this is also a perceptive description of the novel and novel writing. King becomes both the instrument and the prisoner of this apparatus, shifting from an agent of the state to a dociled captive of his own hyper-observation, memory, and compassion. Like all great crime novels, the truth is not a revelation, it is a gradual calcification of options and the sovereignty of facticity.

怀特将未来视为一场纯粹的鲍德里亚式（Baudrillardian）拟像，真实已完全被真实的符号所取代——加粗的词便是那任意的与裁定性的：「Run-around、Hobbs、enable、hydrant、numb、scorn、soluble、aspirin、nasal、hurrah、apostrophised、looks、Eamonn、Basil Rathbone、sheet、Botha、on shore、chamber、rosette、indeed、avow、ormolu、Atlanta、sweat shop、opossum、social。」在未来，侦探不再侦破罪行，而只是测绘一条预先铺设好的信息回路——一台超真实的装置，它不需要任何真实的罪犯，只需要一块洁净的算法网格，而非塞满马蹄铁的脏嘴那桩麻烦营生：「这项调查是怎么回事？你简直没法称之为烂摊子。问题恰恰在此。它太齐整了。他还不如说是被外星人绑架了。」

White sees the future as a pure Baudrillardian simulation where the real has been entirely substituted by the signs of the real – the emboldened words are the arbitrary and the arbitration: “Run-around, Hobbs, enable, hydrant, numb, scorn, soluble, aspirin, nasal, hurrah, apostrophised, looks, Eamonn, Basil Rathbone, sheet, Botha, on shore, chamber, rosette, indeed, avow, ormolu, Atlanta, sweat shop, opossum, social.” In the future, the detective no longer solves crimes, but merely maps a pre-established circuit of information – a hyperreal apparatus that requires no actual criminal, only a clean algorithmic grid, rather than the messy business of the horseshoe-filled gob: “What was it about this investigation? You could hardly call it a mess. That was the problem. It was too neat. He might as well have been abducted by aliens.”

《宴席上的幽灵》是一处抵抗之地，抗击着AI文产业的反乌托邦地平线：一个所有虚构都只不过是机械地图、预先铺设的死亡信息回路、无需任何真实书写行为的超真实装置的未来。对抗这种算法性的扁平化，小说——尤其是这部小说——矗立为一处顽固的人类摩擦之地，或如D·H·劳伦斯所言：「小说是迄今为止所达到的人类表达的最高形式。为何？因为它如此地无能于绝对。」

Phantom at the Feast is a site of resistance combatting the dystopian horizon of the AI literary industry: a future in which all fictions are merely mechanical maps, pre-established circuits of dead information, hyperreal apparatuses that require no actual act of writing. Against this algorithmic flattening, the novel – and this novel in particular – stands as a site of stubborn, human friction, or as D. H. Lawrence put it: “The novel is the highest form of human expression so far attained. Why? Because it is so incapable of the absolute.”
