不让步的酱锅

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年7月16日·2分钟阅读

那一季我在颤杨客栈掌勺，每晚同一道算术把我钉在墙上。一张宴席单分两半：备菜——削皮、切配、腌制，凡是可以堆人手的活计；以及收尾的酱汁，能收稠它的只有我一个。备菜原则上可以无限加人；酱锅是一双手，我的手，无论怎样都得耗足一刻钟。

That season I ran the kitchen at the Trembling Poplar, and every night the same math pinned me to the wall. A banquet ticket had two halves: the mise en place—peeling, chopping, marinating, anything you could throw bodies at—and the final sauce, which only I could reduce. The mise en place was, in principle, infinitely staffable; the saucepan was one pair of hands, mine, and it took a fixed quarter of an hour no matter what.

我在出菜口上方的墙上用粉笔画了账：总时长等于可堆人手的分钟数，加上我那不可堆人手的十五分钟。可加速的那段，姑且叫它削切部分，占去四分之三的活计；我的酱锅占剩下的四分之一，纹丝不动。于是削切部分一拖，整席就要足足一个钟头；哪怕它跑得再快，酱汁这刻钟没收完，菜就出不了这道窗口。

I drew the ledger in chalk above the pass: total time equaled the staffable minutes plus my unstaffable fifteen. The staffable part, call it the peel-and-chop, sat at three quarters of the work; my saucepan held the remaining quarter, immovable. So a banquet took a full hour when the peel-and-chop ran long, and even at its fastest the dish could never leave the line before that quarter-hour of sauce had finished.

东家嗅到了省钱的味。他把削皮的人手翻倍，宴席从一个钟头降到三十四分钟上下。再翻一倍——四倍人手——只又省下五分钟。他发狠了：八组，再十六组，往削切部分砸钱，直到那活计几乎眨眼即毕。厨房里刀子挤刀子，可宴席时长再也不往下掉。它摊平了，倔在那里，就贴在十五分钟上方一点。

The owner smelled savings. He doubled the peelers, and the banquet dropped from an hour to about thirty-four minutes. He doubled again—four times the hands—and we saved only another five. He went wild: eight crews, then sixteen, throwing coin at the peel-and-chop until it was nearly instantaneous. The kitchen bulged with knives, and the banquet time stopped falling. It flattened, stubborn, just above fifteen minutes.

我想跟他说清。每添一组人手，缩短的只是可堆人手的那一段——那可以提速的部分；我独自搅着酱汁的那四分之一，连一口气都不会少。他越是砸钱，每一枚新钱买到的越少。把削切部分推到无穷快，你仍要等着我，等着酱锅，等着这双不能翻倍的手。

I tried to tell him. Every crew I added shrank only the staffable fraction, the part that could be sped; the quarter I alone stirred did not shrink by a single breath. The more he spent, the less each new coin bought. Push the peel-and-chop to infinity and you still wait on me, on the saucepan, on the one hand that cannot be doubled.

两难在签契约那天咬住了他。他向行会许诺宴席只要旧时长的四分之一，凭那批削皮工，账面上——可加速的部分已经消失了。可酱汁仍要那刻钟，于是真正的上限不由他雇了多少双手决定，而由那唯一无法分摊的活计决定：我的活计。想再把总时长往下压，多买人手无用；要么改掉只有我能做的那道工序，要么就认下这道天花板。

The dilemma bit him at the contract signing. He had promised the guild banquets in a quarter of the old time, and he had the peelers to claim it—on paper the staffable part was gone. But the sauce still took its quarter-hour, and so the true limit was set not by how many hands he hired, but by the one task that could not be shared: mine. To shave the total further, he could not buy more labor; he would have to change what only I could do, or accept the ceiling.

他还是签了。那一季夜夜迟到，削皮工闲着，灶上只剩那口酱锅。我这才明白：一个系统的快慢，不取决于你能成倍堆上去的那一段，而取决于你堆不上去的那一段；在可加倍的那一半上花再多钱，也跑不过那必须独自搅动的一只手。

He signed anyway, and we were late every night that season, the peelers idle and the saucepan the only thing on the fire. I learned then that a system's speed is governed not by the part you can multiply, but by the part you cannot; and no fortune spent on the multiply-able half will ever outrun the one hand that must stir alone.

注解：这对应阿姆达尔定律（Amdahl's law）：对系统中某一部分进行优化所获得的整体性能提升，受限于该部分实际占用的时间比例；不可优化的串行部分——故事里那只能由一人搅动的酱锅——为整体加速封顶，故无论可加速部分被投入多少资源乃至无限，总时间都无法跌破那段串行部分所设的上限。

Note: This mirrors Amdahl's law (阿姆达尔定律): the speedup of a task gained by optimizing one part is bounded by the fraction of time that part is actually used; the unoptimizable serial portion—in the story, the single-handed saucepan—caps the whole, so that no matter how infinitely the staffable part is resourced, total time cannot fall below the limit set by that serial portion.
