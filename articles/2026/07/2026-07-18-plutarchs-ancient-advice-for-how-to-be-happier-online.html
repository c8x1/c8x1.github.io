普鲁塔克关于如何在网上更快乐的古老建议

Matthew Sharpe·The Conversation·2026年7月18日·4分钟阅读·原文

许多个世纪以来，查罗内亚的普鲁塔克（Plutarch of Chaeronea，约公元46—119年）的著作一直是西方精英人文教育的核心。我们可以看到，他那部《希腊罗马名人传》（Lives of Eminent Greeks and Romans）以及收在《道德论集》（Moralia）中的哲学随笔，对近代早期作家——从蒙田（Michel de Montaigne）、培根（Francis Bacon）到莎士比亚（William Shakespeare）——都产生了深远影响。

For many centuries, the writings of Plutarch of Chaeronia (c.46-119CE) were at the heart of elite humanistic education in the West. We can see the influence of his Lives of Eminent Greeks and Romans and the philosophical essays in his work Moralia in early modern authors from Michel de Montaigne and Francis Bacon to William Shakespeare.

普鲁塔克许多随笔也有着切乎实用的关注点，在我们这个社交媒体时代看来格外应景。他写到如何冷却搬弄是非、窥探他人生活的欲望，以及如何不被猎奇故事所蛊惑。

Many of Plutarch’s essays also have a practical focus that can seem very timely in our social media age. He writes about how to cool the desire to gossip and pry into other people’s lives and how not to be taken in by salacious stories.

他给出建议：如何不被他人的挑衅激怒，如何从分歧中受益；如何谈论自己而不自夸、不自卖自夸；如何分辨朋友与试图向我们兜售东西的骗子（或谄媚者）；以及如何分配注意力，使心智不被过载、不被打散。

He gives advice about how to avoid being triggered by others’ provocations and how to benefit from disagreement. He advises on how to speak about ourselves without boasting or self-advertisement, how to tell friends from conmen (or flatterers) trying to sell us something, and how to pay attention so our minds are not overloaded and scattered.

几乎让人觉得普鲁塔克是在窥视21世纪，回应着我们商业化社交媒体大规模制造出的种种心理健康挑战。

It almost seems as if Plutarch was peering into the 21st century and responding to the mental health challenges our commercialised social media is producing at scale.

然而正如记者麦克斯·费舍（Max Fisher）在《混乱机器》（The Chaos Machine）一书中所指出的，现实是：社交媒体公司正是看准了普鲁塔克希望帮读者矫正的那些心理弱点，把它们当作「最大化用户参与度」的有效手段。

The reality, as journalist Max Fisher outlines in his book The Chaos Machine, is that social media companies have identified the same psychological vulnerabilities Plutarch hoped to help readers to correct, recognising them as effective ways to “maximise user engagement”.

普鲁塔克属于古希腊罗马文化传统，在那里，哲学不被仅仅视为一种理论追求，而是与对幸福的追求紧密相连。这一传统远早于现代心理学的诞生；哲学家可以承担起治疗师的角色，就人生的种种难题建言献策。

Plutarch belongs to the ancient Greco-Roman culture in which philosophy was not conceived solely as a theoretical pursuit, but was linked to the pursuit of happiness. In this tradition, which long predates the advent of modern psychology, philosophers could take on the role of therapists, proffering advice on life’s challenges.

普鲁塔克在《道德论集》中所阐述的实践伦理学，提出了两条帮助人们活得更幸福、更宁静的路径。

Plutarch’s practical ethics in the Moralia propose two ways to help people to live happier, more serene lives.

其一是揭示培养坏习惯所带来的恶果，以此唤起羞耻感与改变的意愿。比如，那些总是拿自己的生活与他人比较的人，往往会陷入嫉妒和对自我的不满：

The first is to highlight the bad consequences of cultivating bad habits as a way of invoking shame and a desire to change. People who are constantly comparing their lives with others, for instance, are likely to suffer from envy and dissatisfaction with themselves:

「有些人不敢直面自己的生活，视之为最不堪入目的景象……而他们的灵魂，因充斥种种恶习，对内里的东西战栗惊惧，于是向外纵身跳出，逡巡于他人的事务之间，在那里攫取并养肥自己的恶意。」

there are some who cannot bear to face their own lives, regarding these as a most unlovely spectacle […] but their souls, being full of all manner of vices, shuddering and frightened at what is within, leap outwards and prowl about other people’s concerns and there latch onto and fatten their own malice.

即便没有算法的推波助澜，热衷窥探他人隐私的传舌者，也往往会被那些关于他人关起门来做些什么的耸动、夸大的臆测所蛊惑：

Even without algorithmic assistance, the gossip who pries into others’ supposed secrets is likely to be taken in by the most sensational, exaggerated speculations about what others are doing behind closed doors:

「正如密闭在罐中的麦子，量虽增多，质却变劣；一则故事一旦传到搬弄是非者口中，便会生出大量的虚假添加，从而毁掉它本来的可信度。」

For as wheat shut up in a jar is found to have increased in quantity, but to have deteriorated in quality, so when a story finds its way to a gossip, it generates a large addition of falsehood and thereby destroys its credit.

沉湎于种种积怨的缘由——正如算法驱动的社交媒体在今天所助长的——会养成「一种灵魂的恶劣性情，人们称之为易怒」。日复一日吞咽愤慨，会让人「对琐事动辄酸痛、对日常小事动辄怨怼，就像被打得太薄太细的铁片一样」。

The habit of dwelling on causes of resentment, as algorithmic social media can promote today, produces “an evil disposition of soul, which people call irascibility”. A daily diet of outrage make a person “become sore and vexed at trifles and querulous at everyday occurrences, like iron thin and beaten out too finely”.

普鲁塔克的第二条建议，是为人们开列我们今天会称之为认知行为练习的方案。这些方案从细微处入手，旨在逐步养成新的、有益的习惯，以取代旧习。

Plutarch’s second suggestion is prescribing regimens of what we would call cognitive-behavioural exercises. These regimens start small and aim to build up new, beneficent habits in place of the old.

比如，那些总想打听与己无关之事的好事者，应先从这类小事练起：经过公共墙垣时，不去读墙上每一处涂鸦（罗马世界有大量这样的涂鸦，正如我们内城街头一般）。

Busybodies who want to know about things that don’t concern them, for instance, should first practice things like not reading every piece of graffiti on public walls they pass (the Roman world had plenty of such graffiti, as our inner cities do).

读这些涂鸦似乎不会有什么害处，但它确实在伤害你——在不知不觉中给你灌输「去搜寻与自己无关之事」的习惯。

It may seem that no harm will come from reading these, but it does harm you by imperceptibly instilling the practice of searching out matters which do not concern you.

接着，他们应练习走过街边每一扇门时不再探头张望；然后再练习收到每一封信时不立刻拆开。就这样，通过这样一些小小的步骤，他们将养成一种新习惯，并由此把注意力重新收回到自己的事务上。

Then they should practice not looking in at every door they pass on the street. They should then practice not opening every letter they receive straight away. Gradually, through such small steps, they will build a new habit, in doing so winning attention back to their own affairs.

今天，我们可以这样调整普鲁塔克的建议：关掉手机和电脑上的通知提醒；在立刻回应之前先停一停；随后为收发邮件或查看应用设定固定时段；并练习在按下「回复」、遑论「全部回复」之前，仔细阅读、深呼吸。

Today, we can adapt Plutarch’s advice by turning off the alerts on our phones and computers. We can pause before we respond straight away. We can then set times to do emails or check on our apps. And we can practice reading carefully and breathing deeply before hitting “reply”, let alone “reply all”.

这不会一蹴而就。但持续的、协同的重复，能让人逐步重新掌控自己如何、何时去接触在线内容与那些「社交」，而不至于本末倒置、被其牵着鼻子走。

It won’t happen overnight. But concerted repetition can gradually allow a person to regain control over how they engage and when, with online content and “socials”, rather than the tail wagging the dog.

正如古时的斯多葛派（Stoics）与伊壁鸠鲁派（Epicureans）一样，普鲁塔克主张：最幸福的生活，是一种内心宁静、对他人愉悦的生活。幸福的人珍视自己真正看重的东西，品味自己所拥有的，而不是被拽向百十个不同方向，被「错失恐惧」（FOMO，fear of missing out）所折磨。

Like the ancient Stoics and Epicureans, Plutarch argued the happiest life is one of inner serenity and cheerfulness towards others. The happy person prioritises what they really value and savours what they have, rather than being pulled in a hundred different directions and wracked by FOMO (fear of missing out).

不幸福的首要缘由，是过度的自爱。普鲁塔克写道，这种自爱「使人们事事争第一、求必胜，并贪得无厌地想要插手一切」。它让人想要「富有、博学、强壮……并成为国王与城邦官吏的座上宾」。

The chief cause of unhappiness is excessive self-love. This, writes Plutarch, “makes men eager to be first and to be victorious in everything and insatiably desirous of engaging in everything”. It makes people want to be “rich, learned, strong, […] and friends of kings and magistrates of cities.”

在今天，它驱使人们去竞争赢取成千上万永远不会谋面的「关注者」，并无休止地拿自己去和他人精心修饰过的网络形象作比较。

Today, it drives people to compete to win thousands of “followers” they’ll never meet and compare themselves endlessly with others’ artfully curated online profiles.

从普鲁塔克的视角看，广告就是「制造不满的机器」：它在我们心中填满对各种并非真正需要、也无法长久满足我们之物的欲望。他观察到，「我们并没有像水手驾驭风帆那样，依照自己的能力来驾驭自己的冲动；在期望上，我们总是瞄准太高之物」。

Advertisements are dissatisfaction machines, from a Plutarchean perspective: they fill us with desires for things we do not always need and which cannot lastingly satisfy us. He observes that “we do not manage our impulses, as sailors do their sails, to correspond to our capacity; in our expectations we aim at things too great”.

幸福的人专注于自己能掌控之事。他们只与境况不如自己的人相比，以此提醒自己为所拥有之物心存感激。

Happy people focus on what they can control. They compare themselves only with people who are worse off, to remind themselves to be grateful for what they have.

社交媒体却不断促使我们拿自己去和那些呈现着不真实、光鲜亮丽生活形象的人作比较。点赞、分享、关注、浏览量、引用量这些被量化的指标，就是为了让人心生嫉妒。

Social media prompts us constantly to compare ourselves with others who present unrealistic, glamorous images of their lives. Quantified metrics of likes, shares, followers, views and citations are there to fill people with envy.

这些指标诱导用户更多地参与，使他们对他人认可的依赖日益加深：「尽管如此，我们却出于愚蠢，惯于把注意力放在他人身上，而非自己身上。」

These metrics prompt users to engage more, making themselves ever more dependent on others’ approval: “despite all this, we habitually live, out of stupidity, with our attention on others rather than ourselves.”

普鲁塔克今天给我们的忠告，不会是把设备关掉。鉴于工作与休闲方式的改变，这对多数人而言在社会层面已不可能。

Plutarch’s counsel for us today would not be to turn our devices off. This is not socially possible for most people, given the changed nature of work and leisure.

然而，美国近期的法律裁决，以及对社交媒体使用影响——尤其是对年轻人影响——日益增多的研究，都凸显了一点：算法驱动的社交媒体并不促进、甚至可能对个体的福祉产生负面影响。

Yet recent legal findings in the US, as well as growing research on the effects of social media use, especially on young people, underscore that algorithm-driven social media does not promote, and can negatively effect, individual wellbeing.

它也对自由民主政体起着撕裂作用，煽动着彼此的猜疑与仇恨。

It is also has a divisive effect on liberal democracies, stoking mutual suspicion and hatreds.

普鲁塔克针对我们这个彼此相连、商业化的世界所煽起的不安之源所开出的哲学疗法，归结起来都指向「认识你自己」。值得注意的是，这些疗法洞察到：我们天生的自爱，是如何被谄媚者所利用——在今天，则是被那些「投喂」我们的「点赞」与我们的愤慨的算法所利用。

Plutarch’s philosophical therapies for the sources of our unease which have been stoked by our interconnected, commercialised world all turn upon knowing ourselves. Notably, they recognise the ways our natural egotism can be played to by flatterers – or, today, by algorithms that “feed” our “likes” and our outrage.

意识到平台（算法式的谄媚者）被设计成何种模样，以助长社交媒体成瘾、并迎合我们的欲望与恐惧，能让我们更有意识地使用手中的设备，更好地管理那日益淹没我们所有人的信息洪流。

Becoming conscious of the ways platforms (algorithmic flatterers), are designed to foster social media addiction and play to our desires and fears, allows us to become more deliberate about how we use our devices and better manage the information that increasingly floods us all.
