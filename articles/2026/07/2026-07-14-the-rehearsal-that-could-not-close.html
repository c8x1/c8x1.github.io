无法收尾的排演

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年7月14日·2分钟阅读

在街区尽头那间逼仄的轮演剧团里，导演莲给这一季定下一条铁律：剧本里每一场对手戏都必须恰好排演一次，在一场不间断的排演（rehearsal）中走完，每场戏借共演的演员衔接到下一场。两个演员之间的一场对手戏是一条纽带；每个演员所触及的纽带数目，便是他站立的交汇处。剧团负担不起两场排演——戏台只租了一个晚上，拆成两场就意味着押金与整个演出季一同落空。

In a cramped repertory house at the edge of the district, the director Lian had one iron rule for the season: every paired scene in the playbook had to be rehearsed exactly once, in one unbroken sitting, each scene flowing into the next through the actor they shared. A scene between two actors was a bond; each actor stood at the meeting of however many bonds touched them. The company could not afford two sittings—the hall was rented for a single evening, and a split rehearsal meant forfeiting the deposit and the season itself.

莲两种演出季都见过。当纽带结成整齐的环——每个演员所触的场景数都是偶数——排演可从任意一人开场，又在落幕时回到那人，最后一场戏交还给开场者。那是人人艳羡的一种：排演如环闭合，没有人被剩下攥着未完的纽带。但当恰好有两个演员各背着奇数场戏时，规矩便更严：排演必须从这两个奇担者之一开场，在另一人身上收尾。他们才是仅有的合法首尾；若从别处开场，便注定要留下一场无人承接的悬空戏。

Lian had seen both kinds of season. When the bonds formed neat loops—every actor touched by an even number of scenes—the sitting could begin with any performer and return to that same performer at the close, the last scene handing back to the first. That was the coveted kind: the rehearsal closed like a ring, and no one was left stranded holding an unfinished bond. But when exactly two actors carried an odd number of scenes each, the rule turned sterner: the sitting had to open with one of those two odd-burdened actors and end on the other. They were the only permissible bookends; to start elsewhere was to guarantee a dangling scene with no one to receive it.

这一季，莲翻完剧本，血色从脸上褪尽。四个演员——莫、萨、特、维——各背三场戏，三是奇数，而奇数便是奇担。其余演员负担均等。依那从未令她失望的规矩，单场排演此刻已不可能：奇担者总是成对出现，四个奇担意味着凭空多出两条无法消解的悬空线。无论开场选谁，无论排序如何巧妙，都无法把四个奇端压进单场排演所容许的两个。

This season, Lian read the playbook and the blood left her face. Four actors—Mok, Sar, Te, and Vir—each carried three scenes, and three is odd. The rest were evenly burdened. By the rule that had never failed her, a single sitting was now impossible: odd-burdened actors always come in pairs, and four of them meant two irreducible loose ends too many. No choice of opening, no cleverness in ordering, could collapse four odd ends into the two a single sitting allows.

代价便在此处。戏台老板不肯延租，剧团拿不出第二晚的租金，而那位资助整季的来访恩主早有约定：要么一场不间断的排演，要么免谈。莲大可删去一场戏，把四个奇端压回两个，可她每删一场，删的正是恩主亲手写进剧本的戏；动刀即是得罪供养之人。她也可保留全部戏份、接受两场排演，但第二场排演意味着押金与戏台一并落空，剧团赖以活命的演出季也随之葬送。

Here was the cost. The landlord would not extend the hall, the company had no second evening's rent, and the visiting patron—who funded the whole season—had stipulated one unbroken rehearsal or nothing. Lian could strike one scene to collapse the four odd ends down to two, but every scene she struck was a scene the patron had himself written into the playbook; to cut was to insult the hand that fed them. Or she could keep every scene and accept two sittings, but a second sitting meant losing the deposit and the hall, and with it the season the company had staked its survival on.

莲捧着剧本与算术枯坐了一个钟头，竭力凭空生出第三条路。没有。她终于明白：这不可能并非想象或努力不及，而是结构性的，早在任何抉择之前，就已写定在纽带与演员相遇的方式里。四个奇担无从说降。于是她起身，依纽带所容许的唯一诚实之举而行——删去特与维之间的一场戏，使这二人从奇担转为偶担，恰留下莫、萨两个奇担者作为必经的首尾；排演将以莫开场、以萨收尾，每一场幸存的戏都恰好走过一次。

For an hour Lian sat with the playbook and the arithmetic, willing a third way into existence. There was none, and she understood at last that the impossibility was not a failure of imagination or effort: it was structural, written into how the bonds met the actors before any choice was made. The four odd burdens would not be argued down. So she rose and made the only honest call the bonds permitted—she struck a single scene between Te and Vir, converting those two from odd to even and leaving exactly two odd-burdened actors, Mok and Sar, as the mandatory bookends; the rehearsal would open with Mok and close with Sar, every surviving scene walked exactly once.

恩主到场，瞥见那唯一缺失的一场，面露不悦；可当莲把四个奇担与他所给定的单一晚上并置给他看，他说不出哪一场排演能做得更好——因为并不存在。排演依规矩在萨身上收尾，凡能成环之处皆成了环，整季凭一场删戏与两个诚实首尾得以存活。莲所做的并非战胜纽带，而是敬重纽带身上那唯一无从争辩之物：它们的数目。

The patron arrived, saw the single missing scene, and was displeased; but when Lian showed him the four odd burdens and the single evening he had granted, he could not name a sitting that would have done better, for none existed. The rehearsal closed on Sar as the rule required, the ring held everywhere it could, and the season survived by one struck scene and two honest bookends. What Lian had done was not to defeat the bonds but to honor the one thing they could not be argued out of: their count.

注解：这是欧拉路径（Euler paths）的寓言——一条遍历每条边恰好一次的通路。其存在与起讫完全由顶点度数的奇偶性决定：奇度顶点为0时可成回路、从任一点回到自身；恰为2时路径必从一个奇度顶点出发、到另一个结束；而若像本剧四个演员那样全为奇度（四个奇端），则单条路径无解，须删边把奇端压回两个，或接受多笔。

Annotation: This is a parable of Euler paths—a trail that traverses every edge exactly once. Whether such a path exists, and where it must begin and end, is decided entirely by the parity of vertex degrees: with zero odd-degree vertices it closes as a circuit, returning from any vertex to itself; with exactly two it must run from one odd-degree vertex to the other; and when, as with the play's four actors, all are of odd degree (four odd ends), no single path is possible—one must strike an edge to bring the odd ends back down to two, or else accept multiple traversals.
