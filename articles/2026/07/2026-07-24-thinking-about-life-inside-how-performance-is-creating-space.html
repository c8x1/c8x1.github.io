思考内在生活：表演如何为讨论犯罪与惩罚开辟空间

Sarah Bartley and Saul Hewish·The Conversation·2026年7月24日·4分钟阅读·原文

监狱哲学家的非正式角色，由世界各地被监禁的人承担起来。手中有时间，又与同伴、控制系统及道德叙事近距离相处，狱中之人有条件实践哲学。

The informal role of prison philosopher is taken up by incarcerated people across the world. With time on their hands and a proximity to other people, systems of control and narratives of morality, people in prison are well positioned to practice philosophy.

这正是丹尼斯·凯利（Dennis Kelly）六集 BBC 电视剧《等待出路》（Waiting for the Out）今年早些时候发行时所覆盖的领域。该剧改编自安迪·韦斯特（Andy West）的回忆录《内在的生活》（The Life Inside, 2022），书中讲述他在监狱情境中教授哲学的经历，并展开了他自身与犯罪化之间切身关联的经历——他的父亲、叔叔和兄弟都曾入狱。

This is the terrain that Dennis Kelly’s six-part BBC television drama Waiting for the Out, released earlier this year, covered. The series is an adaptation of Andy West’s memoir The Life Inside (2022), which shares his experiences of teaching philosophy in prison contexts and unfolds his own proximity to criminalisation through his father, uncle and brother, who all spent time in prison.

我们是剧场创作者，经常与生活或工作于刑事司法系统之内的人共同创作。索尔（Saul）在艺术组织 Rideout Creative Arts for Rehabilitation 工作，萨拉（Sarah）则是皇家中央演讲与戏剧学院（Royal Central School of Speech and Drama）的研究者与从业者。我们最新的合作探讨法国哲学家米歇尔·福柯（Michel Foucault）1975 年影响深远的著作《规训与惩罚》（Discipline and Punish）在今天的共鸣与价值。

We are theatre-makers who regularly co-create work with people living or working inside our criminal justice system. Saul with arts organisation Rideout Creative Arts for Rehabilitation and Sarah as a researcher and practitioner based at Royal Central School of Speech and Drama. Our latest collaboration explores the resonances and utility of French philosopher Michel Foucault’s influential 1975 work Discipline and Punish today.

福柯这本书是大多数社会科学本科课程的核心读物，他论证自 18 世纪后期以来，惩罚已蜕变为规训，一种更为弥散的监视与社会控制形式。

Foucault’s book, which is core reading on most social sciences undergraduate curriculums, argues that since the late 18th century, punishment has morphed into discipline, a more diffuse form of surveillance and social control.

我们渴望探究这本书对今天有刑事法律制度亲身经历的人是否仍有意义，并理解他们反过来可能为福柯的著作带来何种新洞见。我们也从更晚近的写作中汲取灵感，收获最丰的是恩里克·卡瓦略（Henrique Carvalho）与阿纳斯塔西娅·钱伯伦（Anastasia Chamberlen）对当代惩罚实践所做的细腻审视之作《追问惩罚》（Questioning Punishment, 2023）。

We were keen to investigate whether the book held meaning for people with lived experience of our criminal legal system today and understand what new insights they, in turn, might bring to Foucault’s work. We also drew inspiration from more contemporary writing, most fruitfully Henrique Carvalho and Anastasia Chamberlen’s nuanced interrogation of contemporary practices of punishment, Questioning Punishment (2023).

2025 年春，在最初的十次戏剧工作坊中，我们与「专家公民」（Expert Citizens）的成员合作——这是一个由有多重困境亲身经历者领导、并为其服务的组织。我们共同探索权力、惩罚性机构与监视。

In spring of 2025, across ten initial drama workshops, we collaborated with members of Expert Citizens, an organisation led by and for people with lived experience of multiple disadvantage. We collectively explored power, punishing institutions and surveillance.

随后我们组建了一个由四名「专家公民」成员和六位剧场创作者构成的剧团。在为期四周的高强度编创与排练期间，他们彼此分享创作、亲身经历与理论方面的知识。

We went on to create an ensemble comprised of four Expert Citizens members and six theatre-makers. They shared creative, lived and theoretical knowledge with one another across a four-week intensive devising and rehearsal period.

最终，剧团创作出《惩罚行为：报应、补偿与救赎的故事》（Punishment Acts: Tales of Retribution, Reparation and Redemption），一部 80 分钟的演出，在特伦特河畔斯托克（Stoke-on-Trent）的 B Arts 剧场上演了七个夜晚。

Ultimately the ensemble created Punishment Acts: Tales of Retribution, Reparation and Redemption, an 80-minute production that was staged over seven nights at the B Arts theatre in Stoke on Trent.

故事设定在深夜的一间牢房，介于辗转难眠与清醒之间，牢房的居住者穿行于不同的惩罚历史与空间。借助木偶、寓言与肢体动作的拼贴，演出请观众反思关于惩罚的信念，思考是否还存在其他实现正义的方式。

Set in a prison cell late at night, between restless sleep and wakefulness, the cell’s occupants traverse different histories and spaces of punishment. Through a collage of puppetry, fable and movement, the show asked audiences to reflect on beliefs about punishment and consider whether there are other ways to achieve justice.

《惩罚行为》置身于一片日益热衷于与观众开启对话的剧场创作之中，探讨我们所希望的司法制度应当呈现何种面貌与感受。

Punishment Acts sits in a landscape of theatre work that is increasingly interested in opening up conversations with audiences about what we want our justice system to look and feel like.

詹姆斯·格雷厄姆（James Graham）2025 年的话剧《一拳》（Punch）取材自雅各布·邓恩（Jacob Dunne）的回忆录《是非之辨》（Right from Wrong）。邓恩的书记录了他被定罪过失杀人的经历，以及将他与被他杀害的詹姆斯·霍奇金森（James Hodgkinson）的父母带到一起的恢复性司法过程，使三人得以开始重建各自的生活。《一拳》为更广泛的观众呈现了恢复性司法作为回应伤害的另一种框架的可能性与流程，最初在诺丁汉剧院（Nottingham Playhouse）上演，随后走向伦敦西区（West End）和百老汇（Broadway）。

James Graham’s 2025 play Punch drew on Jacob Dunne’s memoir, Right from Wrong. Dunne’s book documents his conviction of manslaughter and the restorative justice process that brought him together with the parents of James Hodgkinson, the man he killed, so all three could begin to rebuild their lives. Punch staged the possibilities and processes of restorative justice as an alternative framework for responding to harm for a wider audience, initially at Nottingham Playhouse and then on to the West End and Broadway.

与此同时，Synergy 剧团近期上演的埃文·普莱斯（Evan Placey）作品《终身囚犯》（Lifers）则邀请观众思考我们应如何回应老龄化的监狱人口，以及英国监禁中普遍存在的严酷与去人性化条件。让观众关注到我们迅速变化的监狱人口，并邀请他们追问这对监狱的建筑、人员配备与内部状况意味着什么，证明剧场有能力为关于司法的细腻而人道的反思留出空间。

Synergy Theatre Company’s recent production of Evan Placey’s Lifers, meanwhile, invited audiences to consider how we respond to the ageing prison population and the broader harsh and dehumanising conditions of imprisonment in Britain. Bringing audiences attention to our rapidly shifting prison population and inviting them to ask what that might mean for the architecture, staffing and conditions within our prisons evidences the capacity of theatre to hold space for nuanced and humane reflections on justice.

与此同时，Lung 剧团正在巡演《伍德希尔》（Woodhill），这是一部精心编排的纪实作品，呈现了 HMP Woodhill 监狱的严重失职如何导致了对被关押男性的灾难性照护失灵。克里斯·卡彭特（Chris Carpenter）、斯蒂芬·法拉（Stephen Farrar）和凯文·斯卡利特（Kevin Scarlett）都曾被关押于该监狱，2013 至 2018 年间先后被发现死于各自牢房。演出建立在 70 次访谈之上，是对我们这个失灵监狱系统的一份灼人控诉。

Meanwhile Lung theatre company are currently touring Woodhill, a beautifully choreographed documentary piece that stages the acute failings at HMP Woodhill which led to catastrophic failures in care of the men held there. Chris Carpenter, Stephen Farrar and Kevin Scarlett were all held at the prison and each found dead in their cells between 2013 and 2018. Built on 70 interviews, the performance is a searing indictment of our failing prison system.

剧场一直是一种邀请观众重新审视我们与刑事司法系统关系的实践。《惩罚行为》的独特之处在于明确地将哲学、亲身经历与社区表演融为一体。

Theatre has long been a practice that invites audiences to (re)examine our relationship to the criminal justice system. What was distinctive about Punishment Acts is the explicit integration of philosophy, lived experience and community performance.

福柯的观念变得不再那么抽象，因为我们的混合演员阵容能够将其与英国当代监狱、缓刑与支持服务联系起来加以运用与拓展。

Foucault’s ideas became less abstract through the ways our mixed cast could engage with and expand them in relation to contemporary prisons, probation and support services in Britain.

《惩罚行为》并非自传性作品，而是借助虚构框架，在演员自身故事与剧中角色之间拉开一定距离。但正如一位「专家公民」导师所言，他们构思出的那个角色凝结了他们在自身经历中感受到的挫败。

Punishment Acts was not autobiographical and used a fictional frame to enable some distance between cast members’ own stories and the characters in the play. But as one of the Expert Citizens mentors noted, the character they came up with embodied the frustrations they’d experienced in their own journey.

这一点对项目至关重要，也凸显了亲身经历群体如何重新激活哲学并为其提供有用的视角，而非学习只朝一个方向流动。

This was integral to the project and underscored how lived experience groups reanimate and offer useful perspectives on philosophy rather than the learning only going one way.

例如，剧团编创了一幕发生在旅馆的场景，将福柯关于机构如何组织空间与时间以实施控制的论述，与他们自己在旅馆中遭受惩罚性对待的经历融合在一起。

For example, the ensemble devised a scene in a hostel that blended Foucault’s discussion of how institutions organise space and time to enact control with their own experiences of punitive treatment in hostels.

剧中，梅利莎（Melissa）来到一所「核准住所」（Approved Premises）寻求支持，却遭遇一长串僵硬且愈发惩罚性的规则——从洗衣机的使用到公共热水壶的可用时段。这份清单长到令梅利莎赴缓刑约见时迟到。当梅利莎终于见到那位资源不足、超负荷工作的缓刑官时，又有一套规则代替支持被施加于她。最终，她因迟到这一违规行为被送回监狱，重新陷入同一循环。

In the play, the character of Melissa arrives at an Approved Premises looking for support, but is met with a long list of rigid and increasingly punitive rules from the use of the washing machine to the working hours of the communal kettle. The list is in fact so long it makes Melissa late for a probation appointment. When Melissa eventually meets her probation officer, an under resourced and overworked figure, another set of rules is deployed in lieu of support. Ultimately, her infringement of lateness lands her back in prison, back into the same cycle.

梅利莎的经历借鉴了福柯关于惩罚远超监狱大门之外的主张，也呼应了剧团成员的切身经历。他们也认识到，在这些情境（住房、司法、就业）中工作的人同样被常常无法使其真正支持他人的制度所辜负。

Melissa’s journey drew on Foucault’s proposal that punishment expands far beyond the prison gate and echoed the experiences of our ensemble. They also recognised that those working in these contexts (housing, justice, employment) are also being let down by systems that so often do not enable them to meaningfully support people.

主流现场演出、电视剧和电影中探讨正义、伤害与惩罚观念的作品日益增多，值得欢迎。它标志着来自广泛人群的一种日益增长的胃口，去审视我们刑事法律制度的价值观、历史与哲学。

The expansion of mainstream live performance, TV series and films addressing ideas around justice, harm, and punishment is to be welcomed. It marks an increased appetite from a broad range of people to examine the values, histories and philosophies of our criminal legal system.

在舞台上与屏幕上此类再现日益增多的同时，支持扎根社区且基于研究的表演项目至关重要。此类工作将拥有不同技能与经历的人汇聚在一起，突出那些曾与司法系统接触之人的知识，并使我们得以共同探索那些塑造我们惩罚方式的基本观念。

Amid this growth in representation on our stages and screens, it’s vital to support community-rooted and research-informed performance projects. Such work brings people with different skills and experiences together, it foregrounds the knowledge of those who have had contact with our justice system, and enables us to collectively explore the fundamental ideas that inform our approach to punishment.
