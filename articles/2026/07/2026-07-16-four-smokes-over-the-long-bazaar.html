长集市的四种烟火

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年7月16日·2分钟阅读

长集市是一条有顶的长廊，被隔成一个个摊位，沿两侧墙根一户紧挨一户，人走完整条廊都不必踏出檐荫。每户摊位与左右邻户沿一整段木板相接，而非只在角柱上碰一点；只共一根角柱的两户，行会视作不相识。集市的掌事霍尔姆立了一条规矩，管各户黄昏挂出的灯笼：凡沿一段墙相接的两户，必须挂不同烟色的灯笼，好让路人凭烟霭之色辨出一种手艺在何处收尾、下一种从何处起头。

The Long Bazaar was a single covered passage cut into booths, one stall hard against the next along both walls, so that a man could walk its length without ever leaving the shade of a roof. Each stall touched its neighbors along a full edge of planking, not merely at a corner post; where two stalls met only at a post, the guild reckoned them strangers. Master Holm, the bazaar's steward, had one law for the lanterns each stall hung at dusk: any two stalls sharing an edge of wall must hang lanterns of different smoke, so that a wanderer could tell, by the color of the haze, where one craft ended and the next began.

烟色只有四种，都出自同一批老烛匠之手：松、琥珀、丁香、盐。霍尔姆的前任们试过只用三种，集市便败了三次；织工有时被夹在染工与脂烛匠之间，三面墙同时相接，三种烟色怎么也凑不齐三段互异的边。于是定下四种，霍尔姆深信：无论摊位如何切了又切，长廊逼仄的格局所能造出的任何排布，四种烟色都敷得过来。

For smoke the bazaar had only four kinds, cured by the same old chandlers: pine, amber, clove, and salt. Holm's predecessors had tried to get by on three, and three times the bazaar had failed them; the weavers would find themselves boxed between the dyers and the tallowmen along three walls at once, and no third smoke could keep all three edges apart. So four was settled on, and Holm believed four enough for any arrangement the bazaar's narrow geometry could ever throw up, however the stalls were split and resplit.

麻烦出在脂烛匠行会裂成两半的那一季。一派与掌事翻了脸，不愿挨着旧对头坐，便租下廊深处一处摊位，两户夹在制绳匠之间。旧规矩对这种情形一向不置一词。脂烛匠的管事来见霍尔姆，要求他那两户摊位虽隔着三十户之远，却须挂同一色烟火，好让寻蜡的买主顺着一种颜色走到底。霍尔姆一眼看清了窘境的形状：若远处的两户须同色，夹在中间的那段廊便不再是自由之地，四种烟火未必还能跨过去铺满。

The trouble came the season the tallowmen's guild split in two. A faction quarreled with the master and, rather than sit adjoining their old rivals, leased a booth far down the passage, two stalls wedged between the ropemakers. The law had always been silent on such a case. The tallowmen's steward came to Holm with a demand that his two booths, though separated by thirty stalls, hang the same smoke, so that a buyer seeking wax might follow one color end to end. Holm saw at once the shape of the bind: if the distant booths must match, the gap between them was no longer free ground, and four smokes might not stretch across it.

点破代价的是霍尔姆自己的副手，一个叫瑞恩的酸刻女人，三十年来每一场争执她都站到了最后。若两户脂烛摊须同色，夹在中间的摊位便连成一条同受约束的边链，那条链上每隔四户便撞回同一种需要。瑞恩在沙盘上摆集市摆了三十个赶集日，她亲眼见过这路子崩盘：廊两端若有一对须同色的摊位，就能在正中某处，把两户沿墙相接的摊位逼成同一种烟火。这里要抉择的不是四种与五种之间，而是旧规矩与脂烛匠的新要求之间。

It was Holm's own deputy, a sour woman named Wren who had stood through every quarrel for thirty years, who named the cost. If the two tallow booths must share a smoke, then the stalls pinched between them formed a chain of edges all under the same constraint, and in that chain every fourth stall fell back against the same need. Wren had seen the pattern fail on the sand tray where she laid out the bazaar each market day: a single matched pair at the far ends of the lane could force, somewhere in the middle, two edge-sharing stalls into the same smoke. The choice was not between four smokes and five; it was between the old law and the tallowmen's new demand.

霍尔姆权衡了一整夜。答应这要求，等于承认一户的烟色不仅系于廊中邻户，还系于廊那头与它配对的那一户，而四种烟色的保证，只对那些在地面边界不曾被截断的摊位才成立；拒绝，则可保全四种烟色的旧规，代价是告诉一个手艺人：在他自己集市的另一头，他认不得自家手艺的灯笼。天刚亮，他便亲自下廊，一户一户地沿墙用粉笔标边，回来时只能说出一句：凡摊位完整、只沿真实墙段相触之处，四种烟火仍敷得过来。

Holm weighed it through the night. To grant the demand was to admit that a booth's smoke meant not just its neighbor in the lane but its twin across the bazaar, and that the guarantee of four smokes held only for stalls whose borders lay unbroken on the ground. To refuse was to keep the four-smoke law whole, at the price of telling a man he could not recognize his own craft's lantern at the far end of his own market. At first light he went down the passage chalking the edges himself, booth by booth, and came back able only to say that the four smokes still sufficed where stalls were whole and touched only along their true walls.

他裁定旧规照旧：四种烟火，沿墙相接者不得同色；凡一户的烟色只指自家地面而言，此规对长廊的任何切分皆成立。脂烛匠那两户远摊各自按邻户之需挂色，要买蜡的买主开口问就是了。集市沿用四种烟火，霍尔姆在账簿上记下：此规之证，不靠任何人的说理，而靠长廊曾有过的每一种摊位排布那份漫长而耐心的目录，在沙盘上一一摆出，一种接一种，直到再没有哪一种能推翻它。

He ruled that the law stood as it had: four smokes, no two edge-sharing stalls alike, and that this held for any division of the passage so long as one stall's smoke named only its own ground. The tallowmen's distant booths would each hang as their neighbors required, and the buyer who wanted wax would simply ask. The bazaar kept four smokes, and Holm noted in the ledger that the rule was proven not by any man's argument but by the long, patient catalog of every stall arrangement the bazaar had ever held, drawn out on the sand tray one configuration after another until none was left to break it.

注解：此即四色定理（the four-color theorem）：任何平面地图的区域都可用至多四种颜色着色，使任意两个相邻区域颜色不同，相邻指共享一段边界而非仅共一点；此保证对寻常区域成立，但若要求同一国家的飞地同色，则可能需第五种颜色，而该定理最初是靠穷举大量可归约配置完成证明的。

Note: This is the four-color theorem (四色定理): any planar map can be colored with at most four colors so that no two adjacent regions share a color, where adjacency means sharing a boundary edge rather than a single point; the guarantee holds for ordinary regions, but if a country's separated exclaves must share one color, a fifth may be required, and the theorem was first proved by exhaustively enumerating reducible configurations.
