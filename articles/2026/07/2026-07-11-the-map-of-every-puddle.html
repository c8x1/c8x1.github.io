尽记水洼之图

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年7月11日·3分钟阅读

年轻的渡子塔伦在泣礁海上漂了三天三夜，怀中紧紧攥着一张密到行间再无一寸留白的羊皮海图。他在岛屿与港湾之间所有练过的航路上，每一道流、每一处浅滩、每一块礁石都标得清清楚楚，连每次潮到浮标旁那道 foam 卷起的弧度都一一记下。这图，用他自己的话说，是无可挑剔的：把羊皮铺在任何一段练过的水面上，没有一笔会对不上。可眼下，到了他从未驶过的水域，这图却给不出半点有用的指引；他认得的标记一概不见，而那几处勉强相似的轮廓，一次又一次把他引上暗礁棱角，而那棱角是港湾航路上从来不曾有过的。这张在航道上从未让他失手的图，正把他溺死在开阔海面上。

The young ferryman Taren drifted for three days and nights on the Sea of Weeping Reefs, clutching a chart so dense with ink that no white remained between its lines. Every current, every shallows, every rock he had ever threaded on the practice runs between the isle and the harbor was marked there, down to the precise curl of foam at each buoy on each tide. The chart was, by his own boast, perfect: he could lay the vellum over any of those waters and not a stroke would be out of place. Yet here, on water he had never crossed, the chart told him nothing useful; every mark he recognized was absent, and the few shapes that almost matched led him, again and again, onto spurs of rock that the harbor crossings had never possessed. The chart that had never once failed him in the channel was drowning him in the open sea.

他绘图，并非出于虚荣。他绘图，是出于对那块夺走他父亲性命的礁石的恐惧，而绘图逐年变成了一种近乎虔诚的事：每有新航路交到他手里学习，他便把这航路上的每一处怪癖都添进羊皮，直到那怪癖本身成了功课。春潮时朝东倾两指宽的浮标，成了固定的地标；每月初三黄昏必至的那阵风，成了海上的律令。图把所见复刻得越精确，他心里越踏实；心里越踏实，他便把所见复刻得越精确。他并非有意如此。他只是从未被人教过第二种绘图的方法，而港主对他笔下那一丝不苟，向来只有嘉许。

He had not drawn the chart in vanity. He had drawn it in terror of the reef that killed his father, and the drawing had become, year by year, a kind of devotion: each new passage he was given to learn, he added its every quirk to the vellum until the quirk itself was the lesson. A buoy that leaned two fingers eastward in spring tide became a fixed landmark; a gust that came at dusk on the third day of the month became a rule of the sea. The more exactly the chart reproduced what he had seen, the safer he felt, and the safer he felt, the more exactly he reproduced it. He did not choose this. He had simply never been shown any other way to make a chart, and the harbor-master had only ever praised the precision of his hand.

港主临行前还曾给过他一句本该让他止步的告诫：越过泣礁的那条路，不是航道，航道也不是大海。可塔伦那天清晨登船时，把图揣进衣襟，恰恰是因为他深信航道与大海是同一回事，由同样的水与同样的礁构成，一张对航道为真的图，对大海也当为真。两者并不相同。航道里满是出了航道便毫无意义的怪癖；而大海这边，自有真正的潮汐，是航道里的偶然，把他与这些真潮汐隔开了。恰恰是让海图在港湾中无可挑剔的那些细节，到了开阔水面，竟无一毫用武之地。

The harbor-master had even sent him out with a warning that should have stopped him: the route beyond the Weeping Reefs, the master said, is not the channel, and the channel is not the sea. But Taren, stepping aboard that morning, had folded the chart into his coat precisely because he believed the channel and the sea were the same thing, made of the same kinds of water and the same kinds of rock, and that a chart true to one would be true to the other. The two were not the same. The channel was full of quirks that meant nothing beyond it; and the sea, for its part, held true tides that the channel's accidents had hidden from him. The very details that made the chart flawless in harbor were the details that had no purchase on open water.

如今，到第三夜，图已湿透，手指被那图上全无标记的礁石割破，塔伦才看清了自己失败的根性，而这根性并不是他一直害怕的那样。他一直怕的是自己记得不够多。真相恰恰相反：他记得太多，且把所记当作律令。每一道只属于某次潮水的偶然，每一处只属于某只浮标的一次歪斜，他都当成海水所循之律，压进了羊皮的肌理，仿佛那是基岩。他失败，不是因为手不稳、眼不准；他失败，是因为航道在交给他真正的流之外，还给了他许多只是航道之为航道的怪癖，而他一一把它们都奉上了神龛。图把航道复刻下来，连怪癖一并复刻；大海却不复刻。

Now, on the third night, with the chart soaked through and his fingers torn by rock he had no mark for, Taren understood the nature of his failure, and it was not what he had feared. He had feared that he had not memorized enough. The truth was the opposite: he had memorized too much, and memorized it as law. Every accident of a single tide, every one-off lean of a single buoy, he had taken for the rule the water lived by, and pressed into the grain of the vellum as though it were bedrock. He had not failed because his hand was unsteady or his eye untrained; he had failed because the channel had given him, along with its true currents, a great many quirks that were only the channel being the channel, and he had enshrined every one. The chart reproduced the channel, quirks and all; the sea did not.

在同一片海上，另有一位老渡子佩尔，什么图也不带，只揣一根打了结的测深绳，用一天便渡了过去。佩尔说不上航道里哪只浮标朝哪边歪；他是有意让每趟航程的细枝末节从手里滑走，只留住在每一趟他都见过的水域里都成立的东西，那些反复出现的深流。塔伦的图处处绊住他，佩尔的绳却只告诉他一件事，龙骨之下的水深，而这一件事，因为是反复出现的那件事，便已足够。塔伦望着老人的尾流笔直地划开那片撕碎了他船壳的水面，终于明白港主那句话的意思：一张图可以对航道为真，对大海为假，而它对航道为真得越彻底，对大海便为假得越彻底。

There was, on that same sea, an old ferryman named Pell who carried no chart at all, only a length of knotted line for sounding depth, and who made the crossing in a single day. Pell could not have told you the lean of any buoy in the channel; he had deliberately let the particulars of each passage slip from him, keeping only what held across every passage he had seen, the deep-running currents that repeated. Where Taren's chart failed him at every turn, Pell's line told him only one thing, the depth beneath his keel, and that one thing, because it was the thing that repeated, was enough. Taren, watching the old man's wake cut straight through water that had torn his own hull, saw at last what the harbor-master had meant: a chart can be true to the channel and false to the sea, and the more completely it is true to the channel, the more completely it is false to the sea.

他活了下来，回到港湾，再不绘航道之图。他转而在佩尔赠他的绳旁，置一小册，只把自己在不止一趟航程里都见到反复出现的事写下，凡只属于某次潮水的怪癖，一概留白不录。港主翻看那本薄册，对其不够精审一句不提，只说，这册子能过海。塔伦仍怀念那张完美之图所带来的踏实。可他也学到了，以三天与一具撕裂的船壳为代价：那完美之图所带来的踏实，正是险些取他性命的东西。

He survived, and returned, and drew no more charts of the channel. Instead he began to keep, beside the line Pell had given him, a small book in which he wrote down only the things he could see repeating across more than one passage, and deliberately left unrecorded every quirk that belonged to a single tide. The harbor-master, seeing the thin book, said nothing of its lack of precision, only that it would cross the sea. Taren still missed the comfort of the perfect chart. But he had learned, at the cost of three days and a torn hull, that the comfort of the perfect chart was the thing that had nearly killed him.

注解：渡子把单次潮水的偶然与某只浮标的一次歪斜都当作海水所循之律，复刻进图里，结果图对练过的航道丝丝入扣，对未见的大海却处处失准，这正是过拟合（overfitting）：记住了特例与噪声而非底层规律，训练表现愈精，泛化愈差，对应高方差一端；佩尔只记反复出现之物，等于以更多事例、更简之法去伪存真，也正合缓解之道。

Annotation: The ferryman took the accident of a single tide and the one-time lean of a single buoy to be the law the water lives by, and copied them into the chart; the chart then fit the practiced channel flawlessly yet failed at every turn on the sea it had never seen. This is overfitting (过拟合): remembering idiosyncrasies and noise rather than the underlying pattern, so that the more precise the fit to the training, the worse the generalization — the high-variance end. Pell kept only what recurred, which is to sift the true from the accidental with more cases and a simpler method, the very means of mitigation.
