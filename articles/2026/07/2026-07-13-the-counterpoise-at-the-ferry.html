渡口的配重

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年7月13日·2分钟阅读

船夫对徒弟说：「每天破晓，东渡的人有多少，西渡的就有多少；西渡的有多少，东渡的就有多少。两岸各自守着自己的数，中间那根缆绳既不松也不紧。这，我叫做『匀张』。」

"Every dawn," said the ferryman to his apprentice, "as many cross east as cross west, and as many cross west as cross east. The two banks hold their own, and the rope between them goes neither slack nor taut. That is what I call the even stretch."

徒弟皱眉：「那若我只要西岸人满，何不干脆禁了东渡，把人都留在西岸？」

The apprentice frowned. "Then if I want only the west bank full, why not simply forbid the eastward crossing and keep everyone on the west?"

船夫说：「你当那根缆绳是任你拽的。不是。你把西岸堆得高了，缆绳自己就绷紧：往东去的渡客便涌起来，去削你堆起来的那堆。绳的偏侧，朝着把『你所堆之物削薄』的那一岸去。它不问你肯不肯，也不站你这边。它也从不会把整堆削光——只削到缆绳稍稍松一松为止。那堆，仍比先前高。这便是配重：它削弱你硬加的那股力，却并不抹掉它。」

"You imagine the rope is yours to pull," the ferryman said. "It is not. Pile the west bank high and the rope goes taut of itself: more travelers pour east, draining down the pile. The stretch leans toward the side that thins out the very heap you built. It does not ask your leave, nor does it take your side. And it will never drain the whole pile—only enough to slacken the rope a little. The heap stays higher than it was before. That is the counterpoise: it weakens what you imposed, it does not undo it."

那年盛夏，一个商人押着三车粮食来了，铁了心只把东岸的仓房堆满。「我要把秤砣压到东边，就压在那儿。」他对徒弟说。船夫只在一旁看着：粮一车车往东堆，缆绳便朝西绷紧，那些本没打算搬家的人家，此刻竟也扛着自家的麻袋渡往西岸。点算下来，东岸的粮食比商人来之前多了——可比他倒进去的三车，少了整整两车。「这匀张吃了我两车。」商人说。船夫说：「不。是你自己的堆法催生了渡客，渡客又反过来削薄了那堆法。缆绳只收走了多出来的那一份，不多收一粒。」

A merchant arrived that summer with three wagons of grain, bent on stocking only the east warehouses. "I shall tip the balance east and leave it there," he told the apprentice. The ferryman only watched: as the grain was heaped east, the rope tautened westward, and families who had never thought to move now crossed west, carrying their own sacks. When the count was taken, the east held more grain than before the merchant came—but far less than the three wagons' worth he had poured in. "The stretch ate two of my wagons," the merchant said. "No," said the ferryman. "Your own heaping bred the crossing that undid the heaping. The rope took back what was excess, no more."

接着暑热来了。东岸屋舍逼仄，又贴着水面，闷得人发昏；西岸则一路爬上凉快的山岗。「这么说，是渡客偷走了我的热？」徒弟问。「是吞，」船夫说，「热把人从东岸逼出来，而这股逼迫，恰好把他们拽上了凉岗，拽上凉岗这股劲，又把热给喝了下去。压与反压，本就是同一个动作；缆绳朝着『把所压之物吸纳进去』的那一岸偏。再把东岸凉下来，这偏侧便会反过来。匀张只是回应那一推，它不挑季节。」

Then the heat came. The east bank, close-built and low to the water, grew stifling; the west bank climbed into cool hills. "So the crossing steals my heat?" asked the apprentice. "It swallows it," said the ferryman. "Heat presses folk out of the east, and the very pressing draws them up into the cool hills, and the drawing-up drinks the heat. Press and counter-press are one motion; the rope leans toward the side that absorbs what was pressed in. Cool the east again, and the lean will reverse. The stretch answers the shove, it does not choose a season."

商人不甘再被「劫」，便往里筑起一道新墙，把东岸收窄，好把人挤过河去。墙是朝东往西压的，缆绳也确实朝西偏——西岸地广，容得下更少的人头算数。但船夫看出，这偏侧依旧是部分的：墙每收进一尺，便有恰好相称的一股渡潮来回应，不多一寸。「把它收成一线，」商人说，「我便能把人全弄到西岸。」「收成一线，」船夫说，「那股渡潮也跟着收成一线。配重永远跟这股挤压力等量，从不会越过它。你拿不到全部。你赔上石料，缆绳却只会把那挤压力的分量原样奉还。」

The merchant, unwilling to be robbed again, built a new wall inward, narrowing the east bank to squeeze its people across. The wall pushed east-to-west, and the rope did lean west—west being the roomier bank, where fewer heads fit the counting. But the lean, the ferryman noted, was again only partial: for every pace the wall stole, a matching surge of crossings answered it, no more. "Narrow it to a thread," the merchant said, "and I shall have them all on the west." "Narrow it to a thread," said the ferryman, "and the surge narrows to a thread with it. The counterpoise matches the squeeze; it never outruns it. You will not have them all. You will pay the stone, and the rope will hand back exactly the squeeze's worth."

商人在缆绳旁坐了很久。要逼西岸人满，他就得不停地往东堆、不停地收窄、不停地添热——而每一股力都会催生出抵消它的那一股，每一次都只是部分，从不会是全部。他可以拿了那半车粮、那半股渡潮、那半成的搬移，就此了结；要么，就无休无止地赔上车马、石料与炭火，却永不能让缆绳定住哪怕一回。他选了那半，再不向船夫追问。至于那歇在桅缆上的海鸥——既不喂东岸，也不饿西岸，不增不减任何一岸的数——就让那匀张，纹丝不动地留着。

The merchant sat by the rope a long while. To force the west full, he would have to keep heaping east, keep narrowing, keep heating—and each force bred the counter that undid it, partly, every time, never wholly. He could take the half-grain, the half-crossing, the half-removal, and be done; or he could spend wagons and stone and coal without end, and never once hold the rope still. He chose the half, and asked the ferryman no more questions. And the gulls that nested on the mast-rope—neither fed nor starved either bank, changing no one's count—left the stretch exactly as it was.

注解：此概念即勒夏特列原理（Le Chatelier's principle）——处于平衡的系统一旦在浓度、温度或压强上被扰动，便向抵消该扰动的方向移动，且只部分抵消、从不彻底抹除；这种移动是机械式的、非意愿的必然响应，且只能定性指出方向，不能定量给出程度。

Note: The concept is Le Chatelier's principle（勒夏特列原理）—when a system at equilibrium is disturbed in concentration, temperature, or pressure, it shifts to partially counteract the imposed change, never fully undoing it; the shift is a mechanical, non-willed response, and it indicates only direction, not magnitude.
