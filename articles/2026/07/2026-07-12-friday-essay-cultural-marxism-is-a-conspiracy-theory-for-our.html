周五随笔：「文化马克思主义」是我们这个时代的阴谋论

Alexander Howard·The Conversation·2026年7月12日·7分钟阅读·原文

1969年6月17日。著名的德国左翼政治哲学家马尔库塞（Herbert Marcuse）正在罗马的 Teatro Eliseo 剧院发表演讲。马尔库塞正讲得兴起时，观众席里有人打断了他：

June 17, 1969. Prominent left-wing German political philosopher Herbert Marcuse is delivering a lecture at the Teatro Eliseo in Rome. Marcuse is in mid-flow when someone in the audience interrupts him:

「赫伯特（Herbert），告诉我们，你为什么拿中央情报局（CIA）的钱？」

Herbert, tell us why are you getting paid by the CIA?

起哄者是科恩-邦迪（Daniel Cohn-Bendit），他与1960年代的学生运动以及「新左派」（New Left）这一政治与文化倾向关系密切，而马尔库塞本人也正属于这一派别。

The heckler was Daniel Cohn-Bendit, one of the most high-profile figures associated with the student movement of the 1960s and the New Left – a political and cultural tendency with which Marcuse himself was associated.

马尔库塞的回答暗示，这样的指控并非头一遭落在他头上。「我一直被指控拿钱，」他厉声说，「拿克里姆林宫的、北京的、资本主义的、华尔街的。」

Marcuse’s reply suggests this was not the first time such an allegation had been levelled against him. “I have been accused of being paid,” he snapped, “by the Kremlin, by Beijing, by capitalism, by Wall Street.”

这些引语出自罗克希尔（Gabriel Rockhill）的《谁付钱给了西方马克思主义的吹笛人？》（Who Paid the Pipers of Western Marxism?）。该书于2025年出版，在左翼圈子里引起了不小的轰动。罗克希尔论证说，整个冷战期间，中央情报局（CIA）及其盟友基金会与文化组织网络曾暗中试图塑造西方左翼思想的方向。

These quotes come from Gabriel Rockhill’s Who Paid the Pipers of Western Marxism? Published in 2025, the book caused a stir in leftist circles. Rockhill argued that, throughout the Cold War, the CIA and a network of allied foundations and cultural organisations covertly sought to shape the direction of left-wing thought in the West.

他声称，其目标不仅是打击苏联的影响，还要鼓励那些看似对现有秩序进行激进批判、但最终仍与西方资本主义秩序更宏大的政治经济优先事项相容的异议形式。

The goal, he claimed, was not simply to combat Soviet influence, but to encourage forms of dissent that appeared radically critical of the existing order, while ultimately remaining compatible with the broader political and economic priorities of the Western capitalist order.

据罗克希尔所述，战后的知识阶层以多种方式卷入了这些网络，有些是明知故为，有些则是不知不觉。其结果是一种偏爱文化批判与超然反思、而忽视革命政治与有组织的阶级斗争的西方马克思主义版本。

According to Rockhill, the postwar intelligentsia became entangled in these webs in several ways, some knowingly, others unwittingly. The result was a version of Western Marxism that privileged cultural critique and detached reflection over revolutionary politics and organised class struggle.

正是在这里，马尔库塞登场了。马尔库塞是法兰克福学派（Frankfurt School）的一员——这是一群与「社会研究所」（Institute for Social Research）关系密切的德裔犹太知识分子。该研究所1923年成立于法兰克福，聚集了阿多诺（Theodor Adorno）、弗洛姆（Erich Fromm）、霍克海默（Max Horkheimer）以及后来的马尔库塞等思想家。他们想弄清楚，为什么马克思（Marx）所预言的革命未能像预期那样在西方发达资本主义社会中实现。

This is where Marcuse enters the frame. Marcuse was a member of the Frankfurt School, a band of German-Jewish intellectuals associated with the Institute for Social Research. Founded in Frankfurt in 1923, the institute brought together thinkers such as Theodor Adorno, Erich Fromm, Max Horkheimer and, later, Marcuse. They wanted to understand why the revolution predicted by Marx had failed to materialise in the advanced capitalist societies of the West.

正如杰弗里斯（Stuart Jeffries）在2016年出版的该群体思想传记《深渊大酒店》（Grand Hotel Abyss）中所指出的，法兰克福学派的独特之处在于，其成员借助精神分析和哲学来解释资本主义为何远比许多马克思主义者所预料的更为持久。他们还

As Stuart Jeffries notes in Grand Hotel Abyss (2016), his intellectual biography of the group, what distinguished the Frankfurt School was the way its members drew on psychoanalysis and philosophy to explain why capitalism had proved far more durable than many Marxists had expected. They also

关注他们所称的「文化工业」（culture industry）的兴起，从而探索了一种文化与政治之间新的关系——前者充当资本主义的走卒，却又有潜力（多半未能实现）成为资本主义的掘墓人。

engaged with the rise of what they called the culture industry and thereby explored a new relationship between culture and politics, where the former served as a lackey of capitalism and yet had the potential, mostly unrealised, to be its gravedigger.

以这种方式，法兰克福学派把马克思主义的探究方向引向了工厂车间和正式政治领域之外的问题。

In this fashion, the Frankfurt School helped redirect Marxist inquiry towards questions that lay beyond the factory floor and the sphere of formal politics.

杰弗里斯指出，法兰克福学派往往更擅长识别现代资本主义的种种弊病，而难以触及足以克服这些弊病的能量。以阿多诺为例，尽管许多学生拥抱了他对资本主义社会的批判，他自己却对学生激进主义始终深怀怀疑。

Jeffries makes the point that the Frankfurt School was often better at identifying the problems of modern capitalism than the energies capable of overcoming them. Adorno, for one, remained deeply sceptical of student activism, even as many students embraced his critique of capitalist society.

马尔库塞则持不同看法。他在学生抗议中看到了某种新型政治抵抗的可能性——这种抵抗超越了传统产业工人阶级的堡垒，而马克思及其继承者曾经对工人阶级寄予如此厚望。

Marcuse took a different view. He saw in the student protests the possibility of a new type of political resistance emerging beyond the bastions of the traditional industrial working class, upon whom Marx and his inheritors had once pinned such high hopes.

尽管如此，罗克希尔指出一个重心的转移是准确的。法兰克福学派改变了此后许多马克思主义者赖以运作的理论地形。文化与意识问题获得了此前未曾有过的显赫地位，在今后数十年间重塑了学院派马克思主义探究的优先事项。

Still, Rockhill is correct to identity a shift in emphasis. The Frankfurt School changed the theoretical terrain on which many subsequent Marxists would operate. Questions of culture and consciousness assumed a prominence they had not previously enjoyed, reshaping the priorities of academic Marxist inquiry for decades to come.

这究竟是丰富了马克思主义传统，还是稀释了其激进锋芒，仍是一个悬而未决的问题。

Whether this enriched the Marxist tradition or diluted its radical force remains an open question.

这一点值得铭记于心，当我们转向思想史家伍兹（A.J.A. Woods）那部出色的著作《「文化马克思主义」阴谋：右翼为何把西方的衰落归咎于法兰克福学派》（The Cultural Marxism Conspiracy: Why the Right Blames the Frankfurt School for the Decline of the West）。伍兹的分析与罗克希尔一样，奠基于历史唯物主义的范式，尤其借鉴了意大利马克思主义者葛兰西（Antonio Gramsci）的思想。

This is worth keeping in mind as we turn to intellectual historian A.J.A. Woods’ excellent book The Cultural Marxism Conspiracy: Why the Right Blames the Frankfurt School for the Decline of the West. Woods’ analysis, like Rockhill’s, is grounded in the customs of historical materialism, drawing in particular on the work of the Italian Marxist Antonio Gramsci.

然而伍兹讲述的故事却截然不同。他写道，2021年12月，「文化马克思主义」（cultural Marxism）一词被收入《牛津英语词典》（Oxford English Dictionary）。若以该词条为凭，这是一个「有着险恶且饱受争议过往的」表述。

The story Woods tells, however, is very different. In December 2021, Woods writes, the term “cultural Marxism” was added to the Oxford English Dictionary. If the OED entry was anything to go by, it was an expression “with a sinister and controversial past”.

它的源头可追溯到1930年代。它曾较早出现于一份与英国法西斯主义相关的出版物中。但在其大部分历史里，这一说法一直湮没于相对的默默无闻之中。

It can be traced back to the 1930s. It made an early appearance in a publication associated with British fascism. Yet for much of its history, the phrase languished in relative obscurity.

如今却不同了。「文化马克思主义」——有时被称为「文化马克思主义阴谋论」——已成为当代右翼话语中的常见辞令。在美国，包括赫格塞斯（Pete Hegseth）和克鲁兹（Ted Cruz）在内的政客都用这个词来解释他们眼中对社会有害或具破坏性的种种变迁。在拉丁美洲也有类似论调，米莱（Javier Milei）和博索纳罗（Jair Bolsonaro）等领导人曾警告其所谓的恶劣影响。

Not so now. Cultural Marxism – sometimes referred to as the “Cultural Marxism conspiracy theory” – has become a staple of contemporary right-wing discourse. In the United States, politicians, including Pete Hegseth and Ted Cruz, have employed the term to explain social changes they regard as harmful or destabilising. Similar claims have been made in Latin America, where leaders like Javier Milei and Jair Bolsonaro have warned of what they regard as its pernicious influence.

这一理论认为，法兰克福学派断定资本主义短期内不会因自身矛盾的重压而崩溃，遂在1930、1940年代流亡美国期间——他们为保命而逃离了纳粹德国——制定出一种新的共产主义颠覆策略。

The theory holds that the Frankfurt School, having concluded that capitalism was unlikely to collapse under the weight of its own contradictions any time soon, developed a new strategy for communist subversion while living in America during the 1930s and 1940s, having fled from Nazi Germany in fear of their lives.

伍兹指出，在这种叙事中，「法兰克福学派及其信奉者被赋予了一种近乎魔法般的能动性，足以克服一切矛盾与反作用力」。

In this telling, as Woods observes, “the Frankfurt School and their devotees are blessed with a magical level of agency that can overcome all contradictions and countervailing forces”.

简而言之，其论点是：阿多诺及其同伙心怀叵测地从内部颠覆西方社会，逐步渗透大学校园，腐蚀易受影响的年轻心灵。其他关键机构也会随之沦陷。其终极目标，是永久性地颠覆现代生活的文化根基。

The argument, in a nutshell, is that Adorno and his nefarious affiliates strove to undermine Western society from within, gradually infiltrating university campuses and corrupting impressionable young minds. Other key institutions would follow. The ultimate goal was to forever upend the cultural foundations of modern life.

这一理论已证明极为经久不衰。伍兹指出，它被用来解释从女性主义、政治正确、LGBTQ+群体日益可见，到更近期的「黑命攸关」（Black Lives Matter）运动及应对气候变化的努力等多种发展。

The theory has proven remarkably durable. Woods notes that it has been used to account for developments as varied as feminism, political correctness, the growing visibility of LGBTQ+ communities and, more recently, movements such as Black Lives Matter and efforts to address climate change.

伍兹强调，《「文化马克思主义」阴谋》一书并非对法兰克福学派本身的历史叙述。它关注的不是一小撮德国知识分子的由来，而是使关于他们的一个阴谋论得以可能的历史条件。本书也不打算去逐一戳穿围绕它滋生的庞杂理论。

The Cultural Marxism Conspiracy, Woods emphasises, is not a historical account of the Frankfurt School, per se. Its concern is not the origins of a coterie of German intellectuals, but the historical conditions that made a conspiracy theory about them possible. Nor is the book an attempt to debunk the sprawling theory that has grown up around it.

相反，正如伍兹所言，本书旨在

Instead, as Woods put it, the volume seeks

解开一系列批判性的谜题。为什么这一文化衰退叙事如此集中地把矛头对准一群德国马克思主义者？它又如何持续地使政治反动势力信服并为之兴奋？

to work out a series of critical puzzles. Why does this narrative of cultural degeneration focus so much on a collective of German Marxists? How does it continue to convince and excite the forces of political reaction?

伍兹对这些问题的回答采取了一种谱系学的形式。它追踪这一阴谋论在多种语境下的发展，追随它从政治边缘走向主流公共意识的过程。一路上，我们遇见一大群保守派政客、自封的知识分子、邪教头目和各色怪人。

Woods’ answer to those questions takes the form of a genealogy. It tracks the development of the conspiracy theory across a wide range of contexts, following its movement from the political fringe into mainstream public consciousness. Along the way, we meet a host of conservative politicians, self-styled intellectuals, cult leaders and assorted cranks.

从中浮现出的是一个关于适应与重构的故事。伍兹从1960年代开篇。那个时期以反战运动与去殖民化为标志。那也是一个既有社会等级秩序遭受持续压力、曾被边缘化的群体获得新的公共可见度的时刻。与此同时，强大的势力也动员起来捍卫既有秩序。

What emerges is a story of adaptation and reinvention. Woods opens in the 1960s. The period was marked by antiwar activism and decolonisation. It was also a moment when established social hierarchies came under sustained pressure and previously marginalised groups acquired a new public visibility. At the same time, powerful forces mobilised in defence of the existing order.

文化在这幅图景中占据核心位置。伍兹认为，战后最重大的发展之一，是「文化」这一范畴本身的急剧扩张：随着发达国家从工业生产转向，「文化猛然闯入日常生活的前台」。

Culture occupies a central place in this picture. One of the most significant developments of the postwar era, Woods posits, was the dramatic expansion of the category of culture itself: as advanced countries shifted away from industrial production, “culture burst into the foreground of day-to-day life”.

其后果是深刻的。大众传媒开始用伍兹的话说「对日常生活的节奏施加了巨大的权力与影响」。身份、青年文化和生活方式的问题获得了此前未曾有过的政治分量。与之相应的「文化转向」重塑了知识生活，推动了对意义的生产与流通的学术探究。

The consequences were profound. Mass media began, in Woods’ words, “to exert tremendous power and influence over the rhythms of everyday life”. Questions of identity, youth culture and lifestyle acquired a political heft they had not previously possessed. A corresponding “cultural turn” reshaped intellectual life, spurring scholarly inquiry into the production and circulation of meaning.

正是在这样的背景下，应当理解「文化马克思主义」阴谋论——它源自右翼「对在漫长而全球化的1960年代开始展开的社会变迁的抵抗与逆转努力」。

It is against this setting that the cultural Marxism conspiracy theory should be understood, deriving as it does from right-wing “efforts to resist and reverse the social changes that started to unfold in the long and global 1960s”.

这一理论的支持者并未把这些现象当作历史发展来对待，而是把它们编织成一部意识形态夺权的叙事。法兰克福学派成了一只便利的替罪羊，被安插在一部更大的、关于文化变迁与战后秩序所谓衰落的故事里。

Rather than treating these phenomena as historical developments, the theory’s proponents wove them into a narrative of ideological takeover. The Frankfurt School became a convenient scapegoat in a much larger story about cultural change and the perceived decline of the postwar order.

伍兹的叙述按时间顺序推进。最初几章把我们——名副其实地——带回马尔库塞和罗马那个起哄者那里。此处的重点在于1960年代后半期高等教育的扩张与新左派的碎片化。

Woods’ account moves chronologically. The first chapters take us back, quite literally, to Marcuse and his heckler in Rome. The focus here is on the expansion of higher education and the fragmentation of the New Left in the latter part of the 1960s.

在这一章里，有一个人物尤为突出。美国政治活动家拉鲁什（Lyndon LaRouche）起家于激进左翼，随后建立起他自己的一个类邪教运动。伍兹展示了拉鲁什及其偏执的同伙们如何逐步把法兰克福学派变成一个便利的解释工具——既用来解释他们所痛恶的社会文化发展，也用来解释他们自己未能取得任何接近政治影响力可言的失败。

One figure looms especially large in this section of the book. American political activist Lyndon LaRouche started out on the radical left before constructing a cultish movement of his own. Woods shows how LaRouche and his paranoiac band of fellow travellers gradually transformed the Frankfurt School into a convenient explanation for the social and cultural developments they deplored, as well as for their own failure to achieve anything approximating political influence.

拉鲁什的攻击有其政治目的。到1970年代，阿多诺和霍克海默等人已被归咎于从性道德变化到摇滚乐兴起的一切。对任何稍熟悉法兰克福学派著述的人来说，这种反讽令人瞠目。尤其是阿多诺，他大半生都在斥责流行文化，视其为标准化与社会控制的工具。

LaRouche’s attacks served a political purpose. By the 1970s, thinkers such as Adorno and Horkheimer were being blamed for everything from changing sexual mores to the rise of rock music. For anyone even remotely familiar with the writings of the Frankfurt School, the irony is jaw-dropping. Adorno, in particular, spent much of his career castigating popular culture, which he regarded as a vehicle for standardisation and social control.

不过在拉鲁什的版本里，阿多诺却成了流行文化的首要缔造者之一。关键不在于拉鲁什弄错了法兰克福学派——尽管他显然错了——而在于他帮助锻造了一部叙事，让后来者去加以打磨，并将其带进政治主流。

In LaRouche’s version of events, though, Adorno became one of pop culture’s principal architects. The point is not that LaRouche got the Frankfurt School wrong, although he obviously did. It is more that he helped forge a narrative that others would subsequently refine and carry into the political mainstream.

在本书第二章，我们可以看到这一过程如何运作。拉鲁什确立了该理论的基本轮廓，而旧保守主义者林德（William S. Lind）则提供了让它得以更广为流传的话语。

We see this process at work in the book’s second chapter. LaRouche established the basic contours of the theory, but paleoconservative William S. Lind provided the language through which it would achieve wider circulation.

林德是一名美国智库战略家兼活动家，依附于「新右派」（New Right）。1994年，他在一篇与他人合写、发表于美国海军陆战队的期刊文章中首创了「文化马克思主义」（cultural Marxism）一词。伍兹证实，这篇论文在解释「是谁把马克思主义从经济学术语翻译为社会学术语」方面，细节明显语焉不详。

An American think-tank strategist and activist yoked to the New Right, Lind coined the term “cultural Marxism” in 1994, in a co-authored journal article published by the US Marine Corps. The essay, Woods attests, was conspicuously short on detail when it came to explaining who was responsible for translating “Marxism from economic into social and social terms”.

一年后情况变了。1995年，林德把法兰克福学派指认为那个缺失的环节。他另添新意，指控其成员是「政治正确」背后的推动力量——这种祸害正妨碍着全美言论自由。林德描绘的图景阴沉可怖。他声称，政治正确正把这个国家变成一个极权国家。

This changed a year later. In 1995, Lind identified the Frankfurt School as the missing link. In a new twist, he charged its members with being the driving force behind political correctness, a blight that was stymieing freedom of speech across America. The vision Lind depicted was bleak. Political correctness, he claimed, was transforming the country into a totalitarian state.

大学处于这一阴森世界观的中心。这些曾经神圣的高等学问殿堂，如今据称已成了一种新的意识形态正统的孵化器。林德断言，罪魁祸首是「批判理论」（Critical Theory）。它由法兰克福学派知识分子融合马克思主义、弗洛伊德主义与语言学发展而成，他指控它已把美国变成一个由「激进平等主义」统治的社会：一个传统价值可被消解、意识形态顺从可被背书的国家。

Universities stood at the centre of this ominous worldview. Once hallowed sites of higher learning, they had now, allegedly, become incubators for a new ideological orthodoxy. The culprit, Lind contended, was Critical Theory. Developed by Frankfurt School intellectuals through a fusion of Marxism, Freudianism and linguistics, it had, he charged, turned the United States into a society governed by “radical egalitarianism”: a country where traditional values could be undermined and ideological conformity endorsed.

林德坚称，仅仅告诫人们警惕文化马克思主义这一祸害是不够的。他推论道，关切的保守派需要投身教育性活动，以便把这种危险揭露给亲属、朋友和邻居。这一理论必须被推广开来。

Lind insisted that it was not enough merely to warn people about the scourge of cultural Marxism. Concerned conservatives, he reasoned, needed to engage in educational activism in order to expose this danger to relatives, friends and neighbours. The theory had to be popularised.

伍兹第三章考察的是随后发生的事。2007年茶党（Tea Party）的兴起，为文化马克思主义叙事的传播提供了理想环境。茶党是一个去中心化的政治运动，靠的是一团混沌的怒火、网络博客、社交媒体和传统媒体；它催生了伍兹所谓的「情势型知识分子」（conjunctural intellectuals）——一批自命的爱国者，借助新兴数字平台动员支持者、传播观念，而不论其真假。

Woods’ third chapter examines what happened next. The rise of the Tea Party in 2007 provided an ideal environment for the dissemination of cultural Marxism narratives. A decentralised political movement sustained by inchoate rage, online blogs, social media and legacy media outlets, the Tea Party fostered the emergence of what Woods calls “conjunctural intellectuals” – self-appointed patriots who used emerging digital platforms to mobilise supporters and spread ideas, irrespective of whether they were true or false.

茶党活动者并非被动消费政治信息的群体，而是其传播的积极参与者。在这套另类信息生态系统里，关于文化马克思主义的故事——往往透过对多元文化主义和社会正义议题的攻击折射出来——找到了它的受众与有力的新传播渠道。

Tea Party activists were not passive consumers of political data, but active participants in its dissemination. In this alternative information ecoystem, stories about cultural Marxism – often refracted though assaults on multiculturalism and social justice issues – found a receptive audience and a powerful new means of distribution.

《「文化马克思主义」阴谋》最后一章把故事带到现在。2010与2020年代的政治动荡，为心怀阴谋论的人提供了一组新的敌人。伍兹追溯了较早期的反法兰克福学派叙事如何被重新包装，转入针对「批判性种族理论」（Critical Race Theory）、「性别意识形态」（gender ideology）以及最终「觉醒主义」（wokeness）的种种运动。

The final chapter of The Cultural Marxism Conspiracy brings the story into the present. The political upheavals of the 2010s and 2020s supplied the conspiratorially-minded with a fresh set of enemies. Woods traces how older anti-Frankfurt School narratives were repackaged through campaigns waged against Critical Race Theory, “gender ideology” and, eventually, “wokeness”.

「黑命攸关」抗议为此提供了重要的背景。伍兹勾勒了保守派活动家与机构如何通过「意识形态俘获」的语言，重新框定关于种族主义与结构性不平等的辩论，而批判性种族理论成了一个格外有力的靶子。

The Black Lives Matter protests provide an important backdrop here. Woods outlines how conservative activists and institutions reframed debates about racism and structural inequality through the language of ideological capture, with Critical Race Theory becoming a particularly potent target.

特朗普（Donald Trump）对「左翼灌输」不厌其烦的攻击、1776委员会以及「知识暗网」（Intellectual Dark Web），都在本书结尾的篇幅中醒目登场。

Donald Trump’s ad nauseam attacks on “left-wing indoctrination”, the 1776 Commission and the Intellectual Dark Web all feature prominently in the concluding pages of the book.

伍兹不让左翼读者轻易得到慰藉。《「文化马克思主义」阴谋》并不纵容那种可以拿虚假信息或操纵来解释一切的抚慰性虚构，而是坚称政治左派的种种失职在其中也扮演了角色。

Woods refuses readers of a left-wing persuasion easy consolation. The Cultural Marxism Conspiracy does not indulge the comforting fiction that these developments can be explained away by misinformation or manipulation, insisting that the shortcomings of the political left play a role in all of this.

尽管如此，本书并不陷入宿命论。伍兹写道，当下是一个「我们被迫在其中组织与行动的地形」。如果说这部原本沉郁的论著中尚有一丝希望的音符，那便在于它坚持政治结果始终是偶然的，而非预先注定的。正如伍兹提醒我们的，我们看似身陷其中的迷宫也许包含许多虚假出口，但终究有一条出路。

Nevertheless, the book eschews fatalism. The current moment, Woods writes, is a “terrain on which we are forced to organise and act”. If there is a note of hope in this otherwise sobering tract, it lies in the insistence that political outcomes remain contingent rather than predetermined. As Woods reminds us, the maze in which we are seemingly stuck may contain many false exits, but there is a way through.
