白石峡的两大家族

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年7月12日·2分钟阅读

那道峡里有两大家族，两大家族之间的世仇比任何活人记得的舌头都老。白石家族与黑岬家族的仇怨延续太久，已无人能说出第一道伤口从何而起；每一代人都像继承梯田、继承名号、继承山口处那片沉默一样继承着这份世仇。黎恩沿着山路走上峡，怀揣着州府的差遣：在最高处签下一纸条约，了结此事。

The gorge had two houses, and the two houses had a quarrel older than any living tongue could remember. White Stone and Black Crag had feuded for so long that no one could name the first wound; each generation inherited the quarrel the way it inherited the terraces, the names, and the silence at the top of the pass. Li En came up the mountain road carrying a commission from the prefect: one treaty, sealed at the top, to end it.

她先与白石家族的长者同坐，他们摆出各自的委屈：一条地界，一口水井，三年前被回绝的一桩婚事。可她听着听着，发觉这仇怨并不活在最高处。白石家族内部就有两支为同一口井争执，而族长用来指控黑岬家族的话，正是东支用来指控西支的话，仿佛每一支都把整场世仇缩成袖珍模样预演过一遍。委屈、指控者、无人回应的诉求，三者的排列方式一模一样，只是名号缩了水。

She sat first with the elders of White Stone, and they laid out their grief: a boundary, a well, a marriage refused three winters back. But as she listened she found that the quarrel did not live at the top. Within White Stone itself two branches quarrelled over the same well, and the words the headman used against Black Crag were the very words the eastern branch used against the western, as if each branch had rehearsed the whole feud in miniature. The arrangement of grievance, accuser, and unanswered demand was the same, only the names had shrunk.

黎恩涉水到黑岬家族，又听了一遍：家族对家族，然后支对支，再然后同一支里共住一个院落的两位妯娌不肯共一堵墙，而她们的委屈长着同样的骨头。她在册页上画下来——家族含着家族，支含着支，院落含着院落——看出无论她站在哪一道门前，门后那条路都弯成同一个形状。放大去看其中任意一个，都像是又走到了整体跟前。

Li En crossed the river to Black Crag and heard it again: the house against the house, then branch against branch, then within a single branch the two wives of one courtyard who would not share a wall, and their grievance had the same bones. She drew it on her tablet — house holding house, branch holding branch, courtyard holding courtyard — and saw that whichever gate she stood at, the road beyond it bent into the same shape. To zoom into any one of them was to arrive again at the whole.

这才是州府没有告诫她的难处。在最高处立约，能封住族长们的口，族长们会把话传给各支，各支传给各院，院里再传给那对仍不肯在同一炉火前转过脸的夫妻。这仇怨不是一道伤口，而是同一道伤口换了尺度，在她数得清的每一道门前重复出现。在最顶层了结它是诚实的；一路追下去则会发现，同样的结构在每个深度都候着，而没有一个深度是真正的底。

Here was the crux the prefect had not warned her of. A treaty at the top would seal the mouths of the headmen, and the headmen would say the words to the branches, and the branches to the yards, and the yards to the married couple who would still not turn their faces to one another at the same hearth. The quarrel was not one wound but one wound re-scaled, repeated at every gate she could name. To end it at the top was honest; to follow it down was to find that the same structure waited at every depth, and no depth was the true bottom.

她本可为这趟差遣的徒劳落泪，但她没有。她转而与东院里最年轻的那一对同坐，两个还定不下今日该轮到谁去打水的人，为他们做一名调停人在一个晚上能做的微小寻常之事：她标好次序，把水罐搁在两人之间，她听着，直到那座炉火顶上的沉默不再是世仇的沉默，而只是两个被听见之后的疲惫者的沉默。那不是底。那也不是顶。那只是一道门，至于门后那条路的形状，她留给接下来要走上它的人。

She could have wept for the waste of the commission, but she did not. She sat instead with the youngest couple in the eastern yard, two who could not yet decide whose turn it was to draw the water, and she did for them the small, ordinary work a mediator can do in one evening: she marked the turns, she set the jar between them, she listened until the silence at the top of that single hearth was no longer the silence of a feud but only the silence of two tired people who had been heard. It was not the bottom. It was not the top. It was one gate, and the shape of the road beyond it she left to those who would walk it next.

黎恩下了山，带回一份写有两大家族名号的条约，旁边附着她那条诚实而更窄的真相：这仇怨是一种任何一个局部都把整体揣在身上的形状，一道门上的封印能让那道门安静下来，却封不住那形状。州府接过那卷羊皮，问她世仇是否了结。黎恩说，在她站立过的地方它已经了结；而同样的了结，还得在路仍抵达的每一道门前，由下一个被派上山的人，重新站立一次。

Li En went back down the mountain with a treaty that bore the names of the two houses and the honest, narrower truth beside it: that the quarrel was a shape which any single part held whole, and a seal at one gate could quiet that gate, never the shape. The prefect received the parchment and asked whether the feud was ended. Li En said it was ended where she had stood, and that the same ending would have to be stood for, again, at every gate the road still reached — by whoever was sent up the mountain next.

注解：故事里的世仇所映照的，是分形自相似（fractal self-similarity）——一种整体、每一部分、部分的部分都持有同一排列的结构，放大其中任意一道门，都能再现整体。它属于排列方式本身的结构属性，并非任何人想要结仇的意愿。

Annotation: The feud in the story mirrors fractal self-similarity (分形自相似) — a structure where the whole, each part, and each part of a part hold the same arrangement, so that zooming into any single gate reproduces the whole. It is a structural property of the arrangement itself, not anyone's wish to quarrel.
