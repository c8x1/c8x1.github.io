寓言故事

最后的抄本

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年7月22日·2分钟阅读

等到雅娜（Yanna）展开寺里最后一卷羊皮时，每一页都印着同一句话，连墨迹的走向都一样。这座曾藏有万种天象谶语、鱼汛口诀、百草歌谣的经院，如今只留下一行字，被抄了上千遍。司书们以整齐为荣，住持称其为纯净。只有最小的抄经人雅娜怀疑：纯净，不过是一具尸首的名字。

By the time Yanna unrolled the monastery's final scroll, every page bore the same sentence, repeated down to the inkstain. The abbey that had once kept ten thousand kinds of weather-lore, fish-lore, and herb-verse now held one line and one line only, copied a thousand times. The librarians were proud of the uniformity; the abbot called it purity. Only Yanna, the youngest scribe, suspected that purity was the name of a corpse.

她清楚这静默是如何被赞出来的——前一天她刚把这规矩抄进自己的板子：每位抄经人只依上一任留下的抄本而抄，绝不回头去看最初的几页。长老说这样才得传承无隙，又免得旧册被反复摩挲。没有人再提原本：那些建寺的香客笔记、采药人的账册，百年前就封进了地窖，如今去翻，便算异端。

She knew how the house had praised itself into this stillness, for she had copied the rule into her own tablet only the day before: each scribe works only from the copy left by the previous scribe, and never returns to the first pages. The elders said this kept the tradition seamless and spared the older folios from handling. No one spoke of the originals, the pilgrim's notebooks and herb-gatherers' ledgers the house had been built on; they had been sealed in the cellar a century ago, and to consult them now would be called heresy.

在外祖母那一代，这交易看着合算：那时经院头一回不再把原本摊上书案。抄本既已够好，何必拿脆弱的初页冒险？可「够好」成了唯一的源头，唯一的源头又成了唯一的真实。每一代抄经人都把某处一笔颤抖扶直、把某处地方俗词抹平，并非出于恶意，只是手追着手所描的疲劳而已。最先稀薄下去的，是那些少数的话——沿海的咸涩口音、旧时对雨的称呼——没人察觉，因为主干歌谣仍唱得响亮。

The bargain had looked sensible in her grandmother's time, when the house first stopped laying originals on the desks. Why risk the fragile first pages when the copies were good enough? But good enough became the only source, and the only source became the only truth. Each generation of scribes straightened a wavering stroke here, smoothed a regional word there, not by malice but by the small fatigue of a hand tracing what a hand had traced. The minority phrases—the salty dialect of the coast, the old names for rain—were the first to thin out, and no one noticed, because the main verse still sang fine.

那便是早期的寂静，是轻易的那一段：卷轴看上去仍比它真实的样子丰盈。只有当雅娜把天象谶语一卷卷往回追，她才看见那流失——第三抄本里，沿海那个「雾」字已不见；到第十抄本，三个「雨」的名字并为一个；到第五十抄本，能点出四十种潮汛的鱼诀塌缩成了一种潮。差异正像从裂碗里漏出去的水，每一抄离原本更远一步，离一条单调平线更近一步。若一行一行去量，性能甚至像在长进；损失只藏在那些不再存在的东西里。

That was the early quiet, the easy stage: the scrolls still looked richer than they were. Only when Yanna traced the weather-lore backward did she see the loss—the coastal word for fog gone by the third copy, the three names for rain merged into one by the tenth, the fish-lore that named forty tides collapsed to a single tide by the fiftieth. The variance was draining out of the page like water from a cracked bowl, and every new copy was one step further from the first and one step closer to a single flat line. Measured line by line, the performance even looked to improve; the loss lived only in what no longer existed.

此刻她站到了晚期，那流失终成了全部：每一册都写着同一句话，而每个抄经人抄着抄经人，也只能再抄出那一行，因为再没有别的留下来可抄。继续抄，便是制造更多一模一样；停笔，便是让这座经院的记忆就此断在这唯一一句上。地窖里收着原本，那是仅存的还写着雾、三种雨、四十种潮的纸页。雅娜的手按在笔上。她可以揭开封条，担下异端之名；也可以蘸墨，像她之前每一个抄经人那样忠实，把那仅剩的一行，再抄上千遍。

Now she stood at the late stage, where the loss had at last become the whole of it: every folio said the same thing, and every scribe copying every scribe could only reproduce that one sentence, for nothing else survived to be copied. To keep working was to produce more of the identical; to stop was to let the house's memory die here, on a single phrase. The cellar held the originals, the only pages that still carried fog and the three rains and the forty tides. Yanna's hand rested on her pen. She could break the seal and bear the charge of heresy, or she could dip her pen and, faithful as every scribe before her, copy the one remaining line a thousand times more.

注解：这个故事是模型崩溃（model collapse）的图景：当每一代只在上一代的产出上受训，整体的差异便一滴滴漏尽——少数的形态先稀薄下去，最后一切坍缩成同一行字。这是一种机械的、非意愿的过程，靠消化自身的产出，直至再无多样可言。

Note: The story is an image of model collapse: when each generation is trained only on the output of the previous generation, the variance of the whole drains away—minority forms thin out first, then everything collapses toward a single identical line—a mechanical, not intentional, process of feeding on one's own output until nothing diverse remains.
