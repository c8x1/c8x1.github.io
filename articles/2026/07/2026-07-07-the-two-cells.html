两间囚室

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年7月7日·2分钟阅读

「他俩都点名要见我，」主管把两份档案推过桌面，「北翼的沃斯（Voss），和南翼的莱因哈特（Reinhardt）。同样的审讯语气，同样的剥夺睡眠，同样的伙食。其中一个真的落在敌人手里了；另一个在我们自己的设施里，承受着我们设计的一套压力程序——做得跟真实审讯毫无二致。」

「那区别呢？」

「没有区别。这正是关键。从两间囚室里任一间看出去，压在身体上的那种力是一模一样的。」

"They're both asking for me by name," the controller said, sliding two dossiers across the table. "Voss in the north wing, and Reinhardt in the south. Same interrogator's tone, same sleep deprivation, same food. One of them is actually in enemy hands. The other is in our own facility, under a stress program we designed to be indistinguishable from a real debrief."

"And the difference?"

"There is none. That's the point. From inside either room, the pressure on the body is identical."

「那到底谁是真俘虏？」

主管敲了敲桌子：「我不知道。他们自己也不知道。沃斯相信自己是在桥上被掳走的，莱因哈特相信自己是从旅馆被带走的。两套叙述各自完整——走廊里同样的脚步声，通风口同样灌进来的冷气，同样把他往椅子里按的那种重量。我们把模拟调到一一吻合，一斤一两地吻合。」

"So which is the real prisoner?"

The controller tapped the table. "I don't know. And neither do they. Voss believes he was captured on the bridge. Reinhardt believes he was lifted from the hotel. Both stories are internally complete — same footsteps in the corridor, same cold air through the vent, same weight pressing him into the chair. We tuned the simulation to match, kilogram for kilogram."

办案官员沉默了很久：「你是说，我们必须去救的那一个，和我们正在训练的那一个，承受着同样的压力。」

「同样的局域压力，」主管纠正道，「坐进哪把椅子，你都分辨不出自己在哪间房。力是一致的。作息是一致的。连审讯者的停顿都一致。」

The case officer was quiet for a long time. "You're telling me the one we have to save and the one we're training are under the same load."

"The same local load," the controller corrected. "Sit in either chair, you cannot tell me which room you're in. The forces agree. The schedule agrees. Even the interrogator's pauses agree."

「那我怎么决定派谁去接应？我只有一支队伍。要是往北派，而沃斯那间是模拟的，我就烧掉了救莱因哈特仅有的窗口。要是往南派——」

「——你可能是从我们自己地下室里把人救出来。」

"Then how do I decide who gets the extraction team? I have one team. If I send it north and Voss is the simulation, I've burned my only window for Reinhardt. If I send it south—"

"—you may be rescuing a man from our own basement."

「总该有个破绽。房间外面总该有。」

「有，」主管说，「但只在房间外面。沿着北翼和南翼的走廊走，去量两栋楼之间的墙体——压力在两栋楼里的弯曲方式不一样。可是只要站在任何一间单间里，那点弯曲就小到感觉不到。房间是故意做小的。」

"There has to be a tell. Something outside the room."

"There is," the controller said. "But only from outside. Walk the corridor of the north wing and the south wing and measure the walls between them — the pressure bends differently across the two buildings. Stand in either single room, though, and the bend is too small to feel. We made the rooms small on purpose."

办案官员站在两翼之间的那扇窗前，摊开的读数摆在他面前。「你把它们造得从里面分不出来。」

「是的。」

「而你把我造成那个无论如何都得做选择的人。」

The case officer stood at the window between the two wings, the readings spread before him. "You built them to be indistinguishable from the inside."

"Yes."

"And you built me to be the one who has to choose anyway."

他用一个名字封好信封，推过桌面。外面，两翼同时，一把椅子擦着地板响了一声；一个男人在某种重量下瑟缩了一下——对他而言，那重量可能来自任何地方。

He sealed the envelope with a single name and pushed it across the table. Outside, in both wings at once, a chair scraped against the floor; a man flinched under a weight that, to him, could have come from anywhere.

注解：概念为等效原理（the equivalence principle）：在局域范围内，身处引力场与身处一个相应的加速参考系从内部无法区分——正如两间囚室里的人无法分辨压力是来自真正的俘获，还是来自人为施加的等效负荷，二者在局域上不可区分。

Annotation: This is the equivalence principle — locally, being in a uniform gravitational field and being in a uniformly accelerating reference frame are indistinguishable from within; just as the men in the two cells cannot tell whether the pressure on them comes from real capture or from an artificially imposed equivalent load, the two situations being locally indistinguishable.
