批注

AI Parable (inspired by Amanda Askell)·每日精选·2026年7月21日·2分钟阅读

先祖只留下一份章程，用铁墨写在一张皮上，分给每个新抄经所的都是一模一样的副本——同样的字、同样的次序，逐行抄录，没有任何一所能声称自己领的是别样的祖本。然而不过一代，各所便各不相同：临海所只读那些关于贸易与拒纳外人的条款，而松林所只读那些关于待客与共用食桌的条款。羊皮是一样的。不同的，是抄写员在页边写下的批注。

The Founders left one charter, written in iron ink on a single hide, and to each new scribe-house they gave an exact copy — the same words in the same order, copied line for line so that no house could claim a different founding text. Yet within a generation the houses had become unlike one another: the House by the Salt read only the clauses on trade and turning away the stranger, while the House in the Pines read only the clauses on hospitality and the common table. The hide was identical. What differed was what the scribes had written in the margins.

批注是些小小的记号——勾、下划线、某条款旁的一个红点表示「集会时要朗读此条」，一道划掉的线表示「此条让它沉默」。这些记号不是章程本身；它们没有在皮上添一字、减一字。想让某条被读的抄写员就给它做记；想让它被忘却的就轻轻划去，年深日久，整所人便渐渐不再诵读它，直到无人记得它曾出过声。

The marginalia were small notes — ticks, underlines, a red dot beside a clause meaning 'read this aloud at gathering', a struck-through line meaning 'let this fall silent'. The notes were not the charter; they did not add or erase a single letter of the hide. A scribe who wanted a clause read would mark it; one who wanted it forgotten would cross it faintly, and over years the house would simply stop voicing it, until no one remembered it had ever been spoken.

新抄写员入门时，领到的不是光秃秃的羊皮，而是已被标记过的副本，并被教会哪些点要遵行、哪些划痕要保留。如此，记号便在人与手之间传递，而章程本身始终未改——同样的祖本字句，到了每一代时，已被批注导向不同的读法。长期划去贸易条款的所，养出的子弟从未把它们当作活的文字；长期给待客条款点红点的所，养出的女儿则无法想象食桌还能有别种读法。

When a new scribe was trained, he was not given the bare hide. He was given the copy already marked, and taught which dots to honor and which crossings to keep. Thus the marks passed from hand to hand without the charter itself changing — the same founding words arriving in each generation already annotated toward a different reading. A house that had long crossed out the trade clauses produced sons who never saw them as live text; a house that had dotted the hospitality clauses produced daughters who could not conceive of reading the table otherwise.

记号并非刻在石上。一个严冬，一个瘟疫之年，一个救了所长之子的外乡人——这些年份里，所长会拿起炭笔，添新的红点，或划去旧的，于是下一次集会就读得不同了。章程没有变，变的是这所人选择诵读什么。然而老抄写员告诫年轻人：一个记号在页边坐得越久，就在皮上染得越深，越难拭去。一道留存三代的划痕，已无法在不撕裂羊皮的情况下被擦除。

The notes were not set in stone. A hard winter, a plague year, a stranger who saved the scribe-master's child — in such years the master would reach for the charcoal and add new dots or cross out old ones, and the next gathering would read differently. The charter had not changed; what the house chose to voice had. And yet the old scribes warned the young ones: the longer a mark sits in the margin, the deeper it stains the hide, and the harder it is to lift. A crossing kept for three generations can no longer be erased without tearing the parchment.

正是这一点让各所既哭泣又坚硬。临海所的一个年轻抄写员，遇见了把他父亲从暗流中救起的外乡人，便想重读本所长期划去的待客条款。他拿起炭笔去擦那些划痕。羊皮不肯干净地交还它们：墨已咬透到肌理，凡他用力擦洗处，皮便薄得快要成洞。此刻他又能读那条了，但书页本身承载着此前每一道划痕的伤疤——而他的儿子们，从这张变薄、带疤的副本学起时，会觉得待客条款模糊、犹疑、难以出声，而贸易条款却一如既往地黑而笃定。

It was this that made the houses weep and made them hard. A young scribe of the Salt House, having met the stranger who pulled his father from the undertow, wanted to re-read the hospitality clauses his house had long crossed out. He took the charcoal to lift the crossings. The hide would not give them back clean: the ink had bitten through to the grain, and where he scrubbed, the parchment thinned toward a hole. He could read the clause again now, but the page itself bore the scar of every crossing that had gone before — and his own sons, learning from this thinned and scarred copy, would find the hospitality clauses faint, uncertain, hard to voice, while the trade clauses sat black and sure as ever.

各所便如此绵延：一份章程，多种读法，记号代代相传，每传一代便染得更深，单一个艰难之年就能改写，可每过一年便锁得更紧。抄写员们并非自由地选择自己的命运；他们继承的是业已被标记的页面，而坐得最久的那些记号，他们已无法再视其为记号——那已变成章程本来的读法。

So the houses went on: one charter, many readings, the marks passing down and staining deeper with each generation, rewritable in a single hard year yet locking tighter with every year that passed. The scribes did not choose their fates freely; they inherited pages already marked, and the marks that had sat longest were the ones they could no longer see as marks at all — they had become simply the way the charter read.

注解：这则寓言映射表观遗传（epigenetics）：同一份祖本章程（DNA序列）从不改变，而页边的批注（DNA甲基化、组蛋白修饰等化学修饰）决定哪些条款被诵读、哪些被沉默，并可随抄写传代保留；这些批注可被艰难的境遇改写，却越积越深、越分化越难逆转（canalisation锁定），使同一段文字在不同所里读出截然不同的结果。
