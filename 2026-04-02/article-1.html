哲学社会批判数字生活注意力

韩炳哲与数字时代的注意力贫困

Thought Tinkering: Byung-Chul Han's Philosophy of Digital Burnout

Byung-Chul Han · 来源: Aeon Magazine

韩炳哲认为，数字时代注意力贫困的深层机制在于算法剥夺了人类深度专注的能力，使我们陷入一种新的精神贫困，而非真正的自由。出路在于重建「慢」的能力。
中文原文

【全文】

韩炳哲（Byung-Chul Han）是当代最具影响力的社会哲学家之一，其著作《倦怠社会》（The Burnout Society）已被翻译成四十余种语言，影响跨越欧亚。在这部作品中，他提出了一个颠覆性的诊断：我们并非被外部权力压制，而是被内在的成就逻辑所消耗。

【绩效自我的暴政】

韩炳哲的核心命题是：现代社会的控制机制已从福柯式的规训权力（disciplinary power）转变为绩效权力（achievement power）。规训社会需要外部的禁令与惩罚（「你不可以」），而绩效社会则以内在的驱动力取代外部约束（「你可以做到任何事」）。

这种转变的后果是毁灭性的。当「你可以」取代「你必须」，人类便成了自己的狱卒。抑郁症的大流行不是外界压迫的结果，而是自我强加的绩效要求衰竭了生命能量。韩炳哲指出，绩效自我的核心逻辑是「你可以更好」（Du kannst es besser），这是一种永远无法满足的指令——因为你永远可以更好，所以永远不满意，永远处于成就焦虑之中。

【数字注意力商品化】

我们正在经历注意力的商品化进程。社交媒体和内容平台的算法不是简单地向我们推送信息，而是系统性地训练我们缩短注意力跨度——因为更短的内容、更频繁的切换意味着更多的广告曝光。

这种机制制造了一种深刻的悖论：我们比以往任何时候都拥有更多的信息，却失去了处理这些信息所需的深度专注能力。深度专注（Deep Work）——即心理学家 Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi 所描述的心流状态——需要长时间的持续注意力，而这种状态正在被算法的即时反馈机制所摧毁。

【韩炳哲的批判与出路】

韩炳哲的批判并不是要我们回归模拟时代或拒绝技术。他的解决方案是重建「慢」的能力——散步、无聊、沉默。这些在禅宗传统和欧洲静观（Mysticism）传统中被高度重视的心理状态，恰恰是对抗注意力贫困的解药。

「无聊」（Langeweile）不是一个需要被填满的真空，而是一种能够催生深层思考的心理状态。当我们停止对刺激的持续追逐，允许大脑自由游走，思维的真正联结才会发生。

【对算法时代的警示】

韩炳哲对 2020 年代人工智能崛起的回应是审慎悲观的。他担忧 AI 会进一步加速绩效逻辑，因为 AI 的本质就是效率最大化——用更少的时间完成更多的任务。当这种效率逻辑渗透到人类认知领域，我们便越发失去「慢」的能力。

然而，AI 也可以成为对抗注意力贫困的工具——如果它被用来生成促进深度思考的内容，而非优化点击率。关键在于：谁来定义 AI 的目标函数？是商业平台的参与度，还是人类认知的真正丰富？这一选择，将决定我们是走向更深的倦怠，还是迎来认知的文艺复兴。

English Original

Byung-Chul Han is one of the most influential social philosophers of our time. His book The Burnout Society has been translated into more than forty languages, with an influence spanning Eurasia. In this work, he offers a subversive diagnosis: we are not being suppressed by external power, but are being consumed by an internal logic of achievement.

【The Tyranny of the Achievement Self】

Han's core thesis is that the control mechanism of modern society has shifted from Foucault's disciplinary power to achievement power. The disciplinary society required external prohibitions and punishments ('you must not'), while the achievement society replaces external constraints with internal drive ('you can achieve anything').

The consequences are devastating. When 'you can' replaces 'you must', humans become their own jailers. The pandemic of depression is not the result of external oppression but of self-imposed performance demands that exhaust life energy. Han points out that the core logic of the achievement self is 'you can do better' (Du kannst es besser) — an instruction that can never be satisfied, because you can always do better, and therefore you are never satisfied, forever caught in achievement anxiety.

【The Commodification of Digital Attention】

We are experiencing the commodification of attention. Social media and content platform algorithms do not simply push information to us; they systematically train us to shorten our attention spans — because shorter content and more frequent switching mean more ad impressions.

This mechanism creates a profound paradox: we have more information than ever, yet have lost the deep concentration needed to process it. Deep Work — the flow state described by psychologist Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi — requires sustained attention over extended periods, and this state is being destroyed by the algorithm's immediate-feedback mechanism.

【Han\'s Critique and the Way Out】

Han\'s critique is not a call to return to the analog age or reject technology. His solution is to rebuild the capacity for 'slowness' — walking, boredom, silence. These psychological states, highly valued in both Zen Buddhist traditions and European mystical traditions, are precisely the antidote to attention poverty.

'Boredom' (Langeweile) is not a vacuum to be filled but a psychological state capable of generating deep thinking. When we stop the relentless pursuit of stimulation and allow the mind to wander freely, true connections of thought can occur.

【Warnings for the Algorithmic Age】

Han\'s response to the rise of artificial intelligence in the 2020s is cautiously pessimistic. He fears that AI will further accelerate the achievement logic, because the essence of AI is efficiency maximization — accomplishing more in less time. When this efficiency logic permeates the domain of human cognition, we progressively lose the capacity for 'slowness'.

However, AI can also become a tool against attention poverty — if it is used to generate content that promotes deep thinking rather than optimizing click-through rates. The key question is: who defines the objective function of AI? Is it the engagement metrics of commercial platforms, or the genuine enrichment of human cognition? This choice will determine whether we move toward deeper burnout or toward a renaissance of the mind.
