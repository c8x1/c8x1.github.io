哲学社会批判数字生活注意力

韩炳哲与数字时代的注意力贫困

韩炳哲论算法时代的倦怠社会与注意力危机

Byung-Chul Han / 政治哲学学者·Zeit Online / 韩炳哲著作系列·2026年4月2日·1分钟阅读·原文

韩炳哲是当代最具影响力的公共哲学家之一，他对数字时代注意力贫困的批判触及了现代人最深层的焦虑：在一个信息过载的世界中，我们正在失去专注、沉思和深度思考的能力。这位韩裔德国学者以一系列精炼的哲学随笔——《倦怠社会》《透明社会》《爱欲之死》——在全球思想界引起了广泛的共鸣。

Byung-Chul Han is one of the most influential public philosophers of our time. His critique of attention poverty in the digital age touches upon the deepest anxieties of modern people: in a world of information overload, we are losing the ability to focus, contemplate, and think deeply. This Korean-German scholar has resonated widely across the global intellectual community with a series of refined philosophical essays -- The Burnout Society, The Transparency Society, and The Agony of Eros.

韩炳哲哲学的核心是对当代社会「肯定性」范式的批判。在他看来，工业社会的统治基于「你应该」的超我命令，运作机制是负罪感和强制。而当代的成就社会则运行在「你可以」的积极鼓励之上，表面上是自由和解放，实际上制造了一种更隐蔽的自我剥削。主体不再是被迫劳动，而是自愿地将自己推向极限。这种从「你应该」到「你可以」的转变，并没有带来真正的自由，反而导致了普遍的倦怠、抑郁和焦虑。

At the core of Han's philosophy is his critique of the "affirmative" paradigm in contemporary society. In his view, the domination of industrial society was based on the superego command of "you should," operating through guilt and coercion. The contemporary achievement society, by contrast, runs on the positive encouragement of "you can" -- ostensibly about freedom and liberation, but actually producing a more insidious form of self-exploitation. The subject is no longer forced to labor but voluntarily pushes itself to the limit. This shift from "you should" to "you can" has not brought genuine freedom; instead, it has led to widespread burnout, depression, and anxiety.

在《倦怠社会》中，韩炳哲提出了一种新的病理学分类：抑郁症、注意力缺陷多动障碍、倦怠综合征和疲劳——这些都是「肯定性过量」的症状。当一切都变得可能和可获取时，当「不」被消除只剩下「是」时，人类精神就会崩溃。注意力贫困是这种肯定性过量的直接表现：当每时每刻都被刺激填满时，注意力就不再是我们可以自由支配的资源，而是被平台算法所捕获和利用的商品。

In The Burnout Society, Han proposes a new pathological classification: depression, attention deficit hyperactivity disorder, burnout syndrome, and fatigue -- these are all symptoms of "excess positivity." When everything becomes possible and accessible, when "no" is eliminated and only "yes" remains, the human spirit collapses. Attention poverty is the direct manifestation of this excess positivity: when every moment is filled with stimuli, attention is no longer a resource we can freely dispose of, but a commodity captured and exploited by platform algorithms.

韩炳哲对数字技术和社交媒体的批判尤为深刻。他将社交媒体的「点赞」文化视为肯定性暴政的完美体现——一个没有否定性、没有摩擦、没有阻力的光滑世界。在这个世界中，他者被消解为同者的镜像，真正的相遇变得不可能。算法剥夺了人类深度专注的能力：推荐系统精确地为我们定制信息流，使我们在舒适的回音室中自满而不自知。我们以为自己看到了整个世界，实际上只是看到了算法认为我们想看的部分。

Han's critique of digital technology and social media is particularly incisive. He views the "like" culture of social media as the perfect embodiment of the tyranny of positivity -- a smooth world without negation, without friction, without resistance. In this world, the Other is dissolved into a mirror image of the Same, and genuine encounters become impossible. Algorithms strip away the human capacity for deep focus: recommendation systems precisely curate information feeds for us, leaving us complacent in comfortable echo chambers without our own awareness. We think we are seeing the whole world, but in reality we are only seeing what algorithms think we want to see.

在政治层面，韩炳哲在《精神政治学》中分析了新自由主义权力如何通过数据收集、行为预测和情感管理来统治主体。不同于全景监狱式的外部监控，当代的权力运作从内部进行——它不需要强迫你做什么，只需要了解你的欲望和恐惧，然后在你自愿的数字行为中实现治理。这种「精神政治」比任何外部强制都更加有效，也更加难以抵抗。

At the political level, in Psychopolitics, Han analyzes how neoliberal power governs subjects through data collection, behavioral prediction, and emotional management. Unlike the panoptic-style external surveillance, contemporary power operates from within -- it does not need to force you to do anything; it only needs to understand your desires and fears, and then achieve governance through your voluntary digital behavior. This "psychopolitics" is more effective than any external coercion, and also far more difficult to resist.

重建深度注意力的能力需要刻意的努力和制度的支持。韩炳哲建议培养「无聊」的能力——在不被刺激填充的空白时间中让思想自然涌现。他呼吁重建深度阅读的习惯——不是浏览和扫描，而是缓慢地、反复地与一个文本对话。最重要的是，他提醒我们认识到真正的知识不是信息的累积，而是一种通过沉思、质疑和批判性思考而获得的深层理解。在信息泛滥的时代，保持思考的能力本身就是一种抵抗。

Rebuilding the capacity for deep attention requires deliberate effort and institutional support. Han suggests cultivating the ability to be "bored" -- letting thoughts naturally emerge during blank time that is not filled with stimuli. He calls for rebuilding the habit of deep reading -- not browsing and scanning, but slowly and repeatedly engaging in dialogue with a text. Most importantly, he reminds us that genuine knowledge is not the accumulation of information, but a deep understanding acquired through contemplation, questioning, and critical thinking. In an age of information overload, maintaining the ability to think is itself a form of resistance.

韩炳哲对「透明社会」的批判揭示了更深层的问题。当代文化将透明视为最高价值——一切都应该是可见的、可获取的、可测量的。但韩炳哲认为，真正的思想需要不透明——需要秘密、需要沉思的空间、需要不被立即消费的思考时间。当一切都被迫透明化时，深度思考本身就成为不可能。社交媒体上的「公开日记」文化、工作中的「全天候在线」期望、教育中的「学习分析」——这些看似进步的做法实际上在消灭思想生长所需的黑暗和安静。

Han's critique of the "transparency society" reveals a deeper problem. Contemporary culture treats transparency as the highest value -- everything should be visible, accessible, and measurable. But Han argues that genuine thought requires opacity -- it needs secrets, spaces for contemplation, and time for thinking that is not immediately consumed. When everything is forced into transparency, deep thinking itself becomes impossible. The "public diary" culture on social media, the "always-on" expectation in the workplace, "learning analytics" in education -- these seemingly progressive practices are actually eliminating the darkness and quiet that thought needs to grow.

韩炳哲对当代「肯定性」文化的分析在AI时代获得了新的意义。不同于工业社会以「你应该」的超我命令运作，当代成就社会以「你可以」的积极鼓励运行。这种表面上解放性的话语实际上制造了自我剥削的主体——我们自愿地在数字平台上消耗注意力、生产数据，误以为自己在自由选择，实际上是在参与一种更精细的控制。点赞文化是这种肯定性暴政的完美体现——一个没有否定性、没有摩擦、没有阻力的光滑世界。在这个世界中，他者被消解为同者的镜像，真正的相遇变得不可能。

Regarding how to maintain the capacity for thinking in the digital age, Han offers several key suggestions. Cultivate the ability to be "bored" -- letting thoughts naturally emerge during blank time that is not filled with stimuli. Rebuild the habit of deep reading -- not browsing and scanning, but slowly and repeatedly engaging in dialogue with a text. Most importantly, recognize that genuine knowledge is not the accumulation of information, but a deep understanding acquired through contemplation, questioning, and critical thinking. Han reminds us that in a society where everything is being made transparent and datafied, genuine freedom and the capacity to think require opaque spaces -- secrets, pauses, and thinking time that is not immediately consumed. In an age of information overload, learning not to retrieve -- learning to endure not knowing -- may be the first step toward genuine knowledge. Han's philosophy provides us with a guide for maintaining intellectual independence amid the digital flood -- not by rejecting technology, but by not allowing technology to completely dominate our spiritual lives.
