技术的追问：从海德格尔到人工智能时代

从海德格尔到AI时代的技术追问

Martin Heidegger / 当代解读·Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy·2026年3月19日·20分钟阅读·原文

海德格尔对技术的追问在人工智能时代获得了全新的意义。他关于技术「座架」（Gestell）的概念——技术不仅是一种工具，更是一种揭示世界的方式——为我们理解AI如何重塑人类与世界的关系提供了深刻的哲学框架。在AI无处不在的2026年，重新阅读海德格尔的技术哲学，我们会惊讶于它的预见性和解释力。

Heidegger's questioning of technology has acquired entirely new significance in the age of artificial intelligence. His concept of technology as 'Enframing' (Gestell) — technology not merely as a tool but as a way of revealing the world — provides a profound philosophical framework for understanding how AI is reshaping the relationship between humanity and the world. In 2026, as AI permeates everything, re-reading Heidegger's philosophy of technology, we are struck by its prescience and explanatory power.

在《技术的追问》这篇演讲中，海德格尔提出了一个反直觉的观点：现代技术的本质不是技术性的。他将现代技术的本质规定为「座架」——一种将万物（包括人自身）纳入可计算、可利用的框架的方式。在座架的支配下，自然不再作为自在的存在显现，而是作为「备用资源」（Bestand）出现——河流变成了水力发电的资源，大地变成了矿产的储备，森林变成了木材的供应。更关键的是，人本身也被座架所捕获——我们以技术的方式看待世界，也以技术的方式看待自己。

In the lecture 'The Question Concerning Technology,' Heidegger advances a counterintuitive thesis: the essence of modern technology is not itself technological. He defines the essence of modern technology as 'Enframing' (Gestell) — a way of ordering all things, including human beings themselves, into a calculable, exploitable framework. Under the dominion of Enframing, nature no longer appears as something that exists on its own terms but presents itself as 'standing-reserve' (Bestand) — rivers become resources for hydroelectric power, the earth becomes a mineral reserve, forests become timber supplies. More crucially, human beings themselves are captured by Enframing — we see the world in technological terms, and we see ourselves in technological terms as well.

AI是座架的极致体现。当AI系统将人类的每一次点击、每一次搜索、每一次社交互动都转化为可分析、可预测、可优化的数据时，人类存在本身就被「资源化」了。我们不再只是技术的使用者，我们自己成为了技术系统的原材料——为推荐算法提供训练数据，为广告系统提供注意力，为预测模型提供行为模式。海德格尔所警告的「人被技术所座架」的处境，在AI时代以前所未有的规模实现。更深层的问题是：当AI系统开始对我们的思维模式进行建模和预测时，我们的思想本身是否也变成了「备用资源」？

AI is the ultimate embodiment of Enframing. When AI systems convert every human click, every search, every social interaction into data that can be analyzed, predicted, and optimized, human existence itself is 'resourcified.' We are no longer merely users of technology; we have become the raw material of technological systems — providing training data for recommendation algorithms, attention for advertising systems, behavioral patterns for predictive models. The condition Heidegger warned of — 'humanity enframed by technology' — is being realized at unprecedented scale in the AI era. A deeper question still: when AI systems begin modeling and predicting our thought patterns, have our very thoughts also become 'standing-reserve'?

然而，海德格尔并非一个简单的技术悲观主义者。他强调，座架虽然是一种危险，但危险中也包含着救赎的可能。关键在于我们是否能够以不同的方式「揭示」世界——不把万物仅仅视为可利用的资源，而是让它们以自己的方式显现。在海德格尔看来，艺术、诗歌和沉思是抵抗技术座架的方式——它们不是将世界纳入计算框架，而是让世界以其不可还原的丰富性呈现。一件艺术品不是「有用」的，但它打开了一种理解世界的全新方式；一首诗不提供信息，但它让我们听到了语言的原始力量。

Heidegger, however, was no simple technological pessimist. He stressed that while Enframing is a danger, that danger also harbors the possibility of salvation. The key lies in whether we can 'reveal' the world in a different way — not treating all things merely as exploitable resources, but letting them appear in their own way. In Heidegger's view, art, poetry, and contemplation are modes of resistance against technological Enframing — they do not reduce the world to a calculative framework, but let the world present itself in its irreducible richness. A work of art is not 'useful,' yet it opens up an entirely new way of understanding the world; a poem provides no information, yet it lets us hear the primordial power of language.

在AI时代实践海德格尔的技术哲学，意味着培养一种「让事物存在」（Gelassenheit）的态度——不是拒绝技术，而是不让技术完全定义我们与世界的关系。这包括：在使用AI工具的同时保持对技术框架的批判意识——意识到Google搜索结果不是「知识」而是算法排序的信息；在数据化的世界中保留不可被数据化的体验——一次不被拍照记录的日落、一场不被分享的对话；以及最重要的是，认识到人类存在的意义不在于被计算和被优化，而在于与世界建立一种开放的、非工具性的关系。海德格尔提醒我们，技术的危险不在于技术本身，而在于我们忘记了还有其他与世界相处的方式。

To practice Heidegger's philosophy of technology in the AI era means cultivating an attitude of 'letting things be' (Gelassenheit) — not rejecting technology, but not allowing it to entirely define our relationship with the world. This includes: maintaining critical awareness of the technological framework while using AI tools — recognizing that Google search results are not 'knowledge' but algorithmically sorted information; preserving experiences that cannot be datafied in a datafied world — a sunset not photographed, a conversation not shared; and most importantly, recognizing that the meaning of human existence lies not in being calculated and optimized, but in establishing an open, non-instrumental relationship with the world. Heidegger reminds us that the danger of technology lies not in technology itself, but in our forgetting that there are other ways of relating to the world.
