Inference Becomes the Next AI Chip Battleground

GPUs will continue to dominate, analysts say, but growing enterprise demand for inference is creating mainstream opportunities for alternatives.

Wylie Wong , Chips and Hardware Writer February 23, 2026

7 Min Read A recent Futurum Group survey reported GPUs at 58% of data center compute spending in 2025, while for 2026 XPUs (neither GPUs nor CPUs) are expected to lead growth at 22%, outpacing GPUs (19%) and CPUs (14%). Alamy AI inference – running trained models to deliver outputs – has become the new profit center for the industry. Major chipmakers are racing to optimize for latency, power, and cost, pushing a shift toward pairing general-purpose GPUs with specialized silicon and acquiring the engineering talent to build it.
