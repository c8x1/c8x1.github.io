The Paradox of Retrieval: Byung-Chul Han's Critique of Digital Capitalism

他者、同一性与数字资本主义的逻辑张力 — Tingting He · Humanities and Social Sciences Communications (Nature Portfolio)

韩炳哲提出，应通过重构'他者'的异质性来抵抗数字资本主义施加的同质化压力。然而，其批判逻辑本身依赖于辩证法、速度和沉思——这些恰恰是数字资本主义框架内的产物，使他的方案陷入无法企及的悖论之中。

中文原文

在韩炳哲的思想图谱中，"他者"（der Andere）的否定性和中介性是"本真自我"（authentisches Selbst）得以存在的根基。然而，在数字资本主义的支配逻辑下，他者被扁平化为"同一"（das Same），抹去了本真自我在"常人"（das Man）中发现自己、超越自己的可能性。

这一命题的核心张力在于：数字技术本应作为连接不同个体的媒介，却在资本逻辑的驱动下将一切差异压缩为同质化的流量、消费与绩效数据。海德格尔意义上充满张力的"之间"（Zwischen），被平滑的界面和算法推荐所取代。他者的否定性——那种质询、拒绝、唤醒的力量——在点赞与关注的正向循环中被彻底中和。

随之而来的是本真自我的双重危机：一方面，"他者"在数字同质化中趋于消失，丧失了其唤醒功能；另一方面，本真自我也因失去对立面而陷入自恋式的封闭循环。在一个充满"可能自我"却缺乏"他者性"的世界里，个体日益感到倦怠与空虚，却找不到确切的原因。

韩炳哲开出的药方是通过沉思（Kontemplation）和叙事（Narreation）来中断数字资本主义的伪加速，恢复他者的否定性功能，重振本真自我。然而，这一方案本身蕴含深刻的内在悖论：

首先，沉思作为一种慢下来、脱离数字网络的行为，在实践层面依赖于数字设备方能组织和传播——这构成了工具对目的的反讽。其次，速度（Speed）作为现代性的核心范畴，既是他所批判的数字资本主义的产物，也是他用以揭示这一主义运作机制的认识论工具，依赖速度来批判速度，逻辑上难以自洽。

最后，辩证法（Dialektik）本身是黑格尔-马克思传统的产物，具有强烈的现代性特征，将其应用于批判后现代数字资本主义，其理论根基本身就游移不定。韩炳哲的批判因而成为一面精致的镜子：它照亮了数字时代的病症，却无法为患者开出真正超越其病因的处方。

English Original

In Byung-Chul Han's framework, the negativity and mediatory nature of the Other (der Andere) serves as the foundation for the authentic self. Yet under digital capitalism, the Other is flattened into the Same, erasing the possibility for the authentic self to discover and transcend itself within das Man (the They).

The paradox runs deep: digital technology — designed to connect individuals — has been colonized by capital, compressing all difference into homogeneous flows of data, consumption, and performance metrics. What Heidegger called the tense 'between' (Zwischen) has been replaced by smooth interfaces and algorithmic recommendation. The Other's negativity — that power to question, refuse, and awaken — is neutralized in the affirmative loops of likes and follows.

The authentic self now faces a dual crisis: the Other has nearly vanished from digital homogeneity, losing its awakening function; and the authentic self, deprived of its antagonistic counterpart, spirals into narcissistic enclosure. In a world of 'possible selves' without Otherness, individuals feel increasingly exhausted without knowing why.

Han prescribes contemplation and narration to interrupt digital capitalism's pseudo-acceleration and restore the Other's negativity. But the prescription reproduces the disease: contemplation requires digital tools to organize and disseminate; speed — which Han critiques — is also the very instrument of his critique; and dialectics, a product of the Hegelian-Marxist tradition, provides an uncertain foundation for attacking post-modern digital capitalism.

Han's critique is thus a mirror: it illuminates the pathology of the digital age but cannot write a prescription that truly transcends its own causes. The logical tensions are not flaws — they are the condition of our contemporary philosophical predicament.
