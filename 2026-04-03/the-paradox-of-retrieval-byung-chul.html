检索的悖论：韩炳哲对数字资本主义的批判

他者、同一性与数字资本主义的逻辑张力

Tingting He·Humanities and Social Sciences Communications (Nature Portfolio)·2026年4月3日·2分钟阅读·原文

在全面数字化的时代，我们面临一个深刻的悖论：前所未有地，如此多的人能够接触到如此多的信息——然而，正如韩裔德国哲学家韩炳哲所言，我们从未如此缺乏真正的知识。信息不等于知识，检索不等于理解，连接不等于沟通。这位当代最具影响力的公共哲学家直指数字资本主义的核心矛盾：我们检索得越多，实际知道的越少。

In the age of total digitization, we face a profound paradox: never before have so many people had access to so much information -- yet, as Korean-German philosopher Byung-Chul Han argues, we have never been so lacking in genuine knowledge. Information is not knowledge, retrieval is not understanding, and connection is not communication. This contemporary philosopher, one of the most influential public intellectuals today, strikes at the core contradiction of digital capitalism: the more we retrieve, the less we actually know.

韩炳哲将我们的时代描述为「数字过度活跃」的时代——一个以持续连接取代真正理解的社会。我们以为自己知道得更多，因为我们能够获取更多信息。但获取并不等于理解。「检索已经成为一种逃避形式，」韩炳哲写道，「我们检索信息不是为了深化知识，而是为了逃避思考的劳动。」在Google和ChatGPT触手可及的时代，我们不再需要记住任何东西，也不再需要深入思考任何问题——只需搜索即可。但这种便利的代价是深层认知能力的退化：我们正在失去沉思、质疑和独立思考的能力。

Han describes our era as one of "digital hyperactivity" -- a society that has replaced genuine understanding with constant connectivity. We believe we know more because we can access more information. But access is not understanding. "Retrieval has become a form of avoidance," Han writes. "We retrieve information not to deepen our knowledge, but to escape the labor of thinking." In an age where Google and ChatGPT are always at our fingertips, we no longer need to remember anything or think deeply about any question -- just search. But the price of this convenience is the degradation of deep cognitive abilities: we are losing the capacity for contemplation, questioning, and independent thinking.

现代数字平台在架构上被设计为阻止深度思考。无限滚动、通知提醒、算法推荐——每一个机制都被精心设计以捕获和碎片化注意力。用户在社交媒体上的平均会话时间只有几分钟，但每天会打开数十次——这种碎片化的使用模式使任何形式的深度沉浸都不可能发生。与此形成对比的是古典沉思的理想——缓慢阅读一本困难的著作，让思想在安静中展开，允许困惑和不确定性的存在。韩炳哲指出，数字资本主义的注意力经济恰恰在摧毁这种沉思的能力。

Modern digital platforms are architecturally designed to prevent deep thinking. Infinite scrolling, notification alerts, algorithmic recommendations -- every mechanism is carefully engineered to capture and fragment attention. The average user session on social media lasts only a few minutes, but they open it dozens of times per day -- this fragmented usage pattern makes any form of deep immersion impossible. In contrast stands the classical ideal of contemplation -- slowly reading a difficult work, letting thoughts unfold in silence, allowing for confusion and uncertainty to exist. Han points out that the attention economy of digital capitalism is precisely what is destroying this capacity for contemplation.

韩炳哲对「透明社会」的批判揭示了更深层的问题。当代文化将透明视为最高价值——一切都应该是可见的、可获取的、可测量的。但韩炳哲认为，真正的思想需要不透明——需要秘密、需要沉思的空间、需要不被立即消费的思考时间。当一切都被迫透明化时，深度思考本身就成为不可能。社交媒体上的「公开日记」文化、工作中的「全天候在线」期望、教育中的「学习分析」——这些看似进步的做法实际上在消灭思想生长所需的黑暗和安静。

Han's critique of the "transparency society" reveals a deeper problem. Contemporary culture treats transparency as the highest value -- everything should be visible, accessible, and measurable. But Han argues that genuine thought requires opacity -- it needs secrets, spaces for contemplation, and time for thinking that is not immediately consumed. When everything is forced into transparency, deep thinking itself becomes impossible. The "public diary" culture on social media, the "always-on" expectation in the workplace, "learning analytics" in education -- these seemingly progressive practices are actually eliminating the darkness and quiet that thought needs to grow.

在数字时代的「肯定性」文化中，韩炳哲发现了一种新的统治形式。不同于工业社会以「你应该」的超我命令运作，当代成就社会以「你可以」的积极鼓励运行。这种表面上解放性的话语实际上制造了自我剥削的主体——我们自愿地在数字平台上消耗注意力、生产数据，误以为自己在自由选择，实际上是在参与一种更精细的控制。点赞文化是这种肯定性暴政的完美体现——一个没有否定性、没有摩擦、没有阻力的光滑世界。

In the "affirmative" culture of the digital age, Han identifies a new form of domination. Unlike industrial society, which operated through the superego command of "you should," the contemporary achievement society runs on the positive encouragement of "you can." This seemingly liberating discourse actually produces self-exploiting subjects -- we voluntarily consume attention and produce data on digital platforms, mistakenly believing we are making free choices, when in fact we are participating in a more refined form of control. The "like" culture is the perfect embodiment of this tyranny of positivity -- a smooth world without negation, without friction, without resistance.

对于如何在数字时代保持思考能力，韩炳哲提出了几个关键建议：培养「无聊」的能力——在不被刺激填充的空白时间中让思想自然涌现；重建深度阅读的习惯——不是浏览和扫描，而是缓慢地、反复地与一个文本对话；以及最重要的，认识到真正的知识不是信息的累积，而是一种通过沉思、质疑和批判性思考而获得的深层理解。在信息泛滥的时代，学会不去检索——学会忍受不知道——可能是通向真正知识的第一步。

Regarding how to maintain the capacity for thinking in the digital age, Han offers several key suggestions: cultivate the ability to be "bored" -- let thoughts naturally emerge in unstimulated blank time; rebuild the habit of deep reading -- not browsing and scanning, but slowly and repeatedly engaging in dialogue with a text; and most importantly, recognize that genuine knowledge is not the accumulation of information, but a deep understanding acquired through contemplation, questioning, and critical thinking. In an age of information overload, learning not to retrieve -- learning to endure not knowing -- may be the first step toward genuine knowledge.
