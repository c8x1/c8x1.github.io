检索的悖论：韩炳哲对数字资本主义的批判

他者、同一性与数字资本主义的逻辑张力

Tingting He·Humanities and Social Sciences Communications (Nature Portfolio)·2026年4月3日·2分钟阅读·原文

English Original

The Paradox of Retrieval: Digital Capitalism's Contradiction
In the age of total digitization, we face a profound paradox. Never before have so many people had access to so much information — and yet, byung-chul han argues, we have never been less capable of genuine knowledge.
The Korean-German philosopher's latest work strikes at a central contradiction of contemporary digital capitalism: the more we retrieve, the less we actually know.
The Information Paradox
Han describes our era as one of "digital hyperactivity" — a society in which constant connectivity has replaced genuine understanding with the mere appearance of knowledge. We believe we know more because we can access more. But access is not comprehension.
"Retrieval has become a form of avoidance," Han writes. "We retrieve information not to deepen our knowledge but to avoid the labour of thinking."
The Architecture of Distraction
Modern digital platforms are architecturally designed to prevent deep thought. The infinite scroll, the notification ping, the algorithmic recommendation — each is engineered to capture and fragment attention.
Against this stands the classical ideal of contemplation — the slow reading of a difficult text, the patient unfolding of an argument over hours or days. For Han, this contemplative stance is not merely aesthetic but political: it is a form of resistance against the total commodification of attention.
The Burnout Society's Next Phase
Han first gained prominence with his concept of the "burnout society" — a society in which the neoliberal imperative to perform has produced a population of exhausted, depressed, self-exploiting subjects. The paradox of retrieval is, in many ways, the digital extension of this burnout dynamic.
We are not forced to retrieve information. We volunteer to consume it, mistaking the dopamine hit of a news update for genuine intellectual nourishment.
Resistance and the Slow Internet
What is the response? Han points to contemplative practices — not as Luddite rejection of technology but as selective, intentional engagement. The Slow Food movement offers a model: deliberate consumption of fewer, higher-quality inputs.
In the digital realm, this might mean: maintaining focused reading practices, resisting the pull of algorithmic feeds, creating physical and temporal spaces for genuine contemplation.
The stakes are high. Without such resistance, Han warns, we risk not merely personal burnout but the atrophy of the critical faculties on which democratic societies depend.
The Alternative: Contemplative Retrieval
Genuine knowledge requires what Han calls "the interval" — the pause between receiving information and responding to it. This interval is where thinking happens. Digital capitalism's greatest achievement may be the systematic elimination of this space.
The solution is not to retrieve less, but to retrieve differently: with intention, with attention, with the willingness to let knowledge take time.
In the end, the paradox of retrieval is a paradox only if we mistake information for knowledge, access for understanding, and speed for wisdom.

中文原文

在全面数字化的时代，我们面临一个深刻的悖论：人类从未拥有如此丰富的信息获取渠道，却从未如此缺乏真正的认知能力。韩炳哲（Byung-Chul Han）在其系列著作中系统地揭示了这一困境——数字资本主义不仅没有解放我们的认知，反而通过「检索」逻辑彻底改变了我们与世界的关系。

韩炳哲指出，数字化社会的核心运作机制是「检索」（Retrieval）而非「沉思」（Contemplation）。在搜索引擎和推荐算法构建的信息生态中，一切事物都被转化为可检索的数据点。知识的获取不再是缓慢的、需要耐心和深度的过程，而变成了即时的、表浅的信息抓取。这种转变看似提高了效率，实则摧毁了深度思考的能力。

数字资本主义的另一个核心机制是「平滑」（Smoothness）。从iPhone的玻璃面板到社交媒体的无限滚动，平滑的美学消除了摩擦和阻力。韩炳哲认为，正是这种摩擦和阻力的缺失，导致了「他者」（The Other）的消失。当一切都被平滑化处理，当我们不再遭遇真正不同的、具有挑战性的他者时，我们的认知能力便开始萎缩。

韩炳哲将当代社会诊断为「倦怠社会」（Burnout Society）。与福柯的规训社会不同，倦怠社会的主体不是被外部力量压迫的，而是自我剥削的。在「你可以做到一切」的绩效主义口号下，个体不断自我优化、自我鞭策，最终陷入深度倦怠。社交媒体上的「点赞」机制进一步强化了这一逻辑——我们的自我价值不再来自内在的确认，而是来自外部的数据化反馈。

面对这一困境，韩炳哲提出的出路并非简单地拒绝技术，而是重新找回「否定性」（Negativity）的力量——那些不可被检索、不可被量化、不可被消费的体验和关系。只有在与真正他者的相遇中，在深度的无聊和闲暇中，我们才能重建被数字资本主义摧毁的认知和存在能力。
